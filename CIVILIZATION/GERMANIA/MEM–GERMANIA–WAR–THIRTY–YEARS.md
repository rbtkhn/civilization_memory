────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Thirty Years’ War as **Germania’s civilizational
near-death event**: the conflict that shattered population, economy,
and political coherence, imprinting a permanent memory of vulnerability,
fragmentation, and the catastrophic cost of ideological war.

For Germania, this war is not formative glory but **trauma encoded as
discipline**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL FRAGMENTATION AT ENTRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Germania enters the war without unified sovereignty.

Conditions:
• Patchwork of principalities
• Weak imperial enforcement
• Confessional fragmentation
• External intervention pathways

No central authority controls escalation.

War arrives through internal fracture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. IDEOLOGICAL WAR AS SYSTEM COLLAPSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Confessional absolutism dissolves restraint.

Dynamics:
• Religion replaces prudence
• Local conflicts escalate uncontrollably
• Violence decouples from governance

War ceases to be instrumental.
It becomes self-perpetuating destruction.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FOREIGN PENETRATION & SOVEREIGNTY EROSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
German territory becomes a battlespace for others.

Patterns:
• Repeated foreign armies
• Occupation and pillage
• Civilian devastation
• Economic annihilation

Germania fights, but does not control the war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DEMOGRAPHIC & MATERIAL CATASTROPHE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Losses are civilizational in scale.

Consequences:
• Massive population decline
• Agricultural collapse
• Urban decay
• Administrative regression

Replacement capacity collapses.

This is the inverse of later Prussian discipline.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. WESTPHALIAN OUTCOME: SURVIVAL WITHOUT STRENGTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Peace ends annihilation, not weakness.

Results:
• Sovereignty fragmented
• External guarantees imposed
• Internal restraint codified
• Expansion abandoned

Survival is achieved at the cost of ambition.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war becomes a **negative template**.

Encoded lessons:
• Ideological war is existentially dangerous
• Fragmentation invites devastation
• Restraint must precede ambition
• Order must be administrative, not confessional

Fear becomes a stabilizing memory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war reshapes German strategic DNA.

Outcomes:
• Obsession with order
• Aversion to religious universalism
• Emphasis on discipline and administration
• Long delay in unification

Germania learns survival before power.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Fragmentation invites annihilation.
• Ideological absolutism destroys replacement capacity.
• Sovereignty without discipline is fatal.
• Trauma precedes order.
• The Thirty Years’ War teaches Germania what must never recur.

Tension preserved without verdict.
