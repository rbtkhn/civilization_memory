────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Charlemagne as the **foundational sacral monarch**
of Germania: the ruler who transformed fragmented Germanic lordship
into a disciplined, Christian-imperial power structure.

For Germania, Charlemagne represents **order imposed through conquest,
discipline, and faith**, not consensual federation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GERMANIC POWER CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Charlemagne’s base rests in Austrasian and Rhine-centered power.

Key features:
• Warrior retinues
• Land-for-service bonds
• Personal loyalty over civic identity

Authority flows downward from conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SAXON WARS & COERCIVE INTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Saxon Wars define German imperial formation.

Characteristics:
• Forced Christianization
• Destruction of tribal autonomy
• Mass punishment and deportation
• Sacred violence as statecraft

Integration occurs through submission.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SACRAL MONARCHY & DISCIPLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kingship fuses sword and altar.

Core elements:
• Christian law as obedience code
• Royal authority as divinely sanctioned
• Suppression of pagan legitimacy systems

Faith becomes command infrastructure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ADMINISTRATIVE COMMAND STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Governance emphasizes hierarchy.

Mechanisms:
• Counts as military-administrative agents
• Royal inspections
• Capitular enforcement

Order precedes local autonomy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. IMPERIAL IDENTITY FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Germania internalizes empire as discipline, not universality.

Outcomes:
• Obedience culture
• Military professionalism
• Sacral legitimacy over civic law

Imperial memory emphasizes command.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. POST-CHARLEMAGNE TRAJECTORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After fragmentation:
• East Francia retains imperial logic
• Sacral kingship persists
• Foundations laid for the Holy Roman Empire

German imperial identity endures without unity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Germania forms through coercive unification.
• Faith functions as enforcement multiplier.
• Discipline precedes political pluralism.
• Empire is remembered as command structure.
• Charlemagne anchors German sacral authority.

Tension preserved without verdict.
