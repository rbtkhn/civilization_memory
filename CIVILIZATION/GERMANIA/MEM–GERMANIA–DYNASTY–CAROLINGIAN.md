────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Carolingian dynasty as the **disciplinary founder**
of German imperial order: the lineage through which coercive kingship,
Christian law, and hierarchical command crystallized in East Francia.

For Germania, the Carolingians represent **state formation through
conquest, obedience, and sacral authority**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. AUSTRASIAN CORE & MILITARY BASIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Carolingian power is rooted east of the Rhine.

Key features:
• Austrasian warrior aristocracy
• Land-for-service bonds
• Personal loyalty to the king

Authority is militarized from inception.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CHARLEMAGNE & IMPERIAL DISCIPLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Charlemagne institutionalizes coercive unity.

Defining traits:
• Forced integration of Saxons
• Christianization as command tool
• Suppression of tribal autonomy

Imperial identity equals obedience.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SACRAL KINGSHIP & LEGAL COMMAND
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kingship fuses divine sanction with enforcement.

Mechanisms:
• Capitularies as obedience law
• Royal inspections
• Clerical enforcement of loyalty

Faith functions as governance infrastructure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. EAST FRANCIA & POST-DYNASTIC CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After Verdun:
• East Francia retains Carolingian structure
• Kingship becomes elective but sacral
• Foundations laid for Holy Roman Empire

Dynasty ends; imperial logic persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. GERMAN IMPERIAL MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Germania inherits:
• Command-first governance
• Sacral legitimacy over civic consent
• Enduring tension between unity and autonomy

Empire is remembered as hierarchy, not universality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• German imperial order forms through coercion.
• Christianity multiplies enforcement capacity.
• Dynastic collapse does not erase command logic.
• Carolingians anchor German sacral monarchy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
