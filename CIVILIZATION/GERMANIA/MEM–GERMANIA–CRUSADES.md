────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Crusades as Germania’s imperial distraction:
a theater that exposed the Holy Roman Empire’s inability to translate
imperial title into coherent external action.

For Germania, the Crusades reveal authority without unity.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EMPEROR WITHOUT EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
German emperors participate episodically.

Structural problems:
• Decentralized princes
• Competing loyalties
• Papal rivalry
• Weak command coherence

Germania enters the Crusades
as idea, not system.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. NORTHERN CRUSADES & INTERNALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
German crusading energy shifts eastward.

Key developments:
• Baltic Crusades
• Expansion against pagan Europe
• Fusion of crusade with colonization

Crusading becomes internal empire-building.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. PAPAL–IMPERIAL TENSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Crusades deepen Rome–Germania rivalry.

Consequences:
• Undermined imperial authority
• Competing claims of universality
• Strategic incoherence abroad

Germania cannot command a crusade
without papal sanction.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL PATTERN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Crusades reinforce German patterns.

Enduring effects:
• Fragmentation persists
• External projection remains limited
• Authority remains juridical, not mobilizational

Germania learns constraint, not expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Imperial titles require institutional depth.
• Fragmentation limits external ambition.
• Crusading energy can be redirected inward.
• Germania’s strength is legal, not expeditionary.
• The Crusades expose imperial hollowness.

Tension preserved without verdict.
