────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the War of the Austrian Succession as **Germania’s
replacement-depth validation conflict**: the war in which Prussia
demonstrated that disciplined administration, rapid mobilization, and
limited objectives could overcome dynastic legitimacy claims and
numerical inferiority within the German space.

For Germania, this war is not about Austria’s crown.
It is about **Prussia’s arrival**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war is triggered by dynastic transition.

Conditions:
• Death of Emperor Charles VI
• Pragmatic Sanction contested
• Multipolar coalition opportunism
• Fragmented German political landscape

Legitimacy claims invite force testing.

Succession exposes structural weakness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PRUSSIAN INITIATIVE & SILESIAN GAMBIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Prussia acts first.

Characteristics:
• Rapid mobilization
• Clear territorial objective (Silesia)
• Administrative readiness
• Limited political ambition

The war is entered by design, not reaction.

Initiative substitutes for legitimacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. OPERATIONAL DISCIPLINE OVER DYNASTIC RIGHT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Prussian success is procedural.

Advantages:
• Drill and cohesion
• Supply discipline
• Command reliability
• Replacement capacity

Austrian legitimacy cannot compensate for slower execution.

Competence outruns inheritance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COALITION CHAOS & SYSTEM SURVIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The broader war is inconclusive.

Dynamics:
• Shifting alliances
• Continental distraction
• Limited decisive battles
• Diplomatic exhaustion

Germania internalizes a key lesson:
Survival favors systems that can disengage early.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RESULTING POWER RECONFIGURATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• Prussia retains Silesia
• Austria survives but is weakened
• German dualism solidifies
• Military competence becomes currency

No empire is created.
A hierarchy is reordered.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war encodes **replacement-before-expansion logic**.

Lessons:
• Limited wars yield durable gains.
• Administrative readiness outweighs legal claim.
• Speed destabilizes inherited order.
• German power emerges through competence, not proclamation.

This becomes Germania’s preferred conflict grammar.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Succession crises reward prepared opportunists.
• Legitimacy without force invites erosion.
• Replacement depth decides early modern wars.
• Germania’s rise begins inside German space.
• Austrian Succession is Prussia’s proof-of-concept war.

Tension preserved without verdict.
