────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Muscovy as the **compression core** of Russian
civilizational formation: the polity that internalized domination,
centralized authority, and endurance governance to outlive rivals and
expand into empire.

Muscovy is remembered as **statehood forged under pressure**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGINS UNDER DOMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Muscovy emerges within the Mongol-dominated order.

Key conditions:
• Tributary subordination
• Competition among Rus’ principalities
• External arbitration of legitimacy

Survival requires adaptation, not revolt.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CENTRALIZATION THROUGH SERVICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Muscovy consolidates power via:
• Service nobility tied to land grants
• Fiscal centralization
• Coercive enforcement

Authority becomes hierarchical and personal.

Political axiom:
→ Power concentrates fastest under threat.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CHURCH ALIGNMENT & LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Orthodox Church alignment supplies moral continuity.

Dynamics:
• Sacralization of rulership
• Cultural cohesion
• Ideological insulation from Latin West

Faith stabilizes obedience during expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. TERRITORIAL ABSORPTION & DEPTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Muscovy expands by:
• Absorbing rival principalities
• Securing river corridors
• Pushing steppe frontiers outward

Depth substitutes for institutional pluralism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SECURITY STATE LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Persistent insecurity produces:
• Surveillance and control mechanisms
• Collective punishment norms
• Limited tolerance for dissent

Order precedes prosperity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. TRANSITION TO EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
By the early 18th century:
• Muscovy’s methods scale
• Administrative complexity increases
• Identity shifts from principality to empire

The name changes; the logic persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Compression creates durable authority.
• Service binds elites to survival of the state.
• Centralization outperforms pluralism under siege.
• Religious legitimacy substitutes for representation.
• Muscovy’s logic underwrites later imperial forms.

Tension preserved without verdict.
