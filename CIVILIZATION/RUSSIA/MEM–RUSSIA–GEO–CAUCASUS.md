I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Caucasus as **Russia’s most complex internal
frontier**: a mountainous, ethnically dense, and geopolitically
entangled region where empire meets resistance, mediation, and
containment simultaneously.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC CHARACTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core traits:
• High mountain chains and narrow passes
• Fragmented valleys
• Limited maneuver corridors
• Natural defensive strongholds

The Caucasus privileges **local control over mass movement**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRATEGIC FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Caucasus functions as a buffer and gateway.

Roles:
• Barrier between steppe and Middle East
• Control point over Black Sea–Caspian axis
• Shield for southern heartlands

Security here is positional, not expansive.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. IMPERIAL INCORPORATION & RESISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russian control is gradual and contested.

Patterns:
• Prolonged pacification campaigns
• Reliance on forts and roads
• Co-optation of elites alongside repression
• Persistent local resistance

Authority is negotiated under pressure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ETHNICITY, FAITH, & GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The region’s diversity complicates rule.

Dynamics:
• Multiple languages and identities
• Islamic and Christian communities
• Clan-based authority structures

Governance emphasizes containment over assimilation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN SECURITY SALIENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Caucasus remains volatile.

Contemporary factors:
• Insurgency legacies
• Transit routes
• External influence attempts

The region demands constant attention.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Mountains impose permanent friction on empire.
• Control requires patience, infrastructure, and compromise.
• Diversity resists uniform governance.
• The Caucasus converts expansion into containment.
• Russia treats the Caucasus as a holding zone, not a launchpad.

Tension preserved without verdict.
