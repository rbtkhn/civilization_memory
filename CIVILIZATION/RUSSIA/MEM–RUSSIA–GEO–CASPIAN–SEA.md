I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Caspian Sea as **Russia’s enclosed southern
hinge-space**: a landlocked maritime basin linking steppe, Caucasus,
Persia, and Central Asia, where control substitutes for access to open
oceans and where security, energy, and influence intersect.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC CHARACTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core traits:
• Landlocked sea with no ocean outlet
• Multiple littoral states
• Shallow northern shelves; deeper southern basin
• Harsh climate gradients

The Caspian rewards **regional dominance**, not global reach.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. HISTORICAL ENTRY & CONTROL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russian engagement intensifies after Muscovite consolidation.

Key phases:
• Ivan IV’s conquest of Astrakhan (1556)
• Incorporation into Muscovite trade-security network
• Tsarist naval presence as policing force

Control of Astrakhan converts river access into basin leverage.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SECURITY & BUFFER FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Caspian operates as a security moat.

Functions:
• Limits southern naval intrusion
• Provides interior maneuver space
• Shields Volga heartland

Threats are lateral and terrestrial, not maritime.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ENERGY, TRANSPORT, & MODERN SALIENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Modern importance rises sharply.

Key factors:
• Hydrocarbon reserves
• Pipeline politics
• River–sea transport corridors
• Legal ambiguity over maritime status

Energy transforms the Caspian into a strategic bargaining table.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CASPIAN IN RUSSIAN STRATEGIC CULTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Caspian fits Russian preference for:
• Contained theaters
• Dominance without expeditionary exposure
• Incremental influence

It is a **managed basin**, not an arena of prestige.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Enclosed seas reward endurance and proximity.
• Control can substitute for access.
• Energy intensifies but does not redefine geography.
• The Caspian links steppe security to southern strategy.
• Russia treats the Caspian as an interior frontier.

Tension preserved without verdict.
