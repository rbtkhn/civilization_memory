────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Vladimir the Great as the **civilizational
re-coder of Rus’**: the ruler who transformed dynastic rule into
Orthodox-sanctioned sovereignty.

Vladimir represents **legitimacy through faith**, not reform or law.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. POWER CONSOLIDATION THROUGH VIOLENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Vladimir seized power through fratricidal conflict.

Key features:
• Elimination of rivals
• Personal consolidation of authority
• Pagan warrior legitimacy

Authority precedes morality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CHRISTIANIZATION AS STRATEGIC CHOICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conversion (988) is framed as deliberate statecraft.

Drivers:
• Alliance with Byzantium
• Cultural unification
• Moral re-founding of authority

Faith functions as governance infrastructure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SACRAL SOVEREIGNTY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-conversion rule fuses throne and altar.

Outcomes:
• Divine sanction of rulership
• Moral hierarchy imposed from above
• Religious justification for obedience

Legitimacy becomes trans-personal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. SOCIAL & MORAL REORDERING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Vladimir’s later rule emphasizes:
• Charity
• Church construction
• Christian moral codes

Memory highlights transformation, not continuity of violence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DYNASTIC CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Succession after Vladimir remains unstable.

Failure mode:
• No institutionalized succession mechanism
• Return to dynastic conflict

Faith does not resolve structural fragmentation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Vladimir anchors:
• Orthodoxy as Russian identity core
• Sacral monarchy template
• Civilizational alignment with Byzantium

All later Russian sovereignty assumes his choice.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Faith can legitimize power without limiting it.
• Conversion is a geopolitical act.
• Sacred authority outlives dynastic competence.
• Moral re-coding does not prevent succession crises.

Tension preserved without verdict.
