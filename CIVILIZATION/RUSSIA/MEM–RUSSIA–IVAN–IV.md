────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Ivan IV as the **radicalizer of Muscovite autocracy**:
the ruler who fused absolute sovereignty with terror, expanded Russian
territory dramatically, and demonstrated both the power and pathology
of unconstrained central rule.

For Russian memory, Ivan IV represents **autocracy without buffers**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FROM PRINCELY RULE TO TSARDOM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ivan IV redefines rulership.

Key shift:
• Coronation as Tsar (1547)
• Claim to universal, sacral authority
• Elevation above boyars and princes

Authority becomes ontological, not contractual.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. EARLY REFORM & STATE EXPANSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Initial reign shows reformist impulse.

Developments:
• Central administrative councils
• Military reorganization
• Legal reforms
• Expansion eastward (Kazan 1552, Astrakhan 1556)

Russia becomes a multi-ethnic empire.

Expansion precedes internal stabilization.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. OPRICHNINA: TERROR AS GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mid-reign rupture defines Ivan’s legacy.

Features:
• Division of realm into oprichnina / zemshchina
• State-sanctioned terror against elites
• Mass confiscation and executions
• Destruction of Novgorod (1570)

Fear replaces mediation.

The state consumes its own elite.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WAR, OVERREACH, & INTERNAL DECAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Simultaneous wars strain the system.

Key failures:
• Livonian War stalemate
• Economic collapse
• Depopulation
• Elite flight

Expansion without institutional resilience generates fragility.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. PSYCHOLOGICAL RULE & PERSONALISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ivan’s rule is intensely personal.

Characteristics:
• Paranoia
• Ritualized cruelty
• Fusion of faith and violence
• Moral absolutism

Autocracy detaches from rational constraint.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. SUCCESSION CRISIS & AFTERMATH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ivan leaves no stable transition.

Outcomes:
• Dynastic weakness
• Institutional exhaustion
• Prelude to the Time of Troubles

Unbuffered autocracy proves unsustainable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Absolute sovereignty amplifies both power and collapse.
• Terror accelerates compliance but destroys elite capacity.
• Expansion without governance depth is self-defeating.
• Personalist rule destabilizes succession.
• Ivan IV defines the outer limit of Muscovite autocracy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
