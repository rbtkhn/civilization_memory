────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Oleg of Novgorod as the **first territorial
unifier of Rus’**: the ruler who shifted authority from loose trade
leadership to proto-imperial control centered on Kiev.

Oleg represents **expansion as legitimacy**, not law or faith.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SUCCESSION & REGENT AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Oleg ruled as regent following Rurik’s death.

Key features:
• Authority justified through guardianship
• Power exercised through command, not inheritance
• Personal loyalty as enforcement mechanism

Legitimacy derived from effectiveness, not lineage purity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CAPTURE OF KIEV & TERRITORIAL LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Oleg seized Kiev and declared it the “mother of Rus’ cities.”

Significance:
• Control of Dnieper trade artery
• Strategic shift southward
• Foundation of Rus’ territorial core

Memory axiom:
→ Geography determines sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MILITARY EXPANSION & EXTERNAL PROJECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Oleg’s campaigns against Byzantium define early Rus’ ambition.

Key outcomes:
• Demonstration of force against Constantinople
• Trade privileges secured by threat
• Prestige through external intimidation

Power is validated abroad, not negotiated internally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MYTH, PROPHECY, & AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Oleg’s memory is heavily mythologized.

Elements:
• Prophetic epithet (“the Seer”)
• Fated death narrative
• Blending of history and legend

Myth reinforces authority retroactively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINUITY ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Oleg establishes:
• Kiev as civilizational center
• Expansion-first legitimacy logic
• Precedent for coercive unification

He bridges Rurik’s warlord rule and later dynastic consolidation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Early Rus’ legitimacy rests on territorial seizure.
• Trade corridors anchor political authority.
• Expansion substitutes for institutional order.
• Myth stabilizes authority after death.

Tension preserved without verdict.
