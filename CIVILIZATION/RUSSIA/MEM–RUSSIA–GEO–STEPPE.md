────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Eurasian Steppe as **Russia’s primordial
security environment**: the open, flat, mobility-dominant space that
generated invasion risk, forced adaptive governance, and shaped
Russian doctrines of depth, patience, and centralization.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC CHARACTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core traits:
• Flat, treeless expanses
• High mobility, low natural barriers
• Climatic extremes
• Long sightlines and rapid maneuver potential

The steppe privileges **movement over fortification**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INVASION CORRIDOR & TRAUMA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The steppe functions historically as an invasion highway.

Key invaders via steppe vectors:
• Scythians and Sarmatians
• Pechenegs and Cumans
• Mongols and Tatars
• Later nomadic and semi-nomadic forces

Memory emphasizes exposure rather than conquest.

Security lesson:
→ What cannot be blocked must be absorbed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. POLITICAL ADAPTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russian governance evolves in response.

Adaptations:
• Centralized command authority
• Rapid mobilization
• Coercive taxation
• Expansion to push frontier outward

The state substitutes **scale for barriers**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. STEPPE & IMPERIAL EXPANSION LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Expansion into the steppe is defensive in memory.

Dynamics:
• Absorb nomads or displace them
• Convert mobility threats into borderlands
• Replace raid ecology with settlement

Empire grows to neutralize exposure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CULTURAL & PSYCHOLOGICAL EFFECTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The steppe imprints political psychology.

Enduring traits:
• Suspicion of decentralization
• Fear of fragmentation
• Preference for unity under pressure
• Acceptance of hardship as normal

Openness produces inward rigidity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Flat geography produces centralized politics.
• Mobility threats reward endurance over finesse.
• Expansion functions as defense.
• Depth replaces walls.
• The steppe is Russia’s original strategic teacher.

Tension preserved without verdict.
