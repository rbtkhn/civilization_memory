I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Anthony Kaldellis as **Rome’s continuity advocate
and internalist historian**: the modern scholar who rejects decline-and-fall
teleology and insists that the Roman state, identity, and political logic
persisted—self-consciously—long after the conventional “fall” of the
Western Empire.

Kaldellis does not explain Rome’s end.
He denies that Rome ended.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. POSITION INSIDE THE CIVILIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis’ core methodological move is positional.

Unlike Enlightenment or nation-state historians, he asks:
• How did Romans understand themselves?
• What did Roman institutions claim to be doing?
• When did Romans believe their state had failed—or not?

Rome, in this view, is defined by **self-identification and continuity of
practice**, not by external periodization.

Rome does not “become Byzantine.”
It remains Roman by law, language, and political consciousness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ANTI-DECLINE FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis treats “decline” as a modern narrative imposition.

Core objections:
• Decline presumes a golden baseline that Rome itself did not recognize
• Institutional adaptation is misread as decay
• Survival through transformation is labeled failure by outsiders

He reframes Late Antiquity as:
• Administrative reconfiguration
• Strategic contraction, not collapse
• Ideological recalibration under pressure

Change is not loss by default.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. LAW, STATE, AND ROMANNESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For Kaldellis, Romanness is legal-political, not ethnic.

Key continuities:
• Roman law as governing substrate
• Centralized fiscal-military administration
• Imperial ideology of universal rule
• Self-designation as *Rhomaioi*

The state remains legible to itself.
Officials, taxpayers, soldiers, and subjects recognize the same authority.

Rome persists because **its grammar of rule persists**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CHRISTIANITY AS INTERNAL EVOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis rejects the Gibbonian rupture model.

Christianity is treated as:
• An internal Roman transformation
• Absorbed into imperial legitimacy
• Governed, regulated, and instrumentalized by the state

Rather than weakening Rome, Christianity becomes:
• A language of imperial universality
• A moral framework for obedience
• A tool of cohesion under contraction

Rome changes its metaphysics, not its state logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. WESTERN BREAKDOWN VS ROMAN CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis distinguishes:
• Western provincial collapse
• From imperial state survival

The fall of Rome (in the West) is:
• A regional administrative failure
• Not a civilizational termination

The Roman state:
• Re-centers in the East
• Retains legitimacy claims
• Continues taxation, law, diplomacy, and war

476 AD is a Western event—not a Roman one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. POLEMIC AGAINST “BYZANTIUM”
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis treats “Byzantine” as a historiographical error.

Problems with the term:
• Erases Roman self-identity
• Implies civilizational replacement
• Justifies decline narratives
• Serves modern European periodization needs

Romans never called themselves Byzantines.
They called themselves Romans.

The name encodes the misunderstanding.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTRAST WITH GIBBON AND MOMMSEN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Against Gibbon:
• Rejects moralized decline
• Rejects Christianity as civilizational poison
• Rejects irony as explanatory distance

Against Mommsen:
• Accepts institutional adaptation
• Rejects the idea that empire is a post-Roman solution
• Insists empire *is* Roman continuity, not its negation

Kaldellis does not deny crisis.
He denies termination.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kaldellis encodes **continuity under pressure**.

Lessons:
• States can survive by redefining scale
• Identity persists through institutions
• Collapse is often regional, not systemic
• External narratives misread internal logic
• Survival is not the same as flourishing

Rome endures by refusing to agree that it has ended.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Decline is a narrative choice, not a fact.
• Law and administration anchor identity.
• Christianity is absorption, not replacement.
• The Roman state survives where it can still tax and command.
• Rome ends only when Romans say it ends.

Tension preserved without verdict.
