────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Malta as **Rome’s smallest decisive island**: a
micro-anchor whose value lies not in resources or population, but in
positional control over central Mediterranean sea lanes.

For Rome, Malta is not an empire-builder.
It is an **interruption-preventer**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PRE-ROMAN CONTEXT & POSITIONAL VALUE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Malta enters Roman awareness through Punic conflict.

Key features:
• Central Mediterranean location
• Proximity to Sicily and North Africa
• Natural harbors
• Limited inland depth

Malta’s importance is geometric, not economic.

Whoever controls Malta observes movement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ROMAN INCORPORATION & QUIET CONTROL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rome secures Malta during the Second Punic War.

Roman priorities:
• Deny Carthaginian naval usage
• Secure Sicily’s southern approaches
• Maintain unbroken maritime lanes

Malta is governed lightly.

Its value lies in **absence of disruption**, not presence of activity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. GOVERNANCE STYLE: MINIMAL, STABLE, INVISIBLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Roman rule emphasizes:
• Low garrison footprint
• Port security
• Legal incorporation without cultural overhaul

Malta becomes Roman by function,
not by transformation.

Rome does not project identity here.
It enforces predictability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MALTA IN THE IMPERIAL MARITIME SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Malta functions as:
• Observation node
• Emergency harbor
• Anti-piracy support point

It complements Sicily:
• Sicily feeds Rome
• Malta keeps Sicily safe

Small islands amplify large systems.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LATE ANTIQUITY & CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Malta remains marginal but retained.

Patterns:
• Successive rulers value the island
• Control persists across transitions
• Maritime relevance outlives administration

Malta survives by being easy to hold
and costly to lose.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL SIGNIFICANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Malta demonstrates Rome’s mastery of **micro-geometry**.

Lessons:
• Small nodes stabilize large networks.
• Visibility can matter more than force.
• Empire requires quiet anchors.
• Strategic denial does not require spectacle.

Malta is Rome’s sentry stone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Geography can outweigh scale.
• Small islands govern movement.
• Malta complements Sicily through denial.
• Stability is often invisible.
• Empire survives by removing surprises.

Tension preserved without verdict.
