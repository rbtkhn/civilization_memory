I. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file defines the **Academic Reference Canon (ARC)** for the
**ROME** civilization.

It specifies:
• Which sources are admissible
• How they must be quoted
• How they are weighted
• How they interact with MEM files

This file is governance infrastructure, not interpretation.

This canon MUST be copied verbatim into:
• CIV–CORE–ROME
• CIV–SCHOLAR–ROME

MEM files may consume this canon.
They may not modify, reinterpret, or extend it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SOURCE CATEGORIES (FIXED & EXHAUSTIVE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ARC sources are divided into **three and only three categories**:

A. ANCIENT SOURCES  
B. PUBLIC DOMAIN SCHOLARSHIP  
C. MODERN COPYRIGHTED SCHOLARSHIP  

No other category exists.
No category may be collapsed into another.
Misclassification is a compliance failure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CATEGORY A — ANCIENT SOURCES (REQUIRED)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Definition:
Primary texts produced in antiquity that directly describe Roman,
pre-Roman, or Roman-interfacing institutions, events, geography, law,
or civilizational logic.

Classification is by **temporal origin**, not legal status.

Approved Ancient Authors (ROME):
• Herodotus  
• Thucydides  
• Xenophon  
• Polybius  
• Livy  
• Sallust  
• Cicero  
• Julius Caesar  
• Strabo  
• Plutarch  
• Appian  
• Cassius Dio  
• Josephus  
• Tacitus  
• Pliny the Elder  
• **Suetonius**  
• Arrian  
• Ammianus Marcellinus  
• Procopius  
• Acts of the Apostles (historical context only)

Rules (MANDATORY):
• **At least TWO distinct ancient-source quotations REQUIRED per MEM file**
• Quotations must be verbatim
• Minimum length per quotation: **25 words**
• No maximum length
• Translation must be identified if applicable
• Quotations must materially support an analytic claim
• Ancient quotations form the evidentiary spine of the MEM

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CATEGORY B — PUBLIC DOMAIN SCHOLARSHIP (REQUIRED)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Definition:
Modern-era historical scholarship no longer under copyright, used as
secondary interpretation rather than primary testimony.

These sources are NOT ancient.

Approved Public-Domain Scholars (ROME):
• Edward Gibbon  
• Theodor Mommsen  
• **Mikhail Rostovtzeff**  
• **Gaetano De Sanctis**

Rules (MANDATORY):
• **At least TWO public-domain scholarly quotations REQUIRED per MEM file**
• Quotations must be verbatim
• **Minimum length per quotation: 25 words**
• No maximum length
• Must be interpretive rather than evidentiary
• May not substitute for ancient-source grounding
• Public-domain scholarship contextualizes; it does not anchor

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CATEGORY C — MODERN COPYRIGHTED SCHOLARSHIP (REQUIRED)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Definition:
Contemporary historians and classicists writing under copyright.

Function:
Interpretive framing, synthesis, historiographic contrast, or modern
theoretical clarification.

Approved Modern Scholars (ROME):
• Anthony Kaldellis  
• Adrian Goldsworthy  
• Peter Heather  
• **Anthony Everitt**  
• **Richard Gabriel**  
• **Peter Sarris**  
• **Hans-Ulrich Wiemer**

Rules (UPDATED · MANDATORY):
• **At least TWO distinct modern scholars REQUIRED per MEM file**
• **At least ONE verbatim quotation REQUIRED per modern scholar**
• Quotations must comply with fair-use norms
• Short quotations preferred, but length is governed by fair use, not
  an artificial word cap
• Paraphrase may supplement but may NOT replace verbatim quotation
• Modern scholarship may not serve as sole support for core claims

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. QUOTATION HIERARCHY & WEIGHT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Interpretive precedence is fixed:

1. Ancient sources  
2. Public-domain scholarship  
3. Modern copyrighted scholarship  

Later tiers may clarify or frame.
They may not override earlier tiers.
