────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Seven Years’ War as Anglia’s global systems
victory: the conflict in which Anglia conclusively proved that maritime
finance, naval supremacy, and coalition leverage could decide world
outcomes faster and more durably than continental prestige warfare.

For Anglia, the war represents imperial confirmation, not crisis.
It is the moment when Anglia becomes the system-setter rather than a
contender within a European balance.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT & ENTRY CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia enters the war with unusually high strategic clarity.

Context:
• Intensifying colonial rivalry with Francia
• Mature public credit and naval infrastructure
• Acceptance of continental limits
• Priority placed on trade, colonies, and sea control

Strategic premise:
→ The decisive theater is global, not European.

Anglia does not seek continental dominance.
It seeks imperial denial of rivals.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. GLOBAL WAR AS DESIGN, NOT ACCIDENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike Francia, Anglia treats multi-theater war as intentional.

Theaters integrated:
• North America
• Caribbean
• India
• Atlantic and Indian Oceans

Command logic:
• Navy-first prioritization
• Peripheral strikes over central battles
• Economic strangulation over territorial symbolism

Global dispersion becomes strategic leverage.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MARITIME SUPREMACY & DECISION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Naval dominance determines outcomes.

Key effects:
• Blockade of French ports
• Protection of Anglian trade
• Isolation of overseas garrisons
• Mobility of force and supply

Decisive results:
• Capture of Canada
• Collapse of French India
• Caribbean dominance

Victory is secured at sea, then ratified on land.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. FINANCE AS WARFARE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia’s fiscal system outperforms all rivals.

Mechanisms:
• Long-term public debt at low interest
• Reliable taxation
• Parliamentary credit legitimacy
• Ability to subsidize allies

War is sustained without regime destabilization.

Money becomes force multiplier, not liability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINENTAL RESTRAINT & DELEGATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia deliberately limits continental exposure.

Dynamics:
• Support of Prussia as land proxy
• Avoidance of decisive European entanglement
• Acceptance of stalemate in Germany

Anglia trades territory for time.

Continental war is managed, not solved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. SYSTEMIC ASYMMETRY WITH FRANCIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war reveals civilizational divergence.

Contrasts:
• Anglia: naval–financial–parliamentary system
• Francia: court–army–prestige system

Anglia absorbs loss without panic.
Francia absorbs loss as humiliation.

The same war produces opposite memories.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. POST-WAR ORDER & IMPERIAL TAKEOFF
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Treaty of Paris (1763) confirms Anglian ascendancy.

Outcomes:
• Global colonial primacy
• Maritime routes secured
• Commercial empire expanded
• Strategic confidence consolidated

Anglia emerges as the first true global empire.

The war ends rivalry—not conflict.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Seven Years’ War locks in trajectories.

Enduring effects:
• Anglia becomes system manager
• Global war becomes Anglian specialty
• Naval dominance normalized
• Imperial confidence institutionalized

The cost of victory is future overreach—
but not immediate instability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Global wars reward maritime–financial systems first.
• Naval supremacy converts dispersion into advantage.
• Fiscal legitimacy stabilizes war-making capacity.
• Continental restraint enables imperial expansion.
• The Seven Years’ War is Anglia’s imperial birth certificate.

Tension preserved without verdict.
