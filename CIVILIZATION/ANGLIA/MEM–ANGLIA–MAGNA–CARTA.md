────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Magna Carta as **Anglia’s first explicit constraint
on sovereign will**: the moment when legitimacy begins to migrate from
personal authority toward procedure, precedent, and negotiated obligation.

Magna Carta is not democracy.
It is restraint.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Magna Carta emerges from elite revolt, not popular theory.

Context:
• Fiscal extraction for continental war
• Arbitrary royal justice
• Noble resistance to absolutizing kingship
• Absence of institutional dispute resolution

Constraint is forced, not granted.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LAW AS NEGOTIATED LIMIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority is bounded retroactively.

Core principles:
• No taxation without consent (of elites)
• Due process protections
• Limits on arbitrary imprisonment
• Recognition of inherited custom

The king is placed inside the law.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FAILURE & SURVIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Magna Carta initially fails.

Dynamics:
• Immediate royal repudiation
• Civil war
• Reissuance and revision

Its power lies not in enforcement,
but in survivability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. PROCEDURAL MEMORY FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Magna Carta becomes precedent rather than program.

Effects:
• Cited in later disputes
• Reinterpreted across centuries
• Gradually abstracted from noble privilege to general principle

Constraint accumulates through reference.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MISREADINGS & MYTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Later eras universalize Magna Carta.

Tensions:
• Elite document reframed as popular liberty
• Specific concessions treated as timeless rights
• Symbol exceeds original scope

Myth amplifies endurance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Sovereign power made conditional
• Law elevated over will
• Negotiation normalized
• Procedural legitimacy strengthened

Magna Carta initiates Anglia’s long arc away from absolutism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Constraint precedes consent.
• Failure does not negate precedent.
• Law survives through citation.
• Myth can stabilize institutions.
• Magna Carta is Anglia’s first brake.

Tension preserved without verdict.
