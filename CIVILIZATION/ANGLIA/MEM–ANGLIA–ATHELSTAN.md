────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Æthelstan as **Anglia’s unifier**:
the ruler who converted survival into sovereignty by consolidating
previously fractured kingdoms into a single political entity capable of
enduring beyond immediate personal rule, though not yet abstracted into
fully impersonal procedure.

Æthelstan completes what Alfred preserved.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. POST-INVASION CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Æthelstan inherits a hardened but divided landscape.

Conditions:
• Residual Viking power
• Fragmented regional authority
• Hybrid legal and cultural systems
• Emergent defense infrastructure

Unity becomes possible—but not automatic.

The task shifts from survival to consolidation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. UNIFICATION THROUGH LAW AND KINGSHIP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority is asserted comprehensively.

Actions:
• Formal kingship over all England
• Law codes applied across regions
• Integration of former Danelaw territories
• Recognition by foreign rulers

Unity is declared, then enforced procedurally.

Kingship functions as the delivery mechanism;
law functions as the binding agent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DIPLOMACY & EXTERNAL LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Æthelstan secures recognition abroad.

Mechanisms:
• Dynastic marriages
• Treaty systems
• Participation in continental diplomacy

England enters the European order as a unit,
not merely as a victorious kingdom.

External recognition stabilizes internal claims.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LAW AS UNIFICATION TECHNOLOGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legal uniformity replaces coercive occupation.

Effects:
• Standardized justice across regions
• Elite alignment under shared norms
• Reduction in internal revolt pressure

Law transforms conquest into order
without requiring permanent repression.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LIMITED DE-PERSONALIZATION OF AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Æthelstan strengthens institutional practices that reduce dependence on
charisma, though durability still relies on lawful succession rather
than fully impersonal procedure.

Characteristics:
• Delegated governance
• Expanded legal administration
• Reduced reliance on heroic kingship

Authority begins to generalize,
but has not yet abstracted beyond the monarch.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• England exists as a political fact
• Law binds diverse regions into a single realm
• Defense and administration centralize
• Foreign recognition normalizes unity

Æthelstan makes Anglia singular,
but not yet procedurally autonomous.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Survival enables unification.
• Law scales authority more durably than force.
• External recognition stabilizes internal unity.
• Institutions can outlast founders without yet escaping kingship.
• Æthelstan is Anglia’s first completed king, not its final form.

Tension preserved without verdict.
