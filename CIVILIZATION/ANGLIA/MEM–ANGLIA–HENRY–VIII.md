────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Henry VIII as **Anglia’s rupture sovereign**:
the ruler who broke external religious authority, centralized
sovereignty under the crown, and permanently altered the grammar
of legitimacy—while leaving core institutions intact.

Henry VIII fractures continuity in doctrine,
but preserves continuity in structure.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SOVEREIGNTY ASSERTION AGAINST EXTERNAL AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry VIII redefines sovereignty through separation.

Actions:
• Rejection of papal jurisdiction
• Assertion of royal supremacy
• Nationalization of religious authority

Legitimacy is relocated from Rome to the crown.

The break is external, not internal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INSTITUTIONAL CONTINUITY THROUGH RUPTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite doctrinal shock, governance persists.

Mechanisms:
• Parliament used to legalize rupture
• Existing courts retained
• Administrative offices preserved
• Law employed as instrument of change

Revolution is executed procedurally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DISSOLUTION OF MONASTIC POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Religious institutions are dismantled.

Consequences:
• Redistribution of land to loyal elites
• Destruction of alternative authority centers
• Expansion of crown resources

Economic and political power realign without mass revolt.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. PERSONAL RULE & LEGITIMACY VOLATILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry’s authority is highly personalized.

Patterns:
• Dynastic obsession
• Marital instability
• Court faction volatility

The system survives personal excess.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. FAILURE MODES & SYSTEM STRESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Costs of rupture accumulate.

Stresses:
• Religious polarization
• Succession uncertainty
• Short-term instability

Yet collapse does not occur.

Institutions absorb shock.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• National sovereignty over religion normalized
• Parliament legitimized as rupture mechanism
• Religious settlement deferred, not resolved
• External authority permanently excluded

Henry VIII enables later equilibrium under Elizabeth.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Rupture can be executed procedurally.
• External authority breaks are survivable.
• Institutions can legalize revolution.
• Personal instability need not destroy systems.
• Henry VIII is Anglia’s controlled detonation.

Tension preserved without verdict.
