────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Wellington as **Anglia’s land-system executor**:
the commander who translated Anglia’s maritime–financial endurance
strategy into disciplined continental results without abandoning
procedural restraint or coalition logic.

Wellington does not seek decisive glory.
He **prevents defeat until systems decide victory**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. COMMAND PHILOSOPHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wellington’s method is anti-Napoleonic.

Principles:
• Defensive positional warfare
• Logistical prudence
• Refusal of unnecessary risk
• Respect for allied constraints

Victory is accumulated through denial, not pursuit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. THE PENINSULAR SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Iberian theater becomes Anglia’s land laboratory.

Mechanisms:
• Maritime supply via secure ports
• Cooperation with local resistance
• Attritional pressure on occupying forces
• Strategic patience under political pressure

Land warfare is subordinated to sea power.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DISCIPLINE AS LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wellington enforces order as policy.

Effects:
• Civilian restraint
• Supply discipline
• Army reputation preserved

Legitimacy sustains coalition cooperation.

The army becomes an extension of Anglian credibility.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COALITION WARFARE WITHOUT DOMINANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wellington operates inside alliances rather than above them.

Dynamics:
• Coordination with Portuguese and Spanish forces
• Deference to political authorities
• Integration into broader coalition strategy

Authority is exercised procedurally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. WATERLOO AS TERMINAL CONTAINMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Waterloo functions as closure, not culmination.

Characteristics:
• Defensive posture
• Coalition timing
• Reliance on allied arrival

Napoleon is finished by convergence,
not annihilation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Proof that Anglia can fight effectively on land without becoming continental
• Model for coalition-ground warfare
• Reinforcement of restraint as strength

Wellington validates Anglia’s anti-brilliance doctrine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Discipline converts endurance into results.
• Defense can be strategically aggressive.
• Coalitions succeed when commanders resist dominance.
• Land power can serve maritime strategy.
• Wellington is Anglia’s quiet general.

Tension preserved without verdict.
