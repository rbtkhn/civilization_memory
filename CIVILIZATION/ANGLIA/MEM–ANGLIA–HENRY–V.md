────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Henry V as **Anglia’s martial unifier**:
the king who temporarily fused crown, nobility, and populace
through disciplined war leadership, restoring legitimacy
after internal fracture.

Henry V unites Anglia through victory,
not reform.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. LEGITIMACY RECOVERY AFTER CIVIL STRAIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry inherits a damaged polity.

Context:
• Post-Richard II instability
• Lancastrian legitimacy deficit
• Noble mistrust

War becomes the solvent of doubt.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DISCIPLINED WARFARE AS GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry imposes order through command.

Characteristics:
• Strict military discipline
• Moralized leadership
• Shared hardship with troops
• Controlled brutality

Authority is demonstrated, not proclaimed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. AGINCOURT & SYMBOLIC ASCENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Agincourt functions as legitimacy shock.

Effects:
• Elite unity through success
• Popular identification with crown
• External validation of rule

Victory substitutes for consensus.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIMITED ABSOLUTISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry exercises concentrated power selectively.

Dynamics:
• Strong personal authority
• Respect for legal forms
• No attempt at structural absolutism

Force is applied outward, not inward.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. PREMATURE TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Henry’s early death truncates consolidation.

Consequences:
• Succession fragility
• Loss of personal authority anchor
• Reversion to instability

Unity proves non-transferable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Military victory recognized as legitimacy tool
• Limits of charisma without institutions exposed
• War remembered as unifier, not solution

Henry V becomes exemplar, not template.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• War can temporarily heal legitimacy fractures.
• Discipline substitutes for ideology.
• Personal authority unifies but does not persist.
• Victory without institutionalization decays.
• Henry V is Anglia’s moment of heroic coherence.

Tension preserved without verdict.
