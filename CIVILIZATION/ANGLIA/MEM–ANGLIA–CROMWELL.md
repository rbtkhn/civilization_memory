────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Oliver Cromwell as the **religious–military rupture
engine** of the English state: the figure who shattered monarchical
sacral legitimacy, subordinated law to godly force, and proved that
Anglia could survive regicide without civilizational collapse.

Cromwell does not found a durable regime.
He **destroys the inevitability of monarchy** and demonstrates that
authority can be seized, exercised, and exhausted without dynastic form.

He represents rupture without inheritance — a revolutionary parenthesis
that permanently alters Anglia’s legitimacy grammar.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGINS & PURITAN FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cromwell emerged from the lesser gentry, shaped by militant Puritanism
rather than aristocratic entitlement.

Formative traits:
• Calvinist moral absolutism
• Hostility to episcopal hierarchy
• Belief in providential selection
• Personal discipline fused with spiritual certainty

Core belief:
Authority is conferred by God, proven through victory.

Religion functions as command logic, not consolation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVIL WAR & SEIZURE OF POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The English Civil War dissolves inherited legitimacy.

Key characteristics:
• Parliamentary-military alliance
• Destruction of royal command authority
• Creation of the New Model Army
• Fusion of discipline, ideology, and merit

Operational axiom:
God favors the organized and the ruthless.

Victory becomes proof of righteousness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. REGICIDE & LEGITIMACY ANNIHILATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The execution of Charles I (1649) is unprecedented.

Consequences:
• Sacred kingship destroyed
• Monarchy desacralized
• Law subordinated to revolutionary tribunal
• No replacement legitimacy fully installed

Foundational break:
A king can be judged, condemned, and killed by his subjects.

This knowledge cannot be undone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. THE ARMY AS STATE SUBSTITUTE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cromwell’s central innovation is the army as sovereign instrument.

Core mechanisms:
• Military-backed governance
• Purges of Parliament (Pride’s Purge)
• Suppression of factional pluralism
• Rule by ordinance and decree

Statehood is redefined:
Force precedes law; obedience precedes consent.

Civil authority becomes conditional.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. THE PROTECTORATE & FAILED CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Protectorate (1653–1658) attempts stabilization without dynasty.

Characteristics:
• Written constitutions without mass legitimacy
• Reliance on military districts (Major-Generals)
• Religious toleration bounded by Puritan supremacy
• Chronic parliamentary dysfunction

Governing logic:
Godly order must be imposed until society is worthy of liberty.

This moment never arrives.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. IRELAND, EMPIRE, & COERCIVE EXPANSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cromwell’s regime externalizes legitimacy through conquest.

Key actions:
• Brutal Irish campaigns (Drogheda, Wexford)
• Subjugation of Scotland
• Expansion of naval and colonial power
• Assertion of Protestant imperial identity

Empire substitutes for internal cohesion.

Violence becomes theological.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. PERSONAL AUTHORITY & SUCCESSION FAILURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cromwell’s authority is personal, not institutional.

Failures:
• No accepted succession mechanism
• Son (Richard Cromwell) lacks legitimacy
• Army factionalism resurfaces
• Civil exhaustion accelerates

The regime collapses immediately after his death.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. RESTORATION & ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Restoration (1660) reinstates monarchy without sacral innocence.

Absorption dynamics:
• Monarchy returns, but transformed
• Army subordinated permanently to civilian control
• Parliament emerges as legitimacy co-holder
• Regicide memory remains suppressed but operative

Cromwell is rejected.
His precedent is retained.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Sacred legitimacy, once broken, cannot be fully restored.
• Military virtue can overthrow monarchy but cannot replace it.
• Religious absolutism accelerates rupture but blocks consolidation.
• Personal rule without dynasty collapses instantly.
• Cromwell teaches Anglia the limits of revolutionary governance.
• Constitutional monarchy emerges strengthened by surviving Cromwell.

Rupture preserved without redemption.
