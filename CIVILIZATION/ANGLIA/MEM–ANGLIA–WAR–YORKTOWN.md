────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Yorktown as **Anglia’s procedural defeat**: the
moment when Anglia loses a war without losing its civilizational
grammar, demonstrating that institutional continuity can survive
military loss and imperial contraction.

For Anglia, Yorktown is loss without collapse.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yorktown occurs within imperial overextension.

Context:
• Global commitments (Atlantic, Caribbean, India)
• War framed as rebellion containment
• Confidence in naval mobility
• Underestimation of coalition convergence

The conflict is managed as an imperial policing action
rather than a system-threatening war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COALITION ASYMMETRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Defeat emerges from system interaction, not battlefield failure alone.

Dynamics:
• Francien naval intervention
• American land containment
• Temporary Anglian naval absence
• Geographic compression

Anglia is defeated locally, not structurally.

The system fails at a node—not globally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. RESTRAINT IN DEFEAT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia absorbs loss without regime crisis.

Responses:
• Parliamentary debate
• Ministerial turnover
• Negotiated settlement
• No internal repression escalation

Procedure governs retreat.

Legitimacy persists despite defeat.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. SEPARATION WITHOUT CIVILIZATIONAL RUPTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Loss of colonies does not equal civilizational loss.

Outcomes:
• Trade relationships continue
• Legal culture exported
• Strategic refocus toward maritime dominance

Territory is relinquished to preserve system health.

Yorktown becomes an exit, not an endpoint.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yorktown clarifies Anglia’s strengths.

Lessons:
• Coalitions can defeat local forces
• Maritime dominance must be continuous
• Colonies can separate without hostility
• Procedure stabilizes strategic retreat

Anglia learns to lose selectively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yorktown encodes **controlled imperial failure**.

Lessons:
• Defeat need not delegitimize governance.
• Systems can survive contraction.
• Loss can be strategic reallocation.
• Procedure outlasts empire.

Yorktown is Anglia’s dignified withdrawal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Local defeat does not equal systemic collapse.
• Coalition timing matters more than force mass.
• Exit can preserve legitimacy.
• Trade can replace sovereignty.
• Yorktown proves Anglia’s resilience in loss.

Tension preserved without verdict.
