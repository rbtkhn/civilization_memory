────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Pitt the Elder as **Anglia’s imperial mobilizer**:
the statesman who reconceived war as a global maritime contest and
aligned popular legitimacy, naval power, and imperial ambition
during the Seven Years’ War.

Pitt the Elder teaches Anglia how to fight globally.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. WAR AS GLOBAL OPPORTUNITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pitt reframes conflict geography.

Context:
• European balance stalemate
• Expanding colonial competition
• Naval and commercial maturation

Strategic insight:
→ Empire is decided overseas, not in Germany.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MARITIME–IMPERIAL STRATEGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pitt prioritizes sea power absolutely.

Actions:
• Naval expansion
• Colonial offensives
• Peripheral strikes against French holdings
• Support of continental allies as distraction

Britain fights everywhere that France cannot defend.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. POPULAR LEGITIMACY & WAR ENTHUSIASM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike later Anglian leaders, Pitt mobilizes sentiment.

Characteristics:
• Patriotic rhetoric
• Parliamentary persuasion
• Public identification with empire

War gains moral energy without authoritarianism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. IMPERIAL CONFIDENCE FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Victory reshapes self-perception.

Effects:
• Global superiority normalized
• Colonial empire secured
• Strategic confidence institutionalized

Anglia learns it can shape world outcomes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LIMITS & AFTERSHOCKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Imperial success carries deferred costs.

Consequences:
• Fiscal expansion
• Colonial governance strain
• American crisis incubation

Victory creates future dilemmas.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Global war becomes Anglian specialty
• Maritime dominance embraced as destiny
• Popular legitimacy integrated into imperial strategy

Pitt the Elder opens the imperial era.
Others must manage it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Global framing transforms war outcomes.
• Sea power converts reach into dominance.
• Popular legitimacy amplifies strategy.
• Victory creates obligations as well as power.
• Pitt the Elder is Anglia’s imperial ignition.

Tension preserved without verdict.
