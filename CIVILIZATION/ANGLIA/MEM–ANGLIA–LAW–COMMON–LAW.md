────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Common Law as Anglia’s primary civilizational
continuity mechanism: a legal system that encodes authority through
precedent, procedure, and iterative judgment rather than codified
universal principle.

Common Law is not a legal product but a durability process.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Common Law emerges from post-Norman consolidation.

Context:
• Elite fusion after conquest
• Royal courts as dispute arbitrators
• Absence of comprehensive legal codification
• Need for legitimacy across fragmented custom

Law develops through cases, not declarations.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PRECEDENT AS AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority accumulates backward.

Mechanism:
• Prior judgments constrain future decisions
• Continuity valued over coherence
• Stability prioritized over clarity

Law becomes memory encoded in practice.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. PROCEDURE OVER SUBSTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legitimacy is procedural, not moral.

Characteristics:
• Fair process outweighs outcome justice
• Errors corrected incrementally
• Reform occurs through exception handling

Procedure absorbs conflict without rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ADAPTABILITY THROUGH ACCRETION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Change occurs without reset.

Dynamics:
• New realities layered onto old rulings
• Obsolete rules decay quietly
• No single moment of total reform

Law evolves invisibly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LIMITATION OF SOVEREIGN WILL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Common Law constrains rulers indirectly.

Effects:
• Executive bound by inherited process
• Authority diffused across courts
• Power exercised through offices, not persons

Law stabilizes governance by slowing it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. EXPORTABILITY & IMPERIAL TRANSMISSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Common Law travels with institutions.

Outcomes:
• Replicated across Anglian sphere
• Adapted locally without losing core logic
• Legal continuity maintained post-independence

Transmission preserves form, not identity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• High tolerance for ambiguity
• Resistance to revolutionary legality
• Legal continuity across regime change
• Law as stabilizer of political loss

Common Law enables replacement without rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Law can function as accumulated memory.
• Procedure outlasts ideology.
• Slowness is a stabilizing feature.
• Precedent enables adaptation without reset.
• Common Law is Anglia’s legal spine.

Tension preserved without verdict.
