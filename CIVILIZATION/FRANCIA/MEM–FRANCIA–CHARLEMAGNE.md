────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Charlemagne as the **Frankish–Latin reconstitutor**
of post-Roman order: the ruler who fused Germanic kingship, Roman law,
and Christian universality into a renewed Western imperium.

For Francia, Charlemagne represents **continuity reclaimed through
imperial restoration**, not revolutionary rupture.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FRANKISH ORIGINS & DYNASTIC CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Charlemagne emerges from Merovingian decline and Carolingian ascent.

Key features:
• Mayoral consolidation under Pepin III
• Military aristocracy anchored in land grants
• Fusion of warrior loyalty and Christian sanction

Authority is inherited but amplified by conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. IMPERIAL CROWNING & LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The coronation of 800 AD reframes Western authority.

Core dynamics:
• Papal anointing confers universal claim
• Roman imperial symbolism revived in the West
• Frankish power translated into Latin legitimacy

Imperial axiom:
→ Kingship becomes empire through sanctification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ADMINISTRATIVE & LEGAL ORDER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Charlemagne governs through delegation and reform.

Mechanisms:
• Missi dominici
• Capitularies
• Local counts under imperial oversight
• Legal pluralism preserved under imperial frame

Governance emphasizes correction, not uniformity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WAR, CONQUEST, & CHRISTIANIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Military expansion sustains imperial coherence.

Key theaters:
• Saxon Wars
• Lombard conquest
• Spanish March

Christianization operates as governance tool.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CULTURAL & EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Carolingian Renaissance institutionalizes learning.

Outcomes:
• Script standardization
• Clerical education
• Preservation of Latin texts

Culture stabilizes authority across distance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. SUCCESSION & FRAGMENTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Imperial durability proves personal, not institutional.

Aftermath:
• Division among heirs
• Treaty of Verdun (843)
• Gradual territorial differentiation

Empire dissolves into successor polities.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Empire can be restored through legitimacy synthesis.
• Sanctified authority outlasts conquest alone.
• Legal pluralism enables early imperial scale.
• Francia inherits Rome’s universal vocabulary.
• Personal empire precedes institutional endurance.

Tension preserved without verdict.
