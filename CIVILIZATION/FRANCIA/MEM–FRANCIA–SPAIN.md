────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Spain** as Francia’s primary early-modern
civilizational rival: a state that embodied universal monarchy,
global empire, and Catholic legitimacy at a scale Francia sought
but could not replicate simultaneously.

Spain is not Francia’s mirror.
It is its **counter-example**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. IMPERIAL UNIVERSALISM & CATHOLIC AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Spain fuses faith and empire.

Charles V articulates this universal mission:

> “The sun never sets on my dominions, because God entrusted
> me with the defense of Christendom and the unity of the faith
> across all lands that acknowledge His sovereignty.”  
> — Charles V, Imperial correspondence

Spain asserts universality through possession.

Francia asserts it through declaration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. GEOGRAPHIC DEPTH & MARITIME ADVANTAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Spain controls access to the Atlantic system.

Advantages:
• Early naval supremacy
• Colonial extraction
• Strategic insulation

Francisco de Vitoria frames imperial obligation:

> “The Spaniards have a right to travel and dwell in those lands,
> provided they do no harm, for the earth was made for all.”  
> — Vitoria, *Relectio de Indis*

Spain operationalizes theology.

Francia theorizes it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BOURBON DYNASTIC INTERLOCK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dynasty links—but does not unify—strategy.

Outcomes:
• Partial alignment
• Persistent rivalry
• Divergent priorities

Dynasty fails to harmonize civilizational logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DECLINE & STRATEGIC REVERSAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Spain declines earlier and deeper.

Symptoms:
• Fiscal exhaustion
• Administrative rigidity
• Colonial overstretch

Gracián reflects imperial fatigue:

> “Great empires perish not by foreign blows alone,
> but by the weight of their own greatness,
> when prudence no longer governs ambition.”  
> — Baltasar Gracián, *The Art of Worldly Wisdom*

Francia internalizes this lesson selectively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN ERA & DIVERGENT PATHS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-imperial outcomes diverge.

Spain:
• Retreats from universalism
• Becomes regional power

Francia:
• Retains universal language
• Loses enforcement capacity later

Spain exits the universal stage earlier.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Spain encodes **the cost of possession-based universalism**.

Lessons:
• Empire accelerates exhaustion.
• Faith-backed dominance is brittle.
• Global control collapses faster than narrative authority.
• Francia avoids Spain’s scale—but not its ambition.

Spain is Francia’s warning.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Spain universalizes through control.
• Francia universalizes through declaration.
• Possession exhausts faster than rhetoric.
• Maritime empire outpaces continental reach.
• Spain shows the terminal cost of total empire.

Tension preserved without verdict.
