────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Atlantic as **Francia’s arena of universalist
projection**: the maritime space through which Francia sought to convert
continental authority, cultural prestige, and moral ambition into global
reach.

Where Germania experiences the Atlantic as **dependency**, Francia
approaches it as **mission**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ATLANTIC ENTRY AS IMPERIAL TRANSLATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia enters the Atlantic as a continental power seeking extension.

Context:
• Centralized monarchy
• Universalist self-conception
• Competition with Iberian and Anglian empires

The Atlantic is not discovery—it is conversion.

Francia attempts to translate land-based authority into oceanic systems.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COLONIAL & COMMERCIAL PROJECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Atlantic access enables imperial experiments.

Functions:
• Colonial administration (Caribbean, North America, Africa)
• Mercantilist trade systems
• Cultural and legal transplantation

Unlike Germania:
• Authority is asserted
• Sovereignty is claimed
• Law follows the flag

Universalism travels outward.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. NAVAL AMBITION & STRUCTURAL LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia builds fleets but lacks enduring naval dominance.

Constraints:
• Resource competition with land armies
• Intermittent naval investment
• Strategic rivalry with Anglia

Naval power fluctuates with regime focus.

Maritime supremacy is episodic, not structural.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. REVOLUTIONARY & NAPOLEONIC RECODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Atlantic becomes ideologically charged.

Transformations:
• Revolutionary universalism exported
• Anti-monarchical rhetoric globalized
• Imperial ambition intensified

The Atlantic amplifies ideological exposure.

Universalism meets coalition resistance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DEFEAT, CONTAINMENT, & RETREAT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Repeated setbacks recalibrate Atlantic ambition.

Outcomes:
• Loss of major colonial holdings
• Naval containment by Anglia
• Retreat to continental prioritization

The Atlantic reveals the limits of Francia’s reach.

Universal claims exceed maritime capacity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. POST-IMPERIAL CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After empire, the Atlantic remains symbolically important.

Roles:
• Cultural influence
• Overseas territories
• Diplomatic presence

Projection persists without domination.

Influence replaces control.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Atlantic encodes **ambition beyond capacity**.

Lessons:
• Universalism seeks oceanic expression.
• Maritime systems punish inconsistency.
• Projection without naval primacy erodes authority.
• Francia’s Atlantic role oscillates between mission and limit.

The Atlantic tests Francia’s self-image.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia treats oceans as stages for ideas.
• Naval weakness constrains universal ambition.
• Colonial loss accelerates ideological inward turn.
• Atlantic exposure magnifies legitimacy stakes.
• Francia’s reach exceeds its maritime endurance.

Tension preserved without verdict.
