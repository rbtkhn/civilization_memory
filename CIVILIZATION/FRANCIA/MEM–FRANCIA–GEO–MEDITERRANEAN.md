────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Mediterranean as **Francia’s primordial
civilizational classroom**: the space where Roman inheritance,
Christian universality, and imperial imagination first shaped Francia’s
sense of order, legitimacy, and mission.

Where the Atlantic tests ambition, the Mediterranean **forms it**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ROMAN INHERITANCE & CULTURAL FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia’s Mediterranean orientation predates Francia itself.

Inheritance:
• Roman law and administration
• Latin culture
• Christian ecclesiastical networks

Southern Gaul functions as a Roman continuation zone.

Mediterranean order precedes French sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MEDIEVAL & ECCLESIASTICAL CENTRALITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mediterranean anchors Francia’s sacral authority.

Roles:
• Papal proximity
• Monastic networks
• Intellectual transmission
• Crusading pathways

Universalism is learned here.

Faith and empire intertwine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC COMPETITION & LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mediterranean power is contested.

Constraints:
• Italian city-states
• Ottoman expansion
• Iberian naval presence

Francia competes through:
• Diplomacy
• Proxy alliances
• Cultural influence

Direct control is selective.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COLONIAL & IMPERIAL EXTENSIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mediterranean becomes a bridge to empire.

Functions:
• North African expansion
• Levantine engagement
• Imperial logistics corridor

Unlike the Atlantic:
• Proximity stabilizes influence
• History legitimizes presence

Authority feels inherited, not imposed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN TRANSFORMATION & DECLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Twentieth-century shifts erode dominance.

Outcomes:
• Decolonization
• Reduced naval leverage
• Persistence of cultural ties

The Mediterranean remains symbolically central but strategically diluted.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mediterranean encodes **origin and legitimacy**.

Lessons:
• Universalism begins in shared civilization.
• Law and culture travel before force.
• Historical depth stabilizes influence.
• Decline is felt as loss of inheritance, not defeat.

The Mediterranean is Francia’s memory sea.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia’s universalism is Mediterranean in origin.
• Roman inheritance shapes French legitimacy grammar.
• Cultural depth substitutes for control.
• Mediterranean ties endure beyond empire.
• Francia’s sense of mission is older than the nation-state.

Tension preserved without verdict.
