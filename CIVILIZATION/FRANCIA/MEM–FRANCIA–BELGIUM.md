────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Belgium as **Francia’s denied extension**: a space
that repeatedly appears within Francia’s strategic, cultural, and
administrative reach, yet is ultimately removed to prevent French
continental consolidation.

Belgium is near-Francia—but never Francia.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVOLUTIONARY INCORPORATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia temporarily absorbs Belgium.

Context:
• Revolutionary expansion (1795)
• Administrative integration
• Legal standardization
• Strategic depth against coalitions

Belgium functions as northern frontage.

Universalism justifies annexation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. NAPOLEONIC CONTROL & LOSS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium remains vital under Napoleon.

Roles:
• Industrial base
• Military corridor
• Logistical hub

Defeat removes Belgium permanently.

Loss signals the end of northern expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. POST-1815 EXCLUSION LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Congress of Vienna denies French claims.

Rationale:
• Prevent Channel coast control
• Block revival of French hegemony
• Install buffer against Paris

Belgium becomes a containment device.

Francia is excluded by design.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CULTURAL PROXIMITY & POLITICAL DISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite exclusion, ties persist.

Links:
• Francophone population
• Legal and administrative influence
• Intellectual exchange

Cultural closeness does not equal sovereignty.

Influence replaces control.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. 1914 & STRATEGIC AMBIVALENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium’s violation reshapes conflict.

Dynamics:
• German invasion benefits Francia tactically
• Anglian entry guarantees survival
• Belgian suffering reframes legitimacy

Belgium becomes martyr terrain.

Francia benefits without owning.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. MODERN SETTLEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the modern era:
• Belgium embedded in European institutions
• Francia participates through multilateralism
• Direct control remains foreclosed

Constraint becomes normalized.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium encodes **the price of overreach**.

Lessons:
• Northern expansion provokes coalition response.
• Proximity increases threat perception.
• Cultural affinity cannot override balance logic.
• Francia’s limits are enforced geographically.

Belgium marks where Francia must stop.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Belgium is Francia’s unfinished north.
• Coalition politics override cultural logic.
• Buffer states freeze ambition.
• Universalism fails at chokepoints.
• Belgium reminds Francia of imposed limits.

Tension preserved without verdict.
