────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Napoleonic Code as Francia’s definitive legal
reset: the moment when universalist legitimacy was reasserted through
explicit codification, replacing fragmented custom with rationalized,
central authority.

The Code is law as declaration.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Code arises from revolutionary rupture.

Context:
• Destruction of ancien régime legality
• Revolutionary legal chaos
• Demand for uniformity and clarity
• Authority vacuum following regime collapse

Law must be rebuilt visibly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CODIFICATION AS LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority is asserted forward.

Mechanism:
• Comprehensive written statutes
• Universal applicability
• Central authorship
• Explicit hierarchy of norms

Law becomes a statement of order.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. UNIVERSALISM OVER PARTICULARISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Local custom is displaced.

Characteristics:
• Equality before the law
• Abolition of feudal privileges
• Rational abstraction prioritized over tradition

Justice is standardized.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. EXECUTIVE LEGAL PRIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Law is inseparable from state authority.

Effects:
• Executive guarantees legal coherence
• Courts apply rather than discover law
• Legal clarity substitutes for procedural negotiation

Law becomes an instrument of governance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. EXPORT THROUGH CONQUEST & PRESTIGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Code spreads rapidly.

Paths:
• Imperial imposition
• Voluntary adoption by reforming states
• Post-imperial prestige inheritance

Transmission follows authority, not continuity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. STRENGTHS AND LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strengths:
• Clarity
• Predictability
• Equality of status

Limits:
• Rigidity
• Dependence on central legitimacy
• Vulnerability to political fracture

Codification stabilizes only while belief holds.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Francia becomes exporter of legal modernity
• Law reinforces universalist identity
• Legal resets normalize regime change
• Authority remains declarative rather than procedural

The Code institutionalizes reset as virtue.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Codification restores order after rupture.
• Universal law substitutes for procedural trust.
• Clarity compensates for institutional weakness.
• Legal export follows prestige cycles.
• The Napoleonic Code is Francia’s legal rebirth.

Tension preserved without verdict.
