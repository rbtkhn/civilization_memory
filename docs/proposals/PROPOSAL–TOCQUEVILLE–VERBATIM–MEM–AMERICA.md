## 1. MEM–AMERICA–PRESIDENT–JACKSON

**File:** `content/civilizations/AMERICA/MEM–AMERICA–PRESIDENT–JACKSON.md`  
**Why:** Tocqueville visited during the Jackson presidency (1831–1832); *Democracy in America* Vol. I (1835) is the classic external portrait of Jacksonian democracy—majority rule, equality of conditions, expansion of suffrage, and the risks of majority tyranny. The MEM already has Jefferson (people as depository) and Twain; Tocqueville adds the contemporary external diagnostician.

**Suggested placement:** Section III (Populism as legitimacy claim) or Section V (Majority and institutional stress)—after the Jefferson SOURCE block, or in a new short paragraph on “external observer.”

**Suggested verbatim (majority / equality of conditions):**

> "The sovereignty of the Union is an abstract thing which is connected with but few external objects. The sovereignty of the States is perceived by the senses, is understood without effort; every day it produces results which fall under every eye."
> — Alexis de Tocqueville, *Democracy in America*, Vol. I (1835), on American federal structure (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-PRIMARY].

**Alternative (majority tyranny / democratic risk):**

> "I think that the species of oppression by which democratic nations will be threatened will resemble nothing that has preceded it. I seek in vain for an expression that exactly reproduces the idea; the old words despotism and tyranny will not suit. It is a new thing."
> — Alexis de Tocqueville, *Democracy in America*, Vol. II (1840) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL].

**Version:** Bump to 1.6; Upgrade Type: ADDITIVE · Tocqueville verbatim primary (Democracy in America). Add to MEM bibliography.

## 4. MEM–AMERICA–COLONIAL–PHILADELPHIA

**File:** `content/civilizations/AMERICA/MEM–AMERICA–COLONIAL–PHILADELPHIA.md`  
**Why:** Philadelphia encodes “procedural founding”—Congress, Declaration, Convention. Tocqueville’s theme of associations and civil society (“the art of associating together must grow and improve in the same ratio in which the equality of conditions is increased”) fits the city as seat of voluntary coordination and assembly.

**Suggested placement:** Section IV (Constitutional Convention 1787) or at the end of Section V (Civilizational memory function)—associations and local liberty as conditions for the republic Tocqueville later observed.

**Suggested verbatim:**

> — Alexis de Tocqueville, *Democracy in America*, Vol. II (1840) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-PRIMARY].
