## II. DOCTRINES 01 AND 02 — NO PHRASE CONTRADICTED

**Doctrine 01 (Peak Then Exhaustion):** No official statement found that "peak reach guarantees durability" or that "external pressure and internal rigidity cannot converge." Rhetoric stresses "continuity," "sustained governance," and "long-term stability" of **policy**—not a claim that current reach guarantees durability. **No specific phrase contradicted.**

**Doctrine 02 (Rupture-to-Reconstitution):** Official emphasis on "rule of law," "institutional cage," "constitution," and legal/administrative revision (Foreign Trade Law, State Council regulations, Bidding Law) is framed **under Party leadership** and as **party–state** governance (Xi on "full and rigorous Party self-governance," "absolute leadership over the armed forces"). Normalization (law, stability) is presented as **following** consolidation, not as the **mechanism** of consolidation. Authority remains party–state fusion and vertical penetration; law is instrument of governance, not substitute for it. **No specific phrase contradicted.**

## III. DOCTRINE 03 — ONE PHRASE TENSIONED BY OFFICIAL STRATEGY

**Specific phrase in CIV–DOCTRINE–CHINA (Operational Meaning):**
> "**Connectivity does not routinely precede or exist independently of conquest in this pattern.**"

(Definition echoes: "**rather than connectivity preceding conquest**." Hard Constraint: "If connectivity routinely preceded or was independent of conquest, 'conquest-as-condition' would be mis-specified.")

**Concrete government action / strategy (past 30 days and sustained official position):**

- **BRI and connectivity** are officially characterised as **voluntary**, **non-coercive**, and **independent** of coercion or conquest:
  - BRI "**not a tool for expanding geopolitical influence**" but "a platform for cooperation" (SCIO, FMPRC-derived).
  - Participation "**voluntary**"; countries "**voluntarily** signed" Belt and Road cooperation documents; "**independent** decision-making" for participating countries; "**optional**" participation.
  - Framing as "**voluntary, mutually beneficial** framework rather than a **coercive** mechanism"; "participants maintain **independent** choice in engagement."
- **Recent 30-day actions** consistent with this strategy: visa-free expansion for BRI connectivity (Xinjiang hub); "two-way investment" with BRI partners; Pinglu Canal and corridor projects; rhetoric of "voluntary opening up" and "win-win" connectivity.

**Nature of the conflict:**

- The **doctrine** encodes a **historical pattern** (Mongol/Pax Mongolica): in that pattern, connectivity does **not** routinely exist **independently of** conquest; conquest creates the condition for connectivity.
- The **current official strategy** presents BRI connectivity as existing **independently of** conquest or coercion—voluntary, non-coercive, independent choice.
- So the **specific phrase** "connectivity does not routinely … **exist independently of conquest** in this pattern" is **tensioned** by the government’s **self-description** of BRI: that self-description asserts connectivity (BRI) as independent of coercion/conquest, which the doctrine’s pattern would not predict as the norm.

**Caveat:** Doctrine 03 is a claim about a **historical** pattern (Yuan/Mongol), not a direct prescription for current policy. The "contradiction" is between (a) the **pattern** encoded in the doctrine and (b) the **rhetorical strategy** by which China describes its own connectivity (BRI) as independent of conquest/coercion. So this is **rhetorical/self-description** tension with the doctrine’s encoded pattern, not necessarily a finding that "China violated Doctrine 03" as a rule of conduct. The doctrine’s Hard Constraint would be violated if **in fact** connectivity routinely preceded or was independent of conquest in the cases the doctrine purports to explain (historical); current BRI rhetoric does not refute that history—it only presents **current** connectivity as voluntary and independent of coercion.

**Summary:** One **specific phrase** is tensioned: **Doctrine 03 Operational Meaning** — "connectivity does not routinely precede or **exist independently of conquest** in this pattern." **Concrete strategy:** Official characterisation of BRI as voluntary, non-coercive, and based on independent partner choice, i.e. connectivity framed as **existing independently of** conquest/coercion.

## IV. RECOMMENDATION

For STATE–CHINA and doctrine audits: treat Doctrine 03 as **historically** scoped; the tension is between **current self-description** (BRI as voluntary, independent of coercion) and the **pattern** the doctrine describes (connectivity not routinely independent of conquest). No change to doctrine or to material options is required; the finding is for analytical awareness and for cross-checking any future use of Doctrine 03 in BRI/connectivity assessments.
