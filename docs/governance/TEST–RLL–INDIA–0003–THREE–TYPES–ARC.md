## II. TYPE-BY-TYPE ARC VALIDATION

### Type 1: Invader at edge (force at distance; invader's army refuses)

**Hyphasis (326 BC)**  
- **MEM:** Invader's mechanism fails; army refusal; exposure on Alexander.  
- **ARC:** Arrian (*Anabasis*, *Indica*) — canonical source for Alexander's India campaign and army refusal at Hyphasis. ARC–INDIA lists Arrian in ARC-T-ANCIENT.  
- **Conclusion:** Type 1 supported by ARC (Arrian). MEM and ARC align.

**Ghaznavid return from Somnath (1026)**  
- **MEM / RESEARCH:** Force at distance; depth resistance; logistical and local resistance; only Punjab held. No army mutiny but same structural condition (invader limit).  
- **ARC:** Al-Biruni (973–1048), Kalhana (12th c.) — medieval period. Al-Biruni's *Alberuni's India* and Kalhana's *Rajatarangini* may cover Ghaznavid context. CIV–ARC–INDIA does not cite specific passages for Somnath return; MEM–INDIA–WAR–GHAZNAVID and RESEARCH are primary.  
- **Conclusion:** Type 1 parallel (Ghaznavid) is MEM/RESEARCH-derived; ARC provides period coverage. No ARC contradiction; gap is absence of verbatim ARC quote for Somnath in test (optional enhancement: add ARC verbatim when available).

### Type 2: Defender elites (fragmentation; elites defect to invader)

**Plassey (1757)**  
- **MEM:** Defender's mechanism fails; elite defection; Plassey decisive psychologically.  
- **ARC:** Durant, Ferguson (ARC-T-MODERN)—conquest as exploitation, Company, "bribing and murdering," administrative takeover. Support mechanism-failure and political/financial leverage; do not emphasize decisive field battle.  
- **Conclusion:** Type 2 (Plassey) supported by ARC. MEM and ARC align.

**First Panipat (1526)**  
- **MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL:** Defection theory framing; Daulat Khan Lodi invited Babur; defender's mechanism failed before battle.  
- **ARC:** Babur (*Baburnama*), Abul Fazl (*Akbarnama*) — ARC-T-MEDIEVAL, ARC-T-EARLY-MOD. Babur's memoirs are first-hand; Abul Fazl provides Mughal court perspective.  
- **Conclusion:** Type 2 (Panipat) supported by ARC (Babur, Abul Fazl). MEM and RESEARCH align with ARC.

### Type 3: Invader's proxy (invader's local force defects against invader)

**1857 Rebellion**  
- **MEM–INDIA–WAR–1857:** Invader's proxy defection; sepoy mutiny; exposure on Company then Crown; jurisdiction shift.  
- **ARC:** James Mill (*The History of British India*), Mountstuart Elphinstone (*The History of India*) — ARC-T-EARLY-MOD; Ramachandra Guha (*India After Gandhi*) — ARC-T-MODERN. Mill and Elphinstone cover Company/Crown transition; Guha covers post-1947 but 1857 as historical reference. Ferguson (*Empire*) — Company-to-Crown after 1857.  
- **Conclusion:** Type 3 (1857) supported by ARC (Mill, Elphinstone, Ferguson on Company-to-Crown; structural dependence on Indian troops is standard historiography). MEM encoding (invader's proxy defection, jurisdiction shift) aligns with ARC. No contradiction.

---

## III. MEM vs ARC: consistency

- **No contradiction:** All three types have ARC support or no ARC contradiction. Type 1: Arrian (Hyphasis); Type 2: Babur, Abul Fazl (Panipat), Durant, Ferguson (Plassey); Type 3: Mill, Elphinstone, Ferguson (1857, Company-to-Crown).
- **Gap:** Ghaznavid (Type 1 parallel) relies on MEM and RESEARCH; ARC medieval sources (Al-Biruni, Kalhana) are period-appropriate but no verbatim passage cited in this test for Somnath. Optional: add verbatim ARC quote in future MEM or test revision.
- **Scope:** Three-type frame is validated with MEM + ARC. SYNTHESIS 0003 / extension of RLL–INDIA–0002 holds for ARC consistency.

---

## IV. VERDICT

**Three defection types hold** with MEM + ARC. Type 1 (Hyphasis) — Arrian. Type 2 (Plassey, Panipat) — Durant, Ferguson, Babur, Abul Fazl. Type 3 (1857) — Mill, Elphinstone, Ferguson. No ARC source contradicts the three-type frame. **Recommendation:** Three-types-ARC test supports freeze and promotion of SYNTHESIS 0003 and extension of RLL–INDIA–0002 to include third type.
