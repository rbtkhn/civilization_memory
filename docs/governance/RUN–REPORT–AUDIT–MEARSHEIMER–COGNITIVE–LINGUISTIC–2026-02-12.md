## IV. Cognitive Patterns in Transcript

- **Structure first:** He repeatedly frames in terms of **no military solution**, **no plausible story**, **no opportunity** — structural impossibility, not preference. Aligns with rule's "power distribution," "state as unit," "no war-winning strategy."
- **Incentive structure:** Explicit for **individuals** ("he'd be fired," "nobody wants to be the first mover," "there will be a price to be paid") and for **states** ("from their point of view it makes eminently good sense"). Rule emphasizes state-level incentive; transcript shows **same logic applied to elite narrative production**.
- **Demand for mechanism:** "Please explain to me how this is going to be done"; "I've not heard anybody tell a plausible story"; "Give us some specifics... you never get any." This is **structural realism as demand for causal story** — not just "the fact is" but "you must supply a plausible strategy or your argument fails."
- **Reality vs narrative:** "detached from reality," "bears hardly any resemblance to what's actually happening," "in private, facts and logic do prevail... in terms of how you talk to the public... it's necessary to make all these arguments that have little resemblance to reality." Fits rule's **blame/narrative** and **misperception** (picture we have in our head); transcript adds **public/private split** explicitly.
- **Interview register:** First-name ("Danny"), "you and I," "your point's right on the money," agreement then pivot. Same analytical content as in rule; **phraseology is more conversational** than some rule markers drawn from other contexts (e.g. Glenn Diesen interview).

**Verdict:** In **interview/guest** mode, Mearsheimer leads with **sequential points** and **direct structural claim** ("Look, this is very simple"; "The fact is"); he **demands plausible story/specifics** from opponents; he **dismisses** bad arguments ("nonsense," "not even an interesting question," "detached from reality," "blowing smoke," "pipe dream," "Is this guy serious?"); he uses **"you and I"** and **private vs public** to explain elite narrative. Rule and MIND are **largely authenticated**; gaps are **interview-specific markers** and **dismissal/reality-detachment** phrases.
