### Causal chain (consistent)

1. War finance → BoE (1694).  
2. BoE → template for Hamilton → First Bank (1791).  
3. Jackson → destruction of Second Bank → no US central bank (1830s–1913).  
4. Void → private concentration (Morgan) → 1907 panic → Morgan as private LOLR.  
5. 1907 → demand for institutional LOLR → Aldrich Commission → Jekyll Island (1910) → Warburg design input → Federal Reserve Act (1913).  
6. Fed = third central-bank moment; Hamilton's logic returns under new design.

**Verdict:** No logical contradictions. Sequence and causation are aligned across MEMs.

## VI. HAMILTON–BANK OF ENGLAND LINEAGE (ASYMMETRY)

- **BANK–ENGLAND:** "Hamilton: Explicitly modelled the First Bank on the Bank of England—chartered corporation, public-debt management, note issue, lender of last resort logic. See MEM–ANGLIA–HAMILTON."
- **HAMILTON:** Section III ("Finance as Sovereignty") states "National bank creation" and "Credit becomes national glue"; Section X ("Later Central-Bank Return") states "Hamilton's First Bank of the United States (1791) established the template" and "Federal Reserve (1913) represents the third central-bank moment." **No mention of Bank of England or BoE as model.**

**Verdict:** The lineage BoE → First Bank is asserted in BANK–ENGLAND but not reflected in HAMILTON. Not a contradiction, but asymmetric. Recommendation: add one sentence in HAMILTON (e.g. in §III or §V) that the First Bank was modelled on the Bank of England, and/or add MEM–ANGLIA–BANK–ENGLAND to a new MEM CONNECTIONS section in HAMILTON.
