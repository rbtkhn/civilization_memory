# PROPOSAL — New Anglia MEMs Suggested by Churchill Vol. 2 (*The New World*)

**Status:** PROPOSAL (not yet implemented)  
**Scope:** New MEM–ANGLIA files justified by Churchill, *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 2: *The New World* (1956)  
**Governance:** CIV–MEM–CORE · CIV–INDEX–ANGLIA · CIV–MEM–TEMPLATE  
**Last Updated:** January 2026

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This document suggests **new MEMs to create** based on Churchill’s
*The New World* (Vol. 2). Each proposed MEM fills an index gap (no
current MEM for that subject), aligns with a Vol. 2 chapter or theme,
and would carry Churchill Vol. 2 SOURCE blocks (ARC-T-MODERN,
ERC-SECONDARY). Existing MEMs (HENRY–VIII, ELIZABETH–I, WAR–ENGLISH–CIVIL,
DYNASTY–STUART) already cite Vol. 2; this proposal covers **subjects
that have no dedicated MEM yet**.

III. SUGGESTED NEW MEMs (BY INDEX SECTION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**A) WAR (Section C)**

| Proposed MEM | Vol. 2 hook | Civilizational theme | Index section |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| **MEM–ANGLIA–WAR–ARMADA** | Ch. “The Spanish Armada” (Book IV) | 1588; defeat of Spanish invasion; Elizabethan consolidation; naval/defensive legitimacy | C) WAR |

**Rationale:** No current MEM treats the Spanish Armada as a single event. ELIZABETH–I and DYNASTY–TUDOR cover the period but not the battle/campaign as such. Vol. 2 devotes a chapter to it. Would connect to MEM–ANGLIA–ELIZABETH–I, MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–TUDOR, MEM–ANGLIA–ENGLAND.

---

**B) PERSON (Section G)**

| Proposed MEM | Vol. 2 hook | Civilizational theme | Index section |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| **MEM–ANGLIA–CHARLES–I** | “Charles I and Buckingham,” “The Personal Rule,” “The Axe Falls” (Book V) | Personal rule; revolt of Parliament; regicide; sacral kingship broken | G) PERSON |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–CROMWELL** | “The Lord Protector,” “The English Republic” (Book VI) | Interregnum; military republicanism; Lord Protector; legacy for Restoration | G) PERSON |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–JAMES–I** | “The United Crowns” (Book V) | Union of crowns (England/Scotland); James VI & I; legitimacy and multiple kingdoms | G) PERSON |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–JAMES–II** | “The Catholic King,” “The Revolution of 1688” (Book VI) | Catholic king; flight; Glorious Revolution; dynastic and confessional rupture | G) PERSON |

**Rationale:**
- **CHARLES–I:** Vol. 2 centres the Civil War on Charles I and Buckingham, Personal Rule, and the Axe Falls. No PERSON MEM for Charles I; WAR–ENGLISH–CIVIL and DYNASTY–STUART cover the period but not the figure.
- **CROMWELL:** Vol. 2 has a chapter “The Lord Protector.” No MEM for Cromwell; DYNASTY–STUART touches Interregnum but does not focus on Cromwell as figure.
- **JAMES–I:** “The United Crowns” opens Book V; James VI & I as first Stuart in England. No PERSON MEM for James I.
- **JAMES–II:** “The Catholic King” and “The Revolution of 1688” close Vol. 2. No PERSON MEM for James II; 1688 is in DYNASTY–STUART but not as a dedicated figure MEM.

---

**C) OTHER / MISC or LAW (Section H or D)**

| Proposed MEM | Vol. 2 hook | Civilizational theme | Index section |
|--------------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| **MEM–ANGLIA–REVOLUTION–1688** | Ch. “The Revolution of 1688” (Book VI) | Glorious Revolution; William III; constitutional settlement; Parliament’s supremacy | D) LAW or H) OTHER |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–MAYFLOWER** | Ch. “The Mayflower” (Book V) | 1620; Plymouth; early Anglian settlement in America; “New World” in Vol. 2 title | H) OTHER / MISC |

**Rationale:**
- **REVOLUTION–1688:** Vol. 2 ends with the Revolution of 1688. A dedicated MEM would capture the constitutional/legitimacy shift (Bill of Rights, William III, parliamentary supremacy). Could sit under LAW (constitutional) or OTHER. DYNASTY–STUART and LAW–PARLIAMENT already reference 1688 but do not treat it as a single event MEM.
- **MAYFLOWER:** Vol. 2 includes “The Mayflower” in Book V; the volume title *The New World* underscores colonization. No MEM for the Mayflower or Plymouth; UNITED–STATES–AMERICA could be enhanced, or a dedicated MEM–ANGLIA–MAYFLOWER (or MEM–ANGLIA–COLONIZATION–PLYMOUTH) would give Churchill’s “New World” thread a clear home.

IV. CREATION PRIORITY (RECOMMENDED ORDER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. **MEM–ANGLIA–WAR–ARMADA** — Single WAR MEM for 1588; high symbolic value; Vol. 2 Ch. “The Spanish Armada”; supports ELIZABETH–I, DYNASTY–TUDOR.
2. **MEM–ANGLIA–CHARLES–I** — Central to Vol. 2 Book V; regicide as civilizational rupture; supports WAR–ENGLISH–CIVIL, DYNASTY–STUART.
3. **MEM–ANGLIA–CROMWELL** — Vol. 2 “The Lord Protector”; interregnum and military republicanism; supports DYNASTY–STUART, WAR–ENGLISH–CIVIL.
4. **MEM–ANGLIA–REVOLUTION–1688** — Vol. 2 closing chapter; constitutional settlement; supports DYNASTY–STUART, LAW–PARLIAMENT.
5. **MEM–ANGLIA–JAMES–II** — “The Catholic King”; flight and 1688; supports REVOLUTION–1688, DYNASTY–STUART.
6. **MEM–ANGLIA–JAMES–I** — “The United Crowns”; opens Stuart period in England; supports DYNASTY–STUART.
7. **MEM–ANGLIA–MAYFLOWER** — “The Mayflower”; Vol. 2 “New World” theme; supports UNITED–STATES–AMERICA, BRITISH–EMPIRE.
