## 2. Mearsheimer Cognition Criteria (Reference)

- **Power distribution** as primary variable; state as unit of analysis  
- **Force-ratio / offense–defense balance** where applicable (e.g. littoral vs external)  
- **Chokepoint significance** (RLL–0024); **projection corridors**; **outlet control** (RLL–0032)  
- **Incentive structure** explicit: what does the balance of power tell states to do?  
- **Direct thesis** ("The fact is that..."); structural logic; geographic constraints  
- **GEO–MEM blend:** ~2/3 strategic analysis, ~1/3 ARC (or civilizational evidence)

## 3. File-by-File Evaluation

### 3.1 MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–ARCTIC–OCEAN

**Strategic insight:** High. Northern frontier; ice as constraint; Northern Sea Route; submarine sanctuary; resource access; northern approach denial. Power distribution (§ VI) clear: Russia holds position; adversaries face distance and ice.

**Sophistication:** High. Terrain geometry, NSR control logic, SSBN sanctuary, ice as variable. Uses "The fact is that". No historical narrative; pure structural.

**Gaps:** (1) **Incentive structure** not explicit—e.g. "Russia has structural incentive to dominate the NSR and deny others ASW access; other Arctic states have incentive to contest resource and transit rights." (2) **Force-ratio** or capability note absent (e.g. icebreaker balance, SSBN vs ASW capacity).

**Recommendations:** Add one short subsection or paragraph: "Incentive structure: Russia is incentivized to treat the Arctic as sanctuary and corridor; NATO/others to monitor and contest resource and transit. Force balance: icebreaker and SSBN presence favor Russia within the basin; external ASW and surface projection remain constrained by ice and distance."

---

### 3.2 MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–ATLANTIC–OCEAN

**Strategic insight:** Framed as civilizational/system exclusion, not as GEO–MEM. "Structurally denied maritime system"; "someone else's ocean"; chokepoint dependency and indirect access are present but subordinate to Braudel/Lieven/Mahan interpretive frame.

**Sophistication:** High for a Subject MEM (ARC A–D, taxonomy, strategic consequences). **Not** expressed as Mearsheimer strategic cognition. Class = MEM (Civilizational Memory); no GEO–MEM cognitive declaration. Section VI heading typo: "INTERFAAD" → "INTERFACE" (or "MILITARY INTERFACE").

**Gaps:** (1) No GEO–MEM layer: power distribution at Baltic/North Sea exits, force-ratio (Russia cannot contest Atlantic; incentive to avoid dependency), chokepoint chain (Danish Straits → North Sea → Atlantic). (2) Typo in § VI.

**Recommendations:** (a) Fix typo: "MODERN MILITARY INTERFAAD" → "MODERN MILITARY INTERFACE". (b) Either: **Option A** — Add a dedicated GEO–MEM (e.g. MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–ATLANTIC–ACCESS) with pure Mearsheimer cognition (chokepoint chain, force-ratio, incentive structure for Russia to rely on continental depth and alternative seas); or **Option B** — Insert a short "Strategic geography (Mearsheimer frame)" subsection in the existing file: chokepoint dependency (Baltic/North Sea exits), force-ratio (no Russian Atlantic naval preponderance), incentive (denial and alternative corridors over Atlantic command).

### 3.3 MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–BALTIC–SEA

**Strategic insight:** High. Semi-enclosed basin; Danish Straits chokepoint; eastern position value; access control (internal/external); NATO encirclement and "Russian pocket"; power distribution post-enlargement. Panin reference in strategic insights (control of gate vs balanced system).

**Sophistication:** High. Basin geometry, littoral distribution, defensibility and exposure. ARC A–D. Mearsheimer quote. Clear "The fact is that".

**Gaps:** (1) **Incentive structure** could be one sentence: Russia's incentive to keep straits open for trade and egress vs NATO's to monitor and threaten closure in crisis. (2) **Force-ratio** or posture: relative naval/air capacity in basin (e.g. Russian Baltic Fleet vs combined NATO littoral).

**Recommendations:** Add one paragraph under Power Distribution or new "Incentive structure" bullet: "Russia is incentivized to maintain Baltic access for trade and fleet egress while accepting defensive posture; NATO littoral states are incentivized to deny Russian sea control and monitor movements. Force balance in basin favors NATO (combined littoral and straits control); Russia holds eastern head but cannot dominate basin."

### 3.4 MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–BERING–STRAIT

**Strategic insight:** High. Sole Arctic–Pacific chokepoint; sovereignty split (Russia–USA); Diomede Islands; mutual vulnerability; control sequence table. Clear chokepoint logic and power distribution.

**Sophistication:** High. Structured GEO data (parameters, significance, control sequence); ARC A–D. "Balance of presence, not dominance."

### 3.5 MEM–RUSSIA–GEO–BLACK–SEA

**Gaps:** (1) **Incentive structure** explicit: Russia to hold Crimea and dominate basin; Turkey to manage straits and limit Russian Mediterranean egress; Ukraine to retain littoral or face suffocation. (2) **Force-ratio** or fleet balance (pre/post 2022; Montreux limits).

**Recommendations:** Add short "Incentive structure" sentence or bullet: "Russia is incentivized to hold Crimea and Kerch to secure Azov and basin dominance; Turkey to use straits as leverage; Ukraine to deny Russian monopoly. ## 4. Cross-Cutting Findings

### 4.1 Classification Inconsistency

- **Two files are classed as MEM (Civilizational Memory), not GEO–MEM:** ATLANTIC–OCEAN, WHITE–SEA. They lack the GEO–MEM cognitive declaration and the 2/3 Mearsheimer / 1/3 Mercouris blend. For a corpus that treats GEO–MEMs as Mearsheimer-primary, this creates a gap: "ocean" geography that is not auditable under the same GEO–MEM RLLs (e.g. 0024, 0032).

### 4.2 Recurring Gaps Across GEO–MEMs

1. **Incentive structure** is rarely explicit. Most files describe power distribution and constraints but do not state in one sentence what the balance of power tells each actor to do. Adding a single "Incentive structure" sentence or bullet per file would align with CIV–MIND–MEARSHEIMER (incentive structure as core).
2. **Force-ratio or force-posture** (relative capability, offense–defense balance) appears in places but is not standardized. Baltic, Black Sea, Gulf of Finland, Arctic would benefit from one concrete sentence (e.g. who holds numerical or positional advantage in basin/approach).
3. **RLL cross-reference** in body text: Some GEO–MEMs (e.g. Baltic) reference RLL or Panin; others do not. Explicit "RLL–0024 applies" or "RLL–0032 (outlet control)" in relevant sections would aid consistency.

### 4.3 Strengths

- **Chokepoint logic** is strong and consistent in Baltic, Black Sea, Bering, Azov, Gulf of Finland. RLL–0024 (Chokepoint Permanence) is well supported.
- **Power distribution** sections are present and clear in all MEARSHEIMER-declared GEO–MEMs.
- **"The fact is that"** and direct structural thesis are used in most GEO–MEMs.
- **ARC integration** (A–D) is solid where present; Bering has structured GEO data table as a plus.
