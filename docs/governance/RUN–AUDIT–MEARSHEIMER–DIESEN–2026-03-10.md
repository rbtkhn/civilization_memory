## II. Transcript-Derived Marker Inventory

### A. Opening / Sequential Structure

- **"Well, a couple points."** — Matches interview opener (profile v3.3).
- **Sequential argument:** "First of all... Furthermore... So when you marry..."
- **Direct thesis:** "it's quite clear that the war is not going well for the United States"; "The question is how do you get the Iranians to agree to settle this war?"

### B. "The fact is" / Direct Assertion

- "**The fact is** to create regime change and win a decisive victory in Iraq, we had to use land power."
- "**the fact is** they're helping out, especially with regard to intelligence"
- "**There's no question that** you want to have a powerful air force"
- "**There's no question that** the Iranians can take out the petroleum infrastructure"
- "**the historical record is unequivocally clear on this**" (repeated)
- "**It just can't.**" / "**It just doesn't work.**"

### C. Plausible Story / Mechanism Demand

- "**I think nobody can tell a plausible story about how this war ends**"
- "**nobody's been able to tell me a plausible story** as to how you bring this thing to an end"
- "**How do you find the off-ramp?** And I don't think there is an off-ramp at this point."

### D. Incentive Structure

- "**the Iranians have an incentive to continue the war**... to turn it into a protracted war of attrition"
- "**they have no incentive to settle this one on America's terms**"
- "**the more time goes by, the more desperate we are going to be to settle this one**"
- "**we have a vested interest in** making sure that European states don't trade sophisticated technologies with the Chinese"
- "**From their point of view**" (repeated — Iran, Russia, China, Europe)

### E. Misperception / "Picture in Our Head"

- "**the pictures... that he has in his head bear no resemblance to reality**" (Trump)
- "**he has all these pictures in his head that bear no resemblance to reality**"
- "**He's constantly saying things that are just simply untrue. And if he believes them... you can see why he just does foolish things.**"

### F. Historical Record / Air Power

- "**The historical record is unequivocally clear on this.** Strategic air power by itself cannot win a war. It just can't."
- "**You can't win wars, especially against formidable adversaries, with air power alone. It just doesn't work.**"
- "**countries can absorb that punishment**" — WWII, Korea, Vietnam
- "**don't believe... that's going to work. It hasn't worked in the past and there's no reason to think it's going to work now.**"

### G. Structural / Power Distribution

- "**the Iranians are in the driver's seat**"
- "**the idea that we have escalation dominance**... **I think is a facetious argument**"
- "**they have in a way an assured destruction capability**" (Gulf States)
- "**This is not the way the real world works**" — Iran has a say

### H. Blame / Narrative (Second-Order)

- "**Netanyahu bamboozled President Trump**"
- "**Prime Minister Netanyahu**... had been trying to **drag us into a war** against Iran by promising us that it would produce a great victory"
- Europe: "**lick America's boots**" / "**lick President Trump's boots**"
- "**President Trump is a classic bully**" — "**The only way you deal with a bully is you stand up to that bully**"

### I. Positioning / In-Group

- "**if I were an Indian**... I would not get too close to the United States"
- "**The United States is a rogue elephant**"
- "**you want to maximize the amount of leverage you have over the United States**"

### J. Conclusion Delivery (Plain, No Hedging)

- "**I don't see what the offramp is at this point in time**"
- "**I hope I'm wrong. I hope I'm missing something**" — moderate hedge but frames as hope, not doubt
- "**it's hardly surprising that we're in a real mess**"
- "**there's no apparent way to get out of it**"
