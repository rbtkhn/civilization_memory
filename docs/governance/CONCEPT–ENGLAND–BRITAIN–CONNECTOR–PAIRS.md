# CONCEPT: England and Britain Connector Pairs (France–Anglia, Germany–Anglia)

**Status:** ACTIVE  
**Governed by:** CMC 3.4  
**Last updated:** February 2026

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This document defines the **connector pairs** for the Francia–Anglia
and Germania–Anglia interfaces, using the same subject/two-encoding
logic as CONCEPT–FRANCE–GERMANY–DOUBLE–HELIX. England (medieval/early
modern) and Britain (18th c.–present) are treated as distinct temporal
scopes to reflect the 1707 Act of Union and the shift in the
relationship's character.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONNECTOR PAIRS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**England pair (medieval / early modern, pre-1707):**

| Subject   | France                    | Anglia                    |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| England   | MEM–FRANCE–ENGLAND        | MEM–ANGLIA–FRANCE         |

Francia encodes England as territorial rival, expelled (1453). Anglia
encodes France as continental adversary, Hundred Years' War. Same
interface, distinct encodings.

**Britain pair (18th c.–present, 1707+):**

| Subject   | France                    | Anglia                    |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| Britain   | MEM–FRANCE–BRITAIN        | MEM–ANGLIA–FRANCE         |

Francia encodes Britain as maritime rival, necessary ally. Anglia
encodes France as ally against Germany. Same interface, distinct
encodings.

**Germany–Anglia pairs:**

| Subject   | Germany                   | Anglia                    |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| England   | MEM–GERMANY–ENGLAND       | MEM–ANGLIA–GERMANY        |
| Britain   | MEM–GERMANY–BRITAIN       | MEM–ANGLIA–GERMANY        |

Germany encodes England as kinship-turned-rival (full span); Britain
as balancer, naval rival (18th c.+). Anglia encodes Germany for both.
MEM–ANGLIA–FRANCE and MEM–ANGLIA–GERMANY each serve as the Anglia-side
for multiple pairs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. USE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When analysing a node that touches France–Anglia or Germany–Anglia
(e.g. Hundred Years' War, Napoleonic Wars, Entente, naval race), load
the relevant pair and cite both perspectives with explicit attribution.
Preserve tension—do not synthesize away the difference between
encodings.
