III. INFLUENCE OF CHINESE-LANGUAGE SOURCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Direct influence (cited or quoted in Chinese):** **None.** No session MEM quotes or cites a Chinese-language document or publication in the body.

**Indirect influence:** Plausible but **unverifiable from the MEMs alone**.
- Narrative in MEM–CHINA–WAR–MONGOL–JIN is consistent with scholarship that uses 《元史》《金史》;
- MEM–CHINA–BOHAI–TANG and MEM–CHINA–WAR–TAIPING–REBELLION reference "Tang official histories" and "Qing edicts / Taiping documents" but do not quote them.
- Hong Kong, Macau, Pearl, Shenzhen, Amur rely on treaty names and "PRC documentation" without specifying language or edition.

So **influence** of Chinese-language sources is at most **indirect** (via English-language scholarship that uses Chinese primaries, or via unspecified "PRC documentation"). The MEMs do not assert or demonstrate direct use of Chinese-language institutional or scholarly sources.

V. FINDINGS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Presence:** Three MEMs (Mongol–Jin, Bohai–Tang, Taiping) **name** Chinese-language primary sources; others refer to primary materials that exist in Chinese but do not label them as Chinese-language. No session MEM quotes verbatim from a Chinese text or cites a Chinese-language institutional source (People's Daily, CASS, Xinhua, etc.).

2. **Influence:** Any influence of Chinese-language sources is **indirect** (via English secondary literature or unspecified primary documentation). The MEMs do not demonstrate direct reliance on Chinese-language institutional or scholarly output.

3. **Alignment with ARC–CHINA v3.1:** ARC–CHINA now authorizes Chinese-language institutional sources (State Council www.gov.cn, People's Daily, CASS, CIIS, Caixin Chinese) and a language note. The **session MEMs do not yet use** these; they were edited or created before or without reference to them. Future MEM creation or revision could explicitly add Chinese-language primary or institutional citations where relevant (e.g. Hong Kong/Macau citing PRC Basic Law or people.cn on handover; Amur citing PRC or Russian official positions in Chinese where applicable).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RECOMMENDATIONS (OPTIONAL)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **No mandatory change.** The MEMs comply with ARC as it stood; they do not claim to use Chinese-language institutional sources.
- **Optional enhancement:** When revising MEM–CHINA–HONG–KONG, MEM–CHINA–MACAU, or MEM–CHINA–GEO–PEARL–RIVER for other reasons, consider adding one Primary or Secondary line that explicitly references a Chinese-language source (e.g. "Basic Law (Hong Kong/Macau) — Chinese text: npc.gov.cn" or "Xinhua/people.cn on handover narrative") and/or one verbatim short quote from an ARC–CHINA–T–INSTITUTIONAL Chinese-language source, with attribution per ARC v3.1.
- **New MEMs:** For future MEMs that thematise PRC policy, handover, or party-state narrative, consider citing at least one Chinese-language institutional source from ARC-T-INSTITUTIONAL where the primary document or statement is in Chinese.
