A.1 COUNTER-EVIDENCE SCAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Question:** Is there MEM or ENTRY evidence that Arab success was *not* primarily exhaustion/vacuum (e.g. religious motivation, Persian institutional failure alone, timeline mismatch)?

**MEMs consulted:**
- MEM–PERSIA–ISLAM (v1.2): Encodes absorption, continuity, conquest without erasure; Section VII (structural context) states post-conquest mutual incentive and “conquest without erasure.” Does not attribute Arab victory to religion alone or to Persian institutional failure alone; does not contradict exhaustion thesis.
- MEM–PERSIA–SASANIAN–KHOSROW–II (v1.2): Explicit—“The war (602–628) exhausts both empires”; “collapse that left both empires exhausted before the Arab conquests.” Supports sequence: exhaustion → Arab conquest.
- MEM–PERSIA–SASANIAN–DYNASTY (v1.3): “The dynasty collapsed under… Arab-Islamic conquest”; liability section: “Collapse was not undone; **the vacuum was filled by Islam**.” Direct support for vacuum thesis.
- MEM–PERSIA–WAR–ROME (v2.3): Al-Tabari “Thus it continued until the rise of Islam”—wars run until Islam; implies exhaustion then new actor.

**Result:** No counter-evidence found in corpus. No MEM attributes Arab success to religious motivation alone or Persian institutional failure independent of Roman–Persian exhaustion. Timeline (628 peace/collapse → 630s Arab expansion) is consistent. **Counter-evidence scan: passed (ruled out).**

A.2 ALTERNATIVE HYPOTHESES (ACH)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

| Hypothesis | Statement | Discriminating evidence |
|------------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **H1: Exhaustion/vacuum** | Arab success explained by power distribution: two exhausted empires; third actor with fresh capacity enters vacuum; incentive structure (consolidate vs expand into undefended space). | MEM–PERSIA–SASANIAN–DYNASTY “vacuum was filled by Islam”; Khosrow II MEM “exhausts both”; Al-Tabari “until the rise of Islam”; ENTRY 0005. |
| **H2: Religion + exhaustion** | Arab success requires both religious motivation/legitimacy and imperial exhaustion; neither alone sufficient. | MEM–PERSIA–ISLAM emphasizes absorption and Persianization, not Arab religious causation of victory; no MEM says “Arabs won because of faith.” Religion shapes post-conquest encoding, not discriminating cause of conquest. |
| **H3: Persian state failure alone** | Sasanian collapse due to internal failure (fiscal, institutional, legitimacy) independent of Roman–Persian war. | Khosrow MEM and Sasanian MEM tie collapse to war (Heraclius, defection, exhaustion); no MEM isolates internal failure as sufficient cause. |

**Discriminating evidence:** (1) Chronology—Arab expansion immediately after 628 peace and Sasanian non-recovery. (2) MEM–PERSIA–SASANIAN–DYNASTY liability: “vacuum was filled by Islam.” (3) No MEM attributes victory to religion or internal failure alone.

**ACH verdict:** H1 (exhaustion/vacuum) best fits MEM corpus; H2 and H3 not supported as primary. **ACH record: complete.**

A.5 OPERATIONAL MEANING (DRAFT)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If accepted as doctrine (DOCTRINE 02):

- **When assessing Persia (or peer-endurance states) vs a non-peer or post-exhaustion actor:** Apply conflict-type test: is the opponent the same conflict type (parity, denial, endurance) that existing doctrine is optimized for, or a new type (different velocity, legitimacy, or entry timing)? If conflict type has shifted, treat “denial/endurance optimised for peer conflict” as potentially insufficient; consider exhaustion/vacuum and third-actor entry as structural possibilities.
- **When interpreting “absorption” or “continuity” encoding:** State collapse and civilizational persistence can coexist; doctrine should not treat Persian (or similar) “we absorbed X; we endure” as contradicting structural “state collapsed; vacuum filled.”
- **Hard constraint (draft):** Doctrine is invalid or must be reviewed if: (1) evidence shows Arab (or analogous) conquest succeeded without prior exhaustion of both peer empires; (2) denial/endurance doctrine is shown to be effective against the same type of non-peer, post-vacuum actor across cases.
