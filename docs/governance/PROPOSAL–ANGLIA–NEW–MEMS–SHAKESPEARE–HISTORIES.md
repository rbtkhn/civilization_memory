# PROPOSAL — New Anglia MEMs Suggested by Shakespeare’s Histories

**Status:** PROPOSAL (not yet implemented)

**Governance:** CIV–MEM–CORE · CIV–INDEX–ANGLIA · CIV–MEM–TEMPLATE

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This document suggests **new MEMs to create** based on Shakespeare’s
history plays. Each proposed MEM fills an index gap (no current MEM for
that subject), carries a clear civilizational theme (legitimacy,
deposition, civil war, constraint), and is supported by at least one
Shakespeare history play with verbatim quote potential. Creation order
and index placement (PERSON vs WAR) follow CIV–INDEX–ANGLIA sections.

II. SUGGESTED NEW MEMs (BY INDEX SECTION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**A) WAR (Section C)**

| Proposed MEM | Index section | Play(s) | Civilizational theme | Shakespeare quote hooks |
|--------------|---------------|---------|----------------------|-------------------------|
| **MEM–ANGLIA–WAR–ROSES** | C) WAR | *Henry VI* 1–3, *Richard III* | Wars of the Roses (1455–1485) as structural civil war; legitimacy litigated by arms; Lancaster vs York. | “Now is the winter of our discontent…” (*R3* 1.1); “A horse, a horse! My kingdom for a horse!” (*R3* 5.4); rose / civil-war imagery (*H6*). |

**Rationale:** No current MEM treats the Wars of the Roses as a single conflict. MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–LANCASTER and MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–YORK cover the dynasties but not the war as event. WAR–ROSES would sit between WAR–HUNDRED–YEARS and Tudor consolidation, with Shakespeare’s First Tetralogy as literary evidence.

**Connections:** MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–LANCASTER, MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–YORK, MEM–ANGLIA–HENRY–V (legacy collapse), MEM–ANGLIA–RICHARD–III (if created), MEM–ANGLIA–DYNASTY–TUDOR.

**B) PERSON (Section G)**

| Proposed MEM | Index section | Play(s) | Civilizational theme | Shakespeare quote hooks |
|--------------|---------------|---------|----------------------|-------------------------|
| **MEM–ANGLIA–RICHARD–II** | G) PERSON | *Richard II* | Deposition (1399); transfer of legitimacy from divine-right king to parliamentary claimant; “death of kings.” | Gaunt: “This blessed plot, this earth, this realm, this England” (2.1); “That England… hath made a shameful conquest of itself” (2.1); Richard: “For God’s sake, let us sit upon the ground / And tell sad stories of the death of kings” (3.2). |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–RICHARD–III** | G) PERSON | *Richard III* | Terminal York; Bosworth (1485); Plantagenet end; Tudor legitimation through defeat of “villain.” | “Now is the winter of our discontent / Made glorious summer by this sun of York” (1.1); “A horse, a horse! My kingdom for a horse!” (5.4); “I am not in the giving vein to-day” (4.2). |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–KING–JOHN** | G) PERSON | *King John* | Angevin crisis; loss of Normandy; baronial coercion; precondition for Magna Carta (1215). | “Your strong possession much more than your right” (1.1); Bastard’s “commodity” speech (2.1); constraint vs royal will. |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–HENRY–IV** | G) PERSON | *Henry IV* 1 & 2 | Usurper-king; legitimacy deficit; rebellion (Hotspur); Hal’s self-fashioning and “redemption.” | “Uneasy lies the head that wears a crown” (*2H4* 3.1); “I know you all…” (*1H4* 1.2); “Redeem time” / honour and reformation. |
| **MEM–ANGLIA–HENRY–VI** | G) PERSON | *Henry VI* 1–3 | Weak king; loss of France; Wars of the Roses trigger; piety vs capacity. | “So many hours must I take my rest…” (*3H6*); civil war / rose imagery; Cade’s rebellion (*2H6*). |

**Rationale (concise):**
- **RICHARD–II:** Deposition is a recurring Anglian theme; no PERSON MEM for Richard II. Carries Gaunt’s “scepter’d isle” and “death of kings” as cognition glue.
- **RICHARD–III:** Last Plantagenet; Bosworth and Tudor myth. Shakespeare’s encoding (villainy, winter/summer) is central to Anglian memory.
- **KING–JOHN:** Pre-Magna Carta sovereign; MEM–ANGLIA–LAW–MAGNA–CARTA exists but not the king who was constrained. *King John* supports “possession vs right.”
- **HENRY–IV:** Bridge between Richard II and Henry V; usurper’s insecurity. Lancastrian legitimacy deficit is already in DYNASTY–LANCASTER; a PERSON MEM gives “uneasy lies the head” and Hal’s emergence a home.
- **HENRY–VI:** Collapse of Henry V’s legacy; weak kingship and civil war. No current PERSON MEM for Henry VI; Shakespeare’s three parts supply structure.

III. CREATION PRIORITY (RECOMMENDED ORDER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. **MEM–ANGLIA–WAR–ROSES** — Single WAR MEM for the conflict; high structural value; supports DYNASTY–LANCASTER, DYNASTY–YORK, and a future RICHARD–III.
2. **MEM–ANGLIA–RICHARD–II** — Deposition and legitimacy transfer; Gaunt’s speeches are already quoted in ENGLAND and DYNASTY–LANCASTER; a dedicated MEM consolidates the figure.
3. **MEM–ANGLIA–RICHARD–III** — End of Plantagenet line; Shakespeare’s most quoted history after *Henry V*; natural pair with WAR–ROSES.
4. **MEM–ANGLIA–KING–JOHN** — Anchors Magna Carta precondition; *King John* is the only play for that reign; short PERSON MEM.
5. **MEM–ANGLIA–HENRY–IV** — Henriad continuity (Richard II → Henry IV → Henry V); “uneasy lies the head” and Hal’s redemption.
6. **MEM–ANGLIA–HENRY–VI** — Completes First Tetralogy coverage; weak kingship and civil war trigger.
