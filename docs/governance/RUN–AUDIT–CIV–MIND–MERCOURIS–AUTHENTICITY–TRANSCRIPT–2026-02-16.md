## III. Cognitive Patterns in Transcript

- **Legitimacy / institutional continuity:** Present **through** material rather than as explicit label. Istanbul continuity, Medinsky as chief negotiator, "same team," full political agenda (territory, NATO, Orthodox Church, trusteeship) frame Russia as **arbiter of process and outcome**. Negotiation format, delegation composition, and "never cancelled" are **procedural and institutional**. Fits profile: legitimacy as primary explanatory variable operates via **structure of process and authority**, not via the word "legitimacy."
- **Attribution as primary move:** Program is **source-heavy**: Saldo, Marochko, Peskov, Poletaev, Business Insider, Guardian, Raionka, Larry Johnson, prior programs. Repeated **limitation to source** ("Saldo says," "this is what the Russians are saying," "as I discussed in my program yesterday"). Matches profile.
- **Precision / scope:** "Just saying," "perhaps at this point it's worth saying," "Now, just to quickly say on that point," "those things I'm going to discuss in more detail in future programs." Matches profile.
- **Non-closure:** "We will see how things turn out," "Who is to say? We'll see how it plays out," no prediction closure. Matches profile.
- **Hedged puzzlement / assessment:** "I have to say I find this absolutely zany to me"; "I find the explanations... completely incredible" not in this program, but **first-person** assessment ("I don't get any sign...", "to me that sounds...") is used. No "How could anybody...?" — correct per profile.

**Verdict:** In **program/current-events** mode, this transcript enacts **date/topic + program frame**, **attribution-first** structure, **epistemic hedging**, **scope limits**, **recursive returns**, and **non-closure**. Legitimacy and institutional continuity appear through **negotiation format, delegation continuity, and agenda scope**, not as an opening slogan. Profile and rule are **authenticated** for IMAGINE/current-events program style.
