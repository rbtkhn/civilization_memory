# Top 5 Russia MEMs for Full MEM-Template Compliance Upgrade

**Date:** 16 February 2026  
**Purpose:** Identify the five most important MEM–RUSSIA files that must be upgraded to full compliance with CIV–MEM–TEMPLATE v3.4, including the indigenous-language quote requirement (CIV–ARC–RUSSIA v3.5; template § VIII.D.1).  
**Scope:** Russia civilization only.

---

## Selection criteria

- **STATE/SCHOLAR centrality** — Primary MEM in MEM–RELEVANCE–RUSSIA for key dimensions (settlement, attrition/endurance, defection, partnership, leadership liability).
- **Doctrinal/RLL weight** — Referenced in CIV–DOCTRINE–RUSSIA or CIV–SCHOLAR–RUSSIA (RLL–0033, sacrifice-to-authority, endurance grammar, restraint).
- **Compliance gap** — Missing at least one verbatim quote (≥25 words) from an ARC–RUSSIA admissible indigenous-language source (Primary Chronicle, Novgorod First Chronicle, Laurentian Chronicle, Hypatian Chronicle; Karamzin, Solovyov, Klyuchevsky). May also need 20% verbatim quote standard, EQS, or Layer 2 fields.

---

## The 5 MEMs

### 1. MEM–RUSSIA–CONGRESS–VIENNA

**Why:** Primary MEM for **Settlement Precedent / Diplomatic Negotiation** (MEM–RELEVANCE § IV) and **Partnership / Alliance Credibility** (§ VI). Canonical “sacrifice-to-authority” conversion; Russia’s transition from existential survivor to system-arbiter. Tier-1 foundational for how Russia encodes war termination and diplomatic standing.

**Compliance gap:** No indigenous-language quote (no Primary Chronicle, Karamzin, Solovyov, Klyuchevsky). Currently relies on Ferguson (Category D); one short block quote in early sections — likely below 20% verbatim and no ARC–RUSSIA indigenous source.

**Upgrade:** Add ≥1 verbatim quote (≥25 words) from Karamzin or Solovyov on Vienna / post-1812 Russian position / Holy Alliance; or from Klyuchevsky on post-Napoleonic settlement. Verify 20% verbatim and subject-type declaration.

---

### 2. MEM–RUSSIA–WAR–NAPOLEON

**Why:** Primary MEM for **Attrition / Endurance** (§ I). Archetypal endurance-validation conflict; “Russia outlasted the system that produced Napoleon.” Core to RLL–0012 (depth/time), RLL–0014 (victory via enemy collapse), legitimacy-through-suffering.

**Compliance gap:** No indigenous-language quote. Uses Lieven, Tolstoy (not on ARC–RUSSIA indigenous list). Needs at least one quote from Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky on 1812 / Borodino / retreat / sacrifice.

**Upgrade:** Add ≥1 verbatim quote (≥25 words) from Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky on the 1812 campaign, Moscow, or the political/diplomatic aftermath. Verify 20% verbatim and adaptive requirements.

---

### 3. MEM–RUSSIA–ROMANOV–PETER–I

**Why:** Foundational for **coercive modernization** and DOCTRINE 07 (buffer-based shock absorption); primary for fiscal/structural and Baltic/outlet logic. Central to Rurikid→Petrine rupture and imperial grammar.

**Compliance gap:** No indigenous-language quote. Uses Massie, Lieven, Erickson (Category D); no Primary Chronicle or early modern Russian historiography (Karamzin, Solovyov, Klyuchevsky).

**Upgrade:** Add ≥1 verbatim quote (≥25 words) from Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky on Peter, the Baltic turn, or the institutional break. Verify 20% verbatim and Layer 2 (FIGURE-type) fields if applicable.

---

### 4. MEM–RUSSIA–KUTUZOV

**Why:** Primary MEM for **Attrition / Endurance** (§ I). “Endurance doctrine personified”; restraint as strategy; RLL–0033 (restraint articulated, sovereign adoption). Directly supports Kutuzov/Alexander 1812 and Vienna as restraint-adoption precedent.

**Compliance gap:** No indigenous-language quote. No Primary Chronicle, Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky.

**Upgrade:** Add ≥1 verbatim quote (≥25 words) from Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky on Kutuzov, the 1812 strategy, or the refusal of decisive battle. Verify 20% verbatim and FIGURE-type structured fields.

---

### 5. MEM–RUSSIA–ROMANOV–NICHOLAS–II

**Why:** Primary MEM for **Morale Collapse / Defection Cascade** (§ III) and **Leadership Liability / Personalist Constraint** (§ VII). Autocratic rigidity, elite defection, succession crisis; RLL–0033 (sovereign override / exhaustion). Core to 1917 and defection-incentive diagnostics.

**Compliance gap:** No indigenous-language quote. No Karamzin, Solovyov, or Klyuchevsky (period is late for them; Klyuchevsky wrote through late imperial — check for applicable passage). If no suitable Russian early-modern/modern historian on ARC list for Nicholas II, add one Klyuchevsky (or other admissible) quote on imperial decline, autocracy, or 1905/1917 context.

**Upgrade:** Add ≥1 verbatim quote (≥25 words) from an ARC–RUSSIA admissible indigenous-language source (Klyuchevsky is the natural fit for late imperial/autocracy). Verify 20% verbatim and FIGURE-type fields.
