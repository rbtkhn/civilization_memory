# Evaluation: CIV–ARC–CHINA — Adequacy of Chinese-Language Institutional Sources

**Date:** 2026-02-13  
**Scope:** ARC-T-INSTITUTIONAL (Section IX-B) and implied language of listed sources  
**Question:** Is ARC–CHINA adequate for Chinese-language institutional sources when STATE or MEM work requires institutional voice from China in Chinese?

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. SUMMARY VERDICT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Partially adequate with material gaps.** The ARC lists several PRC institutions that operate primarily in Chinese (MFA, PBC, NPC, Xinhua, CASS), but it **does not explicitly authorize or prioritise Chinese-language content**. It instead steers users toward **English** portals for key entries (State Council, Caixin) and lists several English-only or Western institutional sources. For analysis that requires **Chinese-language institutional sources** (official statements, party media, research in Chinese), the ARC is under-specified and tilts English.

III. GAPS FOR CHINESE-LANGUAGE INSTITUTIONAL SOURCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Official — language not specified**  
   State Council is the only official source with an explicit URL, and it is **english.gov.cn**. The ARC does not list the Chinese-language central government portal (e.g. www.gov.cn) or state that MFA, PBC, NPC, Xinhua **Chinese-language** content is authorised. So “Chinese-language institutional” for official is implicit at best.

2. **No party mouthpiece in Chinese**  
   **People’s Daily (人民日报, people.cn)** is not listed. It is the main CPC organ and the primary channel for party line and ideological framing in Chinese. Global Times (listed) is a subsidiary; People’s Daily is the core. For “institutional sources” in Chinese, this is a notable gap.

3. **Research — only one Chinese-dominant body**  
   CASS is the sole listed research source that is clearly Chinese-language-dominant. Missing options that often publish in Chinese and shape policy debate:  
   - China Institute of International Studies (CIIS)  
   - China Institute of Contemporary International Relations (CICIR)  
   - Shanghai Institutes for International Studies (SIIS)  
   - Peking University / Fudan / Renmin University policy-oriented institutes  

   So coverage of **Chinese-language** research is thin.

4. **News — English favoured**  
   Caixin is listed as **caixinglobal.com** (English). The main Caixin site (**caixin.com**) is Chinese and is not listed. So the best Chinese-language financial/investigative outlet is not explicitly authorised in its Chinese form. No other mainland Chinese-language news institution (e.g. Guangming Daily, CGTN Chinese) is listed.

5. **No “Chinese-language preferred” guidance**  
   The ARC does not state when or how to use Chinese-language vs English versions of the same institution. So STATE and MEM authors lack clear permission to cite “MFA (Chinese)” or “Xinhua (Chinese)” as the institutional source.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. WHAT WORKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

- **PRC official institutions are present** (State Council, MFA, PBC, NPC, Xinhua). With a small clarification, MFA, PBC, NPC, Xinhua can be treated as authorised in **both** Chinese and English where available.  
- **CASS** gives one clear Chinese-dominant research source.  
- **Global Times** and **Xinhua** are bilingual; Chinese content is available even if not explicitly named.  
- **Editorial notes** are clear (e.g. state wire, nationalist perspective), which helps when attributing and qualifying Chinese-language content once it is authorised.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RECOMMENDATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**A. Minimal (clarify without adding sources)**  
- In Section IX-B, add a short **language note**: “Where a listed PRC institution (MFA, PBC, NPC, Xinhua, CASS) publishes in both Chinese and English, both language versions are authorised. Prefer the language version that matches the primary document or statement being cited.”  
- For State Council, add the Chinese portal: **www.gov.cn** (or gov.cn) with note “Chinese-language; for English see english.gov.cn.”

**B. Add explicit Chinese-language institutional sources**  
- **People’s Daily (人民日报)** — people.cn. Authoritative for: Party line, ideological framing, major policy narratives. Editorial note: CPC central organ; primary channel for party messaging in Chinese.  
- **Caixin (Chinese)** — caixin.com. Authoritative for: Chinese-language financial and investigative reporting. Editorial note: Same as existing Caixin entry; explicitly authorise Chinese-language site.  
- **One additional research body** in Chinese: e.g. **CIIS (China Institute of International Studies)** — ciis.org.cn. Authoritative for: Foreign policy and strategic research in Chinese. Editorial note: MFA-linked; signals official foreign-policy research.

**C. Optional — subcategory or tag**  
- Add “Language: Chinese-primary” (or similar) to each source where the primary institutional output is in Chinese, so STATE and MEM authors can explicitly choose “Chinese-language institutional” when needed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONCLUSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARC–CHINA is **not yet adequate** for explicit, well-specified use of **Chinese-language** institutional sources. It is adequate for PRC institutional *presence* (official + CASS) and for English-language institutional coverage. To be adequate for Chinese-language institutional sources, the ARC should: (1) clarify that listed PRC institutions may be cited in Chinese where they publish in Chinese; (2) add State Council Chinese portal and at least one party media in Chinese (People’s Daily); (3) optionally add Caixin Chinese and one more Chinese-language research body (e.g. CIIS). Applying recommendations A and B would bring the ARC to adequate for Chinese-language institutional use.
