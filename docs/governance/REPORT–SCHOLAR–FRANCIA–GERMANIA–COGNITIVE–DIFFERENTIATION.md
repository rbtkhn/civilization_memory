## I. Executive Summary

The two Scholar files encode **distinct civilizational epistemes**: Francia’s Scholar is an **accumulative learning ledger** that records beliefs and syntheses without yet binding them as law; Germania’s Scholar is a **constraint grammar engine** that evaluates new learning against bound Recursive Learning Laws (RLLs) and Negative Capability Zones. The differentiation is “cognitive-spiritual” in the sense that each file embodies a different **relation to authority, continuity, and impossibility**—Francia through declaration, rupture-as-renewal, and contested unknowns; Germania through discipline, closure, trauma-encoding, and structural exclusions (“what Germania cannot stably do”).
