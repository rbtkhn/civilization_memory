# CONCEPT: Christianity, Islam, and Judaism as Three Strands of a Tri-Helix

**Status:** ACTIVE  
**Governed by:** CMC 3.5 · Extends double-helix metaphor to three traditions  
**Last updated:** March 2026

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This document defines **Christianity, Islam, and Judaism** as **three
strands of a tri-helix** winding around a **common axis** of shared
sacred geography, shared narrative, and contested legitimacy. It
specifies: what each strand contributes (grammar, not only history),
what the axis is, where the strands pair (structural interactions and
confluence nodes), and how the system may use the metaphor in LEARN or
synthesis.

The tri-helix supports: (a) consistent framing when analysing
Jerusalem, Abrahamic narrative, or moments (e.g. 70 CE, 1033, 1099)
that touch all three; (b) a shared vocabulary for "why these three"
as co-constitutive of the same sacred and political space; (c)
optional use when connector MEMs or same-subject encodings involve
the city or events that all three traditions encode.

**Difference from Rome–Islam double-helix:** The double-helix models
*two civilizations* (concluded or living) with CORE/STATE and paired
MEMs. The tri-helix models *three religious traditions* that may not
have dedicated CIV–CORE or STATE files; they appear across multiple
civilizations (Rome, Islam, France, Anglia, etc.). The axis is
**sacred and narrative** (Jerusalem, Abraham, scripture, custody) as
much as territorial. Connector MEMs in the corpus are often
civilization-tagged (MEM–ROME–*, MEM–ISLAM–*) and encode one or more
of the three traditions from that civilization's perspective.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. THE THREE STRANDS (GRAMMAR, NOT ONLY HISTORY)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Strand 1 — Judaism**  
*Tradition: covenant, law, land, exile, return.*

- **Legitimacy:** Covenant and law (Torah); authority from divine
  revelation and interpretive tradition (rabbinic, etc.); peoplehood
  and land (Eretz Israel) as constitutive.
- **Jerusalem:** Temple as axis (destroyed 70 CE); Western Wall;
  direction of prayer; "next year in Jerusalem." Loss of Temple
  = trauma and turn to text and diaspora; city remains the
  unreplaced center.
- **Temporal nodes:** 70 CE (destruction of Second Temple); 1033
  (presence in city under Fatimid rule, no round anniversary);
  1099 (Crusader conquest, Jewish and Muslim inhabitants killed).
- **Encoding in corpus:** Judaism appears in MEMs as Jewish presence,
  community, or theological referent (e.g. MEM–ROME–PALESTINE–
  JERUSALEM: Temple, governance; MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM:
  dhimmi, coexistence; Crusades MEMs: Rhineland massacres, Jerusalem).
  No dedicated CIV–JUDAISM in current scope; tradition is encoded
  through Roman, Islamic, and other civilization lenses.

**Strand 2 — Christianity**  
*Tradition: incarnation, passion, resurrection, church.*

- **Legitimacy:** Christ as fulfilment of covenant; authority from
  scripture, creed, and apostolic succession; universal mission.
- **Jerusalem:** Site of passion and resurrection; Holy Sepulchre;
  pilgrimage; crusade as armed pilgrimage. City is the place "where
  it happened"; custody and access are theological and political.
- **Temporal nodes:** c. 30/33 CE (crucifixion); 70 CE (read as
  judgment/fulfilment); 1033 (millennial anniversary of passion,
  pilgrimage surge); 1099 (Latin conquest of Jerusalem).
- **Encoding in corpus:** Christianity is diffused across Rome
  (Latin/Byzantine), France, Anglia, Germany (Crusades, clergy,
  piety). MEM–ROME–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM and MEM–ROME–CRUSADES encode
  Christian-Roman perspective; MEM–FRANCE–WAR–CRUSADES, MEM–GERMANY–
  WAR–FIRST–CRUSADE encode Latin-state perspectives.

**Strand 3 — Islam**  
*Tradition: tawhid, prophecy, Qur'an, ummah, sacred zones.*

- **Legitimacy:** Qur'an and Sunna; authority from revelation and
  caliphal or scholarly custodianship; Jerusalem as third holiest
  site (Isra, al-Aqsa).
- **Jerusalem:** Al-Quds; Night Journey (Qur'an 17:1); Dome of the
  Rock; custody = legitimacy asset. 1099 = ontological rupture;
  1187 = reanchoring (per MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM).
- **Temporal nodes:** 638 (conquest of Jerusalem); 1033 (Fatimid
  custody, Christian millennial pilgrimage accommodated); 1099
  (loss to Crusaders); 1187 (Saladin).
- **Encoding in corpus:** CIV–CORE–ISLAM; MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–
  JERUSALEM; MEM–ISLAM–CRUSADES; MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–FATIMID;
  MEM–ISLAM–SALADIN. Islam is a full civilization strand in the
  system with connector pairs (e.g. Jerusalem, Crusades) to Rome.

**Complementarity:** The three strands do not agree on the meaning of
the same place or event. They *coexist around* the same axis—same
city, same narrative referents (Abraham, sacrifice, Temple, passion),
same moments in time—with distinct grammars: covenant and land;
incarnation and church; revelation and ummah. No synthesis is
required; the tri-helix preserves tension.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. THE AXIS OF THE TRI-HELIX
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The three strands wind around a **common axis** with four elements:

1. **Shared sacred geography**  
   Jerusalem (and its precincts: Temple Mount, Holy Sepulchre,
   al-Aqsa) as the place all three encode as holy. Control and
   custody of this geography are contested; no tradition can
   monopolise the city's meaning for the others.

2. **Shared narrative referents**  
   Abraham/Ibrahim (patriarch); the binding of the son (Akedah /
   Isaac or Ishmael); Exodus; prophecy and revelation. Each strand
   interprets these differently; the *referent* is shared.

3. **Shared temporal nodes**  
   Certain dates touch all three: 70 CE (Temple destruction);
   1033 (Christian millennial, Fatimid custody, Jewish presence);
   1099 (Crusader conquest); 1187 (Saladin). These are not the
   same *event* in each tradition but the same *moment* in
   calendar time around which all three have a theology or memory.

4. **Contested legitimacy and custody**  
   The axis is the *question* of who may hold, govern, or
   interpret the city and the narrative—not the same answer, but
   the same question. Sovereignty, pilgrimage, and coexistence
   are the recurring structural forms.

**III-A. STRUCTURAL CONDITIONS (TRI-HELIX PERSISTENCE)**  
- **No single tradition controls the axis permanently.** When one
  tradition holds Jerusalem and excludes or subordinates the others,
  the tri-helix at that node becomes two strands (ruler and
  subject) or one; the *three*-strand structure is most visible
  when custody is shared, contested, or rotationally lost (e.g.
  Fatimid 1033: one sovereign, three traditions present; 1099:
  Latin Christian sovereign, Islam and Judaism wounded but not
  erased from the space).
- **Confluence moments.** Periods when all three are materially and
  symbolically present in the same place (e.g. 1033: pilgrimage,
  tolerance, millennial time) illustrate the tri-helix; they do not
  resolve it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BASE PAIRS (STRUCTURAL INTERACTIONS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Base pairs" are the points where the strands structurally interact.

**1. Custody and coexistence**  
One tradition holds sovereignty (e.g. Fatimid Islam 1033); the others
are present as communities or pilgrims. Tolerance, dhimmi status,
and pilgrimage access are the mechanisms. MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–FATIMID:
pragmatic pluralism; MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM: legitimacy anchor.

**2. Pilgrimage**  
All three traditions practise pilgrimage to Jerusalem (or its
memory). Pilgrimage creates temporal spikes (e.g. 1033 for
Christians) and physical coexistence; it does not require shared
theology.

**3. Violence and rupture**  
Conquest and massacre (1099; Rhineland 1096) wound one or more
strands. MEM–ISLAM–CRUSADES, MEM–ROME–CRUSADES, MEM–GERMANY–
WAR–FIRST–CRUSADE encode these from respective perspectives. The
tri-helix does not smooth over rupture; it notes that the same
*event* is encoded differently (e.g. 1099 as Latin triumph,
Islamic trauma, Jewish trauma).

**4. Transmission and shared narrative**  
Scripture and exegesis flow across traditions (e.g. Hebrew
scripture → Christian Old Testament; shared Abraham, shared
prophets). Transmission is a base pair: one strand preserves or
interprets what another also claims. No single tradition "owns"
Abraham or Jerusalem in the helix.

**5. Anniversaries and calendar time**  
Round dates (70 + 1000 = 1070 for Judaism; 33 + 1000 = 1033 for
Christianity) create moments when one strand's temporal focus
intensifies in the same space where the others are present. 1033
is the clearest confluence year in the document set: Christian
millennial, Islamic custody, Jewish presence.

V. CONNECTOR NODES AND CORPUS REFERENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The **main connector node** in the corpus is **Jerusalem**.

| Subject    | Rome (MEM–ROME–*)           | Islam (MEM–ISLAM–*)            | Judaism (corpus note)                          |
|------------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Jerusalem  | MEM–ROME–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM| MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM   | Encoded implicitly in both (Temple, 70 CE; community, dhimmi). |
| Crusades   | MEM–ROME–CRUSADES           | MEM–ISLAM–CRUSADES              | Jewish presence and Rhineland massacres in MEM–GERMANY–WAR–FIRST–CRUSADE. |
| Fatimid    | —                           | MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–FATIMID       | Fatimid rule = context for 1033 Jewish and Christian presence. |

**Use:** When analysing Jerusalem, 1033, Crusades, or any node that
touches all three traditions, load the **relevant MEMs** (Rome and
Islam connector pair for Jerusalem/Crusades; Fatimid for 1033
custody) and state explicitly that the tri-helix applies: three
traditions, one axis, distinct encodings. Judaism is invoked where
MEM content or historical record includes Jewish presence, Temple,
or trauma (70 CE, 1099, etc.); no separate MEM–JUDAISM–* required for
the concept to hold.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SCOPE AND LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**Scope**  
- The tri-helix is **contemplative and analytical**. It does not
  require every session to adopt it; it is available when
  Jerusalem, Abrahamic narrative, or cross-tradition confluence is
  in play.
- The system does not currently model Judaism as a civilization
  with CIV–CORE–JUDAISM or STATE–JUDAISM. The tri-helix treats
  Judaism as a **tradition** whose grammar and presence are
  encoded through Roman, Islamic, and other MEMs where relevant.
- Christianity is diffused across Rome, France, Anglia, Germany;
  Islam is a full civilization strand. The tri-helix is
  asymmetric in corpus coverage and remains valid as a conceptual
  frame.

**Limits**  
- **Helix** implies winding and interaction, not identity. The three
  strands remain distinct; the metaphor does not assert a single
  "Abrahamic religion" or synthesis.
- **Time asymmetry:** Key dates (70 CE, 638, 1033, 1099, 1187) are
  not symmetric in meaning for each strand. The axis is the
  *coincidence* of the same moment or place, not shared
  interpretation.
- **Other traditions:** Zoroastrian, Samaritan, or other traditions
  that touch the same geography are not excluded; the document
  focuses on Christianity, Islam, and Judaism as the three
  principal strands in scope for this concept.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RELATION TO EXISTING GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- **CONCEPT–ROME–ISLAM–DOUBLE–HELIX:** The Rome–Islam double-helix
  describes two *civilizational* strands and connector MEM pairs.
  The tri-helix adds a *religious-tradition* layer: the same space
  (Jerusalem, Mediterranean, Levant) can be read as two strands
  (Rome, Islam) or as three traditions (Christianity, Islam,
  Judaism) winding around sacred geography and narrative. The two
  concepts can be used together: e.g. "At Jerusalem, the Rome–Islam
  connector pair (MEM–ROME–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM, MEM–ISLAM–
  PALESTINE–JERUSALEM) encodes the city; the tri-helix adds that
  Judaism is the third tradition at the same axis."
- **SCHOLAR connector-perspective rule (cmc-scholar-connector-
  perspective.mdc):** When syntheses concern the same subject across
  civilizations, foreground each civilization's encoding. For the
  tri-helix, when writing about Jerusalem or 1033 in a civilization's
  SCHOLAR file, foreground that civilization's encoding and name
  the other two traditions as complementary strands at the same
  node; do not collapse into a single neutral formulation.
- **MEMs:** MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM, MEM–ROME–PALESTINE–
  JERUSALEM, MEM–ISLAM–CRUSADES, MEM–ROME–CRUSADES, MEM–ISLAM–
  DYNASTY–FATIMID (and related Crusade MEMs) ground the tri-helix
  in the corpus.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. ONE-LINE FORMULATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Christianity, Islam, and Judaism function as three strands of a
tri-helix around a common axis (Jerusalem, shared narrative,
contested custody, and shared temporal nodes): distinct legitimacy
and narrative grammars winding around the same sacred geography and
calendar moments, whose structural interactions—custody and
coexistence, pilgrimage, rupture, transmission, and anniversaries—
constitute the recurring confluence and conflict at the center of
the Abrahamic space.**

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONFLUENCE NODES (EXAMPLE: 1033)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When analysing a **confluence node** (a moment when all three
traditions are materially or symbolically present at the same axis):

1. **Identify the sovereign** (e.g. Fatimid 1033).
2. **State each strand's relation to the node:** Christianity =
   millennial anniversary (1033); Islam = custody and tolerance;
   Judaism = presence, no round anniversary.
3. **Cite MEMs** that ground the node (e.g. MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–
   FATIMID for governance; MEM–ISLAM–PALESTINE–JERUSALEM for
   Islamic encoding of the city).
4. **Preserve tension:** Do not synthesise the three into one
   story; the tri-helix holds precisely because encodings differ.
