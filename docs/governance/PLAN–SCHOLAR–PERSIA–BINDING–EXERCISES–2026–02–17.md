────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EXERCISE DESIGN PRINCIPLES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. **Failure-first:** Each exercise seeks counter-evidence and boundary cases before confirming.
2. **Template gate:** Before any DIB recommendation, Assumptions Box (≥3, linchpin), confidence tier, and ACH (if alternatives evaluated) must be test-complete.
3. **No mandatory binding:** Exercises inform “can/should”; user decides DIB.
4. **Doctrine consistency:** Any new doctrine must not contradict DOCTRINE 01 (four-zone, Levant parity, overextension liability).
5. **MEM-grounded:** Exercises use LEARN mode and named MEMs; STATE may be used where current-events stress-test is relevant.

III. EXERCISE SERIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

**EXERCISE A — SYNTHESIS 0004 (Persia–Islam structural)**

*Goal:* Test whether 0004 is binding-ready and operationally distinct from DOCTRINE 01.

| Step | Activity | Success criterion |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| A.1 | **Counter-evidence scan** — LEARN: Search MEM–PERSIA (Islam, Sasanian, Arab, conquest) for evidence that Arab success was *not* primarily exhaustion/vacuum (e.g. religious motivation, Persian institutional failure alone, timeline mismatch). | At least one MEM or ENTRY consulted; counter-evidence either documented or ruled out. |
| A.2 | **Alternative hypotheses (ACH)** — Formulate 2–3 competing hypotheses (e.g. “Exhaustion only”; “Religion + exhaustion”; “Persian state failure independent of Rome”). Run ACH: which evidence discriminates? | ACH record drafted; discriminating evidence listed. |
| A.3 | **Assumptions Box** — Draft ≥3 assumptions with linchpin status (e.g. “Power distribution and exhaustion explain Arab success”; “Doctrine optimized for peer conflict fails when conflict type shifts”). IF_WRONG for each. | Assumptions Box complete; at least one High linchpin. |
| A.4 | **Overlap check with DOCTRINE 01** — Confirm 0004 does not duplicate 01. 01 = geography/parity/overextension liability; 0004 = structural transition / conflict-type shift. | Clear distinction; no contradiction. |
| A.5 | **Operational meaning** — If accepted as doctrine, what would “doctrine failure when conflict type shifts” constrain? (e.g. STATE: “When assessing Iran vs non-peer actor, apply conflict-type test.”) | One-paragraph operational meaning drafted. |

*Output:* Recommendation: BIND as DOCTRINE 02 (if A.1–A.5 pass) or RETAIN UNBINDING (with reasons).

---

**EXERCISE B — SYNTHESIS 0003 (Khosrow II tri-frame)**

*Goal:* Test whether 0003 adds operational value beyond DOCTRINE 01 or should remain tension-only.

| Step | Activity | Success criterion |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| B.1 | **Redundancy check** — DOCTRINE 01 already encodes Khosrow II as confirming case (overextension → defection → exhaustion). Is 0003’s *tri-frame tension* (Keegan/Mearsheimer/Barnes/Mercouris) adding a *constraint* or only *pedagogy*? | Decision: “constraint” (then binding candidate) or “pedagogy” (then keep UNBINDING). |
| B.2 | **Constraint formulation** — If constraint: e.g. “When analyzing Persian overextension, all four frames (liability, structure, legitimacy, belief/mask) must be considered; no single-frame closure.” Draft as potential doctrine sentence. | One-sentence constraint candidate or “no constraint, tension-only”. |
| B.3 | **Counter-case** — LEARN: Is there a Persian overextension case where *one* frame clearly fails (e.g. liability absent, or structure sufficient alone)? MEM–PERSIA–WAR–ROME, other overextension MEMs. | At least one counter-case considered; impact on binding assessed. |

*Output:* Recommendation: BIND as DOCTRINE 02/03 (only if B.1–B.2 yield a clear constraint) or RETAIN UNBINDING (tension preserved, no new constraint).

---

**EXERCISE C — SYNTHESIS 0001 (Carrhae tri-frame)**

*Goal:* Test whether 0001 can/should be operationalized as doctrine or must remain tension-only.

| Step | Activity | Success criterion |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| C.1 | **Operationalizability** — 0001 states “synthesis forbidden—tension preserved.” Does a *doctrine* that says “Carrhae-type events are multi-frame; no single-frame closure” add CORE/STATE utility? Or is it purely descriptive (Carrhae encoding divergence)? | Decision: operational constraint vs descriptive only. |
| C.2 | **MEM consistency** — LEARN: Re-read MEM–PERSIA–WAR–CARRHAE. Does the MEM itself support “four frames, tension preserved” as a *repeatable* pattern (other battles same treatment)? Or is Carrhae unique? | Repeatability vs uniqueness assessed. |
| C.3 | **Conflict with non-synthesis rule** — Template forbids closure-seeking synthesis. A doctrine that says “preserve tension” is meta; confirm it does not inadvertently freeze a single reading of Carrhae. | No single-frame freeze; meta-rule only. |

*Output:* Recommendation: BIND only if a clear meta-constraint emerges (e.g. “Roman–Persian founding battles: multi-frame required”); otherwise RETAIN UNBINDING.

---

**EXERCISE D — RLL candidate (already in DOCTRINE 01)**

*Goal:* Decide whether to formalize the RLL sentence as RLL–PERSIA–0001 when/if Phase II is activated.

| Step | Activity | Success criterion |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| D.1 | **Phase II gate** — RLL binding applies only in Phase II. No exercise required until Phase II activation is considered. | Document: “RLL formalization deferred until Phase II.” |
| D.2 | **Redundancy** — The RLL text is already the core of DOCTRINE 01. Formal RLL–PERSIA–0001 would duplicate doctrine content unless RLL is scoped differently (e.g. “RLL applies to LEARN/SCHOLAR; doctrine applies to CORE/STATE”). | Decision: duplicate vs distinct scope. |

*Output:* Recommendation: If Phase II activated, either (a) bind RLL–PERSIA–0001 with same content as DOCTRINE 01 and document scope, or (b) leave doctrine-only and do not create RLL.
