I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Serbia** as Islam's **Balkan conquest and integration**: the medieval Serbian kingdom defeated at Kosovo (1389), absorbed into the Ottoman empire for centuries, and later lost to nationalist independence (19th c.). For Islam/Ottoman memory, Serbia is **core Balkan territory**—Orthodox millet under Ottoman rule; then **first-mover in Balkan nationalist exit** (Serbian uprisings, autonomy, full independence). Encoding: conquest, custodianship (millet, toleration), then **fragmentation**—Serbia as exemplar of Ottoman Balkan loss.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST — KOSOVO AND INTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1389: Battle of Kosovo—Ottoman victory over Serbian and allied forces; Serbian polity becomes Ottoman vassal, then integrated. Serbia incorporated into Rumelia; Orthodox population under millet system. Belgrade: key fortress; Danube frontier. Pattern: **Ottoman Balkans**—Serbia as part of "Europe's heart" under Ottoman rule (Baer). Serbian Orthodox Church preserved; elites co-opted or replaced; territory administered from Istanbul.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LOSS — NATIONALIST FRAGMENTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
19th c.: Serbian uprisings (1804–1815); autonomy then de facto independence. 1878: Congress of Berlin—Serbia recognized as independent. Serbia encodes **first major Balkan territorial loss**—nationalist mobilization, exclusivity (Baer), and exit from Ottoman order. Pattern: **fragmentation before empire's demise**—Serbia precedes broader Ottoman exit from Balkans (Balkan Wars, WWI).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC LOGIC (MEARSHEIMER LAYER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Serbia = Danube–Morava corridor; Belgrade as fortress and later nationalist capital. Loss of Serbia = loss of northwestern Balkan anchor; Habsburg and Russian influence fill the gap. Kosovo: symbolic site (1389) for both Serbian nationalist and Ottoman memory—victory then incorporation; for Serbs, defeat then martyrdom. Power distribution: Ottoman retreat; Serbian (and other Balkan) state formation.
