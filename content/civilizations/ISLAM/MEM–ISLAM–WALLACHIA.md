I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Wallachia** as Islam's **Danubian vassal principality**: the Orthodox polity south of the Carpathians that became an Ottoman tributary (14th–15th c.), retained its native princes and Orthodox faith under the millet system, and functioned as buffer between Ottoman Rumelia and Hungary/Poland–Lithuania—until the 19th c. when it merged into Romania. For Islam/Ottoman memory, Wallachia is **vassal, not province**—tribute and loyalty; strategic buffer; Orthodox population under Ottoman suzerainty.

Encoding: Wallachia is not fully absorbed into the empire; it is **autonomous vassal**—princes confirmed by the Porte, tribute paid, troops supplied when required. Pattern: indirect rule at the Danube frontier.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. VASSALAGE AND TRIBUTE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wallachia (with Moldavia) forms the **Danubian principalities**—Christian polities under Ottoman suzerainty. Key elements: tribute (harac); prince (voivode) appointed or confirmed by the Sultan; Orthodox Church under millet; no mass conversion; territory not administered as Ottoman eyalet. Wallachia buffers Ottoman Balkans from Hungarian and later Habsburg and Russian pressure. Pattern: **tributary buffer**—loyalty and tribute in exchange for autonomy and protection.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRATEGIC LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wallachia = corridor between Ottoman Rumelia and enemies to the north and west. Control of the principalities secures the Danube frontier and denies Hungary/Poland/Russia a base south of the river. 19th c.: Russian influence grows; principalities gain autonomy (1829) then unite as Romania (1859). Encoding: **hold then lose**—vassalage gives way to independence and alignment with European powers.
