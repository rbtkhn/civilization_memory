I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Libya as **Islam's western Maghreb edge**: the
territory whose conquest (mid-7th c.) extended dar al-Islam along the
North African coast and whose control—Tripolitania (Tarabulus) and
Cyrenaica (Barqa)—replicated Roman-style **coastal governance** with
Saharan interior left to tribal and caravan logic.

For Islam, Libya is not a core heartland. It is **littoral and corridor**:
the coast linking Egypt to Ifriqiya (Tunisia) and the Maghreb; the
desert edge where trans-Saharan routes meet the Mediterranean. No
single Islamic state held it permanently; Umayyad, Fatimid, Ottoman,
and later national control follow the same pattern—coast and nodes,
not interior saturation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC PARAMETERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Libya: narrow Mediterranean littoral (Tripolitania west, Cyrenaica east);
vast Saharan interior. Key nodes: Tripoli (Tarabulus), Benghazi (Barqa),
oases (Ghadames, Kufra). Structural axiom: same as Roman—**coastal strip
governable; desert is barrier and corridor**. Islam inherits the
terrain; governance by point control and trade regulation persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CONQUEST & SUCCESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
642–645: Arab conquest of Byzantine Tripolitania and Cyrenaica. Part of
the Maghreb expansion; same campaign arc as Egypt. Umayyad then Abbasid
administration; coastal cities and tribute.

Fatimid period (10th–11th c.): Libya under Fatimid sway; corridor
between Egypt and Ifriqiya. No deep territorial integration.

Ottoman (16th c.–1912): Tripolitania and Cyrenaica as regencies
(Trablusgarp). Coastal control; interior tribal; same negative-space
logic as Rome. Barbary corsair base (Tripoli) until 19th c.

1912–1951: Italian colonization; then independence. Post-independence:
republic; no caliphal or sultanic center—Libya remains **periphery** in
Islamic civilizational geography.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Libya functions as **corridor** between Egypt and the Maghreb, and as
**Saharan edge**: trans-Saharan trade (gold, salt) touches the coast
here. No Nile; no single chokepoint like Egypt. Value is **transit and
littoral denial**—whoever holds the coast can regulate or block
east–west movement along the Mediterranean and desert–sea exchange.
Islam holds it as part of the Maghreb–Mashriq continuum; never as
heartland.
