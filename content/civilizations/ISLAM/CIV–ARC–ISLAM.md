I. PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ARC governs author admissibility and temporal precedence for
MEM–ISLAM files only. Quotation quantity and quality are
governed by CIV–MEM–TEMPLATE.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ARC-TEMPORAL CATEGORIES (ARC-T)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ARC-T-ANCIENT — Foundational period; direct witnesses (pre-500 AD / pre-Islam; Qur'an, early tradition).
ARC-T-MEDIEVAL — Medieval transmission (500–1500 AD).
ARC-T-EARLY-MOD — c. 1500–1900; typically out of copyright.
ARC-T-MODERN — c. 1900–present; current scholarship.

Precedence: ANCIENT > MEDIEVAL > EARLY-MOD > MODERN.
Later periods may clarify; they may not override earlier testimony.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. AUTHOR ADMISSIBILITY LISTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ARC-T-ANCIENT:
• Qur'an — revelation, 7th century (verbatim; handle sacred text per CIV–MEM–CORE)
• Ibn Ishaq (d. 768) — Sira Rasul Allah (Life of the Prophet)
• Al-Bukhari (810–870) — Sahih al-Bukhari
• Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (815–875) — Sahih Muslim

ARC-T-MEDIEVAL:
• Al-Tabari (839–923) — Tarikh al-Rusul wa al-Muluk (History of the Prophets and Kings)
• Ibn al-Athir (1160–1233) — Al-Kamil fi al-Tarikh (The Complete History)
• Baha ad-Din ibn Shaddad (1145–1234) — Al-Nawadir al-Sultaniyya (Life of Saladin)
• Ibn Khaldun (1332–1406) — Muqaddimah; Kitab al-Ibar
• Ibn Battuta (1304–1368/69) — Rihla (Travels)

ARC-T-EARLY-MOD:
• Edward Gibbon (1737–1794) — The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire (Chs. 50–52 on Islam)
• Thomas Arnold (1823–1900) — The Preaching of Islam
• Stanley Lane-Poole (1854–1931) — Saladin and the Fall of the Kingdom of Jerusalem; The Mohammedan Dynasties

ARC-T-MODERN:
• Marshall G.S. Hodgson (1922–1968) — The Venture of Islam (3 vols.)
• Bernard Lewis (1916–2018) — The Arabs in History; The Political Language of Islam; What Went Wrong?
• Patricia Crone (1945–2015) — Slaves on Horses; Meccan Trade and the Rise of Islam; God's Caliph
• Hugh Kennedy (b. 1947) — The Prophet and the Age of the Caliphates; The Armies of the Caliphs; The Great Arab Conquests
• Fred M. Donner (b. 1945) — Muhammad and the Believers; Narratives of Islamic Origins
• Jonathan A.C. Brown (b. 1977) — Hadith: Muhammad's Legacy in the Medieval and Modern World; Misquoting Muhammad
• Carole Hillenbrand (b. 1943) — The Crusades: Islamic Perspectives
• Ira M. Lapidus (b. 1937) — A History of Islamic Societies

Only listed authors may be quoted without ARC–DISCOVERY flag.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DISCOVERY FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When a MEM requires sources beyond the list above:

DISCOVERY FLAG (REQUIRED):
"ARC–DISCOVERY: EXPANDED SEARCH
Source: [Author/Work]
Justification: [Why this source is necessary]"

Expanded discovery does not add authors to this canon without an ARC version upgrade.
