I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the abolition of the caliphate (1924) as Islam's
**legitimacy rupture**: the moment when universal Sunni custodianship
ceases to exist as an institution, fragmenting political imagination
and leaving sacred guardianship stateless.

CIV–CORE–ISLAM § I: "1917–1924: sacred-zone shock + imperial collapse
→ legitimacy re-binding attempts (caliphate abolition)."

For Islam, 1924 is not merely the end of a dynasty.
It is the loss of the last universal custodian.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT: LATE-OTTOMAN COLLAPSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The abolition follows empire's demise.

Key dynamics:
• WWI defeat; partition of Ottoman lands
• Turkish War of Independence; Kemalist secularization
• Atatürk's abolition (3 March 1924) as break with imperial past
• Caliphate transferred to none; office extinguished

The caliphate ends by decree,
not by conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGITIMACY VACUUM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Consequences:
• Sudden absence of universal Sunni authority
• Fragmentation of political imagination
• No successor recognized
• Sacred custodianship (Mecca, Medina, Jerusalem) becomes stateless

Islam enters modernity without a universal custodian.

Rebinding attempts (e.g., Sharif of Mecca, Indian Khilafat) fail.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL EFFECTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring meanings:
• Legitimacy inheritance unresolved
• Polycentric structure (no single arbiter) reinforced
• Modern Islamism partly a response to custodianship vacancy
• State and Ummah decoupled at institutional level

The fall is ontological,
not only political.
