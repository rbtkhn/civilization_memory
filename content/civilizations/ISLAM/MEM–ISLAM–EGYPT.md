I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Egypt as **Islam's caliphal heartland after Baghdad**:
the territory whose conquest (641) completed the early Islamic absorption
of Byzantine provinces, and whose control—via the Nile chokepoint and
Cairo—sustained Fatimid, Ayyubid, and Mamluk legitimacy and sacred-zone
defense (Mecca, Medina, Jerusalem).

For Islam, Egypt is not merely a province. It is **the Nile–Levant axis**:
the corridor between Africa and the Levant, the grain and transit base
that replaced Mesopotamia as heartland after 1258.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC PARAMETERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Egypt: Nile valley and delta; desert on three sides; Mediterranean (north);
Red Sea (east); single river sustains population and agriculture. Structural
axiom: Control Nile = control Egypt. Cairo (969) on the Nile becomes
caliphal capital. Suez and Red Sea link to Indian Ocean and Hijaz.
Mediterranean links to Levant and North Africa.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CONQUEST & CALIPHAL SUCCESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
641: Arab conquest of Byzantine Egypt. Umayyad then Abbasid administration.
969: Fatimids found Cairo—Shiʿa caliphate rival to Baghdad. Lewis (ARC):
"The Fatimid caliphs... came to Egypt via North Africa." Al-Azhar; parallel
caliphal center.

1171: Saladin ends Fatimid caliphate; Ayyubid Sunni restoration.
1250: Mamluk succession. 1258: Baghdad falls; Mamluks restore Abbasid
caliphate in Cairo (symbolic). Cairo–Damascus axis = sacred-zone defense
core. Egypt and Levant replace Mesopotamia as Islamic heartland.

1517: Ottoman conquest of Mamluk sultanate; caliphate transfers to Istanbul.
Egypt becomes Ottoman province. No destruction of Cairo; institutional
transfer.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Egypt functions as **Nile chokepoint** and **corridor hinge**. Control Egypt
= control grain, population, and transit between Mediterranean and Red Sea.
CIV–CORE–ISLAM § IV: Nile basin. Post-1258: Cairo as caliphal seat and
sacred-zone defender (Mecca, Medina, Jerusalem). Urban import physics:
Egyptian cities depend on Nile water. No alternative at scale.
