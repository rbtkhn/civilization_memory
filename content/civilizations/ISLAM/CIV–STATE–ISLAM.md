I. ENTITY IDENTIFICATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name: Islamic caliphal polity (universal Sunni custodianship; caliphate as
institution)
Classification: FRAGMENTED-SOURCE
Active: NO

**Temporal bounds:** 622 (Hijra; Medina — first Muslim polity under the
Prophet) – 1924 (abolition of Ottoman Caliphate, 3 March 1924). Used for
timeline traversal and simulation.

Conclusion: 1924 (abolition by decree; Kemalist Turkey extinguished the
office). No successor recognized. Sacred custodianship (Mecca, Medina,
Jerusalem) became stateless at the institutional level. Islam enters
modernity without a universal custodian (MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION;
CIV–CORE–ISLAM § I: 1917–1924 legitimacy re-binding attempts).

Phase history (summary):
• Medina (622–632): Prophetic polity; authority by revelation and proximity.
• Rashidun (632–661): Transitional caliphate; legitimacy by companionship;
  unreproducible (MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–RASHIDUN).
• Umayyad (661–750): Damascus; first imperial caliphal capital; overthrow
  by Abbasid revolution (SYNTHESIS 0006).
• Abbasid (762–1258): Baghdad; civilizational consolidation; fall by
  physical destruction (Mongol sack 1258); caliphate relocates to Cairo.
• Cairo axis (969–1517): Fatimid rival caliphate; Mamluk symbolic Abbasid
  restoration (1261); fall by institutional transfer to Ottoman.
• Ottoman (1517–1924): Istanbul; custodial capital; triple heritage
  (Khans/Caesars/Caliphs); fall by abolition — terminal; no successor
  (MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–OTTOMAN; MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION).

The **caliphal state** as single universal custodian concluded in 1924. The
**civilizational grammar** (Ummah identity, sacred zones, moral custodianship,
polycentric legitimacy) persists in CIV–CORE–ISLAM — a living civilization
without a single caliphal arbiter. This STATE file describes the concluded
caliphal entity and post-1924 vacancy; it does not describe the many living
polities that operate within the Islamic grammar (those have or may have
their own STATE files: e.g. Persia, Turkey, Saudi Arabia).

II. SUCCESSION AND INHERITANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INTERNAL SUCCESSION (within caliphal forms):
Medina → Rashidun → Umayyad (Damascus) → Abbasid (Baghdad) → post-1258
symbolic/relocated (Cairo) → Ottoman (Istanbul 1517–1924). Type: mix of
DIRECT_CONTINUATION, CONQUEST_ABSORPTION, and INSTITUTIONAL_TRANSFER across
capital moves (SYNTHESIS 0006: Damascus · Baghdad · Cairo · Istanbul).

TERMINAL MODE: Abolition (1924).
Caliphate ends by decree, not by conquest. No Islamic successor state
receives the office. Rebinding attempts (e.g. Sharif of Mecca, Indian
Khilafat movement) fail. Vacancy is structural and unresolved
(MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION).

FRAGMENTATION (post-1924):
Universal Sunni authority cannot be filled by any single state. Multiple
nodes hold fragments (custodianship of holy places, moral authority,
Islamist claims to restore caliphate). No claimant is recognized as
successor. Polycentric structure (CIV–CORE–ISLAM) is the enduring reality.

FRAGMENT / CUSTODIAN NODES (typed; no succession claim to caliphate):
• **Saudi Arabia (Hijaz)** — Custody of Mecca and Medina; no caliphal title.
  Type: INSTITUTIONAL_TRANSFER (custodianship only); not ideological claim
  to caliphate.
• **Turkey** — Seat of abolished caliphate; secular republic; declined to
  retain office. Type: CONQUEST_ABSORPTION + DECLINATION (parallel to
  Ottoman declination of Roman title in STATE–ROME).
• **Islamist movements** — Various claims to restore or embody caliphal
  legitimacy; no state recognition. Type: IDEOLOGICAL_CLAIM; fragment
  claimants without institutional transfer.

INHERITED PATTERNS (for reference by other STATE files and MEMs):
• Vacancy by abolition: authority extinguished by holder/successor state
  without transfer. Contrast vacancy by declination (STATE–ROME: possessor
  declines to claim).
• Caliphal capital succession: four terminal modes — overthrow, destruction,
  transfer, abolition (SYNTHESIS 0006). Istanbul = abolition; no relocation.
• Polycentric legitimacy: no single arbiter post-1924; legitimacy fragments
  across nodes (CIV–CORE–ISLAM).

III. STRATEGIC POSITION (THREE PERSPECTIVES)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
*Applied to the conclusion and fragmentation of the caliphal polity, not
to live options.*

A) LEGITIMACY AND INSTITUTIONAL CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────
The caliphate ended by institutional elimination, not conquest. Legitimacy
did not migrate to a successor; it was declared vacant. Rebinding attempts
failed. Islam's civilizational grammar (sacred zones, moral custodianship,
Ummah identity) persists in CIV–CORE–ISLAM, but polycentric structure means
no single state arbitrates Ummah legitimacy. The conclusion revealed
irreversibility: modernity without a universal custodian.

B) POWER DISTRIBUTION AND STRUCTURAL CONSTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────
Structurally, no state could fill the vacancy without triggering
counter-coalition or civilizational rejection. Kemalist Turkey had the
seat but chose sovereign consolidation over caliphal continuity. Saudi
custodianship of holy places does not imply caliphal claim. The force
ratio of 1924 (WWI defeat, partition, nationalist consolidation) made
abolition the low-cost option for the successor state; the cost to
Islamic political imagination was externalized.

C) LEADERSHIP LIABILITY AND MECHANISM
────────────────────────────────────────────
Who ended the office, and by what mechanism? The Turkish Grand National
Assembly (Kemalist) abolished the caliphate by decree. Liability: the
successor state eliminated the institution to secure sovereign identity.
Defection incentive: Turkey exited the caliphal frame first. No other
state was given the option to inherit; the office was extinguished.
When analysing living states (e.g. Turkey, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Islamist
movements), liability exposure and rebinding attempts can be assessed
against this pattern.

Tensions preserved: Legitimacy vacuum vs civilizational continuity;
polycentric reality vs nostalgia for single custodian; abolition as
sovereignty project vs enduring demand for rebinding.

VII. DECISION-RELEVANT HISTORY (PATTERNS — REFERENCE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Patterns below are REFERENCE. They support analysis of living states that
declare influence from this file. Activation: REFERENCE.

Pattern 1 — Vacancy by abolition (1924)
• Universal custodianship ends when the office is extinguished by decree
  without transfer. No successor; rebinding attempts fail. Contrast
  vacancy by declination (STATE–ROME: possessor declines to claim).
• Source: MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION; CIV–CORE–ISLAM § I.
Activation: REFERENCE. Use when assessing post-1924 legitimacy claims,
custodianship without caliphal title, or Islamist rebinding rhetoric.

Pattern 2 — Caliphal capital succession (Damascus · Baghdad · Cairo · Istanbul)
• Four capitals; four terminal modes: overthrow (Damascus), destruction
  (Baghdad), transfer (Cairo → Istanbul), abolition (Istanbul). Structural
  logic: overthrow and transfer preserve institution (relocation);
  destruction and abolition do not.
• Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–ISLAM SYNTHESIS 0006; MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–*;
  MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION.
Activation: REFERENCE. Use when comparing how caliphal authority moved
or terminated across epochs.

Pattern 3 — Polycentric legitimacy (post-1924)
• No single state arbitrates Ummah legitimacy. Legitimacy flows through
  multiple nodes (CIV–CORE–ISLAM). Sacred zones remain; custodianship
  is contested or shared; state and Ummah decoupled at institutional level.
• Source: CIV–CORE–ISLAM; MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION § IV.
Activation: REFERENCE. Use when assessing legitimacy claims of any
Islamicate or Islam-adjacent state.

Pattern 4 — Custodianship without title
• A state may hold custody of sacred places (e.g. Mecca, Medina) without
  claiming the caliphate. Custodianship and caliphal authority are
  separable. Liability and legitimacy exposure differ.
• Source: MEM–ISLAM–CALIPHATE–ABOLITION; CIV–CORE–ISLAM.
Activation: REFERENCE. Use when analysing Saudi Arabia, Jordan, or other
custodial roles.
