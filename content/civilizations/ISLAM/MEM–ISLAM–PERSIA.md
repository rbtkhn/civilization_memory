I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Persia** as Islam's **conquered-and-absorbed peer**: the Sasanian empire whose defeat (651) delivered Mesopotamia and the Iranian plateau to dar al-Islam and whose civilizational grammar—language, administration, court culture, scholarship—shaped the caliphal and sultanic order. For Islam, Persia is not merely a lost enemy; it is **Persianate synthesis**—the fusion of Arabo-Islamic faith with Persian political and cultural forms that defined the Abbasid and later Sunni–Shi'a divide (Safavid Iran).

Encoding: Islam conquers Persia; Persia transforms Islam. Baghdad (Iraq) becomes caliphal center on Persianate territory; Persian becomes a language of high culture and administration alongside Arabic. Safavid conversion to Shi'a (16th c.) creates a lasting Islam–Persia interface: Shi'a Iran vs Sunni Ottoman.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST & ABSORPTION (7th c.)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
651: Fall of the Sasanian empire to Arab conquest. Mesopotamia and Iran pass under caliphal control. Unlike the Roman–Persian parity (neither could absorb the other), **Islam absorbs Persia**—territorially, then culturally. Umayyad then Abbasid administration; Persian elites and scribes integrated into the new order. Ctesiphon/Baghdad: from Sasanian to caliphal capital.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PERSIANATE SYNTHESIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Persianate synthesis: the fusion of Islamic norms with Persian political culture, language (Persian as second language of Islam), and administrative practice. Abbasid court; translation movement (Greek and Persian into Arabic); Persian as language of poetry, statecraft, and scholarship in eastern dar al-Islam. Pattern: **conquest then transformation**—the conquered shapes the conqueror.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SAFAVID & SHI'A IRAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Safavid dynasty (1501–1736): Iran converts to Twelver Shi'a; Persia becomes the Shi'a pole against Sunni Ottoman and Mughal. Islam–Persia interface is now sectarian and geopolitical: Persian Gulf, Hormuz, Mesopotamia contested. CIV–CORE–ISLAM § IV: Hormuz; Gulf as Islam–Persia interface. See MEM–ISLAM–GEO–PERSIAN–GULF.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. STRATEGIC LOGIC (MEARSHEIMER LAYER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Persia (Iran) holds the **eastern flank** of the historic caliphate and the **Gulf chokepoint** (Hormuz). No single Islamic state unites Sunni and Shi'a heartlands; Iran is structurally distinct—Persianate, Shi'a, and sovereign. Power distribution: Iran vs Arab Gulf vs Turkey; sectarian and ethnic fault lines persist.
