I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Damascus as Islam's **first imperial caliphal
capital**—the seat of the Umayyad dynasty (661–750) and the locus where
prophetic community became territorially continuous empire.

For Islam, Damascus represents legitimacy as stability over consensus;
scale before theology. It falls by political overthrow, not conquest—
encoding **continuity-through-transition**.

Grounds: CIV–SCHOLAR–ISLAM SYNTHESIS 0006; MEM–ISLAM–DYNASTY–UMAYYAD.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CAPITAL SHIFT FROM MEDINA (661)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Umayyads shift the caliphal seat from Medina to Damascus.

Bernard Lewis, "The Shi'a," *The New York Review of Books* (15 August 1985) (ARC–ISLAM–T–MODERN):

"In the civil war that followed, Ali himself, after some initial victories, was murdered in 661 AD, and the caliphate became hereditary in the house of Umayya, to which Uthman had belonged."

"The first four caliphs, known in Muslim historiography as the Rightly Guided, were chosen from among the companions of the Prophet. Thereafter the caliphate became hereditary in two successive dynasties."

Damascus anchors imperial scaling. Arabic as administrative language;
Byzantine–Persian bureaucratic inheritance; caliph as imperial sovereign.
Islam becomes a world empire before its institutions mature.

Strategic constraint: Damascus sits at the Levantine crossroads—Byzantium,
Persia, Arabia. Conquest frontiers and administrative gravity converge.
Projection and extraction drive the choice.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FALL BY OVERTHROW (750)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Damascus falls in 750 by **political overthrow**—the Abbasid revolution.
The dynasty is delegitimized; administrative precedents endure.

Encoding: continuity-through-transition. The capital moves; the
institution persists. Andalusian Umayyad survival (Cordoba) continues
the line elsewhere. Damascus post-750 becomes provincial—no longer
caliphal, but never destroyed.
