I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Russia** as Islam's **northern civilizational interface**: the power that was first ruled by Muslims (Golden Horde), then conquered Muslim khanates (Kazan, Astrakhan, Crimea), then became the Ottoman empire's principal rival—and that today holds the North Caucasus and the largest Muslim population in Europe. For Islam, Russia is not a single object. It is **sovereign layer (Horde), then conquered other (khanates lost), then external rival (Ottoman–Russian wars), then hegemon over Muslim lands** (Caucasus, Crimea, Central Asia).

Encoding: Islam once ruled the Rus'; Russia inverted the relationship. Ottoman and Safavid lost the Caucasus and Black Sea littoral to Russia. Constantinople became a Russian objective; Muslim populations passed under Russian and Soviet rule. CIV–STATE–ISLAM: Russia as PARITY_LEGACY, CONQUEST_LEGACY.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GOLDEN HORDE — ISLAM AS SOVEREIGN OVER RUS'
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mongols of the Golden Horde adopted Islam (Berke Khan, 13th c.). The Horde became the sovereign to whom Rus' princes paid tribute and from whom they received yarliks. For dar al-Islam, the Horde was **Islamic sovereignty at the northern frontier**—not caliphal administration but Muslim rule over Slavic principalities. Rus' chose Orthodox Christianity from Byzantium; they remained under a Muslim khanate. Encoding: **Islam as master** over what would become Russia.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INVERSION — RUSSIA CONQUERS MUSLIM KHANATES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Muscovite expansion reversed the relationship. Kazan (1552), Astrakhan (1556), Sibir (1580s), Crimea (1783): Muslim khanates absorbed into the Russian empire. Islam encoded as **conquered**—the faith of polities that lost to Orthodox Russia. The former tribute-payers became the rulers; the former sovereigns became subjects. Russian memory inverts the Horde; Islamic memory preserves **loss**—the khanates and the Crimea.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. OTTOMAN–RUSSIAN RIVALRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russo-Ottoman wars (17th–20th c.): Russia and the Ottoman empire fought over the Black Sea, Crimea, the Caucasus, and the Balkans. Ottoman territorial retreat (Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca 1774, Congress of Berlin 1878, etc.); Russia gained warm-water access and Orthodox populations. For Islam, Russia is **the rival that took Ottoman land**—Constantinople as Russian objective, Straits as Russian goal. MEM–ISLAM–CONSTANTINOPLE: Russia as claimant to the lost center. MEM–ISLAM–GEO–BLACK–SEA: Russian–Islam interface.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CAUCASUS & NORTH CAUCASUS — LOSS AND GRIEVANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ottoman and Safavid contested the Caucasus; both lost to Russia. North Caucasus (Dagestan, Chechnya, Ingushetia): Muslim populations under Russian then Soviet then Russian Federation rule. Resistance (Imam Shamil, 19th c.; Chechen wars 1994–2009) encodes **grievance and occupation**. See MEM–ISLAM–GEO–CAUCASUS. No Islamic state controls the mountain core; Russia holds the northern approaches. Encoding: **frontier lost**; Muslim populations under non-Islamic sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. STRATEGIC LOGIC (MEARSHEIMER LAYER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Power distribution: Russia dominates the Black Sea littoral (post-2014 Crimea), the North Caucasus, and the Caspian northern shore. Ottoman successor (Turkey) and Iran contest the southern rim. Structural: Russia is **the northern pole** against which Ottoman and later Turkish/Iranian policy defines itself. Constantinople/Istanbul as symbolic and strategic prize; Straits as chokepoint. Parity and conquest legacy (CIV–STATE–ISLAM) — Russia is both peer rival and conqueror of Muslim territory.
