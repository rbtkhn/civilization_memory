────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Mediterranean as Islam's **civilizational
interface and conquest corridor**: the enclosed sea where Islamic
expansion met Byzantine, Latin, and later European power—a zone of
transmission (Sicily, Iberia), Crusades theater (Levant), and Ottoman
dominance (eastern basin).

CIV–CORE–ISLAM § IV: "Eastern Med corridors → sacred memory activation
accelerant."

For Islam, the Mediterranean is:
• Eastern basin: Levant, Egypt, North Africa—core Islamic littoral
• Western basin: Al-Andalus (lost); Sicily (transmission)
• Crusades theater: 1099–1291
• Ottoman lake: Eastern Med, 15th–17th C

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC PARAMETERS & STRUCTURAL LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mediterranean (~2.5 million km²) is an enclosed sea bounded by
Europe (north), Africa (south), and Asia (east). Connected to Atlantic
(Gibraltar), Black Sea (Bosphorus), Red Sea (Suez, post-1869).

Structural axiom:
> Control of narrows (Gibraltar, Bosphorus, Suez) governs access. Eastern
> Med = Islamic heartland littoral; western Med = contested or lost.

Terrain: Maritime; island-dotted; coastline fragmentation enables
multi-polar control. No single power has held entire basin continuously.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. POWER DISTRIBUTION & HISTORICAL SEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early Islam: Umayyad expansion along southern littoral (Egypt, North
Africa, Iberia); Byzantine retention of Anatolia and eastern islands.

Edward Gibbon, *Decline and Fall* (ARC–ISLAM–T–EARLY-MOD):

"Syria and Egypt had been exhausted by the Persian war; the Byzantine
arms were broken; and the Saracens issued from the desert with an
enthusiasm not less ardent than that of the crusaders."

Crusades: Latin states (1099–1291) occupy Levant coast; Islamic
counter-mobilization; Saladin restores Jerusalem (1187). Mediterranean
becomes **Crusades theater**.

Ottoman: Eastern Med as Ottoman lake; Constantinople (1453) = naval
base; North African regencies. Marc David Baer, *The Ottomans* (ARC–ISLAM–T–MODERN):

"The Ottomans' multiethnic, multilingual, and multireligious domain
reached deep into Europe's heart."

Modern: European naval hegemony (19th C); then US Sixth Fleet; contested
eastern Med (Turkey, Israel, Egypt, Greece, Libya).
