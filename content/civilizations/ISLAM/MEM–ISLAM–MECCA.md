I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Mecca as Islam's **sacred center**—the site of
revelation, the Kaaba, and the obligatory pilgrimage (hajj). For Islam,
Mecca is non-fungible; its integrity conditions legitimacy across the
Ummah.

CIV–CORE–ISLAM § IV: "Hejaz (Mecca–Medina)"; sacred zones are
NON-FUNGIBLE.

With Medina, Mecca forms the **Hijaz**—the dual anchor of Islamic
sacred geography. Caliphal custodianship (Ottoman, Mamluk, earlier)
has always included protection of the holy cities.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVELATION & ORIGIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mecca is the birthplace of Muhammad and the site of the first
revelations. The Kaaba—pre-Islamic sanctuary—is Islamized as the
qibla (direction of prayer) and the focal point of pilgrimage.

Bernard Lewis, *The Political Language of Islam* (1988) (ARC–ISLAM–T–MODERN):

"The central institution of sovereignty in the traditional Islamic world, the caliphate, is defined by the Sunni jurists to have contractual and consensual features that distinguish caliphs from despots."

Custody of Mecca has historically been a legitimacy claim. The Sharif
of Mecca held symbolic authority; Ottoman and Mamluk sultans protected
pilgrimage routes. 1924 abolition leaves sacred custodianship stateless—
Saudi control follows, but no universal caliphal arbiter.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PILGRIMAGE & UMMAH COHESION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The hajj obliges every able Muslim once in a lifetime. Mecca becomes
the **convergence point** of the global Ummah—physically, ritually,
annually.

Bernard Lewis, *What Went Wrong?* (2002) (ARC–ISLAM–T–MODERN):

"For the first thousand years of its history, Islamic civilization's relationship to Christendom was one of dominance."

Mecca's role predates that dominance and outlasts it. Pilgrimage
networks (MEM–ISLAM–GEO–INDIAN–OCEAN: Mecca from East Africa, India,
Southeast Asia) make the city a hub of civilizational connection.
Loss of Mecca to non-Islamic control would constitute ontological
rupture—unimaginable within Islamic encoding.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CRUSADES MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bernard Lewis (via MEM–ISLAM–SALADIN source), on Crusader-era Islamic
response:

"Even the crusader capture of Jerusalem aroused little attention at the
time, and appeals for help to various Muslim capitals brought no
response. The real counter-crusade began when the crusaders—very
foolishly—began to harry and attack the Muslim holy lands, namely the
Hijaz in Arabia, containing the holy cities of Mecca and Medina."

Mecca and Medina—more than Jerusalem at that moment—triggered
mobilization. Sacred-zone violation of the Hijaz activated legitimacy
crisis. Hierarchy of sacred zones: Hejaz (Mecca–Medina) as primal;
Jerusalem as third.
