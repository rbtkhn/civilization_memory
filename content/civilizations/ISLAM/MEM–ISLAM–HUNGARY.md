I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Hungary** as Islam's **northwestern conquest and loss**: the kingdom that the Ottomans defeated at Mohács (1526), whose central core (Buda, central Hungary) became Ottoman for a century and a half, and which was then lost to Habsburg reconquest (Treaty of Karlowitz 1699). For Islam/Ottoman memory, Hungary is **the limit of sustained European expansion**—Vienna (1683) marked the high-water mark of projection; Hungary was held, then relinquished. Encoding: conquest, custodianship, then **reversal**—dar al-Islam's European frontier contracts.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST — MOHÁCS AND BUDA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1526: Battle of Mohács—Ottoman victory over the Kingdom of Hungary; Hungarian king killed; kingdom fragmented. 1541: Buda (Budapest) under Ottoman control; central Hungary becomes Ottoman province (Budin eyalet). Hungary divided: Ottoman central strip; Habsburg Royal Hungary (west/north); Transylvania as Ottoman vassal. Pattern: **Ottoman Europe at its greatest extent**—Hungary as Islam's northwestern territorial anchor.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LOSS — HABSBURG RECONQUEST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Late 17th c.: Habsburg and allied forces push Ottomans back. 1686: Buda falls. 1699: Treaty of Karlowitz—Ottomans cede Hungary and Transylvania to Habsburgs. Hungary passes out of dar al-Islam. Encoding: **loss of European territory**—the frontier that had advanced now recedes; Ottoman empire becomes increasingly Anatolian and Balkan (south of Danube), less Central European.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC LOGIC (MEARSHEIMER LAYER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hungary functioned as **projection corridor** (Danube axis toward Vienna) and **buffer** (between Ottoman Balkans and Habsburg Austria). Loss of Hungary = loss of strategic depth; Danube becomes frontier again. Vienna 1683 = limit of Ottoman projection; Karlowitz = confirmation of retreat. Power distribution: Habsburg gains; Ottoman contraction.
