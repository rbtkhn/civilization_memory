I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Khwarazm as Islam's **catalyst for Mongol invasion**:
the dynasty whose actions at Otrar (1218)—seizure of a Mongol trade
caravan, execution of merchants and envoys—triggered Genghis Khan's
campaign into the Islamic world and led to the annihilation of the
Khwarazmshah state itself.

For Islam, Khwarazm encodes: the polity that brought the Mongols.
Structural causation: one regime's provocation draws irruption.

Grounds: MEM–ISLAM–MONGOL–EMPIRE; MEM–ISLAM–SILK–ROAD; Weatherford (ARC).

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. RISE & EXTENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Khwarazmshahs emerge as vassals of the Seljuks (1077); achieve
independence (12th C). Under Ala ad-Din Muhammad II (r. 1200–1220),
Khwarazm controls Transoxiana, Persia, Afghanistan—one of the largest
Islamic states of the era. Conflict with the Mongols is not preordained;
trade and diplomacy had linked the steppe and the sown.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. OTRAR & THE TRIGGER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At Otrar (1218), the Khwarazm governor Inalchuq seizes a Mongol trade
caravan—Muslim and Hindu merchants carrying luxury goods from Genghis
Khan's domains. The governor kills the merchants. Genghis sends envoys
to the Sultan demanding justice; the Sultan kills some envoys and
mutilates others, sending them back.

Jack Weatherford (IIT summary, *Genghis Khan and the Making of the Modern World* 2004): Juvaini records that "the whirlwind of anger cast dust into the eyes of patience and clemency while the fire of wrath flared up with such a flame that it drove the water from his eyes and could be quenched only by the shedding of blood."

Encoding: Khwarazm's actions—greed, arrogance, violation of envoy
immunity—supply casus belli. The Mongols invade (1219).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ANNIHILATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Genghis Khan's campaign (1219–1221) destroys the Khwarazmshah state.
Bukhara, Samarkand, Merv, Nishapur fall. The Sultan flees; dies on an
island in the Caspian. His heir is pursued and killed. The dynasty
ceases to exist. Silk Road nodes are shattered (MEM–ISLAM–SILK–ROAD).

Weatherford: "In twenty-five years, the Mongol army subjugated more lands and people than the Romans had conquered in four hundred years."

Khwarazm is the first Islamic polity fully annihilated by the Mongols—
and its own provocation had opened the gate.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MEMORY ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Khwarazm is remembered as:
• The state that brought the Mongols
• Provocation (Otrar) as structural cause
• Annihilation as outcome
• Catalyst for broader Islamic catastrophe (Khwarazm → Assassins → Baghdad)

No civilizational reclamation. The dynasty is gone; its causal role
in 1219–1258 persists.
