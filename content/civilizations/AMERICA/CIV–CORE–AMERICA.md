I. CIVILIZATIONAL IDENTITY & PRIME AXIOMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Constitutional Continuity Civilization (LOCKED)
America generates durability through the written Constitution, amendment
procedure, and judicial interpretation rather than person, dynasty, or
unwritten custom. Revolution was founding; recurrence of revolution
as legitimacy mechanism is treated as civilizational failure.

Authority migrates predictably:
Person → Office → Constitution → Amendment → Precedent

2. Legitimacy-Through-Constitutional Adherence
Legitimacy is not earned through moral declaration alone. It is
sustained through adherence to and amendment within the constitutional
frame. Legitimacy erodes when constitutional constraints are bypassed
or when amendment procedure is replaced by rupture.

Collapse is defined as constitutional discontinuity or replacement by
extra-constitutional authority.

3. Replacement-Through-Election Doctrine
Rulers and representatives are replaced through prescribed electoral
and succession procedures. Transfer of power is procedural, not
hereditary or coup-based. Contested transfer is a stress indicator;
failure to transfer per procedure is a red line.

4. Federalism-as-Constraint Engine
Sovereignty is divided between federation and states. Centralization
and dispersal are in structural tension. Override of federal structure
without amendment weakens legitimacy; state-level defection or
nullification is a failure signal.

5. Separation of Powers Axiom
Executive, legislative, and judicial functions are separated. Checks
and balances constrain any single branch. Consolidation of unchecked
authority in one branch is a red line.

6. Rights-Based Legitimacy Surface
Bill of Rights and subsequent amendments form a constraint surface.
Legitimacy claims are routinely framed in rights and liberty. Systemic
violation of enumerated rights without remedy erodes legitimacy.
