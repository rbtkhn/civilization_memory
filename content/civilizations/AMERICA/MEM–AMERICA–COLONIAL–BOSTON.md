I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Boston as **America's encoding of colonial
resistance and revolutionary centre**: Massachusetts Bay port
(1630), Puritan and commercial hub, then focal point of opposition
to British authority—Stamp Act, Boston Massacre (1770), Boston Tea
Party (1773)—and Siege of Boston (1775–1776) that opens the War of
Independence.

Boston does not declare independence alone.
It **concentrates grievance and triggers armed conflict**; procedure
(Continental Congress, Declaration) follows in Philadelphia.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. MASSACHUSETTS BAY & PORT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Massachusetts Bay Colony; Boston as port and political centre;
Puritan and later commercial; shipping, trade, and intellectual
life. Colonial assembly and grievance tradition; post–Seven Years'
War imperial measures (taxation, quartering) meet organised
resistance. Boston as **node of dissent**—pamphlets, crowds,
political mobilisation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CRISIS EVENTS & SIEGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Boston Massacre (1770)—British soldiers fire on crowd; five dead;
Adams's defence of soldiers (procedure over faction); propaganda
and memory. Boston Tea Party (1773)—protest against tea tax;
destruction of cargo; Britain responds with Coercive Acts; colonial
solidarity. April 1775: Lexington and Concord; British retreat to
Boston. Siege of Boston—Continental Army under Washington; British
evacuation by sea (March 1776). Boston becomes **first theatre of
continental war**; resistance becomes armed conflict.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Boston encodes **resistance hub and revolution trigger**—colonial
grievance concentrated in one place; procedural defence (Adams)
and extra-legal action (Tea Party) both present; siege as opening
of founding war.

Lessons: Port and polity as centre of dissent; first shot and siege
as war opening; Massachusetts and Boston in founding generation
(Adams, Hancock, etc.).
