I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Philadelphia as **America's encoding of
procedural founding**: Penn's city (1682), religious tolerance and
planned growth, then **seat of Continental Congress**—coordination
of resistance, Declaration of Independence (1776), and
Constitutional Convention (1787)—where secession and federal
design are debated and adopted under one roof.

Philadelphia does not fight the war.
It **hosts the assemblies that authorise and frame it**; procedure
and text as civilizational output.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FOUNDATION & TOLERANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
William Penn; Quaker settlement; 1682; religious tolerance and
grid plan; Pennsylvania as "holy experiment." Philadelphia grows
as port and commercial centre; diversity and print culture; colonial
capital and meeting place. **Neutral and central**—between New
England and the South; suitable as congress seat.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CONTINENTAL CONGRESS & DECLARATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
First Continental Congress (1774)—response to Coercive Acts;
grievance and coordination. Second Continental Congress (1775)—
after Lexington and Concord; Washington appointed commander; war
conducted from Philadelphia. July 4, 1776: **Declaration of
Independence**—adopted in Philadelphia; universalist register and
secession as stated right; alliance signal and legitimacy text.
Philadelphia as **procedural centre**—where the break is declared
and the war authorised.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CONSTITUTIONAL CONVENTION (1787)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-war weakness (Articles of Confederation); convention called
to revise; instead, new frame drafted. Philadelphia again: **U.S.
Constitution**—federal structure, separation of powers, amendment
process. Ratification follows; Philadelphia encodes **founding
twice**: Declaration (exit) and Constitution (re-founding).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Philadelphia encodes **procedure as founding**—assembly, declaration,
and constitution in one place; religious tolerance and political
centrality; text and institution as American output.

Lessons: Neutral ground for coordination; Declaration and Constitution
as dual founding moments; Quaker tolerance and federal design as
legacy.
