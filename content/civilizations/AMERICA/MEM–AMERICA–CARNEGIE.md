I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Andrew Carnegie as **America's archetype
of vertical integration and philanthropic redistribution**: the
figure who built Carnegie Steel (vertical integration—ore, rail,
mills), faced labour conflict (Homestead 1892), sold to Morgan
(U.S. Steel, 1901), and devoted the remainder of his life to
philanthropy—libraries, peace, "Gospel of Wealth"—encoding the
tycoon who **justifies accumulation by giving it away**.

Carnegie does not break the republic.
He **mediates** it: industrial concentration, then voluntary
redistribution; procedure (labour law, antitrust) and philanthropy
as parallel responses.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN & STYLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Carnegie rises from rail and steel.

Context:
• Pennsylvania Railroad; investment in iron and steel; vertical
  integration—control of ore, transport, and mills
• Homestead Works; efficiency and scale; labour conflict—
  Homestead Strike (1892); Pinkertons and violence; legitimacy
  of capital vs. labour contested
• "Gospel of Wealth" (1889)—argument that the wealthy should
  redistribute in life; libraries, education, peace; legitimacy
  reframed through giving

Authority is framed as **builder and benefactor**: the tycoon
who concentrates then voluntarily disperses; labour conflict
and philanthropy as twin encodings.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STEEL, HOMESTEAD, SALE TO MORGAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Carnegie Steel peaks then exits through merger.

Elements:
• Dominance in steel production; vertical integration as
  competitive advantage
• Homestead (1892)—strike; lockout; violence; Carnegie's
  absence and delegation; encoding of **labour conflict** and
  private force (Pinkertons)
• Sale to J.P. Morgan (1901)—U.S. Steel; Carnegie exits
  industry with vast wealth; concentrates further in Morgan's
  hands, then Carnegie dedicates to philanthropy

The tycoon **exits** industry and **reinvests** in legitimacy
(libraries, peace); procedure (labour law, antitrust) evolves
separately.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Carnegie encodes **vertical integration, labour conflict, exit,
and philanthropic redistribution**.

Lessons:
• Vertical integration (ore to finished steel) can create
  dominance; then concentration can be sold (to Morgan) rather
  than broken up (unlike Standard Oil).
• Labour conflict (Homestead) encodes private force and
  legitimacy stress; procedure (labour rights) follows later.
• Philanthropy as voluntary redistribution—"Gospel of Wealth";
  tycoon justifies accumulation by giving; tension with labour
  memory preserved.

Carnegie is America's **steel tycoon and benefactor**: Homestead,
sale to Morgan, and libraries.
