I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Lexington and Concord as **America's encoding
of the first shots** of the War of Independence: the British march
to seize colonial military stores (April 19, 1775), militia
resistance at Lexington (first fire) and Concord (bridge, retreat),
and the running fight back to Boston that opens armed conflict and
leads to Siege of Boston and Continental Congress authorisation of
war.

Lexington and Concord do not declare independence.
They **translate grievance into open war**; procedure (Congress,
Declaration) follows.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT & SEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
British regulars march from Boston to Concord to seize arms and
supplies; colonial militia mobilises. Lexington Green: first
exchange of fire; dead on both sides. British proceed to Concord;
fight at North Bridge; British retreat under harassing fire from
militia along the road. "Shot heard round the world" (Emerson)—
symbolic encoding of global significance. British reach Boston;
militia invests the town; Siege of Boston begins. Second Continental
Congress convenes; Washington appointed commander; war authorised.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Lexington and Concord encode **war trigger**—first shots; militia
resistance as legitimate defence; opening of founding war; link
to Boston (colonial resistance hub) and Philadelphia (Congress,
Declaration).
