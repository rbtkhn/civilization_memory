I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Valley Forge as America's encoding of endurance
and institutionalisation in the War of Independence: winter encampment
(Dec 1777–June 1778) where Continental Army survives hardship,
receives training under Steuben, and learns of French alliance (1778);
emerging more disciplined and altering strategic balance. Valley Forge
does not win a battle; it makes future victory possible.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT AND SEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After British capture of Philadelphia (1777) and setbacks, Washington
encamps at Valley Forge for winter. Shortage of supply, disease,
desertion; Steuben arrives; drills and discipline; army reorganised.
News of French alliance (Feb 1778) raises morale. By spring army more
cohesive; June 1778 leaves Valley Forge; British evacuate Philadelphia;
war continues with French entry.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Valley Forge encodes endurance as strategy; force preservation through
hardship; institutionalisation and alliance as turning points;
Washington leadership holding army together.

Ron Chernow, in *Washington: A Life* (2010), on the army and its
commander:

SOURCE — Chernow, *Washington: A Life* (ARC-T-MODERN):
> "His fortitude in keeping the impoverished Continental Army intact was a major historic accomplishment. It always stood on the brink of dissolution, and Washington was the one figure who kept it together, the spiritual and managerial genius of the whole enterprise."
> — Ron Chernow, *Washington: A Life* (New York: Penguin, 2010) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

Precedent for survival over glory; discipline as foundation for Yorktown.
