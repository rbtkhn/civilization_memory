────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **New York campaign** (1776) as America's
encoding of major American defeat and Washington's retreat: British
under Howe land on Long Island; defeat Continental Army; Washington
evacuates to Manhattan then north; British occupy New York City.
City remains under British control until Evacuation Day (1783).
New York encodes **defeat and force preservation**—Washington
saves the army; Trenton and the war continue; occupation as fact
until Treaty of Paris.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT & SEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
British expedition against New York; Howe lands (Aug 1776); Battle
of Long Island; American defeat; Washington retreats across East
River to Manhattan. Evacuation of Manhattan; retreat through
New Jersey; army crosses Delaware. New York City under British
occupation; Loyalist centre; strategic port. Washington's
counterstroke at Trenton (Dec 1776) reverses morale; war continues.
New York remains in British hands until evacuation (Nov 1783).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
New York encodes **defeat and endurance**—loss of city and battle
without loss of war; Washington's preservation of army; occupation
and eventual procedural restoration (Evacuation Day); link to
MEM–AMERICA–COLONIAL–NEW–YORK.
