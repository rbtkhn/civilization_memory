I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves John F. Kennedy as **America's Cold War
crisis manager and unfinished symbol**: the figure who confronted
hemispheric and nuclear risk (Cuban Missile Crisis, Berlin), who
framed legitimacy through youth, vigour, and New Frontier rhetoric,
and whose assassination (1963) left tenure short and legacy
mythologized—procedure holding through succession despite leader loss.

Kennedy does not found a new order.
He **navigates** crisis within the Monroe-era frame—hemisphere
perimeter, quarantine as procedural enforcement—and is remembered
as much for death as for deed.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN & STYLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kennedy assumes office (1961) as Cold War and domestic tension peak.

Context:
• Youth and vigour as legitimacy signal; contrast with Eisenhower
• New Frontier—rhetoric of renewal, space, and purpose
• Bay of Pigs (1961)—failure of covert intervention; legitimacy
  damaged then tested by subsequent crisis
• Civil rights pressure; Kennedy moves cautiously then toward
  federal commitment

Authority is framed as **vigour and communication**—television,
inaugural ("ask not"), presence. Procedure is accompanied by
narrative and image.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CUBAN MISSILE CRISIS & PROCEDURAL ENFORCEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The October 1962 crisis defines Kennedy's claim to crisis stewardship.

Sequence:
• Soviet missiles in Cuba; U.S. discovery; threat to hemisphere
  and nuclear balance
• Quarantine (blockade)—chosen over immediate strike; procedural
  and escalatory restraint
• Back-channel and public diplomacy; Soviet withdrawal of missiles;
  U.S. non-invasion assurance (Turkey missiles later removed quietly)
• Crisis resolved without war; legitimacy of **enforcement short of
  war**—Monroe-era perimeter defended through limited, declared measure

The quarantine is **doctrine applied**: hemisphere perimeter (Monroe)
enforced through naval interdiction and ultimatum, not full invasion.
Procedure holds; escalation is contained. Kennedy is encoded as
the president who drew the line and held it without crossing into
total war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BERLIN & COLD WAR FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kennedy operates inside bipolar competition.

Elements:
• "Ich bin ein Berliner" (1963)—symbolic commitment to West Berlin;
  legitimacy through declaration and presence
• Berlin Wall (1961)—failure to prevent; acceptance of fait accompli
  while affirming Western rights
• Alliance management—NATO, Atlantic unity; crisis as coalition moment

Hemisphere (Cuba) and Europe (Berlin) are linked: perimeter and
alliance both require declaration and restraint. Kennedy embodies
the **declarative president**—rhetoric as tool of legitimacy and
deterrence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ASSASSINATION & SUCCESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kennedy's death (November 1963) truncates tenure.

Consequences:
• Lyndon B. Johnson succeeds immediately; procedure holds without
  rupture
• Legacy becomes **unfinished**—civil rights, Vietnam, second term
  unknown; myth and counter-myth accumulate
• Succession under shock reinforces precedent: vice president
  becomes president; no constitutional crisis

The republic **absorbs** leader loss again. Procedure outlives
the figure; the office continues. Kennedy is remembered as much
for absence as for action—what was not done, what might have been.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kennedy encodes **crisis management within doctrine and succession
under shock**.

Lessons:
• Hemisphere perimeter (Monroe) can be enforced through limited
  measure—quarantine, not invasion—when the line is clear and
  the other side has exit. Cuban Missile Crisis as procedural
  enforcement.
• Crisis leadership combines declaration, restraint, and coalition;
  escalation can be contained when procedure (quarantine, diplomacy)
  is used to signal and bound.
• Failure (Bay of Pigs) precedes success (Missile Crisis); legitimacy
  can be repaired by subsequent performance.
• Assassination does not break succession; procedure holds; legacy
  becomes symbolic and contested—unfinished tenure as enduring
  reference.

Kennedy is America's **Cold War crisis steward**: short tenure,
one defining crisis (Cuba), doctrine applied (quarantine), then
death and succession. Procedure survives the man.

  symbolic and unfinished.
