I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves New York as **America's encoding of colonial
port and pluralism**: New Amsterdam (Dutch, 1624), then New York
(English, 1664); commercial hub, ethnic and religious diversity;
British occupation for much of the War of Independence; Evacuation
Day (1783) when British leave and American authority is restored.

New York does not host the Declaration.
It **encodes commerce, occupation, and return**—port as strategic
prize and site of restored authority.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DUTCH & ENGLISH FOUNDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dutch West India Company; New Amsterdam (1624); Manhattan and
harbour; trade and diversity. English capture (1664); renamed New
York; retained as royal colony; port and commercial growth. Pluralism
and trade as enduring traits; **commerce over creed**—contrast with
Puritan Boston and Quaker Philadelphia in colonial memory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. REVOLUTION & OCCUPATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Resistance to British measures; but New York City falls to British
(1776) and remains under occupation for the war. Strategic port and
base; Loyalist presence; Continental Army cannot retake the city.
**Occupation as fact**—American authority restored only when Britain
evacuates after Treaty of Paris (1783). Evacuation Day (November 25,
1783): British leave; Washington enters; procedural restoration of
American control.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
New York encodes **port as prize and restoration**—commerce and
pluralism; occupation and evacuation; authority restored by
British exit rather than American conquest; precedent for
**reintegration through withdrawal**.

Lessons: Commercial hub and diversity as colonial legacy; occupation
and Evacuation Day as procedural end of war in one place; New York
as future capital of commerce and culture.
