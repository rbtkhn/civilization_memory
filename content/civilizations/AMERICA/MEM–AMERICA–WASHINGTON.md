I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves George Washington as **Anglia's externalized
continuity figure**: the leader who carried Anglian procedural,
legal, and restraint-based civilizational grammar across the Atlantic
while severing imperial sovereignty.

Washington is rupture in allegiance, continuity in form.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Washington emerges from Anglian colonial society.

Context:
• Common law tradition
• Representative assemblies
• Protestant moral order
• Imperial military experience

The American rebellion is fought by Anglian institutions
against Anglian authority.

Winston Churchill, in *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 3 (*The Age of Revolution* [1956–1958]), treats Washington as part of the "first great generation" of American leaders—Washington, Adams, and Jefferson—who emerge from the American Revolution as founders of the republic within the English-speaking peoples' story (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

SOURCE — Churchill, *The Age of Revolution* (Vol. 3) (ARC-T-MODERN):
> "In this campaign a young Virginian officer named George Washington learnt his first military lessons."
> — Winston S. Churchill, *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 3, *The Age of Revolution* (1956–1958), p. 118 (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

III. WAR LEADERSHIP AS RESTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Washington's command style mirrors Anglian logic.

Characteristics:
• Avoidance of decisive annihilation battles
• Preservation of force over glory
• Endurance prioritized over speed
• Deference to civilian authority

Victory is achieved by survival, not domination.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. VOLUNTARY POWER SURRENDER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Washington's defining act is renunciation.

Actions:
• Resignation of military command
• Refusal of monarchy
• Acceptance of term limits

Authority is legitimized by relinquishment.

This act transmits Anglia's deepest norm:
procedure over person.
