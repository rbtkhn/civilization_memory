DOCTRINE 01
Name: FOUR-ZONE GEO AND LEVANT OVEREXTENSION LIABILITY
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source:
• CIV–SCHOLAR–PERSIA (SYNTHESIS 0005)
• DIB–PERSIA (Accepted 2026-02)

Definition:
Persian strategic geography divides into four zones—Caspian (buffer/containment), Gulf (chokepoint advantage), Indian Ocean (periphery), Levant (parity)—each with distinct structural incentives. In parity zones such as the Levant, conquest does not imply consolidation; overextension invites reversal, court defection, and exhaustion. Liability and irreversibility attach to the overextending ruler and the defecting successor. Exhaustion in parity zones is more dangerous than defeat when closure capacity is absent.

Operational Meaning:
• Four-zone frame: assess which zone a given commitment or theatre sits in; incentives differ by zone (chokepoint vs parity vs buffer vs periphery).
• Levant/parity: assume reach without hold; conquest peaks are reversible unless closure capacity is secured; denial and punishment of overextension are equilibrium.
• Overextension risk: maximal reach (Khosrow-style) without consolidation triggers defection and exhaustion; historical confirming case: Khosrow II → Heraclius → Kavadh II defection → Sasanian non-recovery.
• Liability layer: who is on the hook (ruler), who can defect (successor, elites), what is irreversible (exhaustion, conquest conditions for third actors).

Hard Constraints:
• Doctrine is invalid or must be reviewed if: (1) evidence shows Levant parity zone consistently held without reversal despite overextension; (2) overextending ruler retains elite cohesion and no defection where pattern predicts defection; (3) exhaustion is shown to be less consequential than defeat in a parity zone across multiple cases.
