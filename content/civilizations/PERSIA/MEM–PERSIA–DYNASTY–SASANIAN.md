I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Sasanian dynasty as the **restorative dynastic
engine of Persian sovereignty**: the lineage that reconstituted Iranian
imperial authority after Hellenistic and Parthian dilution and hardened
it into a centralized, sacral, and administratively coherent state.

The Sasanians represent **continuity through compression and revival**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC ORIGIN & RESTORATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ardashir I’s overthrow of the Arsacid Parthians (224 AD) is framed not as
a revolution but as a **return to rightful Persian order**.

Early consolidation relied on:
• Reassertion of Iranian kingship traditions
• Suppression of semi-autonomous noble houses
• Revival of imperial ideology
• Clear rupture with Hellenistic pluralism

Legitimacy was claimed through restoration, not novelty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. TERRITORIAL & AUTHORITY CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sasanian strategy emphasized:
• Centralized military command
• Reduction of feudal autonomy
• Direct taxation and land control
• Reintegration of frontier zones

Power was hardened inward before being projected outward.

Incremental tightening replaced loose confederation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SACRAL & COSMOLOGICAL LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sasanian kingship fused:
• Zoroastrian cosmology
• Divine favor (khvarenah)
• King-of-Kings universalism
• Moral struggle against chaos (druj)

The shahanshah embodied cosmic order.

Political authority was inseparable from metaphysical legitimacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Sasanians formalized:
• Professional bureaucracy
• Codified law
• Standing military institutions
• State-aligned priesthood
• Imperial infrastructure and roads

This created a durable imperial core capable of long rivalry with Rome.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. TRANSITION, PRESSURE & ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The dynasty collapsed under:
• Prolonged Roman–Byzantine wars
• Elite factionalism
• Fiscal strain
• Arab-Islamic conquest

Yet collapse did not erase legacy.

Sasanian administrative, fiscal, and court models were absorbed into:
• Early Islamic governance
• Abbasid statecraft
• Persian bureaucratic culture

Empire fell; civilization persisted.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Persia restores itself through ideological revival.
• Centralization follows perceived civilizational humiliation.
• Sacral cosmology stabilizes authority under pressure.
• Administrative depth outlives dynastic defeat.
• The Sasanians made Persian sovereignty hard, formal, and transmissible.
