I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Islamic Republic as **a modern Persian
sovereignty-restoration regime**, not a purely ideological theocracy.

The Islamic Republic represents **continuity through compression**:
religion, revolution, and state fused to defend autonomy under siege.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVOLUTION AS CIVILIZATIONAL RESET MECHANISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The 1979 Revolution overthrows monarchy but restores sovereignty.

Core features:
• Rejection of foreign dependency
• Expulsion of Western enforcement structures
• Reassertion of civilizational dignity
• Fusion of clerical legitimacy with state power

Ideology serves sovereignty, not the reverse.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SHI’A GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Islamic Republic institutionalizes Shi’a authority.

Mechanisms:
• Clerical oversight of state legitimacy
• Martyrdom memory embedded in governance
• Supreme authority framed as guardianship
• Republic preserved beneath clerical filter

Religion becomes **a sovereignty firewall**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SIEGE GOVERNANCE & SANCTIONS ADAPTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Persistent external pressure shapes the regime.

Adaptations:
• Sanctions-resistant economic routing
• Asymmetric military doctrine
• Proxy warfare and deterrence calibration
• Compute, energy, and logistics autonomy

Compression increases system density.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL DIGNITY DOCTRINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legitimacy rests on:
• Refusal to submit
• Willingness to absorb hardship
• Preservation of national honor
• Resistance to humiliation-lock events

Prosperity is secondary to autonomy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. INTERNAL CONTRADICTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tensions persist:
• Youth demographics vs clerical rigidity
• Economic strain vs endurance legitimacy
• Popular dissent vs sovereignty narrative

These contradictions are **managed, not resolved**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. STRATEGIC FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Islamic Republic functions as:
• Siege-state prototype
• Exit-building engine
• Anti-enforcement test case
• Civilizational memory carrier

It prioritizes survival outside incumbent order.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The Islamic Republic is a sovereignty machine.
• Religion operationalizes endurance.
• Compression produces institutional density.
• Dignity substitutes for prosperity.
• Persia survives by redesigning the state under siege.
