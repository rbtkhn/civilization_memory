I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **Siyasatnama** (Book of Government) as Persia's
**governance-law text**: the mirror-for-princes composed by Nizam
al-Mulk (1018–1092), vizier to the Seljuk sultans Alp Arslan and
Malik-Shah, which codifies justice, army, spies, revenue, and the
duties of the sovereign and his agents. It encodes **law as royal
administration**—not a constitution limiting the king but a manual
for lawful rule that legitimates the dynasty through order and
fair dealing.

For Persian civilizational memory, the Siyasatnama represents
**institutional continuity of statecraft** after the Islamic
absorption: the Persianate grammar of just rule, bureaucracy, and
information (barid, spies) expressed in Persian and addressed to
a Turkic sovereign. Contradictions preserved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGINS AND CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Nizam al-Mulk: from Tus (Khurasan); served Seljuk sultans as vizier
  from c. 1063 until his assassination (1092)
• Siyasatnama composed late in life, at royal request (Malik-Shah);
  purpose: to instruct the sultan and his officials in governance
• Themes: justice and redress; reliability of the army; intelligence
  and spies; revenue and land; avoidance of oppression; selection of
  judges and agents; treatment of religious scholars and soldiers
• Composed in Persian; part of the "mirror for princes" (andarz)
  tradition; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

III. GOVERNANCE AS LAW (MERCOURIS FRAME)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It seems to me that the crucial point is that the Siyasatnama does
not create a separate legal code—it **prescribes how the sovereign
and his agents should act** so that the realm is governed lawfully.
Legitimacy here is **through order**: the king who follows justice,
maintains the army, and secures information retains the realm; the
one who oppresses or neglects counsel loses it. Institutional
continuity is the vizier as counsellor and the text as transmitted
advice; Ferdowsi gives kingship in epic, Nizam al-Mulk gives it in
administrative law.

> "I shall not die, these seeds I've sown will save my name and
> reputation from the grave, and men of sense and wisdom will proclaim,
> when I have gone, my praises and my fame."
> — Ferdowsi, *Shahnameh* (prologue), tr. Dick Davis; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

Nizam al-Mulk on the duty of the king:

> "The king must never neglect the business of the realm and the
> needs of the subjects. Every day he should give a fixed time to
> hearing complaints and redressing wrongs, so that no one is
> turned away without a hearing and the oppressed may find relief."
> — Nizam al-Mulk, *Siyasatnama* (Book of Government), tr. Darke; subject verbatim

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Seljuk conquest had brought Turkic military power; Persian
bureaucracy supplied administration. The incentive structure favoured
**codified advice** that could bind successors and officials—the
vizier's legacy as text. The Siyasatnama is not legislation but
**governance law**: the rules by which the court and the realm are
to be run. No separation of powers; the sovereign remains above the
text, but the text defines good rule.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jurisdiction: the sovereign and his delegates; the Siyasatnama
assigns **duties**, not rights against the king. Who is on the hook?
The king who neglects justice and the vizier who fails to advise.
Nizam al-Mulk himself was assassinated (1092); the text outlived
him as the template for Persianate governance. Irreversibility: the
mirror-for-princes tradition entered Persian and Islamic political
thought; later constitutions (1906, 1979) would add *limits* on
sovereignty that the Siyasatnama does not.
