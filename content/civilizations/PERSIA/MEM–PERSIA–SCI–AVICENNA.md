I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Ibn Sīnā (Avicenna)** as Persia's **systematiser
of knowledge**: author of the *Canon of Medicine* and the *Book of
Healing*, who integrated Aristotelian philosophy and Galenic medicine
into a framework that dominated Islamic and Latin European thought for
centuries. Born near Bukhara, he served Persianate courts (Hamadan,
Isfahan); his encoding is **legitimacy through completeness**—the
court that housed the philosopher-physician claimed universal order.

For Persian civilizational memory, Avicenna represents **institutional
continuity of rational inquiry** after the Islamic absorption: not
rupture but **synthesis**. The *Canon* became the standard medical
text in both Islam and the West; the *Healing* supplied metaphysics
and natural philosophy. Contradictions preserved.

II. FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Afshana (near Bukhara); studied in Bukhara; access to Samanid library
• Served at courts in Khwarezm, Gorgan, Hamadan, Isfahan (Buyid, Kakuyid)
• *Canon of Medicine* (al-Qānūn fī l-ṭibb); *Book of Healing* (logic,
  physics, metaphysics, soul); *Remarks and Admonitions*
• Died in Hamadan; buried there

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SYNTHESIS AND COMPLETENESS (MERCOURIS FRAME)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The crucial point, surely, is that Avicenna did not reject Greek
authority—he **ordered** it. The *Canon* organised diagnosis,
treatment, and pharmacology into a repeatable system; the *Healing*
presented philosophy as a single architecture. Persianate legitimacy
here is **completeness**: the king's realm could claim to hold the
sum of rational knowledge. Ferdowsi gave kingship in verse; Avicenna
gave it in argument.

> "I shall not die, these seeds I've sown will save my name and
> reputation from the grave, and men of sense and wisdom will proclaim,
> when I have gone, my praises and my fame."
> — Ferdowsi, *Shahnameh* (prologue), tr. Dick Davis; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

Avicenna on the aim of medicine:

> "Medicine is the art that deals with the states of the human body
> when in health and when not in health, for the purpose of
> preserving health when present and restoring it when absent."
> — Ibn Sīnā, *Canon of Medicine*, I.1 (tr. Gruner); subject verbatim

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Court patronage and library access enabled the project. The Buyid and
post-Samanid dynasties competed for prestige through scholars;
Avicenna's synthesis served **institutional and ideological**
consolidation. His work was not weaponised; it was **encyclopaedic**
—a claim to universal order under sovereign protection.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No sovereign-override. Avicenna advised rulers but did not bear
policy liability. His legacy is epistemic and institutional: the
*Canon* and *Healing* entered curricula across civilizations.
Irreversibility: the synthesis became the reference frame.
