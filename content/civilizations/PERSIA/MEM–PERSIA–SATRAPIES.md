I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **satrapal system** from Persia's perspective:
the Achaemenid invention of provincial governance through appointed
governors (satraps, *xšaçapāvan*—"protector of the realm") who held
civil and military authority under royal oversight. Herodotus documents
Darius's formalization—twenty satrapies, fixed tribute, standardized
weights. The system enables **scalable imperial control** across
heterogeneous territories.

Herodotus describes the tribute structure:

> "Darius it was who first fixed the tribute and instructed the peoples
> in the manner of it; and in accordance with his enactment all later
> monarchs have levied tribute. He fixed it so that those who paid in
> silver paid according to the Babylonian talent."  
> — Herodotus, *Histories*, III.89

India exemplifies satrapy diversity—the twentieth province paid in gold dust:

> "The Indians made up the twentieth province. These are more in number than any nation of which we know, and they paid a greater tribute than any other province, namely three hundred and sixty talents of gold dust."
> — Herodotus, *Histories*, III.94 (trans. A.D. Godley)

The satrapy is the empire's modular unit—governance without homogenization.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL FEATURES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Key mechanisms:
• Satrapal appointment by the king
• Civil–military separation (in some periods)
• Fixed tribute; local law preservation
• Royal Road and courier integration
• Co-option of local elites

Lloyd Llewellyn-Jones contrasts Persian with Roman practice:

> "The Persians never did that. They adopted a more *laisser-faire*
> attitude and, as long as the tribute and the taxes came in, then they
> did not interfere with local systems."  
> — Lloyd Llewellyn-Jones, FiveBooks interview (2020)

The empire governs difference as a feature. Control is modular, not uniform.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The satrapal system encodes:
• Scalable administration: empire grows without central overload
• Tolerance as technology: local systems persist under imperial ceiling
• Tribute as bond: payment links periphery to center
• Delegation with oversight: satraps rule; the king retains authority

The system outlives the Achaemenids—Seleucid, Parthian, and Sasanian
governance inherit satrapal patterns.
