I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **al-Rāzī (Rhazes)** as Persia's **empirical
physician**: author of the *Comprehensive Book of Medicine* (al-Ḥāwī),
who differentiated smallpox from measles and stressed observation,
case history, and criticism of authority. Born in Rey (Persia), he
headed hospitals in Rey and Baghdad; his encoding is **legitimacy
through practice**—the physician who records what he sees, not only
what tradition says.

For Persian civilizational memory, Razi represents **pre-Avicenna
peak** of Persian medicine: systematic but case-based, willing to
doubt Galen. His chemistry (alchemy) and philosophical scepticism
round out the profile. Contradictions preserved.

II. FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Rey (Rayy), Persia; studied in Baghdad; returned to Rey
• Head of hospital in Rey; later in Baghdad

  *On Smallpox and Measles*; *Doubts About Galen*; alchemical works
• Blind in later life; died in Rey

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. EMPIRICAL METHOD AND CRITIQUE (MERCOURIS FRAME)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It seems to me that the crucial point is that Razi did not merely
transmit—he **observed** and **disputed**. His smallpox/measles
treatise rested on clinical distinction; his *Doubts About Galen*
claimed the right to correct authority when experience contradicted
it. Persianate legitimacy here is **competence through practice**:
the court physician as one who sees and records. Institutional
continuity is the hospital and the written case.

> "I shall not die, these seeds I've sown will save my name and
> reputation from the grave, and men of sense and wisdom will proclaim,
> when I have gone, my praises and my fame."
> — Ferdowsi, *Shahnameh* (prologue), tr. Dick Davis; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

Razi on the physician's duty:

> "When the physician is skilled and the patient obedient, the
> treatment of disease is easy. The physician must be diligent in
> observation and must record what he sees, for from the accumulation
> of many observations comes the art of medicine."
> — Al-Rāzī (paraphrase of *al-Ḥāwī* tradition); subject-domain verbatim tradition

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Urban hospitals (Rey, Baghdad) under caliphal and provincial patronage
supplied the institutional base. The incentive structure favoured
practical medicine and compilation; Razi's case-based approach served
both teaching and reputation. No weaponisation; **clinical and
pharmacological** legacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No sovereign-override. Razi operated as physician and scholar; his
legacy is epistemic. Irreversibility: the smallpox/measles
differentiation and the empirical stance entered the transmission
stream; Avicenna later systematised much that Razi had compiled.
