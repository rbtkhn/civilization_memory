I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Thermopylae** from Persia's perspective: the
480 BC battle where Xerxes' army, advancing into Greece, overcame a
small Greek force (Spartans, Thespians, others) holding the narrow
pass. For Greece, Thermopylae is Leonidas's last stand—the birth of
Western defiance. For Persia, it is a **tactical victory**—the Greeks
defeated, the pass forced, the advance to Athens continued. The
encoding divergence is stark: Greece celebrates heroic death; Persia
records a successful engagement.

Herodotus is the primary source for both encodings.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. XERXES' INVASION — CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Xerxes inherits the Greek question from Darius. Herodotus records the
scale of the host:

> "The land army was the greatest of all armies we know of, so that it
> was smaller than that of Darius... but it was not much smaller."  
> — Herodotus, *Histories*, VII.20–21

The invasion is massive. Thermopylae is one engagement in a larger
campaign. The Greeks choose the pass to limit Persian numerical
advantage.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. THE BATTLE — PERSIAN VICTORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Leonidas and perhaps 7,000 Greeks hold the pass. Persian frontal
assaults fail—the narrow terrain favors the defenders. A Greek
traitor (Ephialtes) reveals a path around the pass. Persian troops
outflank the Greeks. Leonidas and his 300 Spartans (and Thespians)
stand and die. The Persians force the pass. The Greek fleet withdraws
from Artemisium. Xerxes advances. Athens will be burned.

From the Persian view: objective achieved. The Greeks were defeated;
the road to central Greece is open.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ENCODING DIVERGENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Greece: Thermopylae = heroic sacrifice; "Go tell the Spartans...";
Leonidas as martyr; moral victory in defeat.
Persia: Thermopylae = tactical success; pass forced; advance continues.

Neither encoding is false. They serve different civilizational needs.
Persian memory does not require Thermopylae's absorption—Salamis and
Plataea will end the invasion; the Greek wars will remain unresolved.
But at Thermopylae, Persia wins the battle.
