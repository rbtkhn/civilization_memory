I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **ʿUmar Khayyām** as Persia's **mathematician of
order and time**: solver of cubic equations, architect of the Jalali
calendar (still underlying the Iranian calendar), and poet of the
*Rubaiyat*. Born in Nishapur, he worked under Seljuk patronage
(Samarqand, Isfahan); his encoding is **double**: science as system
(algebra, calendar) and poetry as questioning (ephemerality, wine,
doubt). For Persian civilizational memory, Khayyam represents
**legitimacy through precision**—the calendar that orders the year—
and **legitimacy through voice**—the quatrain that questions it.

Contradictions preserved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Nishapur; studied in Balkh, Samarqand; worked at Seljuk courts
• *Treatise on the Demonstration of Problems of Algebra* (cubic
  equations; geometric solutions); Jalali calendar (1079, under
  Malik-Shah); observatory at Isfahan (with others)
• *Rubaiyat* (quatrains) attributed to him; sceptical, existential
  tone; wine and transience
• Died in Nishapur; buried there

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ORDER OF TIME AND VOICE (MERCOURIS FRAME)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The crucial point, surely, is that Khayyam gave Persia both **system**
(calendar) and **question** (poetry). The Jalali reform fixed the
solar year for civil and religious use; the *Rubaiyat*, whether or
not every line is his, encode a voice that doubts permanence. Persianate
legitimacy here is dual: the court that orders time and the poet who
asks what end it serves. Ferdowsi gave kingship; Khayyam gave algebra
and the quatrain.

> "I shall not die, these seeds I've sown will save my name and
> reputation from the grave, and men of sense and wisdom will proclaim,
> when I have gone, my praises and my fame."
> — Ferdowsi, *Shahnameh* (prologue), tr. Dick Davis; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

Khayyam (attributed) on time and certainty:

> "The moving finger writes; and, having writ, moves on: nor all thy
> piety nor wit shall lure it back to cancel half a line, nor all thy
> tears wash out a word of it."
> — *Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam*, tr. FitzGerald; subject-domain verbatim

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Seljuk patronage (Malik-Shah, Nizam al-Mulk) supplied observatory and
calendar project. The incentive structure favoured **practical
astronomy** (fiscal, ritual) and **court culture** (poetry). Khayyam's
mathematics was not weaponised; it was **ordering and commemorative**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No sovereign-override. Khayyam advised on calendar and astronomy;
his legacy is epistemic and literary. Irreversibility: the Jalali
calendar persisted; the *Rubaiyat* entered world literature.
