Russia

  • Russian FM (28 Feb): Washington and Tel Aviv “plunging the Middle East into the abyss”; “preplanned and unprovoked act of armed aggression”; demanded immediate halt and return to diplomacy.
  • Putin (1 Mar, message to Pezeshkian): Khamenei’s killing “a murder committed in cynical violation of all norms of human morality and international law.” Khamenei “an outstanding statesman who made a huge personal contribution to the development of friendly Russian-Iranian relations.”

China

  • Wang Yi (1 Mar, call with Lavrov): "China's position on the situation in Iran is as follows: an immediate cessation of military operations, a prompt return to dialogue and negotiations, and a joint opposition to unilateral actions."
  • FM spokesperson (1 Mar): Attack “a grave violation of Iran's sovereignty and security” and “tramples on the purposes and principles of the UN Charter.”

Israel

  • Netanyahu (1 Mar, IDF headquarters): Joint U.S.–Israel campaign (“Operation Lion’s Roar” / “Operation Roaring Lion”); “far more powerful” than June 2025. "Our forces are now striking at the heart of Tehran with increasing intensity, and this will only intensify further in the coming days." Israeli forces had “eliminated the dictator Ali Khamenei” and dozens of senior officials. “Once-in-a-generation opportunity” to topple the government; “strike the terrorist regime right in the face.”

Oman (mediator)

  • Badr al-Busaidi (Omani FM, mediator of US–Iran nuclear talks): Hours before strikes (28 Feb, CBS “Face the Nation”): "A peace deal is within our reach." "I don't think any alternative to diplomacy is going to solve this problem." Cited breakthrough: Iran would “never, ever have a nuclear material that will create a bomb” via “zero stockpiling” of enriched material — “something that is not in the old deal.”
  • Al-Busaidi (1 Mar, X, after strikes began): "I am dismayed. Active and serious negotiations have yet again been undermined... I urge the United States not to get sucked in further. This is not your war." Same day (X): “The door to diplomacy remains open”; Geneva talks “achieved genuine progress.” "The sooner talks are resumed the better it is for everyone." (Oman had urged ceasefire on call with Iran’s Araghchi that day — NAMPA.)
