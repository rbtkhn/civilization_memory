I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **al-Khwārizmī** as Persia's **founder of
algorithmic thought**: author of *al-Kitāb al-mukhtaṣar fī ḥisāb
al-jabr wa-l-muqābala* (The Compendious Book on Calculation by
Completion and Balancing), from which "algebra" derives, and of
works on Hindu–Arabic numerals and astronomical tables. Born in
Khwarezm (Persianate Central Asia), he worked in Baghdad under the
Abbasid caliphate; his encoding is **legitimacy through procedure**—
the rule that can be followed step by step, the name that became
"algorithm."

For Persian civilizational memory, al-Khwarizmi represents **Persianate
contribution to Abbasid science**: geographic and cultural origin in
Khwarezm; institutional base in Baghdad. The civilizational function
is **system and transmission**—algebra and place-value arithmetic
entered Islam and then Latin Europe. Contradictions preserved.

III. PROCEDURE AND SYSTEM (MERCOURIS FRAME)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The crucial point, surely, is that al-Khwarizmi did not only compute—
he **prescribed**. The *Algebra* gave rules for reducing equations to
canonical form; the *Hindu Reckoning* gave procedures for calculation.
Persianate legitimacy here is **repeatability**: the court scholar
produced methods that others could follow. The name "algorithm"
(latinised from "al-Khwarizmi") encodes that function—the person
became the procedure.

> "I shall not die, these seeds I've sown will save my name and
> reputation from the grave, and men of sense and wisdom will proclaim,
> when I have gone, my praises and my fame."
> — Ferdowsi, *Shahnameh* (prologue), tr. Dick Davis; ARC–PERSIA-T-MEDIEVAL

Al-Khwarizmi on the aim of his *Algebra*:

> "What is easiest and most useful in arithmetic, such as men constantly
> require in cases of inheritance, legacies, partition, lawsuits, and
> trade, and in all their dealings with one another, or where the
> measuring of lands, the digging of canals, geometrical computations,
> and other objects of various kinds and sorts are concerned."
> — Al-Khwārizmī, *Algebra*, tr. Rosen; subject verbatim

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL FRAME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Abbasid patronage (House of Wisdom) supplied the institutional base;
Hindu and Greek sources supplied the raw material. The incentive
structure favoured **practical and fiscal** utility: inheritance,
land measurement, calendar. No weaponisation; **epistemic** legacy:
algebra and algorithm entered global vocabulary.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No sovereign-override. Al-Khwarizmi operated as scholar under
caliphal patronage; his legacy is epistemic. Irreversibility: the
*Algebra* was translated into Latin and shaped European mathematics;
the name "algorithm" is irreversible.
