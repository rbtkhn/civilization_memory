I. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file is the **exclusive registry of Persia civilizational axioms**, derived from CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.

It contains:
• Axioms extracted from CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I (Prime Axioms), in standard format
• No learning history, no beliefs, no provisional or rejected axioms (see Section III)

This file does NOT learn, does NOT synthesize, does NOT evolve autonomously. It extracts and records.

**Axiom vs doctrine:** Axioms are interpretive anchors and first-principles (identity, precedence). Doctrines are pattern-constraints with definitions, operational meaning, and hard constraints (CIV–DOCTRINE–PERSIA).

Authority Flow:
CIV–MEM–CORE → CIV–CORE–PERSIA → (extraction) → CIV–AXIOM–PERSIA

Only axioms listed here may be cited as **authoritative axiom** by CIV–CORE engines. CORE Section I remains the source for the wording; this file is the canonical registry.

II. AXIOM REGISTRY (AUTHORITATIVE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The following axioms are derived from CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I. Each is an extraction into standard format.

AXIOM PERSIA-001: Sovereignty as sacred computational law.
  Statement: Autonomy is absolute across territory, data, compute, encryption, AI, industrial command, settlement rails, and logistics routing. Dependency equals suffocation.
  Scope: All sovereignty, dependency, and autonomy analysis.
  Limits: Does not prescribe specific policy; encodes non-negotiable frame.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.1 (Sovereignty as Sacred Computational Law).
  Status: LOCKED in CORE.

AXIOM PERSIA-002: Plateau–fortress to cyber–plateau.
  Statement: The Iranian Plateau now includes synthetic terrain: sanctions-resilient clouds, dark fiber, encrypted logistics, autonomous energy grids, and compute sovereignty. Geography and computation form one defensive organism.
  Scope: Spatial-compute geography, fortress integrity, and siege infrastructure analysis.
  Limits: Does not deny non-compute geography; encodes fusion of physical and synthetic terrain.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.2 (Plateau–Fortress → Cyber–Plateau Civilization).

AXIOM PERSIA-003: Continuity through compression.
  Statement: Persia does not recover from shocks; it densifies. Every rupture is converted into doctrine, governance architecture, and strategic memory. Loss becomes design.
  Scope: Legitimacy, institutional continuity, and post-shock adaptation analysis.
  Limits: Does not guarantee successful densification; encodes mode of response.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.3 (Continuity Through Compression).

AXIOM PERSIA-004: Structural suspicion operating system.
  Statement: External actors are assumed to run encirclement algorithms. Trust is never default; deterrence precedes diplomacy.
  Scope: External-actor assessment, diplomacy, and deterrence analysis.
  Limits: Does not forbid diplomacy; encodes default posture. Diplomacy is hedged (e.g. backchannel + public denial); deterrence precedes settlement.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.4 (Structural Suspicion Operating System).

AXIOM PERSIA-005: Dignity–patience–compute trinity.
  Statement: Patience governs tempo, compute governs endurance, dignity governs legitimacy. Forced trade-offs among the three create instability risk.
  Scope: Legitimacy accounting, tempo, and strategic trade-off analysis, including settlement and negotiation framing.
  Limits: Does not rank the three; encodes trinity and instability condition.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.5 (Dignity–Patience–Compute Trinity).
  Status: HARD CONSTRAINT in CORE.

AXIOM PERSIA-006: Asymmetry as civilizational doctrine.
  Statement: Expansion is disfavored; cost-imposition, deniability, swarm logic, and multi-domain friction dominate.
  Scope: Military-strategic doctrine, power projection, and denial analysis.
  Limits: Does not forbid all expansion; encodes default and dominant mode.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.6 (Asymmetry as Civilizational Doctrine).

AXIOM PERSIA-007: Shi'a memory engine.
  Statement: Martyrdom → endurance → legitimacy → sovereignty recursion. Memory functions as operational software.
  Scope: Legitimacy, narrative governance, and Shi'a identity analysis.
  Limits: Does not define theological content; encodes recursive function.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.7 (Shi'a Memory Engine).

AXIOM PERSIA-008: Survival as primary legitimacy metric.
  Statement: Prosperity is optional; sovereignty and endurance are mandatory.
  Scope: Legitimacy accounting and regime stability analysis, including under sustained military pressure.
  Limits: Does not forbid prosperity; encodes priority order.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–PERSIA Section I.8 (Survival as Primary Legitimacy Metric).
