I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Francia** as the civilizational and territorial
precursor to France: the Frankish kingdom(s) that emerged from the
dissolution of Roman Gaul and that, through partition and recentring,
became the polity we call France.

For France, Francia encodes **continuity of name and dynastic
legitimacy** without implying a fixed territory or a single unbroken
state. It records what the term denoted—the land of the Franks—how
West Frankish and Capetian structures inherited it, and how the
modern French state claims descent from it.

This file does not adjudicate whether France is the "true" heir of
Francia or whether the Île-de-France recentring was inevitable. It
records the transmission of the name and the structural conditions
of that transmission.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FRANCIA — NAME AND SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Francia** (land of the Franks) emerged in the wake of Roman
withdrawal from Gaul. The Merovingian kingdom united Frankish
conquest under Clovis; the name *Francia* applied first to the
Frankish heartland and then to the broader regnum. Einhard anchors
the Carolingian claim:

> "The Franks, from whom the French (Franci) take their name, are said
> to have come originally from Pannonia and to have settled on the
> banks of the Rhine."
> — Einhard, *Vita Karoli Magni*, ch. 1 (ERC-PRIMARY)

After Charlemagne, the **Treaty of Verdun (843)** split the empire.
**West Francia** (Francia occidentalis) became the territorial base
for the kingdom that would later be called France. The name *Francia*
narrowed in practice to the West Frankish realm; the East Frankish
realm became the basis for Germania/Germany.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. WEST FRANKISH KINGDOM AND CAPETIAN RECENTRING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
West Francia was not yet "France." It was a regnum of principalities,
duchies, and counties under a weak crown. Hugh Capet’s election (987)
recentred royal authority in the **Île-de-France** and began the
Capetian dynasty. The term *rex Francorum* (king of the Franks)
persisted; the *regnum Franciae* (kingdom of Francia) gradually
came to denote the territory under Capetian influence.

> "The Capetians did not create France out of nothing; they inherited
> the title of king of the Franks and the prestige of the Carolingian
> legacy, and they built from a narrow base in the Île-de-France."
> — Robert Bartlett, *The Making of Europe* (ERC-SECONDARY)

Structural constraint: **fragmentation** (feudal principalities, Aquitaine,
Burgundy, Normandy) meant that "Francia" as a unified territorial state
was a long project. The birth of "France" as a recognizably centralized
kingdom is a process, not a single date—often dated to the consolidation
under Philip II Augustus or the end of the Hundred Years’ War.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FROM FRANCIA TO FRANCE — KEY STEPS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Transmission path:

• **Name:** *Francia* (Latin) → *France* (vernacular). The regnum
  Franciae became the kingdom of France; the people *Franci* (Franks)
  gave way to *Français* (French) as the kingdom’s identity expanded
  beyond ethnic Frankishness.

• **Institutional continuity:** Unlike the Holy Roman Empire, the
  French crown **did not dissolve**. Capetian direct line continued
  until 1328; Valois and Bourbon succeeded. The state that emerged
  was the same legal entity—the kingdom of France—claiming descent
  from Clovis and Charlemagne.

• **Territorial crystallization:** The "hexagon" and the language of
  *France* as a territorial nation-state emerged through conquest,
  marriage, and royal centralization. Francia as West Frankish kingdom
  is the **direct institutional ancestor** of the French state.

The birth of "France" is thus the **territorial and identitarian
completion** of the kingdom of Francia, not the creation of a new
polity from scratch.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. TENSION — CONTINUITY VERSUS RECENTRING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia encodes a tension: **dynastic and nominal continuity** versus
**geographic and ethnic recentring**. The Capetians ruled from Paris,
not Aachen or Reims alone; "France" came to mean the Île-de-France–
centred kingdom that absorbed Occitania, Burgundy, and Brittany. The
memory of Francia supports both "we are the kingdom of the Franks" and
"we became France when the crown recentred and the realm unified."
Francia is the name of the precursor; France is the name of the
completed state.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. STRUCTURAL CONSTRAINTS (MEARSHEIMER LAYER)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
West Francia’s geography—navigable rivers, Paris basin, access to the
Channel and the Mediterranean—allowed a **core region** to project
power and absorb peripheries over time. Unlike Central Europe, where
multiple centres persisted (HRE), the Capetian core had a structural
advantage: concentration of resources and gradual expansion of royal
domain. Francia did not "become" France by name alone; structural
factors (defeat of Plantagenet claims, consolidation of royal domain,
elimination of internal rivals) made the kingdom of France the dominant
polity in the space once called West Francia.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Jurisdiction:** The transition from Francia to France had no single
legal moment. Succession passed by election (987) and then by dynastic
rule; the crown bore the authority to speak for the regnum. Liability
for the **shape** of the realm—which territories were absorbed, which
princes submitted—rested with the crown and the great vassals who
allied or resisted.

**Irreversibility:** Once the Capetian–Valois–Bourbon kingdom had
identified itself as the kingdom of France and the realm had
territorialized around the hexagon, the earlier, more fragmented
Francia could not be restored. The name "Francia" passed into
historical usage; "France" became the sovereign and cultural carrier.
Who bore the cost—regional elites, Occitan identity, Burgundian
independence—is preserved in other MEMs (e.g. MEM–FRANCE–DYNASTY–CAPET,
MEM–FRANCE–AQUITAINE).

IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia was the land of the Franks; West Francia the post-Verdun
  West Frankish kingdom.
• Capetian recentring in Île-de-France made "France" territorial and
  royal.
• The French state claims direct descent from the kingdom of Francia;
  no dissolution, only dynastic and territorial evolution.
• Continuity of crown and name; discontinuity of territory and
  identity (from Franks to French, from regnum to hexagon).
