────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Sweden** as Francia’s northern lever:
a proxy power employed to fracture continental hegemony, offset
Habsburg pressure, and project influence without direct annexation.

For Francia, Sweden is **instrument**, not mirror.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT AGAINST HABSBURG POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia approaches Sweden through balance logic.

Cardinal Richelieu defines alliance as method:

> “The principal end of policy is to prevent any power from becoming
> so great that it may oppress the liberty of others; for this cause,
> we must assist those who, though distant, strike at the same enemy,
> since the blow delivered far away may save us near at hand.”  
> — Cardinal Richelieu, *Testament Politique*, c. 1640

Distance becomes advantage.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SUBSIDY WARFARE & INDIRECT CONTROL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia funds, Sweden fights.

Mechanism:
• Subsidies
• Diplomatic cover
• Limited coordination

Universalism is advanced indirectly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SWEDEN AS LEGITIMACY MULTIPLIER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Swedish victories validate Francia’s balancing narrative.

The Peace of Westphalia confirms the logic:

> “That the peace now concluded shall be a perpetual law and firm
> settlement of the Empire, whereby all states may live in security,
> none dominating another, but each preserved in its rights.”  
> — Peace of Westphalia, General Settlement Clause, 1648

Francia claims architect status without northern exposure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIMITS OF PROXY DEPENDENAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Proxy power is unstable.

Risks:
• Divergent aims
• Swedish autonomy
• Northern fatigue

Control is never complete.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. POST-WESTPHALIAN FADE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As Sweden declines, Francia pivots.

Northern leverage yields to:
• Direct continental strategy
• Maritime competition
• Southern focus

Sweden becomes memory, not tool.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sweden encodes **proxy power effectiveness and limits**.

Lessons:
• Distance reduces cost but dilutes control.
• Subsidies can substitute for armies—temporarily.
• Balance requires external disciplinarians.
• Instruments acquire their own will.

Sweden teaches Francia indirection.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia prefers leverage over occupation.
• Proxy warfare extends reach without exposure.
• Balance rhetoric legitimizes intervention.
• Instruments decay as conditions change.
• Northern tools are episodic, not permanent.

Tension preserved without verdict.
