I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Iberia as **Francia’s southern mirror and constraint
zone**: the neighboring civilizational space where Francia repeatedly
encountered alternative models of empire, faith, and maritime expansion,
shaping both rivalry and limitation.

Iberia is not Francia’s frontier.
It is Francia’s **comparative foil**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & CIVILIZATIONAL CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberia occupies a hinge position.

Features:
• Mediterranean–Atlantic junction
• Mountain barriers (Pyrenees)
• Early access to oceanic routes
• Proximity without permeability

The Pyrenees separate cultures more than distance would suggest.

Geography limits absorption.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MEDIEVAL DIVERGENAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early trajectories diverge sharply.

Contrasts:
• Francia: feudal fragmentation → centralized monarchy
• Iberia: Reconquista → sacralized imperial mission

Religion becomes constitutive in Iberia.
Administration becomes constitutive in Francia.

Two models of legitimacy crystallize.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EARLY MODERN RIVALRY & DISPLACEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberia moves first into global empire.

Dynamics:
• Spanish and Portuguese Atlantic primacy
• Silver-based imperial finance
• Missionary universalism

Francia arrives later and differently:
• Cultural projection
• Administrative transplantation
• Continental priority retained

Iberian speed contrasts with French depth.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NAPOLEONIC COLLISION & LIMIT EXPOSURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon’s Iberian intervention proves disastrous.

Consequences:
• Guerrilla resistance
• British (Anglian) intervention
• Prolonged attrition
• Prestige erosion

Iberia exposes the limits of Francia’s
administrative–military logic.

Control fails without legitimacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN REALIGNMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-imperial Iberia stabilizes externally.

Patterns:
• Reduced great-power ambition
• Internal modernization
• Peripheral role in European balance

For Francia:
• Southern flank neutralized
• Rivalry replaced by coexistence

Constraint becomes accepted.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberia encodes **the danger of misapplied universalism**.

Lessons:
• Geography blocks seamless expansion.
• Religious legitimacy differs from administrative legitimacy.
• Early imperial success can harden future weakness.
• Francia’s power dissipates when legitimacy is not shared.

Iberia marks Francia’s southern limit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The Pyrenees are a civilizational wall.
• Iberia reveals the cost of forced integration.
• Francia misreads Iberia as extension, not other.
• Guerrilla resistance defeats administrative logic.
• Iberia teaches Francia where authority cannot travel.

Tension preserved without verdict.
