I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Jacobins as **Francia’s revolutionary
universalist engine**: the faction that operationalized abstract
sovereignty into centralized authority, moral discipline, and mass
mobilization.

The Jacobins are universalism in motion.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATION & IDEOLOGICAL CORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Jacobins arise as a political society.

Foundations:
• Enlightenment rationalism
• Rousseauian popular sovereignty
• Moral equality absolutism
• Suspicion of pluralism

Politics is treated as ethical purification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CENTRALIZATION OF THE REVOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Jacobins impose coherence.

Mechanisms:
• Party discipline
• Control of clubs and committees
• Narrative dominance
• Identification of enemies

Plural revolution is rendered singular.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MASS POLITICS & MOBILIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legitimacy is claimed through the people.

Dynamics:
• Street politics
• Sans-culotte alignment
• Pressure-driven decision-making
• Performative virtue signaling

Participation substitutes for procedure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. HOSTILITY TO MODERATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Constraint is interpreted as betrayal.

Targets:
• Constitutional monarchists
• Girondins
• Procedural reformers

Moderation has no moral standing.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. TRANSITION FROM IDEOLOGY TO RULE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Jacobins become governors.

Effects:
• Ideology fused with state power
• Moral certainty weaponized
• Emergency rule normalized

Universalism becomes administrative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FAILURE OF SUSTAINABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jacobins cannot stabilize legitimacy.

Limits:
• Dependence on fear
• Inability to tolerate dissent
• Internal factional collapse

Moral authority proves non-renewable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL LEGACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Prototype of ideological party rule
• Template for revolutionary purification logic
• Long shadow over modern mass politics

The Jacobins define the danger of moralized sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Ideology accelerates governance but shortens lifespan.
• Moral certainty eliminates pluralism.
• Parties can replace institutions—but not sustain them.
• Universalism demands enforcement.
• The Jacobins are Francia’s revolutionary engine—and warning.

Tension preserved without verdict.
