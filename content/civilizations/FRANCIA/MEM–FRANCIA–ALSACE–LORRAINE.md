────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Alsace–Lorraine as the **territorial trauma core**
of modern French strategic memory: a borderland where defeat,
identity fracture, and legitimacy loss condensed into a permanent scar.

Alsace–Lorraine is not remembered as land alone.
It is remembered as **humiliation made geographic**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. TERRITORIAL & CULTURAL PALIMPSEST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Alsace–Lorraine is structurally liminal.

Characteristics:
• Linguistic hybridity (Germanic dialects + French administration)
• Religious duality (Catholic–Protestant balance)
• Legal exceptionalism (local law regimes)
• Borderland social ambiguity

The region never fully dissolves into a single civilizational grammar.

Memory axiom:
→ Borderlands absorb pressure before cores fracture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. 1871 ANNEXATION & NATIONAL TRAUMA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Treaty of Frankfurt (1871) forcibly transfers Alsace–Lorraine
to the newly unified German Empire.

Key features:
• Annexation without plebiscite
• Civilian displacement and forced allegiance choices
• National humiliation layered onto military defeat

Foundational wound:
> “La perte de l’Alsace-Lorraine.”

This loss becomes **the symbol of national diminishment**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. REVANCHISM AS STRATEGIC PSYCHOLOGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-1871 France internalizes revanchism not as policy, but as identity.

Manifestations:
• Educational indoctrination (maps, textbooks, civic rituals)
• Military planning centered on recovery scenarios
• Cultural production framing the region as “violated France”
• Silence in diplomacy masking long-term fixation

Revanchism becomes **latent doctrine**:
Unspoken, persistent, generational.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LEGITIMACY DISPLACEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The loss of Alsace–Lorraine delegitimizes multiple French regimes:

• Second Empire → collapses
• Third Republic → inherits trauma without resolution
• Military authority → mistrusted yet empowered
• Civil institutions → burdened with deferred redemption

Strategic paradox:
→ The Republic must defend legitimacy it did not create
   against a humiliation it did not choose.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. WORLD WAR I & CONDITIONAL RESTORATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The recovery of Alsace–Lorraine in 1918 is framed as redemption.

Yet restoration is incomplete:
• Demographic disruption remains
• Cultural hybridity persists
• Border anxiety is unresolved
• Victory feels earned but fragile

Recovery does not erase the trauma.
It **reframes it as conditional**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. 1940 REOCCUPATION & TRAUMA REACTIVATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
German reoccupation (1940–1944) reopens the scar.

Effects:
• Forced Germanization
• Conscription of locals
• Suspicion of loyalty
• Post-war ambiguity and silence

The region becomes a **recurring diagnostic site**:
When France collapses, Alsace–Lorraine falls first.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. POST-1945 ABSORPTION WITHOUT ERASURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1945, France integrates Alsace–Lorraine fully—
but preserves exceptional legal and cultural regimes.

This reflects implicit recognition:
• The scar cannot be removed
• Only managed
• Only normalized, never forgotten

Border trauma is domesticated, not healed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Territorial loss can outlive regimes.
• Revanchism may remain silent yet decisive.
• Borderlands convert defeat into identity memory.
• Restoration does not reverse humiliation; it conditions it.
• Francia’s defensive rigidity and alliance dependency trace
  directly to this wound.
• Alsace–Lorraine is Francia’s strategic scar tissue.

Tension preserved without verdict.
