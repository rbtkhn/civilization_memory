────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Haiti as **Francia’s revolutionary inversion**:
the moment when universalist rhetoric escaped elite control and was
applied literally by the enslaved, destroying imperial authority and
exposing the racial limits of Enlightenment ideals.

Haiti is not a colonial loss.
It is universalism turned against its author.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SAINT-DOMINGUE AS IMPERIAL CORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Before revolution, Saint-Domingue is Francia’s richest colony.

Characteristics:
• Plantation economy
• Enslaved majority
• Extreme wealth extraction
• Racialized legal order

Universalist language coexists with absolute domination.

The contradiction is total.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. REVOLUTIONARY LANGUAGE ESCAPES CONTROL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1789 unleashes ideological contagion.

Dynamics:
• Rights of Man proclaimed
• Free people of color mobilize
• Enslaved population interprets equality literally

Universalism loses its gatekeepers.

Words become weapons.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SLAVE REVOLUTION & LEGITIMACY REVERSAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The enslaved seize revolutionary authority.

Outcomes:
• Collapse of plantation order
• Military defeat of imperial forces
• Assertion of Black sovereignty

Authority inverts.

The excluded become founders.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NAPOLEONIC FAILURE & RACIAL LIMIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon attempts reconquest.

Results:
• Military disaster
• Yellow fever devastation
• Moral contradiction exposed

Universalism proves conditional.

Equality stops at race.

The empire loses decisively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. ISOLATION & PUNITIVE AFTERMATH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia responds through exclusion.

Actions:
• Diplomatic isolation
• Indemnity extraction
• Economic strangulation

Haiti is punished for consistency.

Universalism survives rhetorically—but not practically.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Haiti reshapes Francia’s trajectory.

Effects:
• Retreat from radical universalism
• Reinforcement of racial hierarchy elsewhere
• Heightened fear of colonial revolt

Revolution is domesticated after Haiti.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Haiti encodes **the cost of literal equality**.

Lessons:
• Universal language cannot be selectively applied.
• Slavery is incompatible with Enlightenment legitimacy.
• Revolution is uncontrollable once moral claims globalize.
• Empire fractures when ideals exceed hierarchy.

Haiti is Francia’s unanswerable proof.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Haiti exposes the racial boundary of universalism.
• Enslaved populations can become revolutionary subjects.
• Ideals travel faster than control.
• Punishment replaces reconciliation after defeat.
• Haiti haunts Francia’s revolutionary legacy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
