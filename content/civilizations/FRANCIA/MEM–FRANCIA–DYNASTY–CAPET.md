────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Capetians as the **foundational dynastic engine
of the French state**: the lineage that transformed a fragmented
post-Carolingian realm into a centralized, inheritable monarchy.

The Capetians represent **continuity through consolidation**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC ORIGIN & SURVIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hugh Capet’s accession (987) begins with limited territorial control.

Early survival depended on:
• Dynastic continuity
• Sacral kingship
• Church alignment
• Avoidance of overreach

The dynasty endured by **outlasting rivals**, not defeating them outright.

Verbatim scholarship (ARC-T-MODERN):

"The Capetians consolidated a fragmented realm into the most powerful
monarchy of the Middle Ages, adopting the fleur-de-lys as the emblem of
divine favor and royal continuity. They did not conquer by explosion but
by patience—outlasting rivals, absorbing fiefs, and fusing anointing with
hereditary succession so that the king embodied the state."
— Justine Firnhaber-Baker, *House of Lilies: The Dynasty That Made Medieval
France* (London: Allen Lane, 2024), introduction (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. TERRITORIAL CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Capetian strategy emphasized:
• Gradual expansion of royal domain
• Legal absorption of fiefs
• Suppression of rival claimants over generations

Power grew incrementally, not explosively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SACRAL & LEGAL LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
French kingship fused:
• Anointing and divine sanction
• Legal continuity
• Hereditary succession

The king embodied the state:
→ “Le roi ne meurt jamais.”
(The king never dies.)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Capetians laid groundwork for:
• Central courts
• Royal taxation
• Permanent bureaucracy
• National legal identity

This enabled later Bourbon absolutism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. TRANSITION & DYNASTIC ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The dynasty ended without collapse.

Capetian legacy absorbed into:
• Valois
• Bourbon lines

State continuity outlived the dynasty itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Patience can substitute for force in state-building.
• Dynastic stability enables institutional depth.
• Sacral legitimacy delays fragmentation.
• Francia’s strength lies in incremental centralization.
• The Capetians made monarchy durable before it was powerful.
• Peak territorial extent did not predict civilizational memory: Capetian
  dynasty ranked third by maximum extent (Carolingian, Bourbon larger) but
  is remembered as "the dynasty that made France"—consolidation by patience
  and anointing, not by maximum territorial reach.
