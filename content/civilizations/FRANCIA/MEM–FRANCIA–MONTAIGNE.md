I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Montaigne as **Francia’s skeptical humanist
stabilizer**: the thinker who confronted civilizational fracture not by
rebuilding certainty, but by cultivating restraint, self-knowledge,
and tolerance in the absence of reliable authority.

Montaigne does not reconstruct truth.
He **teaches how to live without it**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATIVE CONTEXT: CIVIL WAR & CONFESSIONAL COLLAPSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Montaigne writes amid the French Wars of Religion.

Context:
• Catholic–Protestant violence
• Breakdown of moral consensus
• Collapse of trust in institutions
• Personal exposure to political instability

The problem is not ignorance.
It is **fanatic certainty**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ESSAYS AS METHOD: THE SELF AS MEASURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Montaigne invents a new literary–philosophical form.

Method:
• Introspection
• Anecdote
• Classical reference
• Radical intellectual humility

Core posture:
> Que sais-je? (What do I know?)

Knowledge is provisional.
Judgment must be restrained.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SKEPTICISM AS CIVILIZATIONAL DEFENSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Montaigne’s skepticism is protective, not corrosive.

Functions:
• Limits ideological absolutism
• Reduces moral violence
• Preserves coexistence amid disagreement

Unlike Descartes:
• Montaigne does not seek certainty
• He seeks survivability

Skepticism becomes a peace mechanism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO AUTHORITY & TRADITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Montaigne neither defends nor destroys authority.

Positions:
• Accepts custom as stabilizer
• Distrusts abstract reform
• Prefers gradualism to rupture

Tradition is flawed—
but preferable to ideological purification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MONTaIGNE VS DESCARTES (STRUCTURAL CONTRAST)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Montaigne: doubt to limit action
• Descartes: doubt to rebuild certainty

Montaigne cools conflict.
Descartes accelerates system-building.

One dampens revolution.
The other enables it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL EFFECT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Montaigne’s legacy is ethical, not institutional.

Outcomes:
• Humanist moderation tradition
• Influence on liberal tolerance
• Resistance to ideological totalization

Yet:
• His method does not scale to governance
• His restraint is overwhelmed by abstraction

He is remembered—but not followed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Skepticism can preserve life without producing systems.
• Certainty kills faster than ignorance.
• Introspection is a brake on fanaticism.
• Montaigne offers survival without dominance.
• Francia hears Montaigne—but later chooses Descartes.

Tension preserved without verdict.
