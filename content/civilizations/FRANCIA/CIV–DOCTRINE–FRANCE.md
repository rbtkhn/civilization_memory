I. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY

This file is the **exclusive registry of doctrines that guide and
govern the actions of the French state**.

and do not "follow" doctrine. The state (France) is the actor that
is guided by these doctrines. The doctrines are derived from the
FRANCIA civilizational corpus (CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA, MEMs).

It contains:
• Doctrines explicitly frozen in CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA

• No learning chronology

II. DOCTRINE REGISTRY (AUTHORITATIVE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DOCTRINE 01
Name: DECLARATIVE LEGITIMACY
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA (SYNTHESIS 0001)

Definition:
Francia generates authority through articulated universal claims,
law, symbolism, and moral framing rather than procedural endurance
or administrative patience.

Operational Meaning:
• Legitimacy is asserted before it is enforced
• Law and rhetoric precede capacity
• Authority flows from declaration, not survival

Hard Constraints:
• Declarative legitimacy collapses if enforcement lags symbolism
• Moral claims without capacity accelerate loss of authority
• Narrative strength cannot substitute for material control

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DOCTRINE 02
Name: UNIVERSALISM–CAPACITY DIVERGENCE
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA (SYNTHESIS 0002)

Definition:
When Francia's universal claims exceed its material, fiscal,
or military capacity, legitimacy decays faster than territory
or institutions.

Operational Meaning:
• Universalism creates escalation pressure
• Capacity shortfalls expose moral claims
• Decline is reputational before territorial

Hard Constraints:
• Overextension produces elite fracture
• Capacity gaps convert victory into exhaustion
• Universal missions require disproportionate enforcement

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DOCTRINE 03
Name: RUPTURE AS RENEWAL
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA (SYNTHESIS 0001 / 0003)

Definition:
Francia treats regime collapse, revolution, or systemic rupture
as legitimate corrective mechanisms for restoring moral coherence
and civilizational purpose.

Operational Meaning:
• Collapse is reframed as purification
• Defeat enables ideological reset
• Continuity is moral, not institutional

Hard Constraints:
• Repeated rupture degrades institutional memory
• Reset culture undermines long-term endurance
• Renewal without consolidation invites recurrence

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DOCTRINE 04
Name: POST-HEGEMONIC STRATEGIC ANXIETY
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA (SYNTHESIS 0003)

Definition:
After hegemonic defeat, Francia internalizes loss as civilizational
memory, producing defensive strategy, alliance dependence, and
structural rigidity masked by universalist rhetoric.

Operational Meaning:
• Strategy becomes loss-avoidant
• Alliances substitute for sovereignty
• Past defeat governs present posture

Hard Constraints:
• Anxiety hardens doctrine
• Rigidity replaces adaptation
• Universal language persists without hegemonic capacity

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DOCTRINE 05
Name: LEGITIMACY–CAPACITY OSCILLATOR
Status: ACCEPTED · LOCKED · CANONICAL
Source: CIV–SCHOLAR–FRANCIA (SYNTHESIS 0006)

Definition:
Francia exhibits a recurrent oscillation between asserted legitimacy
and enforceable capacity, in which divergence is periodically resolved
through external war, defeat, or regime reset rather than gradual
institutional correction.

Operational Meaning:
• War is used as legitimacy correction
• Victory restores credibility; defeat enables reset
• Conflict frequency rises independently of necessity

Hard Constraints:
• Oscillation increases cumulative war exposure
• Externalization substitutes for internal reform
• Repeated cycles erode long-term strategic patience
