AXIOM FRANCIA-005: Declarative authority binds only the destinationless.
Derivation: ENTRY 0011 (cross-civ Austerlitz), SYNTHESIS 0005, RLL–FRANCIA–0009

Full Statement:
Francia's declarative legitimacy—the power to constitute authority through
proclamation, law, and symbol—operates as binding constraint only upon
those who lack alternative legitimacy destinations. When extraction targets,
subordinate commanders, or satellite states acquire access to competing
legitimacy structures, declarative condemnation loses compulsory force.

Scope: All analysis of Francia's hegemonic or imperial extraction systems
Limits:
• Does not apply to domestic politics (alternative destinations typically absent)
• Does not apply when coercive capacity closes legitimacy destinations (untested)
• May not apply to non-extraction relationships (alliances of equals)

Relationship to AXIOM-001: AXIOM-005 is limit condition on AXIOM-001.
AXIOM-001 states the mechanism; AXIOM-005 states when the mechanism fails.

Test Cases:
• Bernadotte (destination: Sweden) → declaration ineffective
• Ney (no destination) → declaration bound him
• Bavaria (destination: Austrian protection) → declaration ineffective
• Westphalia (no destination: Napoleonic creation) → collapsed with system

ENTRY 0011
Source:
• MEM–RUSSIA–WAR–NAPOLEON–AUSTERLITZ v2.1 (cross-civilizational)
• MEM–GERMANIA–WAR–NAPOLEON (cross-civilizational, referenced)
• SYNTHESIS 0005 (internal derivation)

Ingest Type:
• Cross-Civilizational Divergence Learning

Primary Belief Extracted:
• The same event (Austerlitz 1805) produces opposite civilizational
  memory functions: for the victor, validation of existing grammar;
  for the defeated, elimination of alternatives reframed as wisdom.
  Francia encodes Austerlitz as declarative legitimacy in action—
  Napoleon's concentration defeating coalition aggregation. Russia
  encodes Austerlitz as catastrophic education—"what not to do" when
  facing operational brilliance. Neither reading is incorrect; they
  are grammatically incompatible. The divergence reveals that
  civilizational memory is not a record of what happened but a record
  of what the event meant for survival.

Secondary Beliefs Extracted:

Force-Ratio Manufacturing (Mearsheimer frame):
• Aggregate force-ratios predict nothing; local force-ratios at
  decision points predict everything. Napoleon achieved 3:1 local
  superiority at Pratzen Heights while being 0.86:1 globally inferior.
  The structural lesson: concentration manufactures advantage. Russia
  learned "avoid decisive battle" when the structural lesson was
  "manufacture local superiority." Francia would encode "decisive
  battle validates superiority" when the structural lesson was
  "concentration requires preconditions."

Liability Substrate (Barnes frame):
• Force-ratio concentration requires three preconditions: interior
  lines, unified command, sustainable extraction. Each has a liability
  dimension—interior lines constrain exit options, unified command
  concentrates failure liability, extraction creates visible cost
  ledgers. When liability substrates fail, structural preconditions
  follow. The 1805→1813 transition shows Napoleon losing not force-ratio
  capacity per se, but the liability foundations that enabled concentration.

Defection Typology (Barnes frame):
• Individual defection (marshals) requires: liability exposure >
  protection, legitimacy destination, visible costs, correct timing,
  destination quality independent of the system fled. State defection
  (satellites) requires: pre-existing legitimacy (not purely Napoleonic
  creation), sovereignty-preserving destination, timing before outcome
  determined. The typology explains differential outcomes: Bernadotte
  (collected 26 years), Murat (shot), Saxony (partitioned), Westphalia
  (abolished).

Axiom Implications:
• AXIOM FRANCIA-001 (Authority flows from declaration) requires
  amendment awareness: declarative authority constrains only those
  without legitimacy destinations. When defection becomes viable,
  declaration loses compulsory force.

NCZ Implications:
• Identifies positive war exemplar gap: Francia encodes defeats
  (Seven Years', 1870) but has no ingested MEM encoding victories
  (Austerlitz, Jena, Wagram). The absence prevents analysis of how
  Francia processes triumph versus how it processes failure.

Cross-Civilizational Insight:
• Civilizations encode the same events through incompatible grammars.
  Russia's "wisdom-necessity collapse" (survivors call their constraints
  wisdom) is structurally unavailable to Francia, whose axiom structure
  requires agency—the ability to declare, choose, reconstitute. Francia
  cannot process the insight that "doctrine is the last option standing
  after alternatives are eliminated" without threatening its core
  legitimacy grammar.

RLL Derivation:
• Supports RLL–FRANCIA–0008, 0009, 0010 (proposed)
