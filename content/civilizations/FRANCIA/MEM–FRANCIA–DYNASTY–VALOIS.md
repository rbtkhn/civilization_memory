I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Valois dynasty as **Francia’s crisis-endurance
dynastic phase**: the lineage that inherited Capetian legitimacy but was
forced to govern through prolonged catastrophe, fragmentation, and
ideological fracture without relinquishing the principle of monarchy.

The Valois represent **continuity under sustained breakdown**, not
consolidation or ascent.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC ORIGIN & LEGITIMACY INHERITANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Valois ascend through legal succession, not conquest.

Key conditions:
• Capetian bloodline continuity
• Salic Law enforcement
• Weak initial territorial control
• Immediate external challenge

Legitimacy is inherited intact.
Power is not.

The dynasty begins already under pressure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. THE HUNDRED YEARS’ WAR AS EXISTENTIAL STRESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Valois reign is defined by prolonged external war.

Characteristics:
• English dynastic challenge
• Repeated military defeat and recovery
• Loss and reclamation of royal authority
• Reliance on symbolic legitimacy during weakness

Monarchy survives not by dominance, but by **refusing extinction**.

Joan of Arc functions as legitimacy catalyst, not institutional solution.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. INTERNAL FRAGMENTATION & NOBLE POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Royal authority competes with entrenched aristocratic forces.

Dynamics:
• Powerful feudal houses
• Regional autonomy
• Private warfare
• Court factionalism

The crown persists while control remains partial.

Centralization advances unevenly and reversibly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DEMOGRAPHIC & ECONOMIC SHOCK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Structural collapse compounds political weakness.

Shocks:
• Plague cycles
• Agricultural decline
• Urban contraction
• Fiscal instability

State capacity erodes faster than legitimacy.

Survival requires endurance, not reform.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELIGIOUS FRACTURE & IDEOLOGICAL WAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Late Valois rule confronts confessional division.

Conditions:
• Protestant expansion
• Catholic resistance
• Breakdown of religious consensus
• Escalation into civil war

The monarchy attempts neutrality without authority.

Religion transforms from stabilizer into accelerant.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FAILURE OF LEGITIMACY TRANSLATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Valois retain sacral kingship but cannot convert it into order.

Limits:
• Weak administrative reach
• Inadequate coercive capacity
• Inability to impose confessional settlement

Legitimacy survives symbolically while governance falters practically.

The crown is believed—but not obeyed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. TERMINATION & DYNASTIC TRANSFER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The dynasty ends through exhaustion, not overthrow.

End state:
• Last Valois assassinated (1589)
• Legitimacy vacuum unresolved internally
• Transfer to Bourbon line via legal continuity

The monarchy survives the dynasty.

Valois failure prepares Bourbon absolutism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Valois encode a **negative-positive template**:

Negative:
• Sacral legitimacy alone cannot govern catastrophe.
• Neutrality fails when authority is weak.

Positive:
• Dynastic continuity can survive prolonged collapse.
• Francia can endure crisis without abandoning monarchy.

Trauma becomes instructional.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Legitimacy can outlast power for generations.
• Dynasties may survive by persistence alone.
• War tests belief before institutions exist to support it.
• Religious fracture destroys medieval governance grammars.
• The Valois prove monarchy can survive collapse—but not manage it.
• Bourbon absolutism emerges as corrective response, not rupture.

Tension preserved without verdict.
