────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Burgundy as **Francia’s internal near-rival**: a
political–economic power that nearly detached from Francia’s orbit,
exposing the fragility of centralized authority during dynastic
fragmentation.

Burgundy is Francia’s unrealized alternative path.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. BURGUNDY AS A PARALLEL POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundy accumulates state-like attributes.

Characteristics:
• Territorial continuity (Low Countries to eastern France)
• Extraordinary fiscal wealth
• Court culture rivaling Paris
• Diplomatic autonomy

Burgundy functions as a competing center of gravity.

Unity is aspirational, not guaranteed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DYNASTIC LOGIC & POLITICAL AMBITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundian authority is dynastic, not national.

Dynamics:
• Personal rule
• Marriage diplomacy
• Feudal loyalty over civic identity

Ambition exceeds institutional depth.

Power rests on individuals, not durable structures.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ALLIANCE WITH ANGLIA & CIVIL WAR EXPOSURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundy’s alignment destabilizes Francia.

Context:
• Hundred Years’ War
• Armagnac–Burgundian civil conflict
• Tactical cooperation with Anglia

Internal fracture enables external penetration.

Francia’s weakness is endogenous before it is foreign.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COLLAPSE THROUGH SUCCESSION & ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundy fails to institutionalize.

Outcomes:
• Dynastic extinction
• Territorial absorption by Francia
• Low Countries detach from French core

Rivalry ends through inheritance, not conquest.

The threat dissolves rather than explodes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL IMPLICATIONS FOR FRANCIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundy leaves a permanent imprint.

Lessons:
• Fragmentation invites foreign leverage
• Centralization is survival mechanism
• Near-states must be absorbed or neutralized

Burgundy justifies later absolutism.

Unity becomes doctrine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burgundy encodes **the danger of internal plurality**.

Lessons:
• Rival courts fracture legitimacy.
• Wealth without sovereignty invites collapse.
• Dynastic brilliance cannot replace institutions.
• Francia’s fear of division is historically grounded.

Burgundy is the road not taken—and not missed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Burgundy nearly splits Francia’s destiny.
• Internal rivals are more dangerous than external enemies.
• Centralization emerges as defensive logic.
• Cultural splendor cannot secure sovereignty.
• Burgundy explains Francia’s later intolerance of autonomy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
