I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Franco–Dutch War as **Francia’s failed
absorption attempt**: a conflict in which military dominance proves
insufficient to conquer a commercial–maritime civilization protected
by finance, geography, and coalition response.

For Francia, the war is power without conversion.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war emerges from absolutist confidence.

Context:
• Louis XIV’s consolidation phase
• Desire to neutralize Dutch commercial power
• Assertion of continental supremacy
• Underestimation of maritime systems

Francia assumes force can override finance.

This assumption proves false.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INITIAL SUCCESS & STRUCTURAL RESISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early campaigns favor Francia.

Dynamics:
• Rapid territorial penetration
• Superior land armies
• Psychological shock

Resistance emerges through:
• Dutch water defenses
• Financial resilience
• Naval power
• Coalition formation

Territory is seized—but not absorbed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. COALITION BACKLASH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Continental aggression activates balancing forces.

Actors:
• The Dutch Republic
• Anglia
• The Holy Roman Empire
• Spain

Francia’s local success triggers systemic opposition.

Victory becomes diplomatically expensive.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIMITS OF ABSOLUTIST POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war exposes Francia’s constraints.

Revealed limits:
• Inability to destroy Dutch commerce
• Naval inferiority to combined fleets
• Fiscal strain of sustained campaigns

Prestige grows—but leverage does not.

Force fails to compel submission.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SETTLEMENT WITHOUT RESOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Peace restores balance without dominance.

Outcomes:
• Minor territorial gains
• Dutch autonomy preserved
• Francia contained, not defeated

The objective fails quietly.

Francia learns restraint reluctantly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL IMPLICATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war shapes Francia’s later anxieties.

Lessons:
• Commercial states resist conquest
• Coalitions punish overreach
• Military brilliance cannot erase system mismatch

Francia doubles down on centralization.

Absorption remains aspiration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Franco–Dutch War encodes **the limit of land power**.

Lessons:
• Finance and water defeat armies.
• Prestige cannot compel systems.
• Coalition response defines ceilings of ambition.
• Francia’s power stops at maritime thresholds.

The Dutch survive as proof-of-limit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Land power cannot absorb commercial republics.
• Coalition logic overrides tactical success.
• Absolutism struggles with plural systems.
• Francia’s ambition meets structural resistance.
• The Franco–Dutch War foreshadows later containment.

Tension preserved without verdict.
