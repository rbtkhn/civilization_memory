I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Maximilien Robespierre as the **moral–ideological
rupture engine** of the French state: the figure who terminated dynastic
legitimacy, absolutized revolutionary virtue, and redefined authority as
moral purity enforced by terror.

Robespierre does not stabilize Francia.
He destroys the conditions under which plural legitimacy could survive.

He represents **rupture through moral absolutism** — the necessary
precursor to Thermidorian recoil and Napoleonic consolidation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGINS & REVOLUTIONARY FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Robespierre emerges from Enlightenment legalism radicalized by crisis.

Formative traits:
• Rousseauian moral philosophy
• Legalist absolutism
• Suspicion of compromise
• Identification of virtue with authority

Core belief:
Legitimacy is not negotiated — it is *embodied*.

Morality functions as sovereignty, not constraint.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DESTRUCTION OF LEGITIMACY & MORAL SEIZURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The revolutionary phase (1792–1794) invalidates all prior authority.

Key characteristics:
• Execution of the king
• Criminalization of moderation
• Substitution of virtue for law
• Redefinition of justice as revolutionary necessity

Foundational axiom:
Authority flows from moral correctness, not consent.

Opposition becomes proof of guilt.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. VIRTUE AS STATE SUBSTITUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Robespierre’s central innovation is virtue as governing principle.

Core mechanisms:
• Committee of Public Safety
• Moral surveillance
• Revolutionary tribunals
• Ideological purification

Statehood is redefined:
The Republic is virtue; institutions are instruments.

Pluralism collapses; moral density rises.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. TERROR AS GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Terror institutionalizes righteousness over stability.

Key practices:
• Preventive executions
• Accelerated justice
• Denunciation as civic duty
• Fear as moral clarifier

Governing logic:
Virtue without terror is powerless.
Terror without virtue is criminal.

Violence becomes ethical maintenance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SECURITY–MORAL STATE GENESIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revolutionary justice fuses ethics and repression.

Characteristics:
• Extrajudicial authority
• Ideological policing
• Moralized violence
• Collapse of procedural restraint

Fear is reframed as civic purification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FAILURE OF CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Robespierre cannot translate virtue into durability.

Unresolved tensions:
• Moral absolutism vs. social exhaustion
• Revolutionary purity vs. administrative necessity
• Personal authority vs. collective terror

No survivable legitimacy grammar emerges.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. THERMIDORIAN REVERSAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Robespierre is not overthrown by reactionaries,
but by revolutionaries seeking survival.

Continuities broken:
• Moral absolutism
• Terror primacy
• Ideological monopoly

Thermidor restores plurality without legitimacy clarity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. TRANSITION TO NAPOLEON
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon does not negate Robespierre.
He resolves the vacuum Robespierre creates.

Continuities:
• Centralization
• Revolutionary inheritance
• Legitimacy reset

Difference:
Robespierre moralizes authority.
Napoleon operationalizes it.

Robespierre creates the fire.
Napoleon builds the furnace.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Moral revolutions require coercion to function.
• Virtue absolutism destroys plural legitimacy rapidly.
• Terror framed as ethics accelerates exhaustion.
• Ideology without administrative restraint collapses inward.
• Robespierre prepares the ground for authoritarian stabilization.
• Bonapartism is Jacobinism after burnout.

Rupture preserved without absolution.
