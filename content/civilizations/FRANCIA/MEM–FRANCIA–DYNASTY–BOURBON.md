I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Bourbons as the **absolutist–centralizing apex
of the French monarchical state**: the dynasty that completed the
transition from medieval kingship to a unified, bureaucratic,
ideological sovereignty.

The Bourbons represent **power perfected—and overexposed**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC ORIGIN & CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Bourbon accession under Henry IV (1589) followed civil war and
confessional fracture.

Foundational actions:
• Restoration of internal order (Edict of Nantes)
• Fiscal and administrative reconstruction
• Reassertion of royal supremacy over nobility

Survival logic:
→ Peace precedes authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ABSOLUTIST STATE FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Under Louis XIII–XIV, Bourbon power reached its zenith.

Core mechanisms:
• Court centralization at Versailles
• Subordination of nobility to ritual
• Expansion of royal bureaucracy
• Permanent standing army
• Direct taxation and fiscal extraction

Absolutism functioned as **control through visibility and dependence**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. LEGITIMACY & SACRAL MONARCHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bourbon legitimacy fused:
• Divine right
• Historical continuity
• Performance through order and glory

Iconic formulation:
→ “L’État, c’est moi.”

Legitimacy was total—but brittle under failure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. IMPERIAL AMBITION & STRAIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
France pursued continental and overseas dominance.

Structural costs:
• Continuous warfare
• Fiscal exhaustion
• Colonial overextension
• Rising social inequality

Imperial success outpaced institutional adaptation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. COLLAPSE & REVOLUTIONARY TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Bourbon system collapsed under:
• Debt crisis
• Elite immobility
• Enlightenment delegitimization
• Mass political mobilization

Revolution negated absolutist legitimacy entirely.

Restoration attempts failed to restore credibility.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Absolutism maximizes control but minimizes resilience.
• Court centralization neutralizes elites—but severs feedback.
• Fiscal opacity accelerates revolutionary rupture.
• Bourbon failure created the conditions Napoleon absorbed.
• Francia learned that legitimacy cannot be monopolized indefinitely.
