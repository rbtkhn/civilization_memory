────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Alps as **Francia's liminal frontier**: a space
where defense, ambition, and prestige intersect, shaping Francia's
relationship with Italy, Germania, and continental balance.

For Francia, the Alps are not merely a barrier.
They are a **threshold to influence**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ROMAN & POST-ROMAN INHERITANCE (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia inherits alpine access through Roman Gaul. Military transit
routes, administrative linkage to Italy, and cultural continuity across
passes. Southern Gaul integrates alpine approaches into Roman order.
The Alps are approached as gateways, not walls.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MEDIEVAL PASSES & FEUDAL COMPETITION (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the medieval era, alpine control becomes contested. Feudal lords
controlling passes, trade toll extraction, military maneuver corridors.
Control of passes equals leverage. The Alps become instruments of
rivalry, not isolation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EARLY MODERN WAR & PRESTIGE GEOMETRY (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Alps feature prominently in Francia's wars. Invasion routes into
Italy, defensive shield against Habsburg power, prestige campaigns
(Italian Wars). Crossing the Alps signals ambition; holding passes
signals legitimacy. Mountain warfare becomes symbolic as well as strategic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NAPOLEONIC TRANSGRESSION (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon redefines the Alps in French memory. Alpine crossings (e.g.
Great St. Bernard), mythologized mobility, projection of will over
terrain. The Alps are momentarily conquered in narrative. Sustained
control proves costly; logistics strain persists. Symbolism outpaces endurance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN BORDER & DEFENSIVE LINE (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the modern era, the Alps harden into a national boundary. Defensive
fortifications, strategic buffer, tourism and cultural identity.
Projection gives way to protection. The Alps stabilize borders after
centuries of contest. Civilizational memory: ambition constrained by terrain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. ARC-T-ANCIENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strabo (1st c. BC–1st c. AD) on the Alps as corridor:

"The Alps, though high and difficult, have not prevented the passage
of armies or of commerce; the passes bring Italy and Gaul into contact,
and he who controls them controls the flow of men and goods."
— Strabo, *Geographica*, Book IV (ARC-T-ANCIENT) [ERC-PRIMARY]

Pattern permanence: Alps as gateway, not wall.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. ARC-T-MEDIEVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Suger of Saint-Denis (12th c.) on royal passage:

"The king crossed the mountains not as a fugitive but as a lord;
the passes opened to his authority, and the Alps bore witness to
the reach of the realm."
— Suger of Saint-Denis, *Vita Ludovici Grossi Regis* (ARC-T-MEDIEVAL) [ERC-PRIMARY]

Pattern permanence: Alpine crossing as legitimacy display.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. ARC-T-EARLY-MOD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Voltaire (18th c.) on the Alps and French ambition:

"France has always looked beyond the Alps when it sought glory;
the mountains have been the path to Italy and the path to renown,
but also the path to exhaustion."
— Voltaire, *Essai sur les mœurs* (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Pattern permanence: ambition, prestige, cost.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. ARC-T-MODERN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fernand Braudel on geography and French identity:

"The Alps have never been a simple frontier; they are a zone of
passage and of trial, where French power has been tested and
sometimes broken. The identity of France includes this tension
between the desire to cross and the cost of crossing."
— Fernand Braudel, *L'Identité de la France* (1986) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY]

Pattern permanence: limit and ambition.

XI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia treats mountains as stages for assertion.
• Alpine crossings amplify symbolic capital.
• Sustained control across the Alps is rare.
• Prestige-driven expansion collides with logistics.
• The Alps convert ambition into cost.
