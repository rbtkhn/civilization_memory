I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Alps as **Francia’s liminal frontier**: a space
where defense, ambition, and prestige intersect, shaping Francia’s
relationship with Italy, Germania, and continental balance.

For Francia, the Alps are not merely a barrier.
They are a **threshold to influence**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ROMAN & POST-ROMAN INHERITANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia inherits alpine access through Roman Gaul.

Functions:
• Military transit routes
• Administrative linkage to Italy
• Cultural continuity across passes

Southern Gaul integrates alpine approaches into Roman order.

The Alps are approached as gateways, not walls.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MEDIEVAL PASSES & FEUDAL COMPETITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the medieval era, alpine control becomes contested.

Dynamics:
• Feudal lords controlling passes
• Trade toll extraction
• Military maneuver corridors

Control of passes equals leverage.

The Alps become instruments of rivalry, not isolation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EARLY MODERN WAR & PRESTIGE GEOMETRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Alps feature prominently in Francia’s wars.

Roles:
• Invasion routes into Italy
• Defensive shield against Habsburg power
• Prestige campaigns (Italian Wars)

For Francia:
• Crossing the Alps signals ambition
• Holding passes signals legitimacy

Mountain warfare becomes symbolic as well as strategic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NAPOLEONIC TRANSGRESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon redefines the Alps in French memory.

Events:
• Alpine crossings (e.g., Great St. Bernard)
• Mythologized mobility
• Projection of will over terrain

The Alps are momentarily conquered in narrative.

But:
• Sustained control proves costly
• Logistics strain persists

Symbolism outpaces endurance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN BORDER & DEFENSIVE LINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the modern era, the Alps harden into a national boundary.

Functions:
• Defensive fortifications
• Strategic buffer
• Tourism and cultural identity

Projection gives way to protection.

The Alps stabilize borders after centuries of contest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Alps encode **ambition constrained by terrain**.

Lessons:
• Prestige seeks dramatic crossings.
• Geography punishes overextension.
• Temporary dominance does not equal integration.
• Enduring control requires restraint.

The Alps remind Francia of limits.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia treats mountains as stages for assertion.
• Alpine crossings amplify symbolic capital.
• Sustained control across the Alps is rare.
• Prestige-driven expansion collides with logistics.
• The Alps convert ambition into cost.

Tension preserved without verdict.
