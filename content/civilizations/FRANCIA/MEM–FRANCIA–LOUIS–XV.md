I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Louis XV as the **absolutist erosion phase**
of Francia: the reign in which inherited symbolic authority
outlasted administrative confidence, and legitimacy decayed
through distance, fatigue, and quiet institutional drift.

Louis XV does not rupture Francia.
He **hollows it**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INHERITED ABSOLUTISM WITHOUT MASTERY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XV inherits a perfected absolutist machine.

Conditions:
• Centralized administration
• Court-centered legitimacy
• Cultural dominance
• High fiscal load

Deficit:
• Limited personal authority over the system
• Delegation without strategic direction

Absolutism persists by inertia, not command.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COURT DECADENCE & LEGITIMACY DISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Versailles loses disciplinary function.

Patterns:
• Favoritism (Pompadour, Du Barry)
• Elite cynicism
• Public moral alienation
• Decline in sacral aura of monarchy

Spectacle remains; reverence evaporates.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ADMINISTRATIVE COMPETENCE VS SYMBOLIC TRUST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
State function continues while belief erodes.

Dynamics:
• Capable bureaucracy
• Effective tax extraction
• Expanding legal apparatus

Paradox:
→ The state works, but the crown is no longer trusted.

Competence no longer regenerates legitimacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WAR, HUMILIATION, & PRESTIGE LOSS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Geopolitical outcomes damage authority.

Key failures:
• War of the Austrian Succession
• Seven Years’ War (colonial losses)

Glory turns to embarrassment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. ENLIGHTENMENT PRESSURE & IDEOLOGICAL LEAKAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Critique migrates from salons to public consciousness.

Trends:
• Philosophes challenge absolutism
• Legal rationalism questions privilege
• Moral authority fragments

Ideas circulate faster than reform.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FAILURE TO RESET LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XV recognizes decay but cannot reverse it.

The crown survives structurally, not morally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Absolutism decays quietly before it collapses.
• Administrative competence cannot replace belief.
• Court symbolism without discipline accelerates cynicism.
• Legitimacy erosion precedes revolutionary rupture.
• Louis XV prepares the crisis without naming it.

Tension preserved without verdict.
