I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Carolingian dynasty as the **dynastic bridge**
through which Francia reasserted Roman–Christian imperial continuity
after Merovingian decay.

For Francia, the Carolingians represent **legitimacy restoration through
imperial synthesis**, not ethnic consolidation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC ORIGINS & POWER TRANSFER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Carolingians arise from the mayoral class.

Key transitions:
• Decline of Merovingian sacral kingship
• Ascendancy of Pepin of Herstal and Charles Martel
• Papal sanction for dynastic replacement (Pepin III, 751)

Legitimacy is re-based through Church alignment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. IMPERIAL APEX & UNIVERSAL CLAIM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Charlemagne defines the dynasty’s imperial zenith.

Characteristics:
• Revival of Western Roman imperial title (800)
• Latin-Christian universalism
• Papal–imperial symbiosis
• Administrative coherence across Frankish lands

Francia inherits imperial vocabulary.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. GOVERNANCE & ADMINISTRATIVE LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Carolingian rule emphasizes correction and delegation.

Mechanisms:
• Capitularies
• Missi dominici
• Legal pluralism under imperial frame

Order is maintained through oversight, not uniformity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DYNASTIC FRAGMENTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-Charlemagne fragmentation reshapes Francia.

Key outcomes:
• Treaty of Verdun (843)
• West Francia emerges as political core
• Imperial claim fades, institutional memory remains

Dynasty dissolves into successor states.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LEGACY WITHIN FRANCIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Imperial legitimacy grammar retained
• Church–state mediation normalized
• Administrative rationality preserved

Francia remembers the Carolingians as **imperial ancestors**, not ethnic founders.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Dynastic legitimacy can be reset through sacral sanction.
• Empire can be restored symbolically before institutionally.
• Francia internalizes Roman continuity through Carolingian mediation.
• Fragmentation does not erase imperial memory.

Tension preserved without verdict.
