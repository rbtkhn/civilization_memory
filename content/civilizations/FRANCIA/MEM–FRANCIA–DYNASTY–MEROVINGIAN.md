I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Merovingian dynasty as **Francia’s formative
dynastic bridge**: the lineage that translated Roman collapse into a
new Frankish political order while preserving claims of legitimacy,
law, and sacred kingship.

For Francia, the Merovingians represent **continuity through inheritance**,
not administrative completion.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ROMAN–FRANKISH TRANSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Merovingians inherit a post-Roman landscape.

Key functions:
• Absorption of Roman elites
• Preservation of Latin law
• Alliance with the Church
• Territorial kingship continuity

Rule survives collapse by adaptation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DYNASTIC LEGITIMACY LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority is rooted in bloodline and sacral symbolism.

Characteristics:
• Anointed kingship
• Ritual authority
• Sacred lineage myths

Legitimacy precedes governance capacity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. PLURAL KINGSHIP & PARTITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Division among heirs is accepted.

Effects:
• Multiple coexisting kingdoms
• Negotiated legitimacy
• Persistent dynastic continuity

Unity is symbolic, not administrative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CHURCH AS LEGITIMACY STABILIZER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Church sustains dynastic authority.

Roles:
• Moral endorsement
• Legal continuity
• Cultural transmission

Universalism begins ecclesiastically.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MAYORAL ASCENDANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Administrative weakness invites substitution.

Dynamics:
• Mayors accumulate real power
• Kings retain symbolic authority
• Carolingians emerge as executors

Form persists as function migrates.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. DYNASTIC TRANSFER AS LEGITIMACY EVENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Carolingian takeover is sanctified.

Outcome:
• Papal approval
• Continuity preserved through ritual
• Dynasty replaced without rupture

Legitimacy is re-authored, not destroyed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia preserves lineage longer than function.
• Legitimacy can outlive competence temporarily.
• Church mediation enables dynastic continuity.
• Universal vocabulary precedes imperial ambition.
• Merovingians bridge Rome and Carolingian empire.

Tension preserved without verdict.
