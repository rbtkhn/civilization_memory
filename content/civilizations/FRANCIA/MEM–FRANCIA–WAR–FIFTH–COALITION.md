I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the War of the Fifth Coalition as **Francia’s
demonstration of residual resilience after peak dominance**: a conflict
in which Francia absorbs shock, recovers operational control, and
reasserts authority—without restoring prior systemic advantage.

Victory is achieved.
Margin is narrowed.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT: POST-PEAK ENVIRONMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1807, Francia governs an expanded but strained system.

Conditions:
• Continental System enforcement costs
• Rising nationalist resistance
• Austrian rearmament
• Declining coalition fear threshold

Austria tests whether dominance endures.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INITIAL SHOCK AND ADAPTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Austria strikes while Francia is dispersed.

Results:
• Tactical surprise
• Temporary French dislocation
• Exposure of overextended command structure

Napoleon adapts rapidly.

Elasticity compensates for fatigue.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. WAGRAM AND CONDITIONAL VICTORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Battle of Wagram restores battlefield supremacy.

Characteristics:
• Massive casualties
• Artillery-centric warfare
• Attritional dominance

Victory is real—but costly.

Decisiveness replaces brilliance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DIPLOMATIC CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Treaty of Schönbrunn imposes punishment.

Outcomes:
• Territorial losses for Austria
• Reinforced French control
• Dynastic marriage follows

Control is reaffirmed—but anxiety grows.

Peace is enforced, not trusted.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SYSTEMIC IMPLICATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war reveals structural limits.

Indicators:
• Rising replacement costs
• Declining morale elasticity
• Reduced coalition deterrence

Francia can still win—
but no longer effortlessly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Fifth Coalition encodes **late-stage resilience without renewal**.

Lessons:
• Elastic systems can absorb shocks post-peak.
• Victory does not restore dominance.
• Attrition replaces maneuver as primary tool.
• Decline begins with effortful success.

This is strength under strain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia still outperforms peers militarily.
• Recovery speed matters more than initiative.
• Victory margins signal systemic fatigue.
• Control persists through pressure, not legitimacy.
• Fifth Coalition narrows the future option space.

Tension preserved without verdict.
