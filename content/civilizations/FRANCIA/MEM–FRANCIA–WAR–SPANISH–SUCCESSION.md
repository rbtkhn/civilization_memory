I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the War of the Spanish Succession as **Francia’s
overreach at the peak of absolutist confidence**: the conflict in which
dynastic logic, universalist ambition, and prestige politics triggered
a full coalition response designed to halt French continental and
imperial consolidation.

For Francia, the war is not defeat—but **containment by system design**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. DYNASTIC TRIGGER & STRATEGIC LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war begins as a legitimacy claim.

Context:
• Extinction of the Spanish Habsburg line
• Bourbon dynastic inheritance
• Prospect of Franco–Spanish union
• Fear of hegemonic consolidation

French premise:
→ Dynastic right equals political legitimacy.

Coalition premise:
→ Dynastic union equals systemic threat.

Legitimacy collides with balance-of-power logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ABSOLUTISM MEETS COALITION PHYSICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XIV prosecutes the war as continuation of royal logic.

Will Durant (ARC–FRANCIA) on the war and the Sun King's overreach:

"The War of the Spanish Succession was Louis XIV's last and greatest
gamble. He had placed his grandson on the Spanish throne; Europe
united to prevent the union of France and Spain. The war exhausted
France and broke the spell of invincibility. Louis died in 1715
leaving his country victorious in name but drained in fact—and his
successors inherited the bill."
— Will Durant, *The Age of Louis XIV* (1963) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY]

Characteristics:
• Centralized command
• Court-directed strategy
• Emphasis on honor and dynastic outcome

Opposition forms differently:
• Multi-state coalition
• Financial–maritime backbone (Anglia, Dutch)
• Attritional endurance strategy

Absolutism confronts distributed resistance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MULTI-THEATER STRAIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war expands geographically.

Theaters:
• Low Countries
• Iberian Peninsula
• Italy
• Rhine frontier
• Atlantic and colonial routes

Effects:
• Fiscal exhaustion
• Overextended logistics
• Declining strategic initiative

Victory becomes preservation, not expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NAVAL & ECONOMIC ASYMMETRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Maritime imbalance shapes outcomes.

Dynamics:
• Anglian–Dutch sea control
• Trade disruption
• Colonial vulnerability
• Finance as endurance weapon

French land strength cannot offset maritime constraint.

Continental power lacks global insulation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. INTERNAL STRESS & LEGITIMACY COST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
War strain penetrates the core.

Consequences:
• Famine (1709)
• Tax pressure
• Elite war-weariness
• Public disillusionment

Prestige erodes without collapse.

The system survives—but hardens.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. SETTLEMENT & CONTAINMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Peace resolves the crisis without annihilation.

Treaties of Utrecht / Rastatt:
• Bourbon retained on Spanish throne
• Permanent separation of crowns
• Territorial concessions
• Maritime and colonial limitations

Francia is checked, not broken.

Containment replaces confrontation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• End of expansionist absolutism
• Acceptance of coalition limits
• Shift toward defensive legitimacy maintenance
• Recognition of maritime inferiority

Universal ambition survives—tempered by constraint.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war encodes **the ceiling of dynastic universalism**.

Lessons:
• Dynastic legitimacy triggers coalition alarm.
• Absolutism scales ambition faster than capacity.
• Maritime–financial systems dictate endurance.
• Containment can succeed without decisive defeat.
• Francia’s limits are enforced multilaterally.

The war marks the beginning of strategic restraint.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia reaches maximum reach without collapse.
• Coalitions defeat concentration.
• Legitimacy claims invite systemic resistance.
• Maritime denial constrains continental success.
• The War of Spanish Succession is Francia’s first enforced ceiling.

Tension preserved without verdict.
