I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Louis Pasteur as **Francia's conqueror of disease**:
the scientist who established germ theory, refuted spontaneous
generation, invented pasteurization and vaccines (anthrax, rabies),
and built the Institut Pasteur—science as national and imperial
resource.

Pasteur is not only a discoverer.
He is an **institution-builder**—hygiene as civilizational project.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL CONTEXT: THIRD REPUBLIC & EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pasteur emerges in a Francia that had lost the Franco-Prussian War
(1870) and that made science and hygiene pillars of republican
legitimacy and colonial administration.

Conditions:
• Second Empire; then Third Republic
• Spontaneous generation debate (Pouchet vs Pasteur)
• Colonial medicine; Institut Pasteur in Saigon, etc.
• Dreyfus affair (Pasteur's son-in-law was anti-Dreyfusard; Pasteur
  had died before)

Pasteur in his own words—on chance and preparation:

"Chance favors only the prepared mind."
— Louis Pasteur (attributed; ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Discovery is **preparation**—method, not luck. Science as discipline.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FERMENTATION & GERM THEORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pasteur shows that fermentation is caused by living microorganisms
(yeast, bacteria), not chemical catalysis alone. Wine, beer, and
souring are biological; heat kills germs (pasteurization).

Contributions:
• Germ theory of disease (extension to medicine)
• Refutation of spontaneous generation (swan-neck flasks)
• Pasteurization (1864): heat treatment to kill pathogens

Pasteur on the experimental method:

"In the field of observation, chance favors only the prepared mind."
— Louis Pasteur, address, 1854 (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Observation and experiment replace speculation; the prepared mind
recognizes significance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. VACCINATION: ANTHRAX & RABIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pasteur develops attenuated vaccines for anthrax (1881, public
demonstration at Pouilly-le-Fort) and rabies (1885, Joseph Meister).
Rabies vaccine makes Pasteur a national hero; public subscription
funds the Institut Pasteur.

Contributions:
• Attenuation: weakened pathogen as vaccine
• Anthrax vaccine (sheep, cattle)
• Rabies vaccine (human); Institut Pasteur (1888)

Civilizational function:
Science **saves lives**; the nation funds the institution; the
institution extends to empire (Pasteur institutes abroad).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INSTITUT PASTEUR: INSTITUTIONALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Institut Pasteur (1888) is founded by public subscription after the
rabies success. It combines research, teaching, and public health
(vaccination, serum production).

Strategic logic:
• Science as public good; private and state funding
• Institutional continuity beyond the individual
• Model for Curie (Institut du radium); colonial Pasteur institutes

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pasteur encodes **hygiene and vaccination as civilizational
achievement**: germ theory, pasteurization, vaccines, and the
Institut as national and imperial resource.

Lessons:
• Germ theory replaces miasma; experiment replaces speculation.
• Vaccination as conquest of disease; nation and empire benefit.
• Science institutionalized (Institut Pasteur) outlives the founder.
