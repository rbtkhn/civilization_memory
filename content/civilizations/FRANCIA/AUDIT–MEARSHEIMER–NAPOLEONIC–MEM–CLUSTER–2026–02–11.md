## I. Executive Assessment

The fact is that the Napoleonic MEM cluster is **structurally coherent** when read through a power-distribution lens. The MEMs collectively encode a single, consistent story: continental hegemony was built on force ratio and concentration; it collapsed when the balance of power shifted—naval foreclosure (Trafalgar), coalition concentration (Trachenberg, Leipzig), defection cascade (satellite liability visible), and terminal force-ratio inversion (Waterloo). The cluster does not contradict that story. Where it is **under-specified** from a Mearsheimer perspective is in explicit force-ratio and incentive-structure language at the node level; where it is **well specified** is in the war MEMs (Trafalgar, Leipzig, Waterloo) and in CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM (capacity gap, enforcement failure). The figure MEM (NAPOLEON) and the victory MEM (Austerlitz) are legitimacy- and performance-heavy; they align with the structural arc but do not foreground power distribution as the primary variable. So: **alignment is high; coherence is high; structural explicitness is uneven.**

## II. Structural Logic Encoded Across the Cluster

### Power distribution as driver

The MEMs collectively establish that Napoleonic authority rested on **relative military capacity** and **operational concentration**, not on ideology or law alone. NAPOLEON: "Authority flows from victory, not lineage"; "War sustains legitimacy; victory sustains the state"; "Collapse follows overextension." CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM: "Sea failure produces continental coercion"; "Economic warfare required capabilities France did not possess" (Ferguson). TRAFALGAR: naval death → continental substitute; force ratio at Trafalgar (1.22:1 Allied ships) understates the real gap—training, doctrine, and command produced British supremacy. The cluster is coherent: when power distribution favoured Francia (Austerlitz, Jena), legitimacy held; when it shifted (Trafalgar at sea, 1812 in Russia, Leipzig in Germany, Waterloo in Belgium), legitimacy collapsed.

### Incentive structure

Our incentive structure (Francia’s) was to maximize continental control and use it to compensate for lost maritime capacity. Their incentive structure (coalition, satellites, Britain) was to balance—coordinate, avoid battle until concentrated, offer defection destinations. The MEMs encode this. LEIPZIG: "The question was not whether to remain loyal to Napoleon, but whether defection could be accomplished safely" (Bell); Saxon defection = rational choice once destination appeared. WATERLOO: "Napoleon had funded his wars by plunder and tribute; when the plunder ran out and the tributaries defected, he had no credit to fall back on" (Ferguson). CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM: "Blockade punishes Europe faster than Britain"; continental states had incentive to defect or evade. So the cluster presents **two incentive structures in conflict**—and the one that could coordinate (coalition) and offer exit (defection) won. That is structural logic, and it is coherent across NAPOLEON, CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM, LEIPZIG, WATERLOO.

### Force ratio and coalition

The war MEMs supply **numbers**. Trafalgar: 33 vs 27 (ships); outcome 22 lost vs 0. Leipzig: Day 1 ~0.69:1, Day 4 ~0.41:1; Saxon defection adds effective swing. Waterloo: morning ~1.07:1, evening ~0.62:1 after Prussian arrival. The structural picture is clear: Francia’s advantage was temporary and conditional on concentration and satellites; when coalition concentrated and satellites defected, the force ratio inverted. The cluster is aligned: Austerlitz = concentration and enemy division; Leipzig = coalition concentration + defection; Waterloo = coalition coordination (Trachenberg-style) and no recovery. No MEM claims Francia retained a favourable force ratio after 1812 or at Waterloo.

### Overextension

NAPOLEON: "Collapse follows overextension"; "Napoleon unifies Europe **against** Francia." CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM: "The system transforms naval defeat into systemic overstretch"; "Enforcement costs grow faster than compliance." LEIPZIG: extraction system under stress; Russia 1812 made costs visible. WATERLOO: "Exhaustion made visible"; no allies, no margin. The cluster consistently encodes **overextension** as the mechanism linking maximum extent to collapse. That aligns with doctrine 02 and with RLL–0016 (extent–stability). From a Mearsheimer perspective, overextension is the consequence of **pushing the balance of power beyond sustainable enforcement**—the MEMs support that reading.

## IV. Gaps and Under-Specification (Mearsheimer View)

1. **Force-ratio explicitness in figure MEM.** NAPOLEON emphasizes performance-based legitimacy and "war as governance" but does not state force ratio or balance of power as the **primary** variable. A structural audit would favour one explicit sentence: e.g. "Napoleonic authority was conditional on local force superiority and coalition fragmentation; when coalition concentrated and force ratio inverted, authority collapsed."

2. **Incentive-structure summary.** The cluster shows *our* incentive (continental dominance, substitute for sea) and *theirs* (balance, defection when safe) but does not compress this into a single "incentive structure" formulation in any one MEM. CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM comes closest ("British power rested on credit and trade... the system could not be enforced without occupation").

3. **Credit and material base.** Ferguson is cited (debt, credit, plunder, subsidies) in NAPOLEON and WATERLOO and CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM. The cluster is not thin on this—but the **mechanism** (no credit → no coalition → no recovery from defeat) could be stated as a structural constraint in one continuity insight: e.g. "Hegemonic war-making without fiscal depth collapses when tributaries defect and credit is absent."

4. **Geographic constraint.** Trafalgar encodes maritime foreclosure; the cluster does not explicitly frame **geography** (continental vs maritime, chokepoints, projection) as a variable. A GEO–MEM or a single bullet on "continental power cannot enforce maritime closure by decree" would sharpen alignment.

None of these gaps break coherence; they are opportunities to make the structural logic more explicit for a Mearsheimer reader.

So the Napoleonic MEM cluster is aligned with the doctrines that the system has already attached to it; the Mearsheimer audit does not find doctrinal incoherence.

## VI. Conclusion

The Napoleonic MEM–FRANCIA cluster is **strategically aligned and coherent** under a Mearsheimer (structural-realist) reading. Power distribution, coalition logic, defection incentives, force-ratio dynamics, and overextension are consistently encoded across the figure, policy, and war MEMs. The cluster tells one structural story: continental hegemony built on concentration and satellites collapsed when the balance shifted—naval foreclosure, coalition concentration, satellite defection, and terminal force-ratio inversion. Gaps are present only in **explicitness** (force ratio as primary variable in NAPOLEON; one-sentence incentive-structure summary; geographic constraint). No contradictions were found. The audit supports continued use of the cluster for doctrine 02/05 and for structural-realist analysis; optional enhancements above would strengthen Mearsheimer-voice coherence without changing the narrative.
