────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Pierre-Simon Laplace as **Francia's Newton**:
the mathematician and astronomer who completed the Newtonian synthesis
in celestial mechanics, formalized determinism ("Laplace's demon"),
and survived Revolution, Empire, and Restoration by serving each
regime without ideological commitment.

Laplace is not a revolutionary.
He is a **system-completer**—order through calculation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL CONTEXT: REVOLUTION & EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Laplace emerges from provincial Normandy, rises through the Académie
des sciences, and navigates the Revolution (suppression of the Académie;
reform as École polytechnique), the Empire (minister of the interior;
senator), and the Restoration (marquis; chancellor).

Conditions:
• Newtonian physics ascendant; French mathematical tradition
• Revolution: Laplace removes dedication to Newton in reprints
• Napoleon: "You have brought the spirit of the infinitely small into
  government" (criticism)
• Restoration: Laplace keeps honors; determinism becomes doctrine

Laplace on God—the famous omission:

"I had no need of that hypothesis."
— Laplace, reply to Napoleon on God in *Mécanique céleste* (attributed; ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

The system needs no first cause—only initial conditions and laws.
Determinism replaces divine intervention.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MÉCANIQUE CÉLESTE: NEWTONIAN SYNTHESIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
*Mécanique céleste* (1799–1825) reduces celestial mechanics to
analysis—"Newton explained, with the torch of mathematics" (Napoleon).
Laplace demonstrates stability of the solar system (to first order)
and explains lunar and planetary inequalities.

Contributions:
• Systematic derivation of planetary motion from gravitation
• Stability of the solar system (secular inequalities)
• Tidal theory; figure of the Earth

Calculation replaces mystery; the universe is **clockwork**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DETERMINISM: LAPLACE'S DEMON
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In *Théorie analytique des probabilités* (1812) and *Essai philosophique
sur les probabilités* (1814), Laplace articulates the demon: an
intellect that, given the state of the universe at one instant and
all natural laws, could predict and retrodict every event.

Laplace in his own words:

"We may regard the present state of the universe as the effect of its
past and the cause of its future. An intellect which at a certain
moment would know all forces... and all positions... could embrace
in a single formula the movements of the greatest bodies of the
universe and those of the lightest atom."
— Laplace, *Essai philosophique sur les probabilités* (1814) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Probability is **epistemic**—ignorance of causes—not ontological.
Determinism is complete; chance is appearance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. POLITICAL SURVIVAL: REGIME ADAPTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Laplace serves the Directory (École normale), Napoleon (minister,
senator), and the Restoration (marquis, chancellor). He avoids
ideology; he delivers **competence**.

Strategic logic:
• Science as neutral resource; regime-agnostic
• Institutional continuity (Académie, École) over political loyalty
• Francia's scientific elite as survivor class

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Laplace encodes **determinism as civilizational doctrine** and
**science as regime-agnostic resource**: the universe is calculable;
the scientist serves whoever holds power.

Lessons:
• Newtonian synthesis completed in France; "no need of that hypothesis."
• Determinism: past and future fixed by laws and initial conditions.
• Science survives regime change when it does not challenge power.
