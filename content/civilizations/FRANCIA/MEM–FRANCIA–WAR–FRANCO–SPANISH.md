────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Franco–Spanish War as **Francia’s prolonged
struggle against imperial saturation**: a conflict where universalist
ambition collided with fiscal strain, peripheral revolts, and the
limits of sustained continental war.

The war is not decisive victory.
It is **attritional rebalancing**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. UNIVERSAL MONARCHY AS THREAT PERCEPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia enters the war to block Habsburg universal monarchy.

Cardinal Richelieu frames the logic explicitly:

> “The aim of France is not conquest but to prevent any single power
> from becoming so great that it may impose laws upon all the others.”  
> — Richelieu, *Testament Politique*

War is justified defensively through balance rhetoric.

Universalism is opposed by counter-universalism.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MULTI-THEATER STRAIN & PERIPHERAL REVOLTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war expands across borders and seas.

Pressures include:
• Flanders campaigns
• Italian fronts
• Naval commitments
• Domestic revolts (Catalonia, Portugal)

Spanish chronicler Saavedra Fajardo observes exhaustion:

> “Empires fall not from one blow, but from the burden of too many
> defenses maintained at once.”  
> — Saavedra Fajardo, *Empresas Políticas*

Overextension degrades coherence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FRANCE ASCENDANT, BUT AT COST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia gradually gains advantage.

Factors:
• Administrative centralization
• Fiscal extraction
• Professionalized armies

Yet French society strains.

Mazarin admits fragility:

> “Victory is purchased at the price of patience, money, and silence
> among the people.”  
> — Mazarin correspondence, 1650s

Success depends on endurance, not enthusiasm.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. THE PEACE OF THE PYRENEES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war ends through negotiated settlement (1659).

Outcomes:
• Territorial gains (Roussillon)
• Dynastic marriage
• Spanish decline acknowledged
• No universal resolution

Peace stabilizes rather than transforms Europe.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Franco–Spanish War encodes **anti-hegemony through exhaustion**.

Lessons:
• Universal empire invites coalition resistance.
• Victory through attrition imposes internal cost.
• Balance logic restrains absolutism.
• Francia learns containment, not annihilation.

This war teaches limits through duration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia defines legitimacy through balance.
• Peripheral revolts expose imperial strain.
• Endurance substitutes for decisive victory.
• Dynastic settlement restores equilibrium.
• Universalism is resisted by time.

Tension preserved without verdict.
