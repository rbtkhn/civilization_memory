I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Louis XVI as the **legitimacy-collapse terminal**
of monarchical Francia: the ruler who inherited a hollowed absolutism
and failed to either restore authority or relinquish it coherently.

Louis XVI does not create the Revolution.
He **cannot prevent it**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

Verbatim scholarship (ARC-T-MODERN):

"The continuing relevance of the French Revolution does not mean that
the events of 1789 are simple or that they can offer clear answers to
the questions of our own day."
— Jeremy D. Popkin, *A New World Begins: The History of the French
Revolution* (New York: Basic Books, 2019) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY]

Popkin thus resists **simple blame**—Louis XVI's failure is part of a
complex laboratory of modern politics, not a one-cause verdict.

Descartes had relocated authority from tradition to reason a century and a half earlier:

> "Never to accept anything as true that I did not know evidently to be so."
> — Descartes, *Discours de la méthode* (1637) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Voltaire, who died months before the fall of the Bastille, had framed the monarchy's exposure to opinion:

> "Opinion has caused more trouble on this little earth than plagues or earthquakes."
> — Voltaire, *Dictionnaire philosophique* (1764) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Rousseau had framed the legitimacy the crown could no longer claim:

> "Man is born free; and everywhere he is in chains."
> — Rousseau, *Du contrat social* (1762) (ARC-T-EARLY-MOD) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Louis XVI assumes the throne amid accumulated stress.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reform attempts expose authority weakness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XVI cannot embody a reduced crown.

The crown fits neither absolutism nor democracy.

The execution (1793) is civilizational, not personal.

Dynastic legitimacy is abolished through violence.
