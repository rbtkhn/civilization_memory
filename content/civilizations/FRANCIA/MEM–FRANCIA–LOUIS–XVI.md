I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Louis XVI as the **legitimacy-collapse terminal**
of monarchical Francia: the ruler who inherited a hollowed absolutism
and failed to either restore authority or relinquish it coherently.

Louis XVI does not create the Revolution.
He **cannot prevent it**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INHERITED CRISIS CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XVI assumes the throne amid accumulated stress.

Inherited burdens:
• Fiscal insolvency
• Prestige loss
• Ideological fragmentation
• Court delegitimation

The monarchy is operationally intact but symbolically brittle.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. REFORM WITHOUT SOVEREIGNTY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reform attempts expose authority weakness.

Core failure:
→ Reform requires authority the crown no longer possesses.

Every concession signals weakness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ESTATES-GENERAL & LEGITIMACY TRANSFER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The 1789 convocation triggers authority inversion.

Legitimacy migrates out of the monarchy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONSTITUTIONAL MONARCHY IMPOSSIBILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Louis XVI cannot embody a reduced crown.

The crown fits neither absolutism nor democracy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. FLIGHT, DISTRUST, & DELEGITIMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Flight to Varennes (1791) extinguishes trust.

The king becomes an enemy symbol.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. EXECUTION AS LEGITIMACY TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The execution (1793) is civilizational, not personal.

Dynastic legitimacy is abolished through violence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Hollow legitimacy cannot be repaired under pressure.
• Reform without authority accelerates collapse.
• Procedural concessions transfer sovereignty irreversibly.
• Constitutional monarchy fails when sacral authority lingers.
• Louis XVI marks the end of dynastic Francia.

Rupture preserved without absolution.
