I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Algeria as **Francia's most intimate imperial
contradiction**: a colony treated not as periphery but as extension,
where universalist claims collided with racial hierarchy, settler
domination, and sustained coercion.

Algeria is not an overseas possession.
It is empire brought home—and exposed.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST & SETTLER IMPERIALISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia conquers Algeria through total war.

Characteristics:
• Military pacification campaigns
• Land expropriation
• European settler implantation
• Indigenous legal subordination

Algeria is integrated administratively,
but not civically.

Universalism is suspended in practice.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COLONY AS METROPOLITAN EXTENSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike other colonies, Algeria is framed as France.

Dynamics:
• Departments, not protectorates
• Direct governance from Paris
• Cultural assimilation rhetoric

Reality:
• Political exclusion
• Legal dualism
• Permanent inequality

Inclusion is declarative, not lived.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. UNIVERSALISM VS. COERCION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Algeria exposes universalism's limit.

Patterns:
• Republican language paired with repression
• Citizenship withheld from Muslim population
• Violence normalized as governance

The civilizing mission becomes disciplinary.

Legitimacy depends on force, not consent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WAR OF INDEPENDENCE & MORAL RUPTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decolonization is violent and existential.

Features:
• Insurgency and counterinsurgency
• Torture institutionalized
• Civilian terror
• Settler panic and exodus

The war fractures Francia internally.

Empire collapses psychologically before territorially.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DE GAULLE & IMPERIAL ABANDONMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Resolution requires rupture.

Actions:
• Acceptance of Algerian independence
• Betrayal narrative among settlers and army
• Fifth Republic consolidation

Letting go preserves the state.

Holding on would have destroyed it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. POST-IMPERIAL SHADOW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Algeria remains unresolved memory.

Legacies:
• Immigration tensions
• Identity conflict
• Silence and delayed reckoning
• Moral injury within institutions

The empire ends, but the wound persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Algeria encodes **the terminal contradiction of universal empire**.

Lessons:
• Universalism collapses when hierarchy is permanent.
• Settler colonialism radicalizes resistance.
• Violence corrodes legitimacy faster than loss.
• Abandonment can be preservative.

Algeria is Francia's imperial mirror.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Algeria exposes universalism's hypocrisy.
• Inclusion without equality accelerates revolt.
• Empire can fracture the metropole.
• Letting go can restore coherence.
• Algeria marks Francia's imperial endgame.

Tension preserved without verdict.
