I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the War of the Second Coalition as **Francia’s
existential stress test**: a multi-front confrontation that forced
the Revolutionary state to compress ideology, administration, and
military command into a survivable core under external saturation.

This is not expansion.
It is **revolutionary survival under encirclement**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. COALITION FORMATION & SYSTEMIC PRESSURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The coalition forms to reverse revolutionary contagion.

Participants:
• Britain
• Austria
• Russia
• Ottoman Empire
• Naples and others

Goals:
• Restore ancien régimes
• Contain ideological spread
• Break French military momentum

A British parliamentary address states:

> “The object of the war is not conquest,
> but the security of Europe against principles
> which threaten every throne and altar;
> peace cannot be safe while such a system endures.”
> — British parliamentary debate, 1798

Ideology becomes casus belli.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INTERNAL FRACTURE & COMMAND CRISIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia enters weakened.

Conditions:
• Fiscal exhaustion
• Political instability (Directory)
• Overextended armies
• Maritime isolation

French correspondence reflects strain:

> “We have victories without peace,
> armies without bread,
> generals without confidence,
> and a government without authority.”
> — French official letter, 1799

Survival precedes ambition.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MILITARY RECOVERY & OPERATIONAL ADAPTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite collapse risk, Francia adapts.

Mechanisms:
• Rapid officer promotion
• Operational flexibility
• Concentration against separated enemies
• Exploitation of coalition coordination failures

Russian observations note:

> “The French fight as if necessity
> were their commander-in-chief;
> they move without regard to custom,
> and strike where the enemy is slowest.”
> — Russian officer memoir, 1799

Necessity replaces doctrine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. THE 1799 TURNING POINT & REGIME TRANSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Military crisis catalyzes political consolidation.

Events:
• Defeats in Italy
• Russian withdrawal
• Brumaire coup

Napoleon frames legitimacy pragmatically:

> “The Revolution is finished;
> it is founded on the principles that began it,
> and it ends with the authority necessary to preserve it.”
> — Napoleon Bonaparte, 1799 proclamation

Revolution yields to order.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DIPLOMATIC DECOMPRESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Coalition unity fractures.

Outcomes:
• Russian disengagement
• Austrian isolation
• British strategic recalibration

Peace becomes possible through exhaustion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Second Coalition encodes **compression as survival strategy**.

Lessons:
• Ideology must yield to administration under saturation.
• Coalition breadth creates coordination weakness.
• Regime consolidation follows existential threat.
• Survival precedes legitimacy reconstruction.

Francia endures by shrinking to core competence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Encirclement accelerates regime compression.
• Coalition unity is brittle under divergent aims.
• Revolutionary energy converts to authoritarian order.
• Survival legitimizes consolidation retroactively.
• Francia learns to trade ideology for endurance.

Tension preserved without verdict.
