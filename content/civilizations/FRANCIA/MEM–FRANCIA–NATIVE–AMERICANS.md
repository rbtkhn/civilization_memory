────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Native Americans as **Francia’s indispensable yet
discarded intermediaries** in North America: peoples whose alliances
enabled French presence but whose survival depended on an imperial
system Francia could not sustain.

For Francia, Indigenous nations are partners—
until the system fails.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ALLIANCE-BASED EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia constructs a light-footprint colonial model.

Characteristics:
• Sparse European settlement
• Reliance on fur trade
• Diplomatic alliance networks
• Military cooperation against Anglia

Empire advances through relationship, not replacement.

Native nations are strategic multipliers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DIPLOMACY OVER DISPOSSESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia initially avoids mass displacement.

Patterns:
• Treaty-based coexistence
• Intermarriage and cultural exchange
• Missionary influence without total domination

This contrasts with Anglian settler logic.

Accommodation substitutes for sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MILITARY DEPENDENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Native allies become essential to defense.

Roles:
• Territorial intelligence
• Guerrilla warfare
• Buffer against Anglian expansion

Francia’s military reach is inseparable from Indigenous cooperation.

Alliance masks institutional weakness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. STRUCTURAL BETRAYAL IN DEFEAT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Seven Years’ War ends the alliance system.

Dynamics:
• Francia loses Canada
• Treaties disregard Indigenous sovereignty
• Allies transferred without consent

Diplomacy collapses with imperial retreat.

Partners become liabilities overnight.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. POST-DEFEAT ABANDONMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1763, Francia exits North America.

Consequences:
• Indigenous nations exposed to Anglian expansion
• No enforcement of prior guarantees
• Memory of alliance persists without power

Francia leaves without resolution.

Imperial withdrawal becomes moral abdication.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Native Americans encode **the fragility of alliance-based empire**.

Lessons:
• Influence without permanence fails under pressure.
• Alliances collapse when logistics disappear.
• Moral partnership cannot survive strategic defeat.
• Light empires abandon partners first.

Francia learns the cost of imperial thinness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia’s empire depended on Indigenous agency.
• Defeat converts allies into victims.
• Diplomacy requires enforcement to endure.
• Alliance is not sovereignty.
• Native memory preserves Francia’s imperial limits.

Tension preserved without verdict.
