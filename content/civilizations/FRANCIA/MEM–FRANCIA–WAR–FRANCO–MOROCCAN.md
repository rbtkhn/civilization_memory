I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Franco–Moroccan conflicts as a case of
**successful coercive expansion on the imperial periphery**: Francia
projects authority into North Africa through incremental force,
administrative penetration, and diplomatic isolation of the target
state.

Morocco is not a peer system.
It is a **managed frontier**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT: NORTH AFRICA AS CONTIGUOUS ZONE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Morocco occupies a critical position:
• Proximity to Algeria
• Mediterranean–Atlantic hinge
• Weak central authority
• International competition (Germany, Spain)

Francia frames intervention as stabilization rather than conquest.

Universalism is bureaucratized.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INCREMENTAL MILITARY PENETRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia advances through calibrated escalation.

Methods:
• Punitive expeditions
• Protection of nationals
• Railway and port security
• Support of compliant elites

Force is continuous but limited.

War is fragmented into actions, not campaigns.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DIPLOMATIC CONTAINMENT OF EUROPEAN RIVALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Success depends on coalition control.

Key moments:
• First Moroccan Crisis (1905–1906)
• Algeciras Conference
• Franco–British alignment

Germany challenges—but fails to mobilize enforcement.

Diplomacy secures the rear.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ADMINISTRATION BEFORE LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Control precedes belief.

Patterns:
• Military governors
• Fiscal oversight
• Legal restructuring
• Protectorate logic

Authority is imposed, not negotiated.

Local legitimacy is secondary.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. 1912 PROTECTORATE ESTABLISHMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Formal control is declared.

Outcome:
• Moroccan sovereignty hollowed
• French administration dominant
• Colonial order stabilized

Universalist language masks coercion.

Rule is effective—but externally framed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Franco–Moroccan conflicts encode **imperial competence under
asymmetry**.

Lessons:
• Peripheral systems tolerate coercion.
• Incremental force limits coalition backlash.
• Administration substitutes for legitimacy.
• Universalism adapts into technocratic rule.

Morocco is a success because resistance is isolated.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia dominates when escalation is gradual.
• Colonial wars reward administrative patience.
• Diplomatic rear security is decisive.
• Universalism mutates into bureaucracy.
• Morocco contrasts sharply with Mexico.

Tension preserved without verdict.
