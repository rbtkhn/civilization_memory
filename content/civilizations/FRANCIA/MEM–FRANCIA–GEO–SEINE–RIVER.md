────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Seine River as **Francia's administrative
sovereignty axis**: the river through which centralized authority,
bureaucracy, finance, and symbolic state power are projected from Paris
into the national body.

The Seine is not Francia's origin.
It is Francia's **command instrument**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ROMAN PARISII & URBAN NUCLEUS (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Seine anchors early urban continuity. Roman Lutetia develops around
river crossings, trade concentration, and administrative permanence.
Urban gravity precedes empire; the river creates the chokepoint through
which authority and resources flow.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PARIS AS PERMANENT CAPITAL (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike Loire courts, Paris becomes fixed. Fiscal centralization, legal
codification, and bureaucratic continuity concentrate along the Seine.
Authority detaches from the ruler and resides in the place. The river
is the projection corridor from capital to territory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ADMINISTRATION, TAXATION, AND CONTROL (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Seine enables rapid decree dissemination, resource extraction, and
surveillance. State power becomes procedural; obedience is routinized.
Command rivers privilege speed over depth—a structural constraint that
magnifies both control and fragility.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. REVOLUTIONARY AND MODERN INTENSIFICATION (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revolution radicalizes Seine centrality. Centralization accelerates
risk. The river carries the symbolic and administrative load of the
nation; overcentralization magnifies shock when legitimacy or capacity
fails.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SYMBOLIC & CULTURAL DOMINANCE (STRATEGIC)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Seine carries monuments, media, and national narratives. Symbol
and administration merge. Paris becomes France. The civilizational
memory function: fixed capitals enable bureaucracy; command rivers
privilege speed over depth; the Seine governs even when it destabilizes.

X. ARC-T-MODERN
