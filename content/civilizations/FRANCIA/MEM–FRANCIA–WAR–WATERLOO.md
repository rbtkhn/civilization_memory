I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Waterloo as **Francia’s terminal legitimacy
rupture of revolutionary–imperial authority**: the decisive moment
when Bonapartist charisma, military virtuosity, and universalist
ambition finally exceeded Francia’s material, diplomatic, and moral
capacity.

For Francia, Waterloo is not merely defeat.
It is **exhaustion made visible**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. NAPOLEONIC LEGITIMACY AT TERMINAL STAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
By 1815, Napoleonic authority is already overextended.

Conditions:
• Charismatic legitimacy without dynastic depth
• Continental domination without stable consent
• Militarized governance without political regeneration
• Universalist rhetoric detached from coalition reality

Authority persists through momentum, not renewal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRATEGIC GAMBLE & COMPRESSED TIME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Waterloo is forced by temporal pressure.

Dynamics:
• Coalition convergence
• Inability to delay or divide enemies
• Need for decisive battle
• No margin for strategic patience

Bonapartist logic requires victory *now*.

Time advantage is gone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BATTLEFIELD FAILURE AS SYSTEMIC EXPOSURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The defeat reflects more than tactical error.

Revealed limits:
• Exhausted manpower
• Reduced officer depth
• Coalition coordination superiority
• Loss of surprise and initiative

Military brilliance cannot compensate for systemic depletion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DEFEAT AS LEGITIMACY TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Waterloo extinguishes Bonapartist authority instantly.

Consequences:
• No recovery narrative
• No institutional buffer
• No successor legitimacy

Charisma collapses faster than dynasties ever did.

The empire ends in a single afternoon.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RESTORATION WITHOUT RESOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-Waterloo settlement restores monarchy, not belief.

Outcomes:
• Bourbon Restoration imposed externally
• Legitimacy remains hollow
• Revolutionary memory unresolved

Authority returns without authority grammar.

The wound closes superficially.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Waterloo becomes Francia’s **anti-myth**.

Encoded lessons:
• Universalism cannot survive coalition reality indefinitely
• Charismatic authority has no reserve depth
• Military supremacy cannot substitute for legitimacy regeneration

Defeat finalizes the revolutionary cycle.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Charisma without renewal collapses abruptly.
• Universal ambition invites terminal coalition.
• Military genius cannot outpace exhaustion forever.
