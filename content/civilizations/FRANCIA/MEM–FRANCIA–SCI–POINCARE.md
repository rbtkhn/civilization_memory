────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Henri Poincaré as **Francia's philosopher of
science and founder of dynamical systems**: the mathematician who
pioneered topology, discovered deterministic chaos (three-body
problem), and argued that scientific theories are conventional—useful
rather than uniquely true—while nearly anticipating special relativity.

Poincaré is not a dogmatic realist.
He is a **conventionalist**—science as choice and convention.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL CONTEXT: THIRD REPUBLIC SCIENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Poincaré emerges in a Francia that had made *science* a republican
virtue and that competed with Germania in mathematics and physics.

Conditions:
• Sorbonne and École polytechnique; Bureau des longitudes
• Dreyfus affair (Poincaré testified for Dreyfus)
• Rise of German physics (Maxwell, Hertz); French response

Poincaré on the value of science:

"Science does not teach the truth; it teaches us how to seek it."
— Henri Poincaré, *La Valeur de la science* (1905) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Science is **method**, not revelation. Convention and utility replace
certainty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. TOPOLOGY & THE POINCARÉ CONJECTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Poincaré founds algebraic topology (homotopy, fundamental group) and
poses the Poincaré conjecture (every simply connected closed 3-manifold
is a 3-sphere)—proved only in 2003 (Perelman).

Contributions:
• Topological invariance; classification of surfaces
• Poincaré conjecture (1904)
• Foundation for 20th-century geometry and physics

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CHAOS: THE THREE-BODY PROBLEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Poincaré shows that the three-body problem (Newtonian mechanics) can
exhibit deterministic but unpredictable behavior—no closed-form
solution; sensitive dependence on initial conditions.

Implications:
• Determinism does not imply predictability.
• Laplace's demon is refuted in practice.
• Foundation for chaos theory and nonlinear dynamics.

Poincaré on chance and law:

"Chance is only the measure of our ignorance."
— Henri Poincaré, *Science et hypothèse* (1902) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

But that "ignorance" can be structural—chaos is real, not merely
epistemic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATIVITY PRECURSOR & CONVENTIONALISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Poincaré develops the Lorentz transformation and discusses the
relativity of simultaneity; Einstein's 1905 paper supersedes, but
Poincaré's conventionalism—geometry and physics as conventions—shapes
philosophy of science.

Poincaré on geometry:

"Geometry is not true; it is advantageous."
— Henri Poincaré, *Science et hypothèse* (1902) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-CONTEXTUAL]

Truth yields to utility; science is **choice**, not discovery of
absolute structure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Poincaré encodes **Francia's conventionalist philosophy of science**:
certainty is replaced by convention and utility; chaos limits
Laplace's dream; relativity (and its precursors) unsettle absolute
space and time.

Lessons:
• Science teaches how to seek, not the truth itself.
• Determinism does not imply predictability.
• Geometry and physics are conventional—advantageous, not true.
