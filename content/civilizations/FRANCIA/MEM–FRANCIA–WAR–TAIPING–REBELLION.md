────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Taiping Rebellion as **Francia’s first sustained
encounter with Chinese civil war at civilizational scale**: a conflict
where Francia abandoned ideological evangelism and instead aligned with
imperial order preservation, commercial continuity, and missionary
protection.

Francia does not fight as a sovereign belligerent.
It intervenes as a stabilizer.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FRAMING: HERESY, ORDER, AND SCALE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Taiping movement is interpreted through dual lenses:
• Millenarian heresy threatening civilizational continuity
• Systemic disorder endangering treaty-port commerce

Zeng Guofan frames the rebellion’s danger in civilizational terms:

> “This bandit calamity is unlike ordinary rebellion. It overturns
> the rites, abolishes the classics, confuses father and son, ruler
> and minister, and seeks to replace the teachings that bind the realm.
> If not extinguished, the root of order itself will perish.”  
> — Zeng Guofan, Memorial to the Throne, 1853

Francia recognizes existential disruption, not reform.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FRANCIA’S STRATEGIC POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia’s interests converge around:
• Protection of missionaries
• Stability of treaty ports
• Suppression of anti-foreign violence
• Continuity of Qing administrative order

French diplomatic correspondence emphasizes restraint:

> “France has no interest in encouraging doctrines that dissolve
> authority. Commerce requires peace; missions require protection;
> neither can flourish amid revolutionary fanaticism.”  
> — French Legation dispatch, Shanghai, 1860

Order outweighs ideological affinity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. COORDINATION WITH IMPERIAL AND FOREIGN FORCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia acts indirectly:
• Supports Qing suppression
• Coordinates with Anglia and regional commanders
• Participates in foreign-trained contingents

The Ever Victorious Army becomes a template of hybrid control.

Charles Gordon describes the priority clearly:

> “The task was not conquest but the restoration of order; every
> advance sought to secure towns, reopen trade, and convince the
> population that stability, not revolution, was the path to survival.”  
> — Charles Gordon, *Personal Journals*, 1863

Military success is subordinated to administrative calm.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MISSIONARY PARADOX
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite Christian language, Taiping theology alienates European missions.

French missionaries report:

> “Their doctrine bears Christian names yet denies Christian truth.
> They destroy churches, persecute priests, and impose a violent creed
> that recognizes no authority but their Heavenly King.”  
> — Paris Foreign Missions Society report, 1859

Shared vocabulary masks incompatible order grammars.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. OUTCOME & MEMORY ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The rebellion’s defeat confirms Francia’s emerging China logic:
• Stability precedes moral transformation
• Imperial order is preferable to millenarian collapse
• Intervention must be limited and coalition-based

Francia learns to favor **containment over conversion**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Taiping Rebellion encodes **early order-first intervention logic**.

Lessons:
• Scale transforms rebellion into civilizational threat.
• Missionary language does not equal moral alignment.
• Commercial systems require imperial continuity.
• Francia intervenes to preserve order, not reshape China.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Francia sides with order against millenarian rupture.
• Indirect intervention becomes preferred method.
• Stability is prioritized over ideological sympathy.
• Hybrid forces substitute for formal conquest.
• China teaches Francia restraint before dominance.

Tension preserved without verdict.
