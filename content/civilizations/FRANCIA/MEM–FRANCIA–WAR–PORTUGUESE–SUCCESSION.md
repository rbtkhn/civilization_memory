I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Portuguese Restoration War as **Francia’s
indirect use of peripheral rebellion** to fracture Habsburg dominance
without direct annexation or universal claim.

Portugal is not absorbed.
It is **leveraged**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PORTUGUESE REVOLT AS SYSTEMIC OPENING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Portugal revolts against Spanish rule in 1640.

Context:
• Spanish fiscal extraction
• Imperial overstretch
• Local elite resistance

Portuguese declaration emphasizes autonomy:

> “We take arms not to conquer, but to recover the liberty of a kingdom
> unjustly absorbed.”  
> — Portuguese Restoration manifesto, 1640

Legitimacy is framed defensively.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FRANCIA’S INDIRECT STRATEGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia supports Portugal diplomatically and materially.

Richelieu’s logic applies:

> “One weakens a rival more by sustaining his adversaries than by
> exhausting oneself in direct conflict.”  
> — Richelieu, correspondence

Portugal functions as strategic diversion.

Balance is pursued obliquely.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. LIMITS OF SUPPORT & ALLIANCE LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia avoids total commitment.

Constraints:
• Ongoing Franco–Spanish War
• Fiscal pressure
• Risk of Anglian competition

Support is calibrated, not unconditional.

Portugal fights for itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RECOGNITION & SYSTEMIC EFFECT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Spain recognizes Portuguese independence in 1668.

Effects:

• Reinforced balance-of-power logic
• Peripheral autonomy preserved

Francia gains without governance burden.
