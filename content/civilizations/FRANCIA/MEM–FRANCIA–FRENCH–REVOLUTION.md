I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the French Revolution as **Francia’s legitimacy
detonation**: the event in which universalist moral claims replaced all
inherited authority at once, collapsing monarchy, custom, and restraint
in favor of abstract sovereignty.

The Revolution is not reform.
It is reset.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PRE-REVOLUTIONARY CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The ancien régime fails across multiple layers.

Context:
• Fiscal insolvency
• Privilege delegitimation
• Elite fragmentation
• Enlightenment moral critique
• Loss of confidence in absolutist symbolism

Legitimacy decays faster than institutions can adapt.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. UNIVERSALISM AS REVOLUTIONARY ENGINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revolutionary authority is asserted philosophically.

Core dynamics:
• Rights precede institutions
• Nation replaces crown
• Moral clarity substitutes for procedure
• Law becomes expression of virtue

Legitimacy is derived from principle, not continuity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DESTRUCTION OF CONTINUITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Existing structures are actively dismantled.

Actions:
• Abolition of estates
• Elimination of noble privilege
• Church subordination
• Calendar and symbolic reset

Memory is treated as corruption.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RADICALIZATION & THE TERROR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Abstract legitimacy demands enforcement.

Dynamics:
• Competing interpretations of virtue
• Fear of counterrevolution
• Coercion replaces consent
• Terror becomes governance tool

Moral unanimity is enforced violently.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. REGIME INSTABILITY & CYCLICAL RESET
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority fails to stabilize.

Sequence:
• Constitutional monarchy
• Republic
• Jacobin dictatorship
• Thermidorian reaction
• Directory

Legitimacy resets become normalized.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. EXTERNALIZATION THROUGH WAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revolution exports itself militarily.

Effects:
• Mass mobilization
• Ideological warfare
• Francia framed as universal liberator
• Europe destabilized

War becomes legitimacy amplifier and escape valve.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. TRANSITION TO IMPERIAL SYNTHESIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon resolves revolutionary chaos.

Outcome:
• Order restored through authority
• Universalism retained
• Administration centralized
• Revolution preserved in law, not politics

The Revolution survives by abandoning itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Reset legitimacy normalized
• Universalism fused to identity
• Cycles of moral rupture recur
• Authority remains declarative

The Revolution becomes Francia’s defining fracture—and reference.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Universalism destabilizes without procedure.
• Moral clarity accelerates political violence.
• Destruction outpaces construction.
• Revolution exports legitimacy crisis.
• The French Revolution is Francia’s permanent temptation.

Tension preserved without verdict.
