## II. Recommended Actions (Prioritized)

### Tier 1 — High leverage, low conflict

1. **RLL candidate: Credit as coalition glue (from reading 5)**  
   - **Proposal:** RLL–FRANCIA–0018 (CANDIDATE): Coalition persistence is a function of balancer fiscal capacity; extraction-based hegemony is fiscally brittle. When the hegemon cannot fund allies (only extract), defection follows once a balancer offers subsidies and a destination.  
   - **Derivation:** Second-pass Mearsheimer (Ferguson, WATERLOO, NAPOLEON, CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM).  
   - **Test cases:** Napoleonic coalition (Britain subsidies vs French plunder); optional cross-civ (e.g. later alliance systems).  
   - **Benefit:** Generalizable; supports STATE analysis of “who can pay for the coalition” in any balancing scenario.

2. **Continuity insights (2–3 MEMs)**  
   - **MEM–FRANCIA–WAR–TRAFALGAR:** Add one bullet: “Trafalgar constrained strategy space to continental options only; path dependence made every subsequent move (Continental System, Peninsula, Russia) increase overextension risk. Removing the invasion option also removed British incentive to settle (bargaining space collapse).” (Readings 3 + 7.)  
   - **MEM–FRANCIA–CONTINENTAL–SYSTEM:** Add one bullet: “Continental closure is structurally coalition-producing: the geometry of enforcement (control every coastline) maximized the number of states with incentive to balance or defect.” (Reading 4.)  
   - **MEM–FRANCIA–WAR–NAPOLEON–WATERLOO:** Add one bullet: “Waterloo is the aftermath of structural defeat—the system (allies, satellites, credit) had already collapsed by 1813–1814; the battle executed a corpse.” (Reading 8.)

3. **Use the second-pass doc as discovery index**  
   - In LEARN or STATE, when analyzing Napoleonic France or analogous cases (overextension, coalition, defection), open the second-pass table (Section XI) and pull the relevant reading. No file change—**procedural benefit**: the ten readings become a checklist for “what structural angles did we already derive?”

### Tier 2 — Refinements and doctrine

4. **SYNTHESIS 0005 / defection (reading 6)**  
   - Add to extraction-collapse mechanism: “Observable defection acts as common-knowledge generator: once one satellite defects, others learn that punishment is infeasible, accelerating cascade.”  
   - **Benefit:** Sharper prediction (first defection is both force transfer and signal).

5. **Doctrine 04 (post-hegemonic) optional note**  
   - In CIV–DOCTRINE–FRANCE or in a doctrine-check memo: “Napoleon/Themistocles encodes structural reversal: would-be hegemon becomes supplicant to balancer. Post-hegemonic anxiety is partly memory of that reversal.” (Reading 9.)  
   - **Benefit:** Doctrine 04 gains a canonical image (letter to Prince Regent).

6. **Second-order SDI (reading 10)**

   - **Benefit:** Avoids attributing “learning” that the MEMs themselves say did not occur.
