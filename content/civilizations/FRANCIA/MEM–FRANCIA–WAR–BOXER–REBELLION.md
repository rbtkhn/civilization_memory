────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Boxer Rebellion as **Francia’s violent rupture
with China’s popular sphere**: a moment when anti-foreign legitimacy
collapses into mass violence, forcing Francia into open coalition
intervention.

Unlike Taiping, neutrality is impossible.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ANTI-FOREIGN MOBILIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Boxers frame foreigners as existential pollutants.

Imperial edicts reveal ambivalence turning to alignment:

> “The people’s anger is righteous; the foreigners have oppressed
> the Empire and despised our customs. If they are not expelled,
> calamity will continue to afflict the realm.”  
> — Qing court proclamation, 1900

Popular violence gains partial imperial sanction.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ATTACK ON MISSIONS AND LEGATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
French missionaries and converts are targeted systematically.

Eyewitness testimony records:

> “Churches were burned, the faithful slaughtered, and foreign
> residents hunted as enemies of Heaven. The violence was not
> spontaneous alone but tolerated, even encouraged, by local officials.”  
> — Bishop Alphonse Favier, Peking diary, 1900

Religious presence becomes liability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FRANCIA’S DECISION FOR FORAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia abandons restraint.

Strategic logic:
• Protect nationals
• Restore treaty regime
• Reassert foreign deterrence
• Prevent precedent of impunity

A French parliamentary statement clarifies intent:

> “France does not seek conquest in China, but it cannot permit the
> massacre of its citizens or the nullification of treaties by mobs
> armed with superstition and tolerated by authority.”  
> — French Chamber of Deputies debate, 1900

Force is framed as defensive restoration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COALITION WARFARE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Eight-Nation Alliance executes punitive intervention.

Characteristics:
• Multinational command friction
• Rapid escalation
• Symbolic occupation of Beijing

Order is restored through humiliation, not partnership.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LEGITIMACY CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Boxer Protocol imposes indemnities and constraints.

China’s legitimacy fractures:
• Imperial authority discredited
• Popular resentment hardened
• Foreign presence militarized

Francia recognizes diminishing returns.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Boxer Rebellion encodes **the cost of legitimacy collapse**.

Lessons:
• Popular violence forces overt intervention.
• Coalition punishment restores order but poisons legitimacy.
• Missionary presence becomes strategic vulnerability.
• Francia shifts from order-partner to enforcer.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Anti-foreign legitimacy triggers coalition force.
• Moral presence invites violent backlash.
• Intervention restores order but deepens resentment.
• China’s internal fracture accelerates.
• Francia’s China posture hardens after 1900.

Tension preserved without verdict.
