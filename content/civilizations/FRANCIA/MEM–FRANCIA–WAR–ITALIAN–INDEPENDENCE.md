I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Francia’s role in the Wars of Italian Independence
as a case of **successful but bounded intervention**: Francia supports
national liberation selectively, calibrating universalist rhetoric to
coalition realities, dynastic risk, and domestic legitimacy.

Unlike Mexico, Italy represents **intervention within a shared
civilizational grammar**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT: ITALY AS NEAR SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Italy occupies a liminal position in Francia’s strategic imagination:
• Roman and Catholic civilizational inheritance
• Proximity to French borders
• Austrian dominance as a rival constraint
• Revolutionary resonance without colonial distance

Italian independence is framed as both moral and strategic.

Napoleon III presents intervention as liberation rather than conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. 1848–1849: CAUTION AND RETRACTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early revolutionary waves expose Francia’s limits.

Dynamics:
• Competing Italian revolutionary factions
• Papal sensitivity
• Austrian resilience
• French domestic instability

Francia intervenes episodically (notably in Rome) but avoids full
commitment.

Universalism is checked by Catholic legitimacy concerns.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. 1859 WAR: SELECTIVE ESCALATION AGAINST AUSTRIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Second Italian War of Independence marks decisive engagement.

Context:
• Franco–Piedmontese alliance
• Austrian overextension
• Diplomatic preparation
• Controlled war aims

Napoleon III articulates restraint explicitly:

> “France does not intend to overthrow Europe; she intends to free Italy
> from foreign domination, while respecting the equilibrium necessary
> for general peace.”  
> — Napoleon III, proclamation, 1859

War is framed as **limited liberation**, not systemic rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MILITARY SUCCESS AND POLITICAL MANAGEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Victories at Magenta and Solferino deliver results quickly.

Outcomes:
• Austrian withdrawal from Lombardy
• Enhanced French prestige
• Territorial compensation (Nice, Savoy)
• Preservation of European balance

Crucially, Francia halts escalation.

Victory is converted into settlement, not pursuit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. UNIVERSALISM WITH CONSTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Italian intervention succeeds because:
• Civilizational familiarity exists
• Coalition signaling is managed
• War aims are finite
• Withdrawal is planned

Contrast with Mexico is stark.

Here, legitimacy is **co-produced**, not imposed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. MORAL SHOCK AND STRATEGIC REASSESSMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Solferino produces humanitarian shock.

Henry Dunant later recalls:

> “Never had I seen such misery. The suffering of thousands, abandoned
> without aid, revealed the terrible cost of modern war and imposed a
> moral obligation that no victory could justify ignoring.”  
> — Henry Dunant, *A Memory of Solferino*, 1862

This episode reshapes French sensitivity to escalation and reputation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Italian Wars encode **Francia’s optimal intervention envelope**:

• Intervention succeeds within shared civilizational space
• Coalition preparation limits backlash
• Victory must be bounded and narratively framed
• Moral authority is fragile and must be protected

Italy shows Francia how to win—and when to stop.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Proximity stabilizes legitimacy.
• Universalism works best near its origin.
• Coalition management precedes success.
• Withdrawal timing determines legacy.
• Italy is Francia’s last clean intervention victory.

Tension preserved without verdict.
