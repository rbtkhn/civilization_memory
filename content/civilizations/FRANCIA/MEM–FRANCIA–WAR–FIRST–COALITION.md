I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the War of the First Coalition as **Francia’s
first universalist stress test**: the moment revolutionary legitimacy
collided with dynastic Europe, converting ideological declaration
into existential continental war.

This war is not defensive escalation.
It is **universalism made kinetic**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVOLUTIONARY DECLARATION & WAR LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
War emerges from ideological exposure.

The Legislative Assembly proclaims:

> “The French nation declares that it will treat as enemies
> the peoples who, refusing or renouncing liberty and equality,
> are willing to preserve, recall, or treat with their princes
> and privileged castes.”  
> — Decree of November 1792

Universal principles redefine neutrality.

Legitimacy becomes contagious.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DYNASTIC EUROPE & CONTAINMENT IMPULSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Coalition war is preemptive containment.

Leopold II warns of ideological rupture:

> “The French Revolution threatens all governments,
> not by arms alone, but by doctrines which dissolve
> the foundations of authority itself.”  
> — Leopold II, diplomatic correspondence, 1791

Monarchies mobilize to suppress example, not territory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MASS MOBILIZATION & NEW WAR GRAMMAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia invents national war.

The *levée en masse* declares:

> “Young men shall go to battle; married men shall forge arms;
> women shall make tents and clothes; children shall turn old
> linen into lint; the aged shall arouse courage and preach hatred
> of kings.”  
> — Decree of August 23, 1793

Society becomes weapon.

War becomes total before industry.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INTERNAL TERROR & EXTERNAL SURVIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Survival requires coercion inward.

Saint-Just asserts:

> “Those who make revolutions halfway only dig their own graves.
> The Republic owes protection only to its defenders.”  
> — Saint-Just, Convention speech, 1794

Legitimacy is enforced violently.

Order is saved at moral cost.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. OUTCOME & STRATEGIC TRANSFORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Francia survives and expands.

Results:
• Territorial gains
• Export of revolutionary administration
• Militarization of legitimacy

Victory validates universalism—but normalizes permanent war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The First Coalition encodes **universalism’s escalation logic**.

Lessons:
• Declaration triggers coalition.
• Ideology converts war into survival test.
• Mass legitimacy compensates for institutional collapse.
• Victory entrenches militarized governance.

This war makes Francia dangerous—and durable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Universal claims abolish neutrality.
• Coalitions form against ideas, not borders.
• Mass mobilization substitutes for legitimacy.
• Survival justifies terror.
• Victory hardens revolutionary identity.

Tension preserved without verdict.
