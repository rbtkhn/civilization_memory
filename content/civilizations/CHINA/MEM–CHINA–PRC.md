I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the People’s Republic of China (PRC) as a **revolutionary
reconstitution of Chinese state power**: a regime that resolved the modern
Chinese problem of fragmentation by achieving near-total monopolization of
coercive, administrative, and symbolic authority across a vast territory.

The PRC is remembered as a **system of state reconstruction**, not merely a
government.

Contradictions are preserved without moral synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVOLUTIONARY FOUNDING MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The PRC emerged from prolonged civil war, foreign invasion, and the collapse
of Republican authority.

Foundational characteristics:
• Peasant-based mobilization  
• Leninist discipline  
• Ideological legitimacy over constitutional continuity  
• Victory through endurance rather than decisive battle  

Mao framed seizure as prerequisite to order:
> “枪杆子里面出政权。”
(Political power grows out of the barrel of a gun.)

Authority was consolidated before being normalized.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGITIMACY THROUGH MOBILIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early PRC legitimacy derived from **transformational action**, not stability.

Key mechanisms:
• Land reform  
• Class struggle  
• Mass campaigns  
• Revolutionary justice  

Legitimacy equaled motion:
> “不断革命。”
(Continuous revolution.)

Disruption was the method of consolidation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. PARTY–STATE FUSION & PENETRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The PRC fused Party authority with state administration, reversing the
Republican pattern in which society fragmented the state.

Structural features:
• CCP supremacy over law and institutions  
• Cadre-based governance  
• Vertical penetration to village level  
• Organizational control embedded in administration  

Governance axiom:
> “党政军民学，东西南北中，党是领导一切的。”
(The Party leads everything.)

This penetration exceeded all prior Chinese regimes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RADICALIZATION, FAILURE, & INTERNAL LEARNING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mao era included catastrophic experiments.

Key episodes:
• Great Leap Forward  
• Cultural Revolution  

Consequences:
• Mass death  
• Institutional breakdown  
• Elite trauma  

Failure was internalized rather than delegitimizing:
> “错误也是经验。”
(Errors are also experience.)

The system learned without surrendering authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. REFORM, ADAPTATION, & PERFORMANCE LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-1978 reforms redefined authority without abandoning Party supremacy.

Core shifts:
• Economic liberalization without political pluralism  
• Decollectivization  
• Technocratic governance  
• Global integration  

Deng’s formulation:
> “不管黑猫白猫，抓到老鼠就是好猫。”
(It doesn’t matter if the cat is black or white, so long as it catches mice.)

Legitimacy migrated from ideology to performance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. TERRITORIAL INTEGRATION & FRONTIER MANAGEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The PRC reasserted control over historic peripheries.

Key zones:
• Xinjiang  
• Tibet  
• Inner Mongolia  
• Hong Kong / Macau  

Control logic evolved:
• Military incorporation → infrastructural integration  
• Ideological campaigns → stability maintenance  

Strategic axiom:
> “稳定压倒一切。”
(Stability overrides everything.)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. NATIONALISM, MEMORY, & EXTERNAL–INTERNAL DIALECTIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The PRC fused nationalism with historical grievance.

Core narratives:
• Century of Humiliation  
• Anti-imperial resistance  
• Sovereignty restoration  

Grievance memory functions dually:
• Binding internal loyalty  
• Justifying external firmness  

External pressure reinforces cohesion:
> “发展是硬道理。”
(Development is the hard truth.)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTEMPORARY CONFIGURATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The present PRC is characterized by:

• Centralized authority  
• Organizational discipline  
• Ideological revival  
• Strategic patience  

Control has shifted from mass mobilization toward routinized stability
maintenance.

The Party frames continuity as destiny:
> “中华民族伟大复兴。”
(The great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation.)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The PRC solved the 20th-century Chinese dilemma: building a state stronger than society.
• Coercive consolidation precedes legitimacy normalization.
• Revolutionary authority can outlast revolutionary ideology.
• Performance legitimacy substitutes for electoral consent.
• Stability is treated as a civilizational good, not a policy choice.
• Revolutionary regimes that adapt can outlast their founding generation.

Power is preserved through adaptation, not rigidity.
