────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Yangtze River (长江, Chang Jiang) as the
**heartland corridor and strategic axis** of Chinese civilizational
memory: the space where **control of riverine and coastal logistics**
determined capacity to sustain southern polities, supply northern
capitals (via the Grand Canal), and project force—and where the
Mongol/Yuan naval–riverine pivot (Xiangyang, 1268–1273) became the
**structural condition** for conquest of the Southern Song. The
Yangtze is also the **humiliation axis**: Nanjing (Treaty of Nanjing
1842, fall 1937), Shanghai (treaty port), Chongqing (wartime redoubt
when Nanjing fell). One geography—conquest corridor and humiliation
corridor.

The Yangtze is remembered as **river logic**: control the river
and key nodes (Xiangyang, Nanjing, Shanghai, Chongqing), and you
control bulk movement and political redoubts. Contradictions are
preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & STRUCTURAL MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Yangtze (长江) rises on the Tibetan Plateau and flows east to
the East China Sea—crossing Sichuan, Hubei, Jiangxi, Anhui,
Jiangsu; linking Chengdu, Chongqing, Wuhan, Nanjing, Shanghai.
Key nodes: **Xiangyang** (Han River confluence)—decisive in Song–Yuan
war; **Nanjing**—treaty namesake 1842, ROC capital, fall 1937;
**Shanghai**—treaty port 1842, dominant maritime node; **Chongqing**—
wartime ROC capital 1937–1946, interior redoubt. The Grand Canal
links the Yangtze to the Yellow River and to Beijing (Dadu).

Key structural features:
• Bulk transport: grain (漕运) and goods along the river; Grand Canal southern terminus (Hangzhou area)
• Conquest corridor: Yuan naval–riverine campaign (Xiangyang, downriver); land for speed, water for bulk
• Humiliation corridor: Nanjing 1842 and 1937; Shanghai treaty port; Chongqing as refuge when coast fell
• Chokepoint logic: control Xiangyang = control Han–Yangtze; control Nanjing/Shanghai = control lower Yangtze

Terrain enforces **river logic**: control the river and key nodes, control the heartland.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. YANGTZE AS CONQUEST AND SUPPLY CORRIDOR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Yangtze encodes **conquest and supply**: (1) **Song–Yuan**—Kublai
built naval and riverine capacity; Xiangyang (1268–1273) was the
decisive node; after Xiangyang fell, Yuan forces could project
downriver and along the coast; Song could not hold. (2) **Grand
Canal**—links Yangtze to Yellow River and Beijing; grain moved
north for capital supply; the Canal's southern terminus is in the
Yangtze delta (Hangzhou area). (3) **Bulk logic**—Weatherford: armies
move quickest by horse; bulk moves best by water; the Yangtze and
Canal enabled sustained siege and logistics. MEM–CHINA–WAR–SONG–YUAN
and MEM–CHINA–GRAND–CANAL depend on the Yangtze as the **southern
arm** of the conquest and supply corridor.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. YANGTZE AS HUMILIATION AND REDOUBT AXIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The same river encodes **humiliation and redoubt**: (1) **Nanjing**
(1842)—Treaty of Nanjing signed; five treaty ports opened; Nanjing
gave its name to the first unequal treaty. (2) **Shanghai**—treaty
port 1842; dominant maritime node; foreign concession and commercial
penetration. (3) **Nanjing (1937)**—fall of the ROC capital; flight
to Chongqing. (4) **Chongqing**—interior redoubt; ROC government
held there 1937–1946; no treaty port; legitimacy retreated upriver
when the coast fell. The Yangtze thus carries **both** the humiliation
nodes (Nanjing, Shanghai) and the redoubt node (Chongqing)—contrast
with Pearl River (cession at Hong Kong; SEZ at Shenzhen). MEM–CHINA–NANJING,
MEM–CHINA–SHANGHAI, MEM–CHINA–CHONGQING are the **node MEMs**; this
file is the **geographic frame** for the Yangtze axis.
