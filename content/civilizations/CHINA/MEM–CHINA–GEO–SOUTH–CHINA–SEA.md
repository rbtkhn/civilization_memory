I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the South China Sea (南海) as the **maritime
corridor and contested space** of Chinese civilizational memory:
the sea onto which the Pearl River delta (Canton, Hong Kong,
Shenzhen) opens, through which the Taiwan Strait connects
to the wider Pacific, and across which PRC sovereignty claims
(Nine-Dash Line, island-building, EEZ disputes) are projected.
The South China Sea is not only a trade route but a **theatre
of sovereignty assertion**: control of islands, reefs, and
sea lanes encodes both humiliation (colonial and treaty-era
fragmentation) and reconstitution (PRC claims, naval presence,
reclamation). Multiple claimants (PRC, ROC, Vietnam, Philippines,
Malaysia, Brunei) overlay the same waters—one geography,
multiple sovereignty layers.

The South China Sea is remembered as **maritime chokepoint
logic**: whoever controls key passages and features can
constrain or enable projection. Contradictions are
preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & STRUCTURAL MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The South China Sea lies between the mainland, Taiwan,
the Philippines, Borneo, and the Malay Peninsula—a
semi-enclosed sea with major shipping lanes, fisheries,
and subsurface resources. It connects to the Taiwan
Strait (north), the Luzon Strait (east), the Malacca
Strait (southwest), and the Pearl River delta (northwest).
Key structural features: **first island chain** (Japan–
Taiwan–Philippines) bounds the sea to the east; **Paracel**
and **Spratly** island groups are contested; **Nine-Dash
Line** (PRC claim) encompasses most of the sea.

Key structural features:
• Trade corridor: historic and contemporary shipping; Pearl River delta and Southeast Asian ports
• Sovereignty disputes: PRC/ROC claims (Nine-Dash Line); Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei counter-claims
• First island chain: South China Sea as space between mainland and first chain; projection vs containment
• Island-building and EEZ: contemporary militarization and reclamation; UNCLOS and customary law contested

Terrain enforces **maritime logic**: control of features and lanes shapes who can project and who can deny.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SEA AS TRADE AND HUMILIATION CORRIDOR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The South China Sea encodes **connectivity and humiliation**:
(1) **Historic trade**—Canton system and treaty ports
(Guangzhou, Hong Kong) faced this sea; Western and
Japanese naval power entered through it. (2) **Colonial
and treaty-era**—French Indochina, British Malaya,
Spanish/American Philippines, Japanese expansion;
the sea was a space of **external** projection into
Lingnan and the delta. (3) **Post-1949**—PRC and ROC
both claim the sea; PRC Nine-Dash Line asserts
historic rights over most of the water body—encoding
**reconstitution as claim**: the sea that had been
a corridor for foreign navies is now a space of PRC
sovereignty assertion. MEM–CHINA–GEO–PEARL–RIVER
(delta) opens onto this sea; MEM–CHINA–HONG–KONG
(cession) sits at its northern edge.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SEA AS THEATRE OF SOVEREIGNTY AND CONTAINMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The South China Sea encodes **strategic theatre**: (1)
**First island chain**—Taiwan, Philippines, Japan form
a chain that can constrain mainland maritime breakout;
the South China Sea is the **interior** of that chain,
where PRC naval and coast guard presence has grown.
(2) **Taiwan Strait**—connects the sea to the East
China Sea and the Pacific; cross-strait and South
China Sea dynamics are linked. (3) **Contestation**—
multiple claimants; freedom of navigation operations
(FONOPs); island-building and militarization. For
civilizational memory, the South China Sea is the
**maritime extension** of the delta and strait—the
space where humiliation (foreign navies, treaty ports)
and reconstitution (PRC claims, naval modernization)
are projected onto water. MEM–CHINA–TAIWAN and
MEM–CHINA–TAIWAN–STRAIT are the **adjacent
geographic MEMs**; this file is the **maritime frame**.
