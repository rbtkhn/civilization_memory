I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Deng Xiaoping as the **adaptive stabilizer** of PRC state
power: the figure who **terminated permanent revolution, reconstructed
governance through pragmatism, and enabled long-duration centralization without
ideological collapse**.

Deng represents **selective reversal without repudiation**—a transformation
that preserved Party supremacy while abandoning Maoist mobilization as a
governing method.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. REVOLUTIONARY CREDENTIALS & SURVIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Deng was a veteran revolutionary, not a post-hoc technocrat.

Key traits:
• Long March participant  
• Wartime organizer  
• Party insider repeatedly purged and restored  
• Survival through political elasticity  

Foundational posture:
> “我是一个老共产党员。”
(I am an old Communist Party member.)

This status enabled reform without delegitimizing the regime.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. POST-MAO STRATEGIC PIVOT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Deng’s decisive move was ending ideological primacy in governance.

Strategic actions:
• Abandonment of class struggle as organizing principle  
• Restoration of bureaucratic authority  
• Re-legitimization of expertise  
• Demobilization of mass campaigns  

Operational maxim:
> “解放思想，实事求是。”
(Emancipate the mind; seek truth from facts.)

Stability replaced struggle as survival logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ECONOMIC RECONSTITUTION & PERFORMANCE LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Economic growth became the regime’s core legitimacy mechanism.

Key mechanisms:
• Market instruments under Party control  
• Special Economic Zones (特区)  
• Agricultural decollectivization  
• Foreign capital without political pluralism  

Pragmatic axiom:
> “不管黑猫白猫，捉到老鼠就是好猫。”
(It doesn’t matter if a cat is black or white, so long as it catches mice.)

Performance substituted for ideology.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. IDEOLOGY & CONTROLLED DILUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ideology was hollowed without being dismantled.

Features:
• Marxism retained as symbolic framework  
• Mao preserved as founding authority  
• Flexibility without pluralism  

Boundary condition:
> “四项基本原则不能动摇。”
(The Four Cardinal Principles must not be shaken.)

Ideology became constraint, not engine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. POLITICAL AUTHORITY & LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Political liberalization was explicitly rejected.

Actions:
• Suppression of organized dissent  
• Refusal of competitive legitimacy  
• Reinforcement of Party monopoly on coercion  

Hard boundary:
> “稳定压倒一切。”
(Stability overrides everything.)

Predictability replaced participation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. MILITARY, SOVEREIGNTY, & TERRITORIAL LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sovereignty was reinforced pragmatically.

Key moves:
• PLA modernization without politicization  
• “One Country, Two Systems” (一国两制)  
• Strategic patience on unresolved territories  

Strategic realism:
> “韬光养晦。”
(Hide capabilities and bide time.)

Consolidation preceded assertion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. INTERNATIONAL REPOSITIONING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revolutionary confrontation was abandoned.

Shifts:
• Integration into global economic systems  
• Strategic accommodation with the United States  
• Entry into rule-based institutions  

External axiom:
> “中国要发展，离不开世界。”
(China’s development cannot be separated from the world.)

Integration served regime survival.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. FAILURE MODES & INTERNAL COSTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reform generated new structural tensions.

Costs:
• Inequality  
• Corruption  
• Ideological vacuum  
• Regional disparity  

Implicit trade-off:
Growth secured legitimacy while planting future stress.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Revolutionary regimes survive by abandoning revolutionary methods.  
• Performance legitimacy can outlast ideology but creates growth dependency.  
• Selective reversal preserves authority better than repudiation.  
• Deng stabilized the PRC by narrowing acceptable political futures.  
• Later centralization rests on Deng’s depoliticization of governance.

Tension preserved without verdict.
