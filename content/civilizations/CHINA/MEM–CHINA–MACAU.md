I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Macau (澳门) as the **second cession–return
node** of the Pearl River delta in Chinese civilizational memory:
Portuguese-administered from the Ming/Qing period, returned to the
PRC in 1999 as a Special Administrative Region (SAR) under "one
country, two systems." Macau encodes **return as legitimacy claim**
alongside Hong Kong—the same delta that had been partitioned
(British Hong Kong, Portuguese Macau, PRC Guangdong) was again
under one sovereign, with SAR status preserved by PRC design.
RLL–CHINA–0003 (cession–return–legitimacy) applies.

Macau is remembered not only as a city-territory but as the
**parallel to Hong Kong**: different colonial power (Portugal),
different chronology (presence from 16th c., formalisation and
return 1999), but the same **grammar of return**—territory
reincorporated; sovereignty reasserted. The contrast with
Shenzhen (PRC SEZ, never lost) is structural: Macau, like Hong
Kong, was **lost** then **returned**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & STRUCTURAL MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Macau sits on the western side of the Pearl River delta mouth,
adjacent to Guangdong and across the estuary from Hong Kong.
Portuguese presence dates to the 16th century (Ming); the
territory was administered by Portugal under varying arrangements
(lease, occupation, treaty) until the **Sino-Portuguese Joint
Declaration (1987)** and **handover (1999)**. Structurally,
Macau encodes **foreign administration** and then **return**—
not a single treaty cession like Hong Kong Island (1842), but
a long period of Portuguese control followed by negotiated
reversion to PRC sovereignty. The delta thus holds two return
nodes: Hong Kong (1997), Macau (1999).

Key structural features:
• Portuguese presence: 16th c.–1999; Ming/Qing era origins; different path from British Hong Kong
• Return 1999: PRC sovereignty; Macau SAR; "one country, two systems"
• Pearl River delta: same delta as Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Shenzhen; second SAR after Hong Kong
• Contrast with Hong Kong: different colonial power and chronology; same legitimacy grammar (return)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PORTUGUESE PRESENCE & STATUS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Macau's status under Ming and Qing was not identical to Hong
Kong's under the Treaty of Nanjing. Portuguese settlement and
trade were tolerated or regulated by the court; territorial
sovereignty remained contested or ambiguous in Chinese narrative—
"leased" or "administered" rather than formally ceded in a single
unequal treaty. For civilizational memory, however, the **effect**
is comparable: a portion of the Pearl River delta was under
foreign administration; the PRC did not control it until 1999.
The humiliation era (19th–20th c.) saw both Hong Kong and Macau
as **partitioned delta**—British and Portuguese presence on the
same coastline where the Qing had lost control. Republican and
PRC narrative treat Macau, like Hong Kong, as territory to be
**recovered**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. HANDOVER 1999 & SAR RECONSTITUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The **handover (1999)** returned Macau to PRC sovereignty as a
Special Administrative Region. "One country, two systems" applied
here as to Hong Kong—PRC-designed framework, negotiated reversion,
different legal and administrative status preserved by PRC design.
**Return** is the legitimacy claim: territory restored, sovereignty
reasserted. With Macau's return, the Pearl River delta was again
fully under Chinese sovereignty—Hong Kong (1997), Macau (1999),
Guangdong and Shenzhen already PRC. The delta that had been
partitioned was **reconstituted**. MEM–CHINA–GEO–PEARL–RIVER
frames the delta; Macau is the second cession–return node within it.
