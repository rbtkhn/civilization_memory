I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Hungary** as Rome's **northwestern frontier interface**: the medieval kingdom that adopted Latin Christianity, became a power on the Danube, and alternated between Byzantine alliance and rivalry—marriage (John II Komnenos–Piroska/Eirene), military contest (Manuel I Komnenos and the Balkans), and last appeal (Constantine XI to Hungary before 1453). For Rome/Byzantium, Hungary is Catholic neighbour and potential ally against Ottoman and Balkan pressure; never absorbed, often needed.

Encoding: Hungary is not Byzantine territory. It is **frontier polity**—buffer and sometimes threat; dynastic link and doctrinal divide (Latin vs Orthodox). The Balkans exposure (MEM–ROME–GEO–BALKANS) includes the Hungarian vector.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL CONTEXT — DANUBE FRONTIER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Kingdom of Hungary (c. 1000 onward) occupies the middle Danube and Pannonian plain—north and west of Byzantine Balkan holdings. Danube = invasion corridor and frontier; Hungary controls the western approach. Byzantine–Hungarian relations: marriage alliances (e.g. John II–Piroska), campaigns (Manuel I in Hungary and the Balkans), and diplomatic appeals when Constantinople is threatened. Hungary is **structural neighbour**: Catholic, monarchical, capable of raiding or reinforcing the empire.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. KEY ENCODINGS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**John II Komnenos:** Marriage to Eirene (Piroska) of Hungary—dynastic link; Hungarian princess as Byzantine empress. **Manuel I Komnenos:** Hungary and the Balkans as theatre; Byzantine–Hungarian contest and accommodation. **Constantine XI:** Appeals to Venice, Genoa, **Hungary** for aid before 1453—Hungary as potential last-minute ally; help did not arrive in time. Pattern: Hungary as **ally of necessity** and **rival by confession**; never under Byzantine rule.
