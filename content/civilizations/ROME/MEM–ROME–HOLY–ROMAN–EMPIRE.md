I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Holy Roman Empire as **Rome's Western fragment**:
a political entity that claimed Roman succession but held only a portion
of Roman inheritance, dependent on papal coronation and never recognized
by the actual Roman Empire in Constantinople.

For Rome, the HRE represents **borrowed legitimacy without structural
continuity**.

The HRE is not post-Roman.
It is **pseudo-Roman** — claiming what it could not structurally possess.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FRAGMENT INHERITANCE (RLL–ROME–0006 APPLICATION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Per RLL–ROME–0006 (Fragment Inheritance Law):
> Universal civilizational authority cannot be wholly inherited after
> collapse. Multiple claimants will each hold fragments and claim the
> rest, producing permanent rivalry rather than succession.

The Holy Roman Empire held:
• Western territorial authority (Frankish, then Germanic lands)
• Papal-dependent legitimacy (coronation required until 1508)
• Latin Christian religious alignment
• Roman legal and administrative vocabulary

The Holy Roman Empire did NOT hold:
• Eastern territories (Constantinople's domain)
• Structural continuity from Augustus
• Recognition from Constantinople (the actual Roman Empire)
• Orthodox Christian communion
• Mediterranean maritime coherence
• Roman institutional continuity (Senate, cursus honorum, etc.)

The very name — "Holy Roman Empire" — proves fragmentary status.
Constantinople never called itself "Eastern" or needed qualifiers.
It was simply *Rome*. The HRE's triple qualifier compensated for
what structure could not provide.

> "The Holy Roman Empire was neither holy, nor Roman, nor an empire."
> — Voltaire, *Essai sur l'histoire générale* (1756)

Voltaire's observation captures the fragment thesis precisely:
the claim exceeded the structural basis in all three dimensions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ORIGIN: PAPAL CREATION, NOT ROMAN SUCCESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The HRE originated in Pope Leo III's coronation of Charlemagne (800 AD).

This was not succession but **fabrication**:
• No transfer of authority from Constantinople
• No dynastic continuity from Roman emperors
• No territorial inheritance from the Western Empire (fallen 476)
• Authority derived entirely from papal grant

The Papacy created an emperor to serve papal interests:
• Military protection against Lombards and Byzantines
• A Western power dependent on Rome for legitimacy
• Counterweight to Constantinople's indifference

> "The coronation of Charlemagne was a papal invention designed to
> solve a papal problem. The Pope needed an emperor; he made one."
> — Peter Brown, *The Rise of Western Christendom* (2003)

The HRE was born dependent. This dependency never fully resolved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS: ELECTIVE MONARCHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike actual Rome, the HRE lacked stable succession.

Key structural defects:
• Emperor elected by German princes (after 1356, seven Electors)
• No primogeniture — each succession was negotiated
• Imperial authority dependent on princely consent
• Territorial fragmentation accelerated over centuries

By the late medieval period, the HRE comprised hundreds of
semi-sovereign entities: kingdoms, duchies, bishoprics, free cities,
and minor lordships. The emperor was first among equals at best.

> "The Empire was a constitutional monstrosity, a patchwork of
> jurisdictions held together by legal fictions and ceremonial
> deference rather than effective central authority."
> — James Bryce, *The Holy Roman Empire* (1864)

This fragmentation was structural, not accidental. The HRE could
never consolidate because its legitimacy depended on the very
princes whose autonomy consolidation would threaten.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MEARSHEIMER SHARPENING: POWER WITHOUT AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mercouris reading frames the HRE as a legitimacy project —
an attempt to inherit Roman authority through symbolic continuity
and papal sanction.

The realist sharpening asks: **What power did the HRE actually hold?**

Structural assessment:
• No standing army (dependent on princely contributions)
• No centralized taxation (imperial revenue minimal)
• No bureaucratic apparatus (administration was princely, not imperial)
• No territorial coherence (scattered domains across Central Europe)

The HRE claimed universal authority but could not project power
beyond German-speaking lands. Even within those lands, imperial
authority was contested by princes, cities, and the Papacy itself.

The polyphonic tension:
• Mercouris: The HRE mattered because people believed it mattered
• Mearsheimer: The HRE was structurally hollow — a title without teeth

Both readings are valid. The HRE was BOTH symbolically powerful
AND structurally weak. Its longevity (1,006 years) derived from
its very weakness: no one needed to destroy what could not threaten.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RIVALRY WITH CONSTANTINOPLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Constantinople never recognized the HRE as legitimate.

From the Byzantine perspective:
• There was only one Roman Empire — theirs
• Charlemagne was a barbarian usurper
• Papal coronation was ecclesiastical overreach
• The Western "empire" was a Frankish kingdom in costume

This non-recognition persisted for centuries. Diplomatic protocols
carefully avoided acknowledging HRE emperors as "Roman" when
Constantinople was involved.

The HRE's claim to be Roman was thus **unilateral** — asserted
by the claimant, denied by the entity that actually held continuity.

> "The Byzantines regarded the Western emperor as a pretender and
> the papal coronation as a canonical irregularity. For them, there
> was one Roman Empire, and it was centered in Constantinople."
> — Judith Herrin, *Byzantium* (2007)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FOUR-WAY RIVALRY (POST-1453)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After Constantinople fell in 1453, the HRE became one of four
fragment claimants to Roman inheritance:

1. **Papacy** — Religious authority (Western Christianity)
2. **Holy Roman Empire** — Political authority (Western, Germanic)
3. **Moscow** — Political + religious authority (Eastern, Orthodox)
4. **Ottomans** — Territorial possession (but declined Roman title)

Per RLL–ROME–0006, none inherited the whole. Each held fragments
and claimed the rest.

The HRE's position after 1453:
• Could now claim to be the *only* empire (Constantinople fallen)
• But Moscow simultaneously claimed Third Rome succession
• And the Papacy retained its own universal pretensions
• And the Ottomans held Constantinople's actual territory

The vacancy created by 1453 (per RLL–ROME–0002) enabled claims
but could not be filled. The HRE exploited the vacancy rhetorically
but lacked structural basis to actually inherit Eastern authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. DISSOLUTION (1806)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon dissolved the Holy Roman Empire in 1806.

The dissolution was structural recognition of long-standing reality:
• The HRE had not functioned as a coherent polity for centuries
• German princes had aligned with Napoleon's Confederation of the Rhine
• Emperor Francis II abdicated rather than see the title captured

> "The Empire died not because Napoleon killed it, but because it
> had long since ceased to live. The formal dissolution merely
> acknowledged what everyone already knew."
> — Peter Wilson, *The Holy Roman Empire* (2016)

The Western fragment claimant was extinguished.
But the fragment thesis persists: no successor inherited what the
HRE had claimed. The title simply vanished, proving it had been
a claim without structural foundation.

IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Fragments can persist for centuries without structural basis.
• Claims substitute for inheritance when inheritance is impossible.
• Symbolic authority can outlast structural authority.
• The HRE's longevity derived from its weakness, not its strength.
• Dissolution revealed what fragmentation had long established.

POLYPHONIC TENSIONS (v2.0):
• The HRE was Roman in claim AND fragment in structure.
• It held symbolic authority AND lacked structural power.
• It persisted for 1,006 years AND was hollow throughout.
• Its dissolution was sudden AND long overdue.

Tension preserved without verdict.
