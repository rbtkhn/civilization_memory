I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Kingdom of León as **Rome’s juridical and
imperial-memory carrier in northern Iberia**: the polity that most
explicitly claimed continuity with Visigothic and Roman legitimacy,
framing reconquest not as innovation, but as restoration of lawful order.

León is not Rome reborn territorially.
It is **Rome remembered as law**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. LEÓN AS VISIGOTHIC–ROMAN HEIR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León inherits authority through succession, not conquest narrative.

Foundations:
• Asturian resistance framed as survival of legitimate rule
• Visigothic law preserved and reapplied
• Roman-Christian kingship ideology retained
• Toledo remembered as lost capital of lawful Spain

León does not invent Spain.
It **recalls it**.

This memory differentiates León from frontier polities that define
themselves primarily through expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LAW AS PRIMARY CIVILIZATIONAL INSTRUMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León governs through written law and precedent.

The *Liber Iudiciorum* (Fuero Juzgo) remains central:

> “The king himself is bound by the law, for the law is made not for one
> man alone, but for all, so that justice may be preserved among the
> people.”
> — *Liber Iudiciorum*, Book II

Law functions as legitimacy storage.
Authority is continuous even when territory is not.

This mirrors late Roman and post-Roman governance logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. THE IMPERIAL TITLE & RESTORATION CLAIM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León’s kings adopt imperial language deliberately.

Alfonso VI styles himself *Imperator totius Hispaniae*.

This claim signifies:
• Supremacy among Christian polities
• Continuity with Roman–Visigothic universality
• Jurisdictional, not administrative, empire

The empire exists as **title and memory**, not bureaucracy.

Rome persists symbolically, awaiting material recovery.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CORTES OF LEÓN (1188): INSTITUTIONAL LEGACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León pioneers representative governance.

The Cortes of 1188 include:
• Nobility
• Clergy
• Urban representatives

The decree affirms consent and law:

> “No new law shall be established nor war declared without the counsel
> and consent of the bishops, nobles, and good men of the cities.”
> — Decrees of the Cortes of León, 1188

This institutionalizes Roman-Christian legalism in medieval form.

León governs through **procedure**, not force alone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LIMITS OF LEONESE POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León’s weakness is demographic and geographic.

Constraints:
• Sparse population
• Inland orientation
• Limited commercial networks
• Pressure from both Muslim south and Christian rivals

Law sustains legitimacy.
It does not generate expansion capacity.

This imbalance opens space for Castilian absorption.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. ABSORPTION INTO CASTILE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In 1230, León merges dynastically with Castile.

Outcome:
• Legal traditions persist
• Imperial language fades
• Political center shifts southward
• Roman memory subordinated to expansion logic

León does not collapse.
It is **outgrown**.

Roman legality yields to Castilian scale.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
León encodes **Rome as lawful memory rather than force**.

Lessons preserved:
• Legitimacy can survive territorial loss.
• Law outlasts armies.
• Imperial titles preserve civilizational claim.
• Representation can precede strength.
• Memory sustains continuity until absorbed by power.

León is Rome’s conscience in Iberia.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• León remembers Rome when others exploit it.
• Law precedes expansion but cannot replace it.
• Empire can exist as title without territory.
• Representation emerges from legality, not democracy.
• Roman memory survives longest where law is sacred.

Tension preserved without verdict.
