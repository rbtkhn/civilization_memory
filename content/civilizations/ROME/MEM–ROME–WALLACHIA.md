I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Wallachia** as Rome's **Danubian buffer and lost Orthodox polity**: the principality south of the Carpathians and north of the Danube that emerged in the late medieval period under Byzantine cultural and religious influence, and that passed into the Ottoman sphere as vassal before the fall of Constantinople. For Rome/Byzantium, Wallachia is **Orthodox frontier**—same confession, political orbit then lost; buffer between Byzantine Balkans and Hungarian/steppe pressure. Encoding: Byzantine influence (church, rite); then Ottoman vassalage; never fully absorbed by Byzantium or Rome.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL CONTEXT — DANUBE AND CARPATHIANS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wallachia occupies the lower Danubian plain (today southern Romania)—bounded by the Danube (south), Carpathians (north), and the Black Sea (east). It emerges as a principality (14th c.) in the zone where Byzantine, Hungarian, and later Ottoman interests met. Orthodox Christianity (Byzantine rite) and Byzantine-derived legitimacy (e.g. titles, church hierarchy) link Wallachia to Constantinople. Pattern: **buffer principality**—tribute and allegiance could swing; Byzantine influence recedes as Ottoman power advances.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. KEY ENCODINGS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Byzantine influence:** Wallachia receives Orthodox ecclesiastical and cultural influence; Byzantine commonwealth extends to the lower Danube. **Ottoman vassalage:** By the 15th c. Wallachia pays tribute to the Ottomans; principality survives as vassal, not as Byzantine ally. **Loss to Ottoman sphere:** Wallachia passes out of Byzantine political orbit before 1453; encodes **frontier loss**—Orthodox polity that could have been buffer becomes Ottoman tributary. Pattern: same faith, different sovereign.
