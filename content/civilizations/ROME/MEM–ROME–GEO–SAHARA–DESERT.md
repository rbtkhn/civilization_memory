────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **Sahara** as Rome's **southern limit**: the desert that defined the empire's edge in Africa without ever being conquered or integrated. Rome held the Mediterranean littoral (Africa Proconsularis, Cyrenaica, Tripolitania, Egypt); the Sahara remained **beyond**—a barrier that replaced legions with geography.

For Rome, the Sahara is not a province. It is **the line where rule stops**. Desert stopping power is absolute; pursuit brings exhaustion without victory. Rome learns where NOT to go.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. MEARSHEIMER STRATEGIC ANALYSIS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

II.A. STOPPING POWER OF TERRAIN — MAXIMUM

The Sahara exhibits maximum stopping power:
• ~9,200,000 km²; world's largest hot desert
• Extreme aridity; no sustainable supply lines
• Oasis-dependent transit; caravan logic, not military
• Nomadic populations (Garamantes, Berber groups) control interior
• Roman power reaches the **limes** (fortified edge) and stops

Rome does not conquer the Sahara. Rome **seals** the southern frontier. The desert fights Rome's battles.

II.B. ROMAN ENGAGEMENT — EDGE CONTROL ONLY

Roman presence is **littoral and oasis-fringe**:
• North: coastal strip from Mauretania to Cyrenaica; Limes Tripolitanus
• Oasis outposts (e.g. Garamantes contact for trade/raiding control)
• No deep penetration; no trans-Saharan empire
• Desert as buffer against southern threats (real or imagined)

Commerce with sub-Saharan Africa (gold, exotic goods) passes through intermediaries. Rome receives the goods; Rome does not control the route.

II.C. GARAMANTES — FRONTIER INTERFACE

The Garamantes (Fezzan, Libyan desert) function as Rome's primary Saharan interface: sometimes raiders, sometimes trading partners. Tacitus and other sources note punitive expeditions and diplomatic contact. Rome contains; Rome does not absorb. Pattern: point control at the edge; interior remains **terra incognita** in strategic terms.

II.D. BARNES DIMENSION — N/A

Liability/mechanism analysis is not primary here: the Sahara is a structural (terrain) constraint. No single jurisdiction or personal-liability chain governs the desert; the encoding is "where rule stops," not "who is on the hook."
