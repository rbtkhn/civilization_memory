I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Mughal dynasty as **India’s supreme
absorption test case**: a foreign-origin regime fully integrated
into the Indian civilizational substrate.

The Mughals represent **plural empire without civilizational rupture**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST INTO ADMINISTRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mughal state begins as military conquest.

Survival depends on:
• Revenue rationalization
• Elite co-option
• Religious accommodation
• Persianate–Indic synthesis

Akbar’s reign marks the absorption pivot.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PLURAL GOVERNANCE MODEL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mughal authority stabilizes through:
• Religious tolerance (early)
• Rajput incorporation
• Bureaucratic standardization
• Agricultural surplus extraction

The empire governs diversity **without erasing it**.

This aligns with India’s plural cohesion engine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CENTRALIZATION VS DHARMIC BALANAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Later emperors tighten control.

Aurangzeb’s reign exposes limits:
• Religious rigidity increases friction
• Military expansion strains resources
• Legitimacy–hardship coupling weakens

Plural equilibrium fractures before collapse.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. BRITISH SUCCESSION WITHOUT CIVILIZATIONAL RESET
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mughal collapse does not end India’s continuity.

Instead:
• Administrative forms are inherited
• Revenue systems persist
• The Raj rules through Mughal scaffolding

Foreign exit does not erase absorption.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL JUDGMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mughals are remembered as:
• Legitimate Indian rulers
• Cultural synthesizers
• Administrative predecessors

India absorbs even its conquerors.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• India can absorb foreign rule without losing identity.
• Plural governance is a durability multiplier.
• Religious imbalance accelerates legitimacy decay.
• Administrative inheritance outlives dynasties.
• Mughal India proves continuity beats origin.

Empire absorbed, not rejected.
