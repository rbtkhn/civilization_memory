I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **British Burma** as **India's eastern frontier
under imperial control**—the territory that Britain first annexed
as part of British India (1826–1937), then separated as a distinct
Crown Colony (1937–1948), creating administrative distance from
the subcontinent before independence.

For India, British Burma encodes **eastern frontier then severed**:
same empire, first part of India, then separate; Bengal and Assam
interface converted into imperial outpost; civilizational proximity,
administrative separation by design.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ANNEXATION & ADMINISTRATIVE SEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burma enters British control through three Anglo-Burmese Wars.

Control sequence:
• First Anglo-Burmese War (1824–26) — Arakan, Tenasserim,
  Assam ceded; Lower Burma annexed; administered from Bengal
• Second Anglo-Burmese War (1852–53) — Lower Burma (Pegu)
  annexed; Rangoon as port; still part of British India
• Third Anglo-Burmese War (1885) — Upper Burma annexed;
  Mandalay falls; Burma fully under British control
• 1826–1937 — Burma administered as part of British India;
  Bengal/Assam frontier; Rangoon as eastern port
• 1937 — Government of India Act 1935 separates Burma from
  British India; Burma becomes distinct Crown Colony
• 1948 — Burma independent; not part of Partition; clean
  handover; India and Burma separate sovereign states

Key contrast: Burma was **part of British India** for over a
century, then **administratively severed** (1937) before both
gained independence. Ceylon (MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–CEYLON)
was never part of British India.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ECONOMIC & STRATEGIC FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Burma's colonial role centres on **rice, timber, and eastern
frontier control**.

Key functions:
• Rice export — Irrawaddy delta; surplus to Bengal and empire;
  Rangoon as port
• Teak, oil — Timber and petroleum; extraction for empire
• Bengal/Assam frontier — Eastern approach to India; overland
  route (Manipur, Assam); Calcutta–Rangoon maritime link
• Naval and commercial — Rangoon as eastern Indian Ocean node;
  complement to Calcutta and Bombay

Burma encodes **eastern extraction**—rice and timber for empire;
frontier control; administrative linkage then severance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, British Burma encodes:

• **Eastern frontier** — Bengal and Assam interface with Burma;
  historical Indic–Burmese interface (Buddhism, trade); empire
  extended British India eastward
• **Part then separate** — Burma part of British India (1826–1937);
  administrative severance (1937) before independence; different
  from Ceylon (always separate)
• **Pre-Partition separation** — Burma independent 1948; no
  Partition; clean handover; contrast with India–Pakistan fracture
• **Eastern neighbour** — Myanmar (Burma) as India's eastern
  neighbour; civilizational proximity; political separation
  embedded by imperial design

British Burma is remembered as **frontier absorbed, then severed**—
the eastern interface part of British India, then administratively
separated before both gained sovereignty.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO CEYLON & CALCUTTA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–CEYLON preserves Ceylon as **always
separate** from British India—Crown Colony, different jurisdiction.
This MEM preserves **British Burma** as **part then separate**—
administered with India for a century, then severed. MEM–INDIA–
BRITISH–EMPIRE–CALCUTTA preserves Bengal as imperial capital;
Burma was eastern frontier administered from Bengal. Same empire;
different trajectories. Tension preserved.

VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Burma was part of British India; Ceylon never was.
• Administrative severance (1937) preceded independence; Burma
  and India separate before 1947.
• Eastern frontier—rice, timber, Bengal/Assam interface; empire
  extended India eastward, then severed the link.
• Burma's clean independence (1948) contrasts with India's
  Partition (1947); no fracture for Burma.
• Civilizational proximity survives political separation;
  Myanmar as India's eastern neighbour.

Tension preserved without verdict.
