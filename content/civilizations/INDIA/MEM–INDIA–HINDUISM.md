I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Hinduism** as **India's indigenous civilizational
substrate**—the plural tradition (dharma, varna, temple, epic,
philosophy) that encodes legitimacy, social order, and sacral
geography across Indic space.

Hinduism is remembered not as a single doctrine but as **plural
tradition**—multiple paths (Vaishnavism, Shaivism, Shaktism),
multiple texts (Vedas, epics, Puranas), unified by dharma and
embedded in Indic geography. Indigenous; not transmitted from
without; absorbed by Islam (Sultanate, Mughal) and colonial
administration without replacement.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PLURAL TRADITION — NO SINGLE ORTHODOXY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hinduism encodes **plurality without church**.

Key features:
• **Dharma** — Cosmic and social order; duty, righteousness,
  legitimacy; varies by varna, ashrama, context
• **Varna** — Four-fold scheme (Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya,
  Shudra); ritual and occupational hierarchy; encoded in
  dharmashastra (MEM–INDIA–LAW–CASTE–SYSTEM)
• **Plural theism** — Vaishnavism, Shaivism, Shaktism; multiple
  deities; temple and pilgrimage; no single creed
• **Textual plurality** — Vedas, Upanishads, epics (Mahabharata,
  Ramayana), Puranas; no single canon
• **Sacral geography** — Ganges, Kashi, Mathura, Rameshwaram;
  pilgrimage encodes civilizational space (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES–RIVER)

Hinduism is **embedded**—in land, in hierarchy, in epic. It does
not travel as a missionary faith; it absorbs and accommodates.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGITIMACY & STATE FORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hinduism encodes **legitimacy** for Indic states.

Key elements:
• **Dharmic kingship** — Rama (ideal king); Ashoka (dharmic
  overlay); king as upholder of dharma
• **Mauryan and Gupta** — Hinduism (and Buddhism) as legitimacy
  substrate; Ashoka turns to Buddhism; Gupta revives Brahminical
  patronage (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MAURYA; MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–GUPTA)
• **Temple and court** — Temple as political and economic node;
  royal patronage; legitimacy through sacral architecture
• **Epic encoding** — Mahabharata (contested dharma); Ramayana
  (ideal kingship); civilizational grammar (MEM–INDIA–LIT–
  MAHABHARATA; MEM–INDIA–LIT–RAMAYANA)

Hinduism provides **vocabulary for legitimacy**—dharma, sacrifice,
temple—not monopoly. Buddhism, Islam, and colonial administration
operate within or alongside.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. INTERFACE WITH ISLAM & COLONIAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hinduism **persists** under Islamic and British rule—absorbed,
not replaced (MEM–INDIA–ISLAM; MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE).

Key elements:
• **Sultanate and Mughal** — Islamic rule over predominantly
  Hindu society; temple desecration episodic, not systematic;
  plural coexistence and extraction; Hinduism endures
• **British** — Census classifies "Hindu"; Hinduism becomes
  administratively legible; missionary pressure; Hinduism
  reconfigured, not erased
• **Partition** — Pakistan (Islamic state); India (secular,
  plural); Hinduism as majority tradition in republican
  India; constitutional secularism constrains Hindu
  establishment (MEM–INDIA–PARTITION; MEM–INDIA–REPUBLIC)

Hinduism is **indigenous substrate**—Islam and Britain overlay;
Hinduism persists. Plural cohesion (India) vs Islamic state
(Pakistan) encodes Partition's religious dimension.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Hinduism encodes:

• **Indigenous substrate** — Born in Indic space; not transmitted;
  plural tradition; dharma as civilizational grammar
• **Legitimacy vocabulary** — Kingship, temple, epic; legitimacy
  through dharmic performance
• **Plurality** — Multiple paths; no single orthodoxy; accommodates
  Buddhism, Islam, Sikhism within Indic space
• **Sacral geography** — Ganges, pilgrimage; land as sacred;
  civilizational continuity through place
• **Interface** — Hinduism with Islam (absorption, coexistence);
  Hinduism with Buddhism (shared substrate, then divergence);
  Hinduism with law (varna encoded, contested)

Hinduism is remembered as **civilizational core**—the indigenous
substrate that persists across regime change; plural, embedded,
legitimating.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELATION TO BUDDHISM, ISLAM & LITERATURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–BUDDHISM preserves Buddhism as **born in India, transmitted
outward**; Hinduism and Buddhism share Indic substrate; Buddhism
declines in India, Hinduism endures. MEM–INDIA–ISLAM preserves
Islam as **external interface**; Hinduism persists under Islamic
rule; plural coexistence. MEM–INDIA–LIT–MAHABHARATA and MEM–INDIA–
LIT–RAMAYANA preserve epic encoding; Hinduism provides vocabulary.
This MEM preserves **Hinduism**—the indigenous substrate; plural
tradition; legitimacy source. Same civilizational space; different
encoding. Tension preserved.
