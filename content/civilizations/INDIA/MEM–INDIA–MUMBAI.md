I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Mumbai** as **India's commercial and financial
capital**—the city that evolved from colonial Bombay into the
primary node for finance, film, and maritime commerce in republican
India.

Mumbai is remembered as **commercial primacy**—the city that trades
while Delhi governs. Administrative capital moved (Calcutta → Delhi);
commercial capital remained.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FROM BOMBAY TO MUMBAI
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Control and naming sequence:

• Bombay (1661–1995) — British possession; East India Company;
  Bombay Presidency; colonial maritime gateway (see MEM–INDIA–
  BRITISH–EMPIRE–BOMBAY for British period)
• Mumbai (1995–present) — Renamed by Maharashtra government;
  reclaims Marathi name; continuity of function despite rename

The rename encodes **linguistic reclamation**—colonial toponym
replaced; commercial function unchanged. Mumbai remains India's
financial capital.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. POST-COLONIAL FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After independence, Bombay/Mumbai retains and extends commercial
primacy.

Key functions:
• Financial capital — Reserve Bank of India (1935, moved Bombay
  1937); stock exchanges (BSE); banking headquarters; corporate
  headquarters
• Film industry — Bollywood; Hindi cinema; cultural export
• Port and trade — Jawaharlal Nehru Port; container traffic;
  maritime gateway
• Migration magnet — Labour; opportunity; demographic density;
  slum and skyscraper coexistence

Mumbai encodes **commercial concentration**—finance, film, port—
without seeking administrative primacy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Mumbai encodes:
• Commercial capital — Delhi governs; Mumbai trades; functional
  division persists
• Colonial continuity — British Bombay's commercial role survives;
  infrastructure repurposed; Gateway of India, Ballard Pier
• Maritime gateway — Western interface; Arabian Sea; distinct
  from Calcutta's eastern/Ganges orientation
• Linguistic reclamation — Bombay → Mumbai (1995); name changes;
  function endures

Mumbai is remembered as **the city that makes money**—commercial
and financial node, not administrative centre.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO DELHI & CALCUTTA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–DELHI preserves Delhi as administrative capital.
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–CALCUTTA preserves Calcutta as imperial
capital (1773–1911); MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–BOMBAY preserves
Bombay under British rule. This MEM preserves **Mumbai**—post-colonial
commercial capital; continuity from Bombay; rename as reclamation.
Delhi = govern; Mumbai = trade; Calcutta = regional (Bengal).
Tension preserved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Commercial capitals persist when administrative capitals move.
• Colonial maritime nodes survive as republican financial centres.
• Rename (Bombay → Mumbai) reclaims toponym; function unchanged.
• Delhi and Mumbai encode different capital functions—authority
  vs commerce—complementary, not competitive.

Tension preserved without verdict.
