I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Independence** as the **moment of sovereign
transfer**—the act through which Britain relinquishes authority
and India acquires sovereignty.

For India, Independence encodes **sovereignty gained**:
Indian Independence Act 1947; Dominion of India; 15 August 1947;
transfer of power from Crown to Indian hands. The same moment
produces Partition (fracture) and Independence (sovereignty)—
opposite encodings of the same day.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. TRANSFER OF POWER — MECHANICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Independence is achieved through **negotiated transfer**, not
military expulsion.

Key elements:
• Indian Independence Act 1947 — British Parliament; partitions
  India and Pakistan; establishes Dominion status; 15 Aug 1947
  as transfer date
• Mountbatten — Last Viceroy; first Governor-General of India;
  presides over transfer; accelerates timeline
• Dominion of India — Sovereign; British monarch as head of state;
  Parliament, Cabinet, Governor-General; Crown link residual
• Congress, Muslim League — Elite acceptance of transfer terms;
  Partition as price of exit

Sovereignty is **granted** by Britain and **accepted** by Indian
elites. Legitimacy flows from consent, not conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SOVEREIGNTY ACQUIRED — WHAT INDIA GAINS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
India gains:
• Territorial sovereignty — Over territory not assigned to Pakistan;
  borders contested (Kashmir) but sovereignty asserted
• Legislative authority — Parliament; law-making; no British veto
• Executive authority — Cabinet; Prime Minister; Governor-General
  as Crown representative, not British agent
• Administrative continuity — ICS, army, police; inherited intact;
  loyalty transfers to new sovereign
• International personality — Recognized state; UN membership (1947)

Independence is **sovereignty acquired**, not empire expelled.
The mechanism is transfer; the outcome is sovereign continuity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Independence encodes:
• Sovereignty gained — 15 August 1947; India acquires what Britain
  relinquishes
• Transfer, not revolution — Negotiated; elite bargaining; no
  military expulsion
• Dominion as bridge — Residual Crown link; Republic (1950) completes
  the break
• Same moment, dual encoding — Independence (sovereignty) and
  Partition (fracture) occur together; tension preserved

Independence is remembered as **the day sovereignty came**—
even as Partition is remembered as the day the wound opened.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO PARTITION & REPUBLIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–PARTITION preserves **Britain's exit**—
cost externalized, boundary-making, handover mechanism. MEM–INDIA–
PARTITION preserves **India's wound**—fracture, Kashmir, trauma.
This MEM preserves **India's sovereignty**—transfer of power,
Dominion, sovereignty acquired. MEM–INDIA–REPUBLIC preserves the
**republican completion**—1950, Constitution, institutional
continuity. Same decade; different encodings. Tension preserved.
