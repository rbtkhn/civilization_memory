I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Buddhism** as **India's transmitted dharma**—
the tradition born in Indic space, exported to Tibet, China,
Southeast Asia, and Central Asia, then declining in India while
flourishing abroad.

Buddhism is remembered not as India's dominant tradition but as
**source and transmitter**—India is birthplace; India exports
dharma; India does not retain Buddhism as majority tradition.
"धर्म गया, राज्य नहीं।" (The religion went; the state did not.)
Transmission precedes conquest; transmission outlasts domicile.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. BIRTH & INDIC SUBSTRATE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Buddhism emerges from **Indic context**—Gangetic plains, 6th
century BCE; shared vocabulary with Hinduism (dharma, karma,
samsara); distinct path (Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path,
no atman, sangha).

Key elements:
• **Siddhartha Gautama** — Bihar; Bodh Gaya, Sarnath; Buddha
  as teacher, not deity (in early tradition)
• **Sangha** — Monastic order; transmission through monks;
  textual preservation
• **Mauryan patronage** — Ashoka (3rd c. BCE); Buddhism as
  state religion; missionary expansion; pillar edicts
  (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MAURYA)
• **Nalanda** — Buddhist university (5th–12th c.); scholastic
  centre; transmission to Tibet, China; destroyed (c. 1193)

Buddhism is **born in India**; Mauryan and Gupta patronage
propel it; Nalanda becomes transmission node.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. TRANSMISSION — OUTWARD FLOW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Buddhism **flows outward**—India is source; recipients are
Tibet, China, Central Asia, Southeast Asia (MEM–INDIA–SILK–
ROAD; MEM–INDIA–TIBET; MEM–INDIA–CHINA).

Key vectors:
• **Central Asia** — Oasis nodes (Kashgar, Dunhuang); monks,
  texts, art; Sanskrit corpus preserved and transmitted
• **Tibet** — 7th–11th centuries; Indian monks, Tibetan
  translation; India is source, Tibet is preserver
  (MEM–INDIA–TIBET: "India is source, Tibet is preserver")
• **China** — India → Central Asia → China; Buddhism as
  foreign import; Indian monks (Kumarajiva, Xuanzang);
  India at eastern reach (MEM–INDIA–CHINA)
• **Southeast Asia** — Theravada to Sri Lanka, Burma, Thailand;
  maritime transmission; Indian cultural sphere

India exports **dharma**; Silk Road is **conduit**. Transmission
precedes conquest; India does not conquer to transmit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DECLINE IN INDIA — PERSISTENCE ABROAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Buddhism **declines** in India—Hindu revival, Islamic incursion,
Nalanda destroyed; Buddhism survives in transmitted form abroad.

Key elements:
• **Hindu revival** — Gupta patronage of Brahminical tradition;
  Buddhism loses royal support in core Indic space
• **Nalanda destruction** — c. 1193; Bakhtiyar Khalji; scholastic
  centre destroyed; transmission interrupted
• **Buddhism abroad** — Tibet, China, Southeast Asia retain
  Buddhism; India loses Buddhist majority; Buddhism becomes
  minority tradition in India
• **Modern revival** — Ambedkar conversion (1956); Dalit Buddhism;
  Buddhism as protest against caste; minority tradition
  (MEM–INDIA–LAW–CASTE–SYSTEM)

India is **source**, not **domicile**. Buddhism travels; India
does not retain it as dominant tradition. Transmission outlasts
domicile.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Buddhism encodes:

• **Source** — Born in Indic space; India exports dharma;
  transmission precedes conquest
• **Transmission** — Silk Road; Tibet, China, Central Asia,
  Southeast Asia; India at eastern reach
• **Decline at home** — Buddhism declines in India; Hinduism
  endures; structural contrast with Hinduism (indigenous,
  enduring)
• **Preservation abroad** — Tibet, China preserve Sanskrit
  corpus; India is source, recipients are preservers
• **Alternative to varna** — Buddhism initially rejects caste;
  sangha as alternative community; modern Dalit Buddhism
  encodes protest (MEM–INDIA–LAW–CASTE–SYSTEM)

Buddhism is remembered as **transmitted dharma**—India's gift
to Asia; source, not domicile; transmission outlasts domicile.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELATION TO HINDUISM, TIBET & SILK ROAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–HINDUISM preserves Hinduism as **indigenous substrate**;
Buddhism and Hinduism share Indic context; Buddhism diverges,
transmits, declines in India; Hinduism endures. MEM–INDIA–TIBET
preserves Tibet as **preserver**—India is source, Tibet receives;
"India is source, Tibet is preserver." MEM–INDIA–SILK–ROAD
preserves transmission vectors; Buddhism flows east. This MEM
preserves **Buddhism**—the transmitted tradition; source, not
domicile. Same civilizational space; different trajectory.
Tension preserved.
