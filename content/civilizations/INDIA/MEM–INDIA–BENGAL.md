I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Bengal** as **India's Ganges-delta heartland
severed at Partition**—the fertile eastern region that was divided
by the Radcliffe Line in 1947, with West Bengal remaining in India
and East Bengal (later Bangladesh) severed.

Bengal is remembered as **delta heartland and Partition fracture**—
Ganges basin, Calcutta, imperial capital legacy, and the wound of
administrative severance.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & CIVILIZATIONAL CORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bengal sits at the **Ganges delta**—the fertile, humid, riverine
eastern zone where the Ganges and Brahmaputra meet the Bay of Bengal.

Core features:
• Alluvial plains; dense settlement; surplus agriculture
• Rice, jute, silk; riverine and maritime connectivity
• Hindu–Muslim plurality; Sufi and Vaishnava traditions
• Calcutta (Kolkata) as imperial capital (1773–1911) and regional
  centre

Bengal encodes **Ganges heartland**—demography, sacred geography,
and imperial administrative legacy (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES;
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–CALCUTTA).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PARTITION FRACTURE — 1947
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Partition (1947) severs Bengal along the Radcliffe Line.

Effects:
• West Bengal — Remains in India; Calcutta retained; refugee influx
  from East; Hindu-majority districts
• East Bengal — Assigned to Pakistan; becomes East Pakistan;
  Muslim-majority districts; Dhaka as capital
• Demographic upheaval — Displacement; violence; mixed zones
  shattered
• 1971 — East Pakistan becomes Bangladesh; Bengal divided between
  two sovereign states (India, Bangladesh), not one

Bengal encodes **Partition fracture**—the delta severed by
boundary-making; civilizational continuity across border;
administrative discontinuity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Bengal encodes:
• Ganges heartland — Delta core; demography; sacred geography;
  empires reconstitute along Ganges
• Imperial capital legacy — Calcutta as Company and Crown seat;
  Bengal Presidency; Plassey and Clive as Bengal entry
• Partition fracture — Radcliffe Line; East Bengal severed;
  West Bengal retained; wound event
• Post-1971 — Bangladesh independent; Bengal divided between
  India and Bangladesh; cultural continuity, political separation

Bengal is remembered as **heartland severed**—the delta divided
by boundary-making without demographic absorption capacity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO PARTITION & CALCUTTA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–PARTITION preserves the wound event; MEM–INDIA–
BRITISH–EMPIRE–PARTITION preserves British exit and Radcliffe.
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–CALCUTTA preserves Calcutta as imperial
capital. This MEM preserves **Bengal**—the regional substrate;

VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  divided, not erased.
