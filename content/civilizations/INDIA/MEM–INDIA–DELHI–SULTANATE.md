I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **Delhi Sultanate** as India’s first
sustained Islamic sultanate in North India: the regime that
ruled from Delhi along the Ganges for over three centuries,
deflected Chagatai Mongol pressure, endured Timur’s sack (1398),
and was succeeded by the Mughals (1526) when defender cohesion
failed at First Panipat.

The Sultanate represents **plural sultanate rule**—Islamic
authority over a predominantly non-Islamic society—without
civilizational erasure. It is the polity that **faced** Mongol
raids and **deflected** conquest (MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–CHAGATAI,
MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATION & EXTENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Delhi Sultanate emerged c. 1206 from Turkic–Afghan conquest
and consolidation in North India. Successive dynasties (Slave,
Khalji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi) ruled from Delhi; control
extended along the Ganges and intermittently into the Deccan
and the northwest.

Key features:
• Islamic sultanate; Persianate administration and court culture
• Ganges core (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES–RIVER)—Delhi among the empires
  that reconstitute along the basin
• Northwest frontier—the axis of Mongol (Chagatai) pressure and
  later Mughal entry (MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS–RIVER)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. GOVERNANCE & PLURAL SOCIETY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Sultanate governed through:
• Sultan-centered authority; iqta (revenue assignment); military
  and administrative elites
• Persianate–Indic interface; court patronage (e.g. Amir Khusrau);
  coexistence with Hindu and other communities
• No systematic conversion or erasure; rule as extraction and
  order, not as civilizational replacement

India absorbs the Sultanate as **one regime among many**—empires
reconstitute along the Ganges; the Sultanate is one of them.
Legitimacy is sultanic and pragmatic, not dharmic; the substrate
persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MONGOL INTERFACE — DEFLECTION & SHOCK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Delhi Sultanate is the polity that **deflected** Chagatai
Mongol conquest (MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–CHAGATAI): repeated raids on
the northwest; Sultanate resistance, fortification, tribute;
no Chagatai administration in India.

In 1398 Timur’s invasion and sack of Delhi delivered a major
shock: mass violence, pillage, symbolic shattering of Sultanate
authority (MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE). The Sultanate survived but
was weakened; it reconstituted—and then, in 1526, **defender
cohesion failed** at First Panipat. Lodi nobility had fragmented;
Babur (Timurid–Mongol lineage) entered. Same structural pattern
as Plassey: elite defection, political fragmentation; exposure
on the indigenous polity (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL, defection
theory CIV–SCHOLAR–INDIA SYNTHESIS 0003).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. SUCCESSION BY MUGHALS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Sultanate did not fall to Mongol khanate rule. It fell to
**Mughal** rule—a dynasty of Mongol–Timurid lineage that entered
via fragmentation (defender elites, Panipat) and was then
**absorbed** into India (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL, MEM–INDIA–
MONGOL–EMPIRE).

Structural continuity: Delhi Sultanate deflected Chagatai;
endured Timur; then lost to Babur when the defender’s mechanism
failed. The Mughals inherit the Ganges core and Persianate
administrative habits; the Sultanate is succeeded, not erased.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• Delhi Sultanate is encoded as one of the empires along the
  Ganges—regimes change, basin persists (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES–RIVER).
• It is the polity that deflected Mongol conquest and endured
  Timur’s sack; no Mongol khanate ruled India.
• It is succeeded by the Mughals via defection/fragmentation
  (Panipat); Mughals are then absorbed as Indian.
• Sultanate administrative and cultural forms persist in Mughal
  and later practice; continuity without mythologizing the
  Sultanate as final authority.

Rule learned:
India can host Islamic sultanate rule without losing civilizational
continuity; the same polity can deflect steppe pressure and then
fall when defender cohesion fails—the defection pattern applies
to the Sultanate’s end as to Plassey.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Delhi Sultanate: first sustained Islamic sultanate in North India.
• Deflected Chagatai; endured Timur; fell to Mughals at Panipat.
• Ganges core held; northwest corridor was the pressure axis.
• Succession by Mughals = defender-elite defection (same type as
  Plassey); Mughals then absorbed as Indian.
• Sultanate is one regime in the Ganges reconstitution sequence—
  Mauryan, Gupta, Sultanate, Mughal, British, republican India.
