SITE (Layer 2)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• **Location:** Agra, Mughal North India (Yamuna riverfront)
• **Date:** Construction c. 1632–1653
• **Function:** Funerary monument (tomb of Mumtaz Mahal, later Shah Jahan); symbolic apex of Mughal legitimacy and Indo-Islamic synthesis; no administrative or military function

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **Taj Mahal** as **Mughal legitimacy performed
in stone**—the funerary monument through which Shah Jahan commemorates
Mumtaz Mahal and projects imperial authority, and through which
India later encodes Mughal absorption as cultural achievement.

For India, the Taj encodes **memory and legitimacy**—commemoration
as power display, Persianate–Indic synthesis, and post-Mughal
preservation (British, Republic) as shared heritage without
political continuity.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. COMMISSION AND MEANING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Shah Jahan (r. 1628–1658) orders the tomb after Mumtaz Mahal's
death (1631).

Key elements:
• Purpose — Tomb for Mumtaz Mahal; Shah Jahan later interred
  alongside; dynastic and personal commemoration
• Site — Agra; Yamuna riverfront; visibility and access
  controlled by court
• Patronage — Imperial treasury; crafts and materials from
  across the empire and beyond (e.g. lapis, marble); legitimacy
  through expenditure and scale
• Design — Dome, minarets, garden, reflection; Indo-Islamic
  and Persianate forms (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL; MEM–INDIA–PERSIA)

The Taj is **not** a seat of government—it is **symbolic capital**:
the emperor's capacity to mobilise labour, wealth, and aesthetic
tradition to fix memory and project authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING — SYNTHESIS AND ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, the Taj encodes:

• **Indo-Islamic synthesis** — Persianate and Indic elements;
  Mughal absorption as Indian (MEM–INDIA–ISLAM; MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL)
• **Legitimacy through commemoration** — Imperial grief and
  display; dynasty and devotion combined
• **No civilizational rupture** — The monument is remembered
  as Indian heritage; Mughal rule absorbed, not rejected
• **Post-Mughal preservation** — British and Republic maintain
  the site; symbol persists after empire ends (MEM–INDIA–MUGHAL–EMPIRE;
  MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE)

The Taj is remembered as **apex of Mughal building**—and as
**shared patrimony** once the empire is gone. Regime ends;
monument remains.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. RELATION TO MUGHAL EMPIRE AND AGRA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–MUGHAL–EMPIRE preserves the territorial and
administrative state; MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL preserves
absorption and plural governance. This MEM preserves **one
monument**—legitimacy performed in stone, not the empire's
revenue or succession. Agra was Mughal capital before Shah
Jahan's shift to Delhi (Shahjahanabad); the Taj fixes Agra's
symbolic weight. Tension preserved.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The Taj encodes legitimacy through commemoration and display.
• Indo-Islamic synthesis; Mughal absorbed as Indian.
• Monument outlives empire—British and Republic preserve it.
• No administrative function; symbolic and patrimonial only.

Tension preserved without verdict.

VII. INDIGENOUS LANGUAGE QUOTE (I-B)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• **Original:** (منقش بر درگاه تاج محل) مرقد منور؛ روضه رضوان۔
• **Transliteration:** (Munaqqash bar dargāh-i Tāj Mahal) Marqad-i munawwar; rawża-i riḍwān.
• **Translation:** (Inscribed at the Taj) Luminous tomb; garden of paradise (paradisiacal commemoration).
• **Source:** Conventional Persian epitaph / Mughal funerary inscription tradition; Taj Mahal, Agra. [ARC–INDIA-T-MEDIEVAL]
