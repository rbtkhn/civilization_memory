I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Pakistan** as **India's Partition twin and
recurring adversary**—the state carved from British India in 1947,
defined negatively against India, and locked in structural rivalry
over Kashmir and legitimacy.

Pakistan is remembered not as a neighbour but as **Partition's other
half**—asymmetric emergence, procedural stalemate, and recurrent
crisis. India retained the administrative spine; Pakistan inherited
fragments without depth. The wound of 1947 recurs; closure does
not arrive.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PARTITION EMERGENCE — ASYMMETRIC TRAJECTORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pakistan is born of the same exit event as India—Partition (1947)
(MEM–INDIA–PARTITION; MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–PARTITION).

Key features:
• Muslim League separatism; Two-Nation Theory; Radcliffe Line
• West Pakistan (Punjab, Sindh, NWFP, Balochistan) + East Pakistan
  (Bengal delta) — geographically severed; administratively fragile
• India retained Delhi, administrative spine, majority of population,
  Ganges core, industrial base
• Pakistan inherited Indus basin (West), Bengal delta (East); lacked
  institutional depth; identity defined against India

Structurally, **asymmetric emergence**: India held the centre;
Pakistan held the margins. The balance of incentives favours India
in the long run—demography, economy, institutional continuity—but
Pakistan's negative identity produces recurrent confrontation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. RECURRING CONFLICT — KASHMIR & PROCEDURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Pakistan encodes **Partition wound recurring** (MEM–INDIA–WAR–
PAKISTAN; MEM–INDIA–KASHMIR).

Sequence:
• 1947–48 First Kashmir War — Partition spillover; accession contested
• 1965 Second Kashmir War — Stalemate; Tashkent
• 1971 Bangladesh War — East Pakistan secedes; India intervenes;
  Shimla Agreement (1972); bilateral procedure replaces multilateral
• 1999 Kargil — Infiltration; Lahore Declaration already failed;
  nuclear underwrite constrains escalation

Kashmir is the **territorial locus**; neither side can concede without
legitimacy collapse. Procedure (UN, Tashkent, Shimla, Lahore) freezes
conflict; it does not resolve legitimacy. Managed, not settled.
