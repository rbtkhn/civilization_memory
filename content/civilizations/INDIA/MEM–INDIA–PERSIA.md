I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Persia** as **India's northwestern civilizational
interface**—the nexus between India and the transcontinental Silk Road,
the Achaemenid satrapy that first incorporated the Indus, and the
Persianate court tradition absorbed by the Mughals.

Persia is remembered not as conqueror nor as mirror but as **nexus
and transmitter**—Silk Road corridor; Persianate synthesis; India
at eastern reach; Persia holds the centre, India supplies and transits.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ACHAEMENID SATRAPY — INDUS AS PERSIAN FRONTIER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
India enters recorded history as **Persian satrapy**—the twentieth
province of the Achaemenid Empire (MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS–RIVER; ARC–
PERSIA-T-ANCIENT).

Key elements:
• **Herodotus** — India as twentieth province; greatest tribute
  (360 talents of gold dust); Indus voyage commissioned by Darius
• **Scylax** — Caspatyrus to Indus mouth to Red Sea; Darius
  "subdued the Indians and made use of this sea"; first recorded
  circumnavigation of Arabia
• **Indus as frontier** — Persia incorporates Indus basin; India
  at eastern edge of Persian dominion
• **Alexander** — Conquers Persia before India; India enters
  Greek historiography through Persian frame (MEM–INDIA–WAR–
  ALEXANDER)

India is **eastern frontier** of Persian space—satrapy, not centre.
Persia holds the nexus; India supplies tribute and trade.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SILK ROAD NEXUS — PERSIA AS CORRIDOR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Persia functions as **Silk Road nexus**—the central corridor
through which goods and ideas flow between China and the
Mediterranean (MEM–INDIA–SILK–ROAD).

Key elements:
• **Overland corridor** — Persia as transit; India at eastern
  reach; cotton, spices, gems flow west via Persian nodes
• **Maritime** — Persian Gulf; Hormuz; Indian Ocean links to
  Persian ports; Zaytun–Hormuz under Mongol
• **India's position** — India supplies and transits; Persia
  holds the nexus; India is source and way station, not
  corridor controller
• **Transmission** — Buddhism flows east (India → Central Asia
  → China); material goods flow west; Persia as intermediary

Persia encodes **connection**—India connects to transcontinental
trade through Persian space; India does not control the corridor.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. PERSIANATE SYNTHESIS — MUGHAL ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mughals absorb **Persianate court culture**—language, art,
administration (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL; MEM–INDIA–ISLAM).

Key elements:
• **Persian as court language** — Mughal administration; elite
  culture; Persianate–Indic synthesis
• **Timurid lineage** — Babur from Fergana; Mughals as Timurid–
  Mongol–Persianate dynasty; Persianate absorption into Indic
  substrate
• **Art and architecture** — Persianate forms; Mughal miniatures;
  Taj Mahal; synthesis not replacement
• **Administration** — Persianate bureaucracy; revenue systems;
  Mughal inherits and adapts

Persia encodes **cultural transmission**—Persianate tradition
flows into India; Mughals absorb it; India does not become
Persian. Synthesis, not substitution.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Persia encodes:

• **Northwestern interface** — Persia as corridor; India at
  eastern reach; Achaemenid satrapy; Silk Road nexus
• **Transmission** — Persianate court culture; Mughal absorption;
  synthesis, not replacement
• **Connection without centrality** — India supplies and transits;
  Persia holds the nexus; India does not control the corridor
• **Historical depth** — Achaemenid to Mughal; Persian space
  contiguous with Indic space; interface endures
• **Maritime link** — Persian Gulf; Indian Ocean; Hormuz; India–
  Persia trade via sea

Persia is remembered as **nexus and transmitter**—the corridor
through which India connects to transcontinental space; the
tradition Mughals absorb; interface, not dominion.

VI. RELATION TO SILK ROAD, MUGHAL & INDUS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

holds the nexus. MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL preserves Mughal

satrapy; Herodotus. This MEM preserves **Persia**—the interface;
