I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves India’s encounter with the **Chagatai Khanate**
—the Mongol successor state in Central Asia—as **deflection**:
repeated raids on the northwest and the Indus corridor; Delhi
Sultanate resistance, fortification, and tribute; no Chagatai
conquest or administration in India.

Chagatai pressure is the **first sustained Mongol-world threat**
to India. Genghis Khan never invaded India; his successors
(Chagatai) did raid it. India deflected conquest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CHAGATAI KHANATE — CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Chagatai Khanate emerged after Genghis Khan’s death (1227) as
the Mongol realm in Central Asia (Transoxiana, Turkestan). It
bordered the Delhi Sultanate’s northwest frontier—the same axis
used by Alexander, later by Timur and the Company (MEM–INDIA–
GEO–INDUS).

Structural condition:
• Steppe cavalry and raid-based extraction
• No stable sedentary administration in India; raids, not
  occupation
• Northwest corridor: Punjab, Indus, approaches to Delhi

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DELHI SULTANATE — RESISTANCE & DEFLECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Delhi Sultanate (c. 1206–1526) faced repeated Chagatai (and
allied Mongol) incursions from the early thirteenth century.

Sultanate responses:
• Military resistance and frontier fortification
• Tribute and buy-off when expedient
• No submission to a Mongol khanate; no Chagatai governor or
  tax regime in India

Key outcome: **Deflection**. Steppe pressure was real; conquest
was not achieved. The Sultanate preserved a distinct political
center on the Ganges (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES)—one of the empires
that reconstitute along the basin. India was raided; India was
not ruled by the Chagatai.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRUCTURAL PARALLEL — INVADER AT THE EDGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Chagatai raids function structurally as **invader pressure at
the edge**: the Mongols could strike the northwest but could not
sustain conquest or administration deep in the subcontinent. Depth,
monsoon, and Sultanate cohesion (when present) limited Chagatai
reach—analogous to force-at-distance limits in MEM–INDIA–WAR–
ALEXANDER (Hyphasis) and MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE (deflection).

Contrast: Persia was **conquered** by the Mongols (Ilkhanate);
India was **raided** by the Chagatai and **deflected** (MEM–
PERSIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE). Same steppe threat; opposite outcome.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• No Chagatai India; no Mongol khanate administration in the
  subcontinent.
• Delhi Sultanate survives as the polity that faced and deflected
  Mongol pressure—before Timur (1398) and before Mughal entry
  (1526).
• Northwest corridor is confirmed as the recurring axis of
  steppe and external pressure (Alexander, Chagatai, Timur,
  Company).

Rule learned:
India can deflect sustained steppe raiding without losing the
Ganges core. Deflection is one mode of India’s encounter with
Mongol-world power; absorption (Mughal) is another (MEM–INDIA–
MONGOL–EMPIRE).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Chagatai raided India; Chagatai did not rule India.
• Delhi Sultanate deflected Mongol conquest; Ganges core held.
• Northwest corridor: same axis as Alexander, Timur, Company.
• Deflection (Chagatai) and absorption (Mughal) are two modes
  of India–Mongol interface.
• Contrast with Persia: Ilkhanate conquered Persia; Chagatai
  did not conquer India.

Tension preserved without verdict.

VIII. MEM CONNECTIONS

  India–Mongol interface; parent/sibling memory; Timur and Mughal
  follow.

  raids, Alexander, Timur, Company.
• MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES — Delhi Sultanate among empires along the
  Ganges; core holds under Chagatai pressure.
• MEM–INDIA–DELHI–SULTANATE — Sultanate is the polity that deflected
  Chagatai raids; regime memory.
• MEM–INDIA–WAR–ALEXANDER — Invader at edge (Hyphasis); force at
  distance; parallel to Chagatai deflection.
• MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL — Mughal entry (1526) follows Chagatai
  deflection; absorption phase of India–Mongol interface.
• MEM–PERSIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE — Persia: conquered (Ilkhanate); India:
  deflected (Chagatai). Cross-civilization contrast.
• MEM–PERSIA–SILK–ROAD — Chagatai realm along Silk Road; Pax
  Mongolica and raid economy.
• MEM–INDIA–WAR–GHAZNAVID — Pre-Mongol Islamic (Ghaznavid)
  pressure; force at distance limit; same northwest corridor.
• MEM–INDIA–WAR–TIMUR — Shock without conquest (1398); Sultanate
  survived; Chagatai deflection preceded Timur.
• MEM–INDIA–ISLAM — India–Islam interface; Sultanate deflected
  Chagatai; endured Timur.
