I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Robert Clive’s campaigns as **India’s decisive
loss to corporate–procedural conquest**, not military defeat.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. MODE OF CONQUEST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Key features:
• Limited European manpower
• Financial leverage
• Elite defection
• Treaty manipulation

Battle of Plassey is decisive psychologically, not militarily.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INTERNAL FRACTURE EXPLOITATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
British success depends on:
• Mughal decline
• Regional rivalry
• Fiscal exhaustion
• Absence of unified command

India collapses politically before collapsing militarily.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• Extraction replaces administration
• Sovereignty hollowed out
• Infrastructure repurposed
• Civilizational endurance delayed, not destroyed

Rule learned:
Internal disunity invites procedural domination.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• India fell to contracts, not cannons.
• Corporate power can replace empires.
• Fragmentation enables capture.
• Endurance resumes only after unity restoration.
• Clive marks the cost of temporal misalignment.

Tension preserved without verdict.
