I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Afghanistan** as **India's northwest corridor
and buffer**—the highland zone through which trade, ideas, and
invaders have flowed into the subcontinent across millennia.

Afghanistan is remembered not as Indian territory but as **the
gateway**—Khyber Pass, Kabul, Bactria; the same axis used by
Alexander, Ghaznavids, Mughals, and the British. India has never
controlled Afghanistan; Afghanistan has repeatedly been the platform
from which pressure reaches India.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & CIVILIZATIONAL POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Afghanistan sits at the **northwest approach** to the subcontinent—
the highland corridor between Central Asia and the Indus–Ganges
plains.

Core features:
• Khyber Pass, Bolan Pass — Entry to Punjab and Indus basin
• Kabul — Hub; Ghaznavid base (Mahmud of Ghazni); Mughal transit
• Bactria — Alexander's furthest east; Hellenistic legacy
• Silk Road node — Overland link to Central Asia, Persia, China
  (MEM–INDIA–SILK–ROAD)

Afghanistan encodes **invasion axis**—the corridor through which
external force reaches India. Same geography serves trade and
conquest (MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS–RIVER; MEM–INDIA–PUNJAB).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. HISTORICAL ENCODING — INVADERS & BUFFER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Afghanistan is the **platform**, not the target.

Key sequences:
• **Alexander** — Crosses Hindu Kush; Bactria; Hyphasis at Punjab
  edge (MEM–INDIA–WAR–ALEXANDER)
• **Ghaznavids** — Mahmud of Ghazni raids from Ghazni (Afghanistan);
  Punjab annexed; Ganges core not held (MEM–INDIA–WAR–GHAZNAVID)
• **Mughals** — Babur enters from Kabul; Kabul–Delhi axis; Mughal
  empire absorbs corridor (MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL)
• **British** — Anglo-Afghan Wars (1839–42, 1878–80, 1919); Durand
  Line (1893); buffer zone; "graveyard of empires"

India has never held Afghanistan. Afghanistan functions as **buffer**
—when stable, it absorbs pressure from the northwest; when unstable,
it becomes a platform for pressure on India (Pakistan, militants,
great-power rivalry).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. POST-1947 — BUFFER LOST, PLATFORM GAINED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Partition (1947) and Pakistan's creation alter India–Afghanistan
geometry.

Effects:
• **No direct border** — India and Afghanistan do not share a
  border; Pakistan lies between them
• **Pakistan controls approach** — Islamabad's relation to Kabul
  shapes India's access; India's interest in Afghan stability
  runs against Pakistan's strategic depth
• **Soviet intervention (1979–89)** — Afghanistan as Cold War
  buffer; India's tilt toward USSR; Pakistan–US–mujahideen axis
• **US intervention (2001–21)** — Taliban fall; India engages
  Kabul; Taliban return (2021); India's buffer calculus resets

Afghanistan encodes **buffer and platform**—when the highland
is unstable, pressure reaches India through Pakistan and militants.
India cannot control Afghanistan; India cannot ignore it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Afghanistan encodes:

• **Invasion axis** — Same corridor as Alexander, Ghaznavid,
  Mughal, Company; northwest as entry point
• **Buffer zone** — Highland between India and Central Asia;
  when stable, absorbs pressure; when unstable, transmits it
• **Platform for pressure** — External powers (Soviet, US,
  Pakistan) use Afghanistan to project force or influence toward
  India
• **Connection without control** — India connects to Afghanistan
  via Silk Road memory and cultural transmission (Buddhism,
  Sanskrit); India does not control the corridor
• **Post-Partition geometry** — No direct border; Pakistan
  interposed; India's Afghan policy mediated by Pakistan

Afghanistan is remembered as **gateway and buffer**—the corridor
India has never held and cannot afford to see hostile.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELATION TO SILK ROAD, INVASIONS & PAKISTAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–SILK–ROAD preserves India at eastern reach; Afghanistan
is the overland node. MEM–INDIA–WAR–ALEXANDER and MEM–INDIA–WAR–
GHAZNAVID preserve invader entry via Afghanistan. MEM–INDIA–
DYNASTY–MUGHAL preserves Mughal absorption of the corridor. MEM–
INDIA–PAKISTAN preserves Pakistan as the state that now controls
the approach. This MEM preserves **Afghanistan**—the corridor;
buffer; platform. Same geography; different encoding. Tension
preserved.
