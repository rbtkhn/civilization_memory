I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Gandhi as **India’s moral–mobilizational
reconstructor**: the figure who converted civilizational patience,
mass discipline, and ethical framing into a decisive anti-imperial
force.

Gandhi is not a state-builder.
He is a **legitimacy disruptor** who made imperial rule unsustainable.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. MODE OF STRUGGLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gandhi’s method weaponizes restraint.

Core mechanisms:
• Nonviolent mass mobilization
• Moral asymmetry against coercive rule
• Civil disobedience as legitimacy attack
• Personal asceticism as political signal

Power shifts from force to conscience.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CIVILIZATIONAL FIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gandhi draws on deep Indian substrates:
• Ahimsa
• Dharma
• Village self-rule imaginaries
• Religious plural symbolism

His authority emerges from resonance, not command.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. LIMITS & CONTRADICTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Structural limits:
• No administrative blueprint
• Underestimation of partition dynamics
• Reliance on elite restraint
• Vulnerability to communal fracture

Gandhi dissolves empire but cannot prevent rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• British legitimacy collapse
• Mass political awakening
• Moral framing of independence
• Post-independence ethical reference point

Gandhi defines **how** India exits empire,
not how it governs afterward.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Moral legitimacy can defeat superior power.
• Mass discipline substitutes for arms.
• Ethics can be strategic without being coercive.
• Liberation and governance are separate problems.
• Gandhi is civilizational ignition, not architecture.

Tension preserved without verdict.
