I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Partition as **Britain's exit mechanism**—
the colonial handover and boundary-making process through which
imperial rule ended without civilizational transfer.

For India, British Partition is remembered as **cost externalized**:
Britain accelerated departure; India and Pakistan bore the cost
of boundary-making, displacement, and violence.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EXIT MECHANICS & HANDOVER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
British exit is driven by exhaustion—World War II, nationalist
pressure, imperial capacity collapse—not by design. Key actors:

• Mountbatten — Last Viceroy (1947); accelerates timeline;
  June 1948 → August 1947; compresses handover
• Radcliffe Commission — Boundary commission; Sir Cyril Radcliffe;
  Punjab and Bengal divided; line drawn without local knowledge;
  published after independence (17 August 1947)
• Congress, Muslim League — Elite bargaining under British
  arbitration; British legitimacy absent; boundary substitutes
  for governance

Authority devolves via **boundary**, not consent. Exit precedes
stability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COST EXTERNALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Britain leaves; India and Pakistan absorb the cost.

Consequences externalized:
• Boundary-making — Radcliffe Line; arbitrary borders; no
  demographic absorption capacity; violence decentralized
• Displacement — ~14 million displaced; ~1 million deaths;
  mass migration; Britain not present to enforce order
• Kashmir — Left unresolved; accession incomplete; Britain
  withdraws before settlement
• Administrative vacuum — Policing, logistics collapse;
  Britain hands over and departs

Cost is **transferred**, not borne. Britain exits; successors
inherit the wound.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, British Partition encodes:
• Exit acceleration — Compressed timeline; haste guaranteed
  bloodshed; delay doctrine by negation
• Boundary substitutes for governance — Radcliffe draws line;
  administration cannot follow; order collapses
• Cost externalized — Britain departs; India and Pakistan
  bear displacement, violence, Kashmir
• Colonial legacy — Boundary-making as imperial exit mechanism;
  unfinished process; wound event

British Partition is remembered as **handover without responsibility**—
the mechanism through which imperial rule ended and cost was
transferred.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RELATION TO MEM–INDIA–PARTITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–PARTITION preserves Partition as **India's wound event**—
civilizational fracture, Kashmir, administrative spine retained,
Pakistan emergence, trauma. This MEM preserves Partition as
**Britain's exit mechanism**—handover, Radcliffe, cost externalized,
colonial legacy. Same event; different encoding. Tension preserved.
