I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **Republic of India** as **institutional
continuity after rupture**—the constitutional framework through which
India reconstitutes itself after Partition without dissolving its
administrative spine.

For India, the Republic encodes **procedure as survival**:
Constitution (26 January 1950), Parliament, federal structure,
judicial oversight—the inherited administrative grid repurposed
under sovereign legitimacy.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONSTITUTIONAL TRANSITION — DOMINION TO REPUBLIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
India becomes a Republic on 26 January 1950.

Transition:
• Dominion of India (15 Aug 1947–26 Jan 1950) — British monarch
  as head of state; Mountbatten, then Rajagopalachari as
  Governor-General; sovereignty acquired; Crown link residual
• Republic of India (26 Jan 1950–present) — Constitution in force;
  President as head of state; full sovereign continuity
• Constitution — Adopted 26 Nov 1949; in force 26 Jan 1950;
  longest written constitution; parliamentary democracy; federal
  union; fundamental rights; directive principles

The Republic completes the **sovereign transfer** begun at
independence. Dominion was transitional; Republic is permanent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INSTITUTIONAL CONTINUITY — ADMINISTRATIVE SPINE RETAINED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
India retains the administrative spine of British India.

Core institutions:
• Parliament — Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha; Westminster-derived;
  adapted to Indian plurality
• Civil service — IAS, IPS; continuity from ICS; administrative
  grid survives Partition
• Judiciary — Supreme Court; federal structure; constitutional
  review; inherited common-law tradition
• Federalism — States and Union Territories; linguistic
  reorganization; plurality accommodated

British procedural tools persist; **legitimacy is now indigenous**.
India repurposes the grid under its own civilizational logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING — LEGITIMACY & PROCEDURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, the Republic encodes:
• Institutional continuity — Administrative spine retained;
  Partition fractures territory, not the state apparatus
• Procedure as legitimacy — Constitution substitutes for
  dynastic or colonial authority; consent through procedure
• Plural accommodation — Federalism, linguistic states,
  secular framework; plurality managed, not suppressed
• Strategic patience — "Right to decide later" embedded
  in institutions; delay as rational strategy

The Republic is remembered as **survival through continuity**—
the mechanism through which India absorbs Partition without
dissolving.

V. RELATION TO INDEPENDENCE & PARTITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE–INDEPENDENCE preserves the **moment of
sovereignty**—1947, transfer of power, Dominion. MEM–INDIA–PARTITION
preserves the **wound**—fracture, Kashmir, cost. This MEM preserves
the **republican completion**—1950, Constitution, institutional
continuity. Same decade; different encoding. Tension preserved.
