I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Punjab** as **India's northwestern corridor
severed at Partition**—the five-rivers region (Panj = five, ab = water)
that was divided by the Radcliffe Line in 1947, with East Punjab
remaining in India and West Punjab assigned to Pakistan.

Punjab is remembered as **invasion corridor and Partition fracture**—
the same axis used by Alexander, Ghaznavids, Mughals, and the Company;
and the locus of the most violent Partition displacement.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC & CIVILIZATIONAL CORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Punjab is the **five-rivers region**—the Indus tributaries (Jhelum,
Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej) and the plains between them.

Core features:
• Fertile alluvial plains; wheat, cotton; agrarian surplus
• Northwest corridor — Same axis as invasions (Alexander, Ghaznavid,
  Mughal, British); Khyber and Bolan passes to the west
• Sikh majority (Indian Punjab); Muslim majority (pre-Partition
  western districts); Hindu–Sikh–Muslim plurality before 1947
• Indus basin — Punjab drains to Indus; Partition severed Punjab
  from Indus (MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS)

Punjab encodes **invasion corridor**—the northwestern entry axis
into the subcontinent; and **Partition fracture**—the region most
violently divided.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PARTITION FRACTURE — 1947
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Partition (1947) severs Punjab along the Radcliffe Line.

Effects:
• East Punjab — Remains in India; Sikh-majority districts;
  Chandigarh as capital (shared with Haryana)
• West Punjab — Assigned to Pakistan; Muslim-majority districts;
  Lahore as capital
• Mass displacement — ~5 million in Punjab alone; reciprocal
  violence; trains, columns, refugee camps
• Indus severed — East Punjab loses Indus; West Punjab holds
  Indus; India retains Sutlej–Beas (Indus Waters Treaty 1960)

Punjab encodes **Partition's most violent locus**—the corridor
where displacement was largest and violence most intense.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, Punjab encodes:
• Invasion corridor — Same axis as Alexander (Hyphasis), Ghaznavid,
  Mughal, Company; northwest as entry point
• Partition fracture — Radcliffe Line; West Punjab lost; East
  Punjab retained; wound event
• Sikh heartland — Indian Punjab as Sikh-majority state; Khalistan
  tension; identity and territory
• Indus severance — East Punjab cut off from Indus; India survives
  separation; MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS

Punjab is remembered as **corridor severed**—the northwest divided
by boundary-making; invasion axis becomes Partition axis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS preserves Indus as origin spine; Partition
