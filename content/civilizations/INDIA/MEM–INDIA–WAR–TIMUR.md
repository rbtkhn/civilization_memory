I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Timur's sack of Delhi (1398)** as India's
decisive **shock without conquest**: mass violence, pillage, and
the symbolic shattering of Sultanate authority—without lasting
Timurid occupation. Timur withdraws; the Delhi Sultanate survives
but is weakened; the Ganges core holds.

1398 is not conquest. It is **raiding and decapitation**—analogous
in scale of violence to what Persia experienced from the Mongols
(MEM–PERSIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE)—but not **occupation**. India endures
shock; India does not become Timurid.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INVASION & SACK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Timur (Timurid, not direct Genghisid) invades North India in 1398.

Key features:
• Timurid army penetrates to Delhi; city sacked
• Mass violence, pillage, symbolic shattering of Sultanate
  authority
• No lasting Timurid occupation; Timur withdraws

Structural outcome: **Shock without conquest**. The Sultanate
survives but is weakened—legitimacy and capacity damaged
(MEM–INDIA–DELHI–SULTANATE). The Ganges core holds; regimes
reconstitute along the basin (MEM–INDIA–GEO–GANGES).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRUCTURAL PARALLEL — SHOCK WITHOUT CONQUEST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1398 functions structurally as **invader shock at the edge**:
Timur could strike Delhi but could not sustain conquest or
administration. Depth, logistics, and Sultanate reconstitution
limit Timurid reach—analogous to force-at-distance limits in
MEM–INDIA–WAR–ALEXANDER (Hyphasis) and MEM–INDIA–WAR–GHAZNAVID
(return from Somnath).

Contrast: Persia was **conquered** by the Mongols (Ilkhanate);
India was **sacked** by Timur and **survived** (MEM–PERSIA–
MONGOL–EMPIRE). Same steppe-world threat; opposite outcome—
shock without political replacement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• No Timurid India; no lasting Timurid administration in the
  subcontinent.
• Delhi Sultanate survives; reconstitutes; then falls to Mughals
  at Panipat (1526) when defender cohesion fails (MEM–INDIA–
  DELHI–SULTANATE, MEM–INDIA–DYNASTY–MUGHAL).
• 1398 is encoded as **trauma**—sack, pillage, legitimacy shock—
  and as **survival**: India endures; Ganges core holds.

Rule learned:
India can endure steppe shock (sack, decapitation) without losing
the Ganges core. Shock without conquest is one mode of India's
encounter with Mongol-world power; deflection (Chagatai) and
absorption (Mughal) are others (MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–EMPIRE).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Timur sacked Delhi; Timur did not rule India.
• Sultanate survived; reconstituted; fell later at Panipat.
• Shock without conquest = trauma + survival; no political
  replacement.
• Northwest corridor = axis of Timur's entry (same as Alexander,
  Chagatai, Company).
• Contrast with Persia: Mongols conquered Persia; Timur sacked
  India and left.

Tension preserved without verdict.
