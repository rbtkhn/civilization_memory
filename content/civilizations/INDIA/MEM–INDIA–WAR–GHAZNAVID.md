I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Ghaznavid campaigns in India** as India’s
early **force-at-distance / invader limit**: Mahmud of Ghazni’s
raids (e.g. Somnath), return hardships (1026), and limited
territorial hold—only Punjab annexed; broader Indian territories
remained difficult to control. No army mutiny; logistical and
geographic resistance and local harassment (e.g. Jat population)
function as the structural limit.

Ghaznavid pressure is the **pre-Mongol** Islamic invader limit—
analogous in type to Hyphasis (invader at edge; force at distance
binds) and to Ghaznavid return from Somnath (1026): hardship in
Sind, Jat harassment, protracted withdrawal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GHAZNAVID CAMPAIGNS — RAID & RETURN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mahmud of Ghazni (Ghaznavid Sultanate, Afghanistan) launched
repeated campaigns into North India from the late tenth century.

Key features:
• Raids for loot and tribute; Somnath (1026) as symbolic and
  material target
• Return from Somnath (1026): “significant hardships” crossing
  Sind desert and Sindh-Sagar Doab; “persistent harassment and
  delays from the local Jat population”; protracted withdrawal
• Ghaznavids permanently annexed only Punjab; “broader Indian
  territories remained difficult to control”

Structural outcome: **Force at distance hits limit**. Not army
mutiny (Hyphasis-type) but logistical/geographic and local
resistance as binding constraint. Depth and monsoon and local
resistance limit invader hold—same structural type as MEM–
INDIA–WAR–ALEXANDER (Hyphasis) and MEM–INDIA–MONGOL–CHAGATAI
(deflection).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRUCTURAL PARALLEL — INVADER AT THE EDGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ghaznavid campaigns function structurally as **invader pressure
at the edge**: the Ghaznavids could raid deep (e.g. Somnath) but
could not sustain conquest or administration across the
subcontinent. Return hardships and local resistance limit
reach—analogous to force-at-distance limits in MEM–INDIA–WAR–
ALEXANDER (Hyphasis) and to Ghaznavid return from Somnath (1026)
cited in RESEARCH–INDIA–MILITARY–HISTORY–DEFECTION–THEORY and
CIV–SCHOLAR–INDIA SYNTHESIS 0003.

Contrast: Ghaznavids held Punjab; they did **not** hold the
Ganges core. India’s depth and local resistance function as
structural limit—pre-Mongol, pre-Timur, pre-Company.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes:
• Ghaznavids annexed Punjab; did not conquer the Ganges core or
  the subcontinent.
• Return from Somnath (1026) encodes **force at distance** and
  **depth resistance** as structural limit—no army mutiny but
  same binding constraint.
• Northwest corridor is confirmed as the recurring axis of
  external pressure (Alexander, Ghaznavid, Chagatai, Timur,
  Company) (MEM–INDIA–GEO–INDUS).

Rule learned:
India can limit invader hold through depth, logistics, and local
resistance—force at distance binds before conquest is achieved.
Ghaznavid limit is the **pre-Mongol** instance of the same
structural type (Hyphasis-type).
