I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the **caste system** as **India's civilizational
hierarchy encoded and contested through law**—the structure of varna
and jati that has been hardened by legal classification, constrained
by constitutional reform, and perpetuated by social practice across
regimes.

Caste is remembered not as a single verdict but as **legal encoding
in tension**—ancient dharmashastra, colonial census, republican
Constitution; law both reinforces and reforms; neither erasure nor
closure.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ANCIENT ENCODING — DHARMA & ADMINISTRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Caste enters Indian civilizational memory through **dharmic and
administrative encoding**.

Key elements:
• **Varna** — Four-fold scheme (Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra);
  dharmashastra; ritual and occupational hierarchy
• **Jati** — Endogamous birth-groups; local variation; multiplicity
  beyond varna scheme
• **Dharmashastra** — Law as moral and ritual order; caste as
  dharmic structure; enforcement through royal authority
• **Arthashastra** — Law as administration; caste acknowledged in
  statecraft; order maintained, not created (MEM–INDIA–LIT–ARTHASHASTRA)

Ancient law **encodes** caste—it does not invent it. The structure
precedes legal formulation; law stabilises and legitimates.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. COLONIAL ENCODING — CENSUS & HARDENING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
British administration **hardens** caste through imperial legibility
(MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE).

Key elements:
• **Census** — Enumeration of castes; classification for revenue,
  administration, representation; categories fixed in print
• **Schedule** — Lists of "criminal tribes," "depressed classes";
  administrative categories that acquire legal force
• **Representation** — Communal awards; separate electorates;
  caste as political category (prelude to Partition dynamics)
• **Imperial legibility** — British need to classify produces
  categories that outlast empire; caste becomes administratively
  real in new ways

Colonial law **hardens** caste—enumeration and classification
create legible categories that persist. Law does not dissolve
caste; it reifies it for administrative use.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. REPUBLICAN ENCODING — CONSTITUTION & REFORM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Republic **constrains and reforms** caste through constitutional
law (MEM–INDIA–REPUBLIC).

Key elements:
• **Article 15** — Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of
  religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth
• **Article 17** — Abolition of untouchability; enforcement
  through legislation
• **Scheduled Castes (SC)** — Constitutional category; reserved
  seats, quotas; positive discrimination as redress
• **Scheduled Tribes (ST)** — Indigenous communities; separate
  reservation; tribal autonomy
• **Other Backward Classes (OBC)** — Mandal Commission (1980s);
  extended reservations; political mobilisation around caste
• **Reservations** — Education, employment, legislature; procedure
  as reform mechanism; legitimacy contested

Republican law **constrains** caste—untouchability abolished,
discrimination prohibited—and **recognises** it—reservations
require enumeration; categories persist. Law reforms and reifies
simultaneously.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL ENCODING — TENSION PRESERVED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For India, caste through law encodes:

• **Civilizational continuity** — Varna and jati predate law;
  law encodes, does not create; structure persists across regimes
• **Legal reform** — Constitution constrains; untouchability
  abolished; reservations as redress; procedure as legitimacy
• **Legal reification** — Reservations require categories; census
  and schedule persist; law fixes what it seeks to reform
• **Tension** — Law cannot erase caste by decree; law cannot
  ignore caste without abandoning redress; neither closure nor
  erasure
• **Legitimacy contested** — Reservations as justice vs reservations
  as privilege; civilizational hierarchy vs constitutional equality;
  which encoding India keeps remains contested

Caste through law is remembered as **encoding in tension**—
constraint and recognition; reform and reification; civilizational
continuity and constitutional rupture. No synthesis.

VI. RELATION TO GANDHI, REPUBLIC & BRITISH EMPIRE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEM–INDIA–GANDHI preserves anti-untouchability as moral–mobilizational
legacy; Harijan; legitimacy attack on hierarchy. MEM–INDIA–REPUBLIC
preserves Constitution as institutional framework; Article 15, 17;
procedure as legitimacy. MEM–INDIA–BRITISH–EMPIRE preserves colonial
administration; census; imperial legibility. This MEM preserves
**caste through law**—the structure encoded, hardened, constrained,
and contested across regimes. Same structure; different encodings.
Tension preserved.
