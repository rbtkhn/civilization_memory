I. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file is the **exclusive registry of India civilizational axioms**, derived from CORE (Section I) and from Scholar (syntheses promoted to axiom via DIB–INDIA).

It contains:
• Axioms extracted from CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I (Prime Axioms), in standard format
• Axioms promoted from CIV–SCHOLAR–INDIA synthesis via DIB–INDIA (when first-principles)
• No learning history, no beliefs, no provisional or rejected axioms (see Section IV)

This file does NOT learn, does NOT synthesize, does NOT evolve autonomously. It extracts and records.

**Axiom vs doctrine:** Axioms are interpretive anchors and precedence rules (identity, first principles). Doctrines are named pattern-constraints with definitions, operational meaning, and hard constraints (CIV–DOCTRINE–INDIA). Scholar synthesis may be promoted to axiom or doctrine per DIB–INDIA, whichever is appropriate.

CIV–MEM–CORE → CIV–CORE–INDIA → (extraction) → CIV–AXIOM–INDIA
CIV–SCHOLAR–INDIA → (promotion to axiom via DIB–INDIA) → CIV–AXIOM–INDIA

Axiom Admissibility:

• Scholar-derived: Synthesis promoted to axiom via DIB–INDIA; record with Source: SYNTHESIS ####.

Only axioms listed here may be cited as **authoritative axiom** by CIV–CORE engines. CORE Section I remains the source for the initial set; this file is the canonical registry and may be mirrored back into CORE/Scholar for citation.

II. AXIOM REGISTRY (AUTHORITATIVE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The following axioms are derived from CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I (Civilizational Identity & Prime Axioms). Each is an extraction into standard format; the authoritative wording for identity remains in CORE.

AXIOM INDIA-001: Accretive continuity precedes rupture.
  Statement: Indus → Vedic → Mauryan → Gupta → Indo-Persian → Mughal → British → Republic form cumulative layers, not rupture cycles. India survives by absorption, not reset.
  Scope: All civilizational continuity and identity analysis.
  Limits: Does not deny regime change or conquest; encodes mode of survival (absorption vs reset).
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.1 (Accretive Continuity Civilization).

AXIOM INDIA-002: Pluralism is structural resilience.
  Statement: Linguistic, caste, religious, and regional diversity function as a redundancy field against systemic collapse. Pluralism is structural resilience, not ideological preference.
  Scope: Institutional design, legitimacy, and cohesion analysis.
  Limits: Does not prescribe policy; encodes how diversity functions in India’s grammar.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.2 (Plural Cohesion Engine).

AXIOM INDIA-003: Dharma–state–procedure operate as one control system.
  Statement: Dharmic legitimacy + Kautilyan realism + Indo-Persian administration + British procedural inheritance + republican federalism operate as one civilizational control system.
  Scope: Legitimacy accounting and governance pattern analysis.
  Limits: Does not rank elements; encodes fusion, not sequence.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.3 (Dharma–State–Procedure Fusion Stack).

AXIOM INDIA-004: Time is weaponized; decades and generations are operational units.
  Statement: India weaponizes time. Decades are tactical units; generations are operational cycles.
  Scope: Strategic patience, delay, and long-cycle analysis. HARD CONSTRAINT in CORE.
  Limits: Does not forbid short-term action; encodes tactical/operational scale.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.4 (Strategic Patience Doctrine).

AXIOM INDIA-005: Internal fragmentation follows external penetration.
  Statement: Internal fragmentation historically follows external penetration, not endogenous decay.
  Scope: Collapse and unity analysis; subcontinental cohesion.
  Limits: Does not deny internal conflict; encodes causal precedence.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.5 (Subcontinental Unity Principle).

AXIOM INDIA-006: Dharma-legitimacy filters authority.
  Statement: Authority is judged by balance, proportionality, continuity, and order. Raw coercion without dharmic framing degrades legitimacy.
  Scope: Legitimacy accounting and authority evaluation.
  Limits: Does not define dharmic; encodes filter, not content.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.6 (Dharma-Legitimacy Filter).

AXIOM INDIA-007: Diffusion precedes conquest.
  Statement: Power projects through culture, diaspora, trade, education, standards, and technology—conquest is disfavored.
  Scope: Influence and power-projection analysis.
  Limits: Does not forbid conquest; encodes preference and default mode.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.7 (Diffusion-First Influence Model).

AXIOM INDIA-008: Autonomy precedes bloc loyalty.
  Statement: Autonomy over bloc loyalty. Multipolarity is India’s natural equilibrium, not a forced objective.
  Scope: Alignment, non-alignment, and external-order analysis. LOCKED in CORE.
  Limits: Does not forbid alignment in specific cases; encodes default and equilibrium.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.8 (Civilizational Non-Alignment OS).

AXIOM INDIA-009: Temporal advantage compounds with patience.
  Statement: Demography, memory, and long cycles compound power when patience is maintained.
  Scope: Long-cycle and demographic-strategic analysis.
  Limits: Does not guarantee outcome; encodes compounding condition.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.9 (Temporal Advantage Weaponization).

AXIOM INDIA-010: Computation is domesticated through plural frameworks.
  Statement: India domesticates computation through plural, federal frameworks rather than centralized technocracy.
  Scope: Technological sovereignty and compute governance.
  Limits: Does not forbid central elements; encodes default mode.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.10 (Algorithmic Absorption Reflex).

AXIOM INDIA-011: Caloric–demographic–industrial form one long-logistics engine.
  Statement: Youth demography + food resilience + industrial expansion form one long-logistics engine.
  Scope: Economic-industrial and demographic-strategic analysis.
  Limits: Does not rank the three; encodes trinity, not sequence.
  Derivation: CIV–CORE–INDIA Section I.11 (Caloric–Demographic–Industrial Trinity).
