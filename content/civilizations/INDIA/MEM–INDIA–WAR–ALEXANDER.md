I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Alexander’s invasion as **India’s first recorded
external probe by a universal empire**—and as an early demonstration
of India’s depth resistance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INVASION DYNAMICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Alexander enters through the northwest.

Characteristics:
• Tactical superiority
• Fragmented Indian polities
• Limited geographic penetration
• No interior consolidation

Victory occurs at edges, not core.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DEPTH RESISTANCE & WITHDRAWAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Key constraints:
• Monsoon cycles
• Extended supply lines
• Troop exhaustion
• Unfamiliar scale

India defeats invasion without decisive battle.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CIVILIZATIONAL OUTCOME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effects:
• Greek presence transient
• No cultural overwrite
• Mauryan consolidation follows

Foreign shock catalyzes internal unification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• India neutralizes conquest through scale.
• Depth defeats speed.
• Fragmentation invites entry; absorption defeats exit.
• External victories do not equal civilizational control.
• Alexander marks the first failed universalist intrusion.

Tension preserved without verdict.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. EVIDENCE — ARC-T-ANCIENT (Cross-ARC: Herodotus, ARC–PERSIA-T-ANCIENT)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Northwest India under Achaemenid rule—the context Alexander encounters—is attested in Herodotus [ERC-PRIMARY]. Per CIV–ARC–TEMPLATE § V-A, India TSP MEMs may cite ARC–PERSIA with explicit declaration.

> "The Indians made up the twentieth province. These are more in number than any nation of which we know, and they paid a greater tribute than any other province, namely three hundred and sixty talents of gold dust."
> — Herodotus, *Histories*, III.94 (trans. A.D. Godley) (ARC–PERSIA-T-ANCIENT)

> "But as to Asia, most of it was discovered by Darius. There is a river Indus... Darius, desiring to know where this Indus issues into the sea, sent ships manned by Scylax, a man of Caryanda, and others in whose word he trusted; these set out from the city Caspatyrus and the Pactyic country, and sailed down the river towards the east and the sunrise till they came to the sea; and voyaging over the sea westwards, they came in the thirtieth month to that place whence the Egyptian king sent the Phoenicians... After this circumnavigation Darius subdued the Indians and made use of this sea."
> — Herodotus, *Histories*, IV.44 (ARC–PERSIA-T-ANCIENT)
