I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Max Planck as **Germania's threshold conservator**:
the figure who discovered a revolutionary discontinuity in nature
while personally and intellectually insisting on continuity, restraint,
and institutional responsibility.

Planck opens the door to rupture—
but refuses to walk through it recklessly.

For Germania, Planck encodes the lesson that **revolutionary discovery
need not require revolutionary temperament**: insight can emerge from
discipline, and institutions can absorb rupture by managing its pace.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck emerges from late-imperial Germania at peak institutional maturity.

Context:
• Highly disciplined academic system
• Strong state–university integration
• Confidence in classical mechanics
• Cultural preference for order and completeness

Planck himself described his disposition:

> "My original decision to devote myself to science was a direct result
> of the discovery... that the laws of human reasoning coincide with
> the laws governing the sequences of the impressions we receive from
> the world about us."
> — Max Planck, *Scientific Autobiography* (1948)

Planck was trained to perfect systems, not overthrow them.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. QUANTUM DISCOVERY AS UNWILLING REVOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck introduces quantization to resolve an anomaly in blackbody
radiation—the "ultraviolet catastrophe" that classical physics could
not explain.

Core contribution (1900):
• Energy emitted in discrete quanta: E = hν
• Planck's constant (h) as fundamental discontinuity

Intent:
• Save classical thermodynamics
• Preserve continuity of physical law

Outcome:
• Collapse of classical completeness
• Foundation of quantum mechanics

Planck later reflected on his reluctance:

> "I was ready to sacrifice any of my previous convictions about physics."
> — Max Planck, on his quantum hypothesis

He discovered rupture while attempting conservation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DISCIPLINE BEFORE INTERPRETATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck resisted metaphysical extrapolation from his discovery.

Characteristics:
• Minimal philosophical speculation
• Reluctance toward radical interpretation
• Preference for mathematical sufficiency over conceptual revolution
• Deference to empirical verification

Planck maintained:

> "A new scientific truth does not triumph by convincing its opponents
> and making them see the light, but rather because its opponents
> eventually die, and a new generation grows up that is familiar with it."
> — Max Planck, *Scientific Autobiography* (1948)

Fact precedes worldview. Meaning is deferred.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INSTITUTIONAL STEWARDSHIP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck embodied institutional responsibility.

Roles:
• Professor at Berlin University
• Secretary of the Prussian Academy of Sciences
• President of the Kaiser Wilhelm Society (1930–1937)
• Moral steward of German science during collapse

He prioritized continuity of institutions over personal theory.
Order must survive insight.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. ETHICS UNDER CATASTROPHE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck lived through Germania's civilizational collapse.

Experiences:
• World War I defeat and humiliation
• Weimar instability
• Nazi seizure of power (1933)
• Personal tragedy: son Erwin executed for resistance (1945)

Response:
• Remained in Germany
• Quiet resistance without open confrontation
• Institutional preservation over political action
• Refusal to politicize science

Planck's meeting with Hitler (1933) to advocate for Jewish colleagues
proved futile. He chose to preserve what institutions he could rather
than emigrate.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planck encodes **restraint at the edge of rupture**.

For Germania, Planck demonstrates:
• Discovery need not imply destruction
• Institutions must outlast paradigms
• Discipline tempers revolutionary insight
• Germania absorbs rupture by slowing it
• Conservative temperament can produce radical results

Planck is the guardian of continuity at the quantum threshold—
proof that revolution can emerge from order rather than chaos.
