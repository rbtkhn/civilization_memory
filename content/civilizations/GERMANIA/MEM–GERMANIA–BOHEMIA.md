────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Bohemia** as Germania’s most persistent case of
**internal adjacency**: culturally distinct, administratively integrated,
and politically volatile under pressure.

Bohemia is neither external frontier nor absorbed core.
It is a fault line within empire.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EARLY KINGDOM & IMPERIAL EMBEDDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bohemia enters the German sphere through imperial association, not
conquest.

Cosmas of Prague records guarded autonomy:

> “The Czechs accepted the emperor as protector, not master, and kept
> their laws even while serving his wars.”  
> — Cosmas of Prague, *Chronica Boemorum*, Book II

Integration is conditional and reversible.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. HUSSITE SHOCK & DOCTRINAL FRACTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Hussite Wars rupture imperial cohesion.

Aeneas Silvius Piccolomini observes:

> “In Bohemia, faith became weapon and law collapsed; obedience could
> no longer be commanded, only fought for.”  
> — Piccolomini, *Historia Bohemica*

Religious divergence converts internal diversity into civil war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. HABSBURG ADMINISTRATION & CULTURAL RESISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Habsburg rule restores order but not harmony.

Czech estates submit administratively while preserving memory.

Order exists without consent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. NATIONAL AWAKENING & LANGUAGE POLITICS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
19th-century revival reframes Bohemia as suppressed core.

German administration becomes symbol of alien control.

Compatibility erodes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. 20TH-CENTURY DISINTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Munich and its aftermath sever trust.

Czech memory encodes betrayal; German memory encodes strategic delay.

The shared imperial grammar collapses.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. POST-WAR SEPARATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1945:
• German population removed
• Historical interweaving erased
• Bohemia becomes nationally sealed

The interface ends violently, not gradually.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Internal adjacency amplifies ideological shocks.
• Administrative inclusion does not guarantee loyalty.
• Religious fracture destabilizes imperial order.
• Language politics accelerate separation.
• Germania’s internal diversity fails without restraint.

Contradictions preserved without verdict.
