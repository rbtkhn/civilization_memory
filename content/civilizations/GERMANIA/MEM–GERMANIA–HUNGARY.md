I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Hungary** as a **persistent civilizational
counter-force within German imperial structures**: a polity that
repeatedly resists absorption, constrains administrative depth, and
forces Germania to govern through compromise, delay, or bifurcation.

Hungary is not Germania’s periphery.
It is Germania’s **internal limit case**.

This file does not narrate Hungarian national destiny.
It records how Hungary functions as **constraint memory** inside
German imperial attempts.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EARLY KINGDOM & LEGAL PARTICULARISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hungary enters Central Europe as a sovereign legal culture.

King Stephen I establishes a Christian kingdom without feudal
subordination to German princes.

Stephen writes:

> “I have commanded that guests and strangers be received kindly,
> for a kingdom of one language and one custom is weak and fragile.”  
> — Stephen I of Hungary, *Admonitions*

Pluralism is a strength—but sovereignty is non-negotiable.

Hungary’s legitimacy grammar is **legal autonomy**, not imperial hierarchy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MEDIEVAL RESISTANCE TO IMPERIAL ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hungary resists incorporation into the Holy Roman Empire.

While dynastic marriages occur, **institutional absorption does not**.

The *Golden Bull of 1222* asserts elite resistance:

> “If we or our successors attempt anything contrary to these
> liberties, all bishops and nobles may lawfully resist us.”  
> — *Golden Bull of Hungary* (1222)

Resistance is legalized.
Authority is conditional.

This produces a durable refusal to centralized imperial administration.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. HUNGARY AS MILITARY BUFFER, NOT ADMINISTRATIVE CORE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hungary functions as a **military frontier** against Ottoman power,
but not as a German administrative extension.

Niccolò Machiavelli observes frontier states:

> “Those who defend another’s state with their arms do so
> unwillingly, and their loyalty ends when danger passes.”  
> — Machiavelli, *The Prince*

Hungary defends empire—but does not internalize it.

Defense does not equal obedience.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. HABSBURG RULE & STRUCTURAL NON-INTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Under Habsburg rule, Hungary remains **legally distinct**.

Administration is negotiated, not imposed.

Joseph II admits failure:

> “I have attempted to govern Hungary as I do my other lands,
> and I have failed entirely.”  
> — Joseph II, correspondence (1780s)

German administrative logic encounters a **sovereignty firewall**.

Uniformity collapses under resistance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. 1848 REVOLUTION & REFUSAL OF SUBSUMPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hungary revolts not to reform empire—but to exit it.

Lajos Kossuth declares:

> “We demand a Hungary governed by Hungarians,
> responsible to the nation alone.”  
> — Kossuth, Speech to the Diet (1848)

This is not liberalism.
It is **jurisdictional rejection**.

Germania cannot govern Hungary by ideology or force alone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. THE AUSGLEICH AS HUNGARIAN IMPOSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The 1867 Compromise is not mutual synthesis.

It is Hungarian leverage after imperial defeat.

Gyula Andrássy states:

> “Without Hungary, the empire cannot stand;
> without autonomy, Hungary will not remain.”  
> — Andrássy, parliamentary speech

Dualism emerges as **forced accommodation**, not shared vision.

Hungary becomes a **permanent veto actor** inside empire.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. WORLD WAR I & EXIT ACCELERATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total war breaks the compromise.

Hungary disengages faster than Austria.

Count Mihály Károlyi admits:

> “The war destroyed the illusion that compromise
> could preserve the state.”  
> — Károlyi, memoirs

When crisis exceeds tolerance, Hungary exits.

Constraint converts to rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For Germania, Hungary encodes recurring lessons:

• Legal autonomy defeats administrative absorption
• Compromise preserves structure but empowers veto
• Delay strategies accumulate asymmetric exits
• Dual sovereignty collapses under total war
• Constraint civilizations shape imperial ceilings

Hungary teaches Germania **where governance stops working**.
