I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Merovingian dynasty as **Germania’s pre-imperial
substrate**: a lineage that established conquest-based kingship but failed
to complete the transition to durable administrative order.

For Germania, the Merovingians represent **rule without discipline**—
power held personally, not systematized.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONQUEST KINGSHIP MODEL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Merovingian authority derives from victory and lineage.

Core traits:
• Warrior kingship
• Personal retinues
• Land distribution as reward
• Bloodline legitimacy

Rule remains mobile and patrimonial.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FRAGMENTATION AS STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Division among heirs is normal, not pathological.

Consequences:
• Repeated territorial partition
• Competing royal courts
• Weak central enforcement

Unity dissolves by inheritance logic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ABSENCE OF ADMINISTRATIVE DEPTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Merovingian governance lacks durable systems.

Deficits:
• No standing bureaucracy
• Minimal fiscal coordination
• Reliance on personal loyalty

Power decays between rulers.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. SACRALITY WITHOUT DISCIPLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kingship is religiously infused but weakly enforced.

Characteristics:
• Sacred aura
• Ritual legitimacy
• Limited coercive reach

Sacrality legitimizes rule but cannot stabilize it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MAYORAL SUBSTITUTION DYNAMIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective power migrates to mayors of the palace.

Pattern:
• Administrative competence displaces lineage
• Carolingians govern before ruling
• Dynasty hollowed from within

Replacement precedes formal transition.

Johannes Fried notes that Carolingian ascendancy was gradual rather than
revolutionary: the mayors of the palace "accumulated real power while
Merovingian kings retained ritual significance," until finally "symbol
and substance were reunited in a single lineage" (*The Middle Ages*).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. TERMINATION & ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Merovingians are replaced, not overthrown.

Outcome:
• Peaceful dynastic transfer (751)
• Retention of conquest norms
• Discarding of ineffective structures

Germania absorbs function, not form.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Conquest alone cannot sustain rule.
• Lineage without system decays rapidly.
• Administrative competence becomes legitimacy.
• Germania replaces dynasties before expanding.
• Merovingians are a necessary but insufficient foundation.

Tension preserved without verdict.
