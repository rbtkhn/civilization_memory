I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Heinrich Schliemann as Germania's archetype of
archaeological recovery: the transformation of epic narrative into
material proof, and the use of discovery to strengthen civilizational
continuity claims.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ARCHAEOLOGY AS CIVILIZATIONAL CLAIM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann's excavations convert myth into evidence, yielding a
Germanic method for anchoring prestige in material record.

Civilizational effects:
• authority through discovery
• legitimacy via excavation
• narrative reinforced by artifact

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. METHOD VS. MYTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann operates between rigorous method and romantic obsession.
The tension yields both breakthroughs and distortions.

Implications:
• discovery accelerates legitimacy
• method weakness threatens credibility
• narrative bias can pre-shape evidence

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. NATIONAL PRESTIGE AND EXTERNAL VALIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann's work becomes a prestige instrument for Germany:
proof of cultural depth through ancient recovery beyond its borders.

This reinforces:
• external validation seeking
• cultural projection over territorial control
• archival legitimacy through physical proof

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LIMITS AND LIABILITIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann's methods were intrusive and sometimes destructive.

Structural liability:
• discovery can destroy the record it seeks to preserve
• personal ambition can override institutional restraint
• national prestige can distort methodological rigor

These limits are part of the memory object.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELATION TO GERMANIC DISCIPLINE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann's success depends on disciplined documentation after the fact.
Germanic administrative competence absorbs his discovery into stable record.

Without procedural correction, discovery becomes myth reinforcement only.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Schliemann encodes the legitimacy of evidence recovery but warns that
evidence without method degrades credibility.

The lesson is dual:
• discovery strengthens memory
• uncontrolled discovery damages it

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Evidence recovered is a legitimacy asset only when method is sound.
• Prestige-seeking can distort archaeological rigor.
• Material proof can stabilize narrative claims.
• Discovery without restraint produces long-term credibility loss.
• Germania prefers recovered record to ungrounded myth.

Tension preserved without verdict.
