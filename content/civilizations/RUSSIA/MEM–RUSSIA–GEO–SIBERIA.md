────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. STRATEGIC PURPOSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Siberia as a **strategic depth multiplier**:
a continental mass whose scale, climate, and remoteness convert
apparent emptiness into decisive military advantage.

The fact is that Siberia is not a liability. It is the single
greatest defensive asset any state possesses. No invader has
ever conquered Russia while Siberia remained available for
retreat, industrial relocation, and resource extraction.

States do not attack Siberia.
They exhaust themselves trying to reach it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC DIMENSIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia's strategic value derives from scale beyond military reach.

Physical parameters:
• ~13.1 million km² (larger than any country except Russia itself)
• ~5,500 km east-west (Moscow to Vladivostok)
• ~3,500 km north-south (Arctic to Mongolian border)
• Average January temperature: -25°C to -50°C

Strategic implications:
• Logistics collapse: no army can supply operations across this distance
• Time dilation: campaigns that begin in summer end in winter
• Attrition guarantee: climate destroys forces before combat

Siberia is not defensible in the conventional sense.
It is **invader-proof by geometry**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. STRATEGIC DEPTH LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Strategic depth means the ability to trade space for time.

Siberia provides:
• Unlimited retreat capacity (no terminal geography)
• Industrial relocation sanctuary
• Population dispersal zone
• Resource base beyond enemy reach

The structural effect:
• Invaders must advance thousands of kilometers
• Each kilometer extends supply lines
• Winter arrives regardless of military success
• The defender chooses when to fight

"Russia's strategic depth is its ultimate weapon. No European
power has the logistical capacity to project force across the
Russian landmass and sustain operations in Siberia. The space
itself destroys armies."
— John J. Mearsheimer, *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics* (2001)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CLIMATE AS FORCE MULTIPLIER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberian climate is not weather. It is a strategic weapon.

Operational constraints:
• Winter immobilizes mechanized forces
• Permafrost limits construction and logistics
• Short summers compress operational windows
• Extreme temperatures degrade equipment and personnel

The defender is adapted.
The attacker is destroyed.

Historical pattern (structural, not narrative):
• Every invasion of Russia that reached winter failed
• Climate attrition exceeds combat attrition
• Time favors the defender absolutely

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RESOURCE RESERVOIR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia converts strategic depth into economic power.

Resource base:
• Oil and natural gas (West Siberian Basin: world's largest)
• Minerals: gold, diamonds, nickel, platinum, rare earths
• Timber: ~25% of world's forest reserves
• Fresh water: Lake Baikal (~20% of world's surface fresh water)

Strategic implications:
• Autarky potential: Russia can survive sanctions and blockade
• War-sustaining capacity: resources enable prolonged conflict
• Leverage over dependent states: energy exports as power

The state that controls Siberia controls resources sufficient
to sustain great-power competition indefinitely.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. INDUSTRIAL SANCTUARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia provides strategic industrial depth.

Sanctuary logic:
• Industries relocated beyond enemy air/missile range
• Production continues despite territorial loss in west
• Redundancy prevents decapitation strikes

Permanent structural advantage:
• No adversary can simultaneously occupy European Russia
  AND project power into Siberia
• Industrial relocation buys time for mobilization
• Depth enables survival of state capacity

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. TRANSPORT CORRIDORS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia's rivers and railways are strategic arteries.

River systems:
• Ob, Yenisei, Lena flow north (Arctic access)
• East-west movement requires rail or road

Rail network:
• Trans-Siberian Railway: single strategic corridor
• BAM (Baikal-Amur Mainline): redundancy route

Strategic vulnerability:
• Rail interdiction could isolate Pacific coast
• Limited east-west routes create chokepoints
• Infrastructure concentration enables targeting

The defender must protect corridors.
The attacker cannot reach them.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. POWER DISTRIBUTION IMPLICATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia structures Russia's position in the international system.

For Russia:
• Strategic depth guarantees survival against invasion
• Resources enable sustained great-power competition
• Industrial sanctuary prevents knockout blows
• Climate advantage deters aggression

For potential adversaries:
• Conquest of Russia is logistically impossible
• Partial occupation gains nothing strategic
• Siberian resources remain beyond reach
• Winter nullifies technological advantages

For China:
• Siberian resources are accessible via partnership
• Conquest unnecessary if extraction rights secured
• Russian weakness increases Chinese leverage

The structural reality: Siberia makes Russia unconquerable.
This shapes all strategic calculations.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. TERRAIN AS PERMANENT CONSTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Geography does not change. Political control changes.
Siberia's strategic logic remains constant across all regimes.

Permanent patterns:
• Depth defeats invasion
• Climate favors defense
• Resources sustain state power
• Remoteness preserves sanctuary

This applied to:
• Tsarist Russia vs. Napoleon
• Soviet Union vs. Nazi Germany
• Russian Federation vs. any future adversary

The terrain is permanent. The advantage is permanent.
