────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Siberia as **Russia’s depth engine and endurance
multiplier**: a vast continental space that converts distance, climate,
and scale into security, resources, and civilizational insulation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC CHARACTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core traits:
• Enormous longitudinal depth
• Extreme climate and seasonal variation
• Sparse population density
• River-dominated transport corridors

Siberia privileges **time, patience, and logistics** over maneuver.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INCORPORATION & EXPANSION LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russian expansion into Siberia is rapid but thin.

Key dynamics:
• Cossack-led penetration
• Riverine advance (Ob, Yenisei, Lena)
• Forts preceding settlement
• Tribute extraction (yasak) over direct administration

Expansion is **additive**, not integrative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SECURITY & STRATEGIC DEPTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia functions as a defensive asset.

Effects:
• Absorbs invasion pressure
• Enables strategic withdrawal
• Dilutes enemy reach
• Provides sanctuary for industry and population

Depth substitutes for fortification.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RESOURCE RESERVOIR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia underwrites state power.

Resources:
• Fur (early driver)
• Minerals and metals
• Energy (oil, gas)
• Timber and water

Extraction is state-centered and long-horizon.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DEMOGRAPHY, CONTROL, & GOVERNANAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Governance emphasizes presence over density.

Patterns:
• Penal colonies and exile
• Administrative minimalism
• Infrastructure nodes over uniform rule

Control is achieved by **reach**, not saturation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. SIBERIA IN RUSSIAN STRATEGIC CULTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Siberia shapes national psychology.

Enduring meanings:
• Inexhaustible reserve
• Place of hardship and renewal
• Ultimate fallback space

The existence of Siberia lowers urgency elsewhere.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Scale converts weakness into resilience.
• Distance is a weapon when time is available.
• Thin control can still yield strategic dominance.
• Resources stabilize long wars and late systems.
• Siberia anchors Russia’s belief in survival through space.

Tension preserved without verdict.
