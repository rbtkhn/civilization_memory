I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Belarus as a **stability-oriented western corridor**
in Russian civilizational memory: a territory associated less with
identity rupture and more with continuity, absorption, and defensive
depth.

Belarus is remembered as **buffer made interior**, rather than contested
origin space.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. HISTORICAL POSITIONING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belarusian lands historically lie between:
• Russian core territories
• Baltic and Polish influence zones

Characteristics:
• Flat terrain
• Limited natural barriers
• Frequent transit by armies

Geographic axiom:
→ Flat corridors demand political alignment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CONFESSIONAL & CULTURAL PATTERN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belarus experiences:
• Orthodox dominance with Catholic overlays
• Lower elite polarization
• Weaker separatist traditions

In memory:
• Cultural blending produces compliance more than revolt.

Belarus lacks a sharp civilizational break narrative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ROLE IN IMPERIAL & SOVIET SYSTEMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Under empire and USSR:
• Belarus serves as defensive depth
• Administrative integration is deep
• National differentiation is muted

The region is treated as **structural interior**, not frontier.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WAR & INVASION MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belarus absorbs repeated invasions:
• Napoleonic Wars
• World War I
• World War II

Memory emphasizes:
• Destruction endured
• Population sacrifice
• Loyalty under occupation

Belarus becomes synonymous with suffering corridor.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. POST-SOVIET ALIGNMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1991:
• Belarus maintains close alignment with Russia
• Functions as strategic buffer
• Limits western penetration

Continuity is prioritized over reorientation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For Russia, Belarus functions as:
• Western shield
• Transit stabilizer
• Political depth zone

Belarus reduces exposure rather than amplifying it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Flat geography incentivizes alignment.
