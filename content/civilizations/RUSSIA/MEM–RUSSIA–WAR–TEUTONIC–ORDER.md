────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the wars between Rus’ principalities and the
Teutonic Knights as a **civilizational boundary conflict**: a struggle
over faith, sovereignty, and cultural survival rather than territory
alone.

These wars are remembered as **defensive and existential**, not
imperial.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. NORTHERN CRUSADES CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Teutonic Knights operate as militant Christian expansionists.

Features:
• Papal sanction
• Armed conversion
• Fortress-based expansion
• Political absorption of conquered lands

War is justified as salvation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. RUSSIAN VULNERABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rus’ faces Western threat during maximum weakness.

Conditions:
• Mongol domination
• Fragmented defenses
• Limited manpower
• Strategic exhaustion

Western timing exploits civilizational fatigue.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BATTLE OF THE NEVA (1240)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Swedish-aligned forces are repelled.

Memory emphasis:
• Defensive victory
• Protection of access routes
• Emergence of Alexander Nevsky

Victory reinforces selective resistance doctrine.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. BATTLE ON THE ICE (1242)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Alexander Nevsky defeats Teutonic forces on Lake Peipus.

The *Novgorod First Chronicle* records:

> "Prince Alexander went against the Germans on the ice of Lake Peipus,
> and there was a great battle. The Germans fled, and Alexander pursued
> them, striking them down upon the ice. And many were drowned in the
> lake, and many were taken captive."
> — *Novgorod First Chronicle*, entry for 1242 (trans. Michell & Forbes)

Significance:
• Halt of eastward crusading expansion
• Preservation of Orthodox frontier
• Symbolic triumph of faith over force

The battle becomes civilizational myth.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. WAR AIMS & LIMITS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Rus’ does not pursue annihilation.

Characteristics:
• Defensive objectives
• No conversion campaigns
• No territorial absorption westward

War aims are bounded by survival, not dominance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. MEMORY & MYTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The conflict is remembered through moral framing.

Emphases:
• Faith defended
• Invader repelled
• Civilization preserved

Military details recede; meaning endures.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
These wars establish a durable boundary logic:
• West = cultural-religious threat
• East = political domination without conversion

Russia internalizes differentiated threat perception.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Not all domination threatens civilization equally.
• Cultural absorption is a red-line threat.
• Defensive war can be civilizationally decisive.
• Symbolic victories outlast strategic stalemates.
• The West–East distinction becomes permanent in Russian memory.

Tension preserved without verdict.
