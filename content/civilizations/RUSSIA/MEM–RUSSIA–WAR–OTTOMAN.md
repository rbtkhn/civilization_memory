I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Russo–Ottoman wars as the **southern imperial
crucible** through which Russia secured warm-water access, defeated a
declining rival empire, and converted frontier conflict into permanent
strategic depth.

For Russian civilizational memory, these wars validate **attritional
imperial expansion over repeated campaigns**, not single decisive wars.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL ANTAGONISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Russia and the Ottoman Empire collide over geography.

Core drivers:
• Control of Black Sea access
• Influence over Orthodox populations
• Steppe frontier security
• Decline of Ottoman military-administrative capacity

The conflict is systemic, not episodic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. EARLY CAMPAIGNS & LEARNING PHASE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Initial Russian campaigns reveal limits.

Features:
• Logistic strain in southern theaters
• Naval inferiority
• Climatic and disease pressures

Each war functions as rehearsal for the next.

Losses become institutional memory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CATHERINE ERA & SYSTEMIC BREAKTHROUGH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Under Catherine II, the balance shifts.

Key elements:
• Potemkin’s southern infrastructure
• Suvorov’s battlefield dominance
• Diplomatic isolation of the Ottomans
• Naval emergence in the Black Sea

Wars transition from contest to dominance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. KEY WARS & TREATIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Notable conflicts:
• War of 1768–1774 → Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca
• War of 1787–1792 → Treaty of Jassy

Outcomes:
• Russian protectorate rights over Orthodox Christians
• De facto control of Black Sea littoral
• Annexation of Crimea (1783)

Treaties formalize military facts.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. IMPERIAL NORMALIZATION OF THE SOUTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Post-war consolidation follows.

Actions:
• Settlement and urbanization
• Naval base construction
• Administrative integration
• Multi-confessional governance

The south shifts from frontier to core.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Repeated wars can generate cumulative advantage.
• Declining empires invite patient encroachment.
• Infrastructure determines war outcomes over time.
• Diplomacy locks in attritional gains.
• Southern access reshapes Russia’s global posture.

Tension preserved without verdict.
