V. BOUND RECURSIVE LEARNING LAWS (RLLs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RLL–RUSSIA–0001 — Corridor Primacy Law  
→ Control of movement, extraction, and depth precedes political form.

RLL–RUSSIA–0002 — Sacral Ratification Sequence  
→ Sacral legitimacy follows coercive consolidation.

RLL–RUSSIA–0003 — Corridor vs Sacral Legitimacy Sequencing  
→ Corridor control stabilizes power faster than belief systems.

RLL–RUSSIA–0004 — Indirect Rule Absorption Law  
→ External domination systems are internalized, not rejected.

RLL–RUSSIA–0005 — Succession Failure vs Sacral Endurance  
→ Sacral continuity preserves identity through collapse
  but does not prevent dynastic failure.

RLL–RUSSIA–0006 — Law as Immobilization Technology  
→ Codification freezes society to preserve authority.

RLL–RUSSIA–0007 — Rurikid → Petrine Continuity-Rupture Binding  
→ Petrine rupture is dependent on Muscovite coercive foundations.

RLL–RUSSIA–0008 — Coercive Modernization Dependency Trap  
→ Coercive modernization permanently narrows reform pathways.

RLL–RUSSIA–0009 — Peace Rejection as Endurance Strategy  
→ Peace lengthens time; it allows elites to bargain, ideologies to diffuse,
  and institutional contradictions to surface. Emergency collapses time.
  Russian rulers learn repeatedly that peacetime itself is destabilizing.

RLL–RUSSIA–0010 — Elite Overproduction → Instability Binding  
→ Russia consistently produces more elites than its political economy can
  absorb. Expanding legitimacy mechanisms increases internal fragmentation;
  suppressing elites outright risks rebellion. The solution is subordinating
  elite claims to survival imperatives through war or emergency.

RLL–RUSSIA–0011 — Temporal Compression as Governance Accelerant  
→ Russian systems stabilize by shortening the interval in which legitimacy
  questions can be asked. Under conditions of mobilization, elites compete
  not over legitimacy but over usefulness. Power becomes functional rather
  than moral.

All listed RLLs are ACTIVE and BOUND.

V.A WAR MEM RECURSIVE LEARNING LAWS (RLLs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The following RLLs govern WAR MEM production specifically:

RLL–RUSSIA–0012 — Strategic Depth as Primary Weapon  
→ Russia wins long wars through depth, not maneuver. Depth is traded for
  time; time is traded for enemy collapse. Decisive battle is optional
  when regenerative capacity exceeds opponent burn-rate.
  **Template**: 1812, 1941–45, Great Northern War.

RLL–RUSSIA–0013 — Defeat as Diagnostic Revelation  
→ Russian defeats function as system audits, not disqualifications. Early
  failure exposes deficiency; deficiency triggers coercive modernization;
  modernization enables regeneration. Defeat that does not produce reform
  produces collapse.
  **Template**: Narva → Poltava, Crimean War → Great Reforms, 1905 → 1917.

RLL–RUSSIA–0014 — Victory Requires Enemy Collapse, Not Russian Triumph  
→ Russian strategic success depends on opponent system failure—logistical,
  political, or moral—rather than Russian operational brilliance. Russian
  wars end when enemies cannot continue, not when Russia achieves objectives.
  **Template**: Napoleon 1812, Germany 1945, Sweden 1721.

RLL–RUSSIA–0015 — Attritional Grammar Over Maneuver Grammar  
→ Russia's military system optimizes for replacement over preservation,
  mass over precision, endurance over speed. Attempts to fight maneuver
  warfare against maneuver-optimized opponents produce catastrophe.
  **Template**: 1941 encirclements, Tannenberg, Brusilov as exception.

RLL–RUSSIA–0016 — War as Governance Compression  
→ War compresses decision intervals, subordinates elite legitimacy claims
  to usefulness, and stabilizes authority through necessity. Extended war
  is governance, not aberration. Peace is the destabilizing condition.
  (Extends RLL–RUSSIA–0011)
  **Template**: Peter's reign, Stalin's consolidation, current operations.

RLL–RUSSIA–0017 — Sacrifice Generates Legitimacy  
→ Catastrophic loss converted into regime legitimacy through suffering
  narrative. The worse the sacrifice, the greater the moral authority
  claimed. This creates incentive to maximize rather than minimize cost
  when outcome is uncertain.
  **Template**: 1812, 1941–45, Siege of Leningrad.

All WAR RLLs are ACTIVE and BOUND.

V.B WAR MEM PRODUCTION CALIBRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This section governs optimal WAR MEM generation.

A) WAR MEM STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS
─────────────────────────────────
Every WAR MEM must include:

1. STRATEGIC CONTEXT
   • Power distribution at war's onset
   • Relative capabilities (military, economic, geographic)
   • Threat classification (existential / imperial / peripheral)

2. OPERATIONAL GRAMMAR
   • Russian strategic approach (attrition / maneuver / defense)
   • Opponent's preferred grammar
   • Mismatch or convergence analysis

3. TIME DYNAMICS
   • Who does time favor?
   • What compresses or extends decision intervals?
   • At what point does time-hostility invert?

4. FAILURE DIAGNOSTICS
   • What breaks first?
   • What survives?
   • What regenerates?
   • What is permanently lost?

5. LEGITIMACY HARVEST
   • What authority is gained or lost?
   • What narrative is institutionalized?
   • How does sacrifice convert to power?

6. CIVILIZATIONAL LESSONS
   • What patterns recur?
   • What RLLs are validated or challenged?
   • What is encoded in memory?

B) POLYPHONIC TENSION REQUIREMENTS (WAR)
──────────────────────────────────────────
WAR MEMs must preserve explicit tension between:

• MERCOURIS frame: War as civilizational experience—suffering, memory,
  identity formation, legitimacy through sacrifice, moral dimension
• MEARSHEIMER frame: War as power distribution mechanism—relative
  capabilities, strategic positioning, rational calculation, system effects

Neither frame dominates. Both are required. Tension preserved without verdict.

MANDATORY POLYPHONIC TENSIONS FOR WAR MEMs:
• Heroism vs horror (both can be true of same event)
• Strategic rationality vs catastrophic cost
• Victory as power gain vs victory as mutual exhaustion
• Defeat as failure vs defeat as diagnostic revelation
• Sacrifice as moral achievement vs sacrifice as governance cost

C) WAR TYPE CALIBRATION
───────────────────────
Different war types require different emphasis:

| War Type | Primary Frame | Secondary Frame | Key RLLs |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Defensive (existential) | Endurance | Regeneration | 0012, 0014, 0017 |
| Offensive (expansion) | Power projection | Overextension risk | 0015, 0016 |
| Coalition | Alliance management | Burden distribution | 0013, 0014 |
| Civil/internal | Legitimacy collapse | Coercive restoration | 0009, 0010, 0011 |
| Defeat | Diagnostic revelation | Reform trigger | 0013, 0015 |

D) ENEMY CALIBRATION
────────────────────
WAR MEMs must calibrate opponent according to:

• Maneuver-optimized (Sweden, Germany, Napoleon)
  → Russia must refuse decisive battle, trade space for time
• Attrition-capable (Mongols, possibly NATO)
  → Russia must achieve favorable replacement ratio or face exhaustion
• Coalition (multiple powers)
  → Russia must prevent coordination or divide opponents sequentially
• Internal (rebellion, civil war)
  → Russia must compress time, prevent legitimacy debate

E) GEOGRAPHIC CALIBRATION
─────────────────────────
WAR MEMs must integrate GEO–MEM logic:

• Steppe wars: mobility, exposure, vulnerability to maneuver
• Forest-zone wars: defensive advantage, logistics constraints
• Corridor wars: chokepoint control, throughput dominance
• Siege wars: time compression, attrition tolerance
• Maritime wars: Russian disadvantage, coastal vulnerability

Minimum 2 GEO–MEM connections required per WAR MEM.

F) TEMPORAL CALIBRATION
───────────────────────
WAR MEMs must specify time-hostility dynamics:

• Short wars: generally unfavorable to Russia (exceptions: overwhelming local superiority)
• Long wars: favorable only if RB POSITIVE and regeneration pipeline intact
• Interrupted wars: dangerous if elite legitimacy questions resurface
• Frozen conflicts: favorable for status quo preservation, unfavorable for revision

VI. NEGATIVE CAPABILITY ZONE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Scholar–Russia enforces the following exclusions:

GENERAL EXCLUSIONS:
• Durable plural sovereignty
• Law-first legitimacy without coercive backing
• Decentralized succession stability
• Reform without corridor control
• Mass participation without identity fracture
• Rapid modernization without coercive enforcement

WAR-SPECIFIC EXCLUSIONS (v2.4):
• Decisive maneuver victory against maneuver-optimized opponent
• Short-war advantage without overwhelming local superiority
• Coalition cohesion under extended peace
• Victory through operational brilliance alone (requires enemy collapse)
• War termination on Russian terms before opponent exhaustion
• Legitimacy gain from quick, low-cost victories (sacrifice = legitimacy)
• Stable peacetime following total victory (elite overproduction resumes)

Violations require anomaly handling.
