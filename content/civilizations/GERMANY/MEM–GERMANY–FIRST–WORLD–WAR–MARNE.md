I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Marne as **Germania's strategic reversal**:
the battle where the Schlieffen Plan failed, quick victory became
impossible, and the Western Front congealed into trench warfare.

For Germania, the Marne encodes **the pivot that condemned Germany
to two-front attrition**—not French élan but German operational
error: von Kluck's turn southeast, the gap between 1st and 2nd
Armies, and Hentsch's authorization of retreat.

The Marne is remembered as the moment the war of movement ended
and the war of exhaustion began. Germany had advanced 300 miles
in 30 days; at the Marne, the advance stopped.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. THE SCHLIEFFEN PLAN AND ITS FAILURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The plan required France's defeat in six weeks, then pivot to Russia.

By early September 1914:
• German 1st and 2nd Armies had swept through Belgium
• Paris was threatened; French government fled to Bordeaux
• Plan XVII had collapsed; 300,000 French casualties in August

John Keegan summarizes the reversal:

"The gap between the German First and Second Armies grew to thirty
miles. Into this gap the BEF advanced—not rapidly enough, the
French would forever complain, but advance it did. German commanders
on the ground saw disaster approaching. When Lieutenant Colonel
Hentsch arrived from headquarters with authority to coordinate, he
found both army commanders contemplating retreat. He authorized it.
The Schlieffen Plan died in that decision."
— John Keegan, *The First World War* (1998)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. VON KLUCK'S PIVOT: THE OPERATIONAL ERROR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The plan required sweep west of Paris. Von Kluck (1st Army) turned
southeast, east of Paris, exposing his flank to the Paris garrison.

The gap opened. Gallieni's Sixth Army struck. The BEF advanced into
the breach. Moltke, at Luxembourg, lost control; Hentsch's mission
to assess became authorization to retreat.

The structural lesson: centralized plan, decentralized execution,
weak coordination at the hinge—the German system failed at the
critical moment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC CONSEQUENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Marne determined the character of the war.

• Quick victory impossible
• Two-front war confirmed
• Trench lines emerge; "Race to the Sea" follows
• Attrition replaces Bewegungskrieg

For Germania, the Marne is **the road not taken**—the victory that
would have freed Germany to face Russia alone, and the defeat that
committed Germany to four years of Western Front slaughter.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Marne encodes **operational overreach and coordination failure**.

• Plan exceeded execution capacity
• Flank exposure undetected until exploited
• Retreat authorization from staff officer, not field commander
• One decision transformed strategic geometry

The Marne pairs with 1870 as inverse: Franco-Prussian War achieved
quick decision; the Marne foreclosed it. Germany's war of movement
became France's war of endurance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LIABILITY / MECHANISM (BARNES DIMENSION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jurisdiction: Moltke (OHL) vs. von Kluck and Bülow (army commanders)—
who had authority to authorize retreat? Hentsch, a staff lieutenant
colonel, carried implied authority from Moltke; his assessment became
retreat order. Command structure fragmented at the hinge.

Personal liability: Von Kluck's pivot exposed the flank; Moltke lost
control from Luxembourg. Defection incentives: Both army commanders
contemplated retreat before Hentsch arrived; neither wanted to hold
alone. Irreversibility: The retreat was 40 miles. The Schlieffen Plan
could not be restarted. Two-front war became structural fact.
