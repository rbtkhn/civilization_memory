### 2026-02-19 14:00 · MEM
The session closed two gaps: France had lacked an account of the Ottoman Empire and the Holy Roman Empire to set beside Germany's. The analysis surfaced the contrast. On the Ottomans, Germany weighs the adversary at the gates—the pressure that justified Habsburg leadership—while France weighs the ally that broke the Habsburg ring and the birth of raison d'état. On the Empire, Germany weighs a millennium of governance through negotiation and fragmentation; France weighs the eastern obstacle Richelieu fractured and Napoleon dissolved. The same past, opposite readings. The axis between them turns on that tension. When you return to Germany, or when someone inherits this work, the record preserves what was offered and why it mattered.
