────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Native Americans as **the primary casualty of
Anglia’s settler–procedural expansion**: populations subjected to treaty,
law, removal, and coercion as Anglian-derived institutions expanded
across continental space.

Dispossession is procedural, not chaotic.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. TREATY AS INSTRUMENT, NOT SHIELD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early relations are contractual.

Dynamics:
• Treaties negotiated asymmetrically
• Sovereignty acknowledged then retracted
• Legal language weaponized

Consent is temporary.

Procedure becomes a tool of erasure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. SETTLER LOGIC VS INDIGENOUS SPACE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglian expansion assumes land as alienable.

Clash:
• Fixed property vs communal territory
• Agricultural settlement vs migratory patterns
• Bureaucratic mapping vs lived geography

Procedure favors settlers by design.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. REMOVAL AS ADMINISTRATIVE SOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Displacement is legalized.

Mechanisms:
• Forced relocation policies
• Military enforcement
• Judicial validation

Violence is routinized.

Removal is framed as order maintenance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LEGITIMACY PRESERVATION THROUGH DISTANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core legitimacy remains intact.

Patterns:
• Frontier violence normalized
• Moral responsibility diffused
• Expansion justified as destiny or necessity

Procedure absorbs brutality.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SURVIVAL AND CONTAINED SOVEREIGNTY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Native societies endure in constrained form.

Outcomes:
• Reservations
• Limited self-governance
• Legal recognition without parity

Sovereignty survives—but shrinks.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Native Americans encode **procedural dispossession**.

Lessons:
• Law can erase as effectively as force.
• Treaties without enforcement hollow legitimacy.
• Expansion logic overrides pluralism.
• Survival does not equal justice.

Native experience marks Anglia’s frontier cost.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Treaties failed structurally.
• Procedure masked coercion.
• Expansion required removal.
• Survival occurred despite law.
• Native Americans expose the dark edge of order.

Tension preserved without verdict.
