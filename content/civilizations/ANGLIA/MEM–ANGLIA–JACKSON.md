I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Andrew Jackson as **Anglia’s populist rupture
figure**: the leader who reintroduced mass emotion, personal authority,
and direct majoritarian pressure into an Anglian-derived procedural
system—testing how much institutional restraint could absorb before
distorting.

Jackson does not abandon Anglian structure.
He **pressurizes it from below**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN CONDITIONS & SOCIAL BASE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson emerges from frontier society.

Context:
• Weak institutional density
• High violence tolerance
• Personal honor culture
• Suspicion of distant elites
• Expanding suffrage among white males

Authority shifts from deference to identification.

Legitimacy becomes emotional and personal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. POPULISM AS LEGITIMACY CLAIM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson reframes authority as embodiment.

Core posture:
• “The people” as moral source
• Direct electoral mandate emphasized
• Elites portrayed as corrupt or detached

Procedure is tolerated only when aligned with popular will.

This departs from Anglian elite-mediated balance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EXECUTIVE PERSONALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson expands executive assertiveness.

Patterns:
• Aggressive use of veto
• Loyalty-based appointments (spoils system)
• Confrontational posture toward Congress and courts

Institutions bend around the office-holder.

Charisma substitutes for consensus.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. THE BANK WAR & INSTITUTIONAL HOSTILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson targets abstract authority.

Bank of the United States:
• Viewed as elite concentration
• Financial abstraction framed as tyranny
• Destroyed through executive action

Outcome:
• Short-term popular legitimacy
• Long-term financial instability
• Precedent for politicized economic control

Anglian financial restraint is weakened.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LAW, ORDER, AND EXCLUSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson’s populism is selective.

Contradictions:
• Champion of mass democracy
• Enforcer of racial hierarchy
• Indian Removal as executive coercion
• Court decisions ignored when inconvenient

The rule of law becomes conditional.

Procedure yields to perceived necessity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RELATION TO ANGLIAN GRAMMAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson strains inherited logic.

Tensions:
• Popular will vs institutional mediation
• Personal authority vs procedural legitimacy
• Emotional mobilization vs elite balance

The system survives—but is altered.

Populism becomes a recurring force.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Populism normalized as legitimacy source
• Executive power personalized
• Anti-institutional rhetoric mainstreamed
• Cycles of elite backlash and mass mobilization

Jackson opens a new channel of authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Jackson encodes **democratic pressure against procedural restraint**.

Lessons:
• Institutions can absorb populism—but at cost
• Personal legitimacy competes with structural legitimacy
• Mass democracy destabilizes elite-mediated balance
• Executive power expands under popular mandate

Jackson tests the load-bearing limits of Anglian inheritance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Populism redefines legitimacy without redesigning structure.
• Personal authority can override procedure temporarily.
• Democratic expansion intensifies institutional stress.
• Financial abstraction becomes a populist target.
• Jackson is Anglia’s procedural system under mass pressure.

Tension preserved without verdict.
