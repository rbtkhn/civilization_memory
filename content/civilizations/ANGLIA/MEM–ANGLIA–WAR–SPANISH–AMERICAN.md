I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Spanish–American War as **Anglia’s maritime
reassertion through its American extension**: the moment when a
procedural republic pivots from continental annexation toward
**naval power, overseas access, and informal empire**, aligning more
closely with classic Anglian strategic grammar.

Where the Mexican–American War encoded **territorial absorption**,
the Spanish–American War encodes **maritime leverage without full
colonial integration**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT & SYSTEM POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
By 1898, the American extension of Anglia is system-mature.

Context:
• Completed continental consolidation
• Industrial overcapacity seeking outlets
• Expanding naval doctrine (Mahanian influence)
• Declining Iberian empire as low-risk opponent

The system no longer seeks land for survival.
It seeks access, nodes, and credibility.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGAL–PROCEDURAL WAR INITIATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war is framed within procedural legitimacy.

Mechanisms:
• Congressional declaration of war
• Formal humanitarian casus belli (Cuba)
• Limited objectives publicly stated
• Civilian supremacy preserved

War is justified as **liberation**, not conquest.

Procedure masks strategic expansion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. MARITIME–OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The conflict is brief and decisive.

Characteristics:
• Naval superiority decisive
• Rapid overseas deployment
• Joint operations (naval–land coordination)
• Minimal mass mobilization

Victory reflects:
→ Industrial–naval integration
→ Logistical reach
→ Institutional confidence

This is system projection, not desperation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ACQUISITION WITHOUT ANNEXATION LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Outcomes redefine expansion.

Results:
• Cuba placed under indirect control
• Puerto Rico annexed
• Philippines acquired as overseas possession
• Guam secured as naval node

Territory is treated instrumentally.

Control > settlement.
Access > absorption.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. IMPERIAL CONTRADICTIONS & DEBATE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Expansion triggers domestic tension.

Contradictions:
• Anti-imperial tradition vs overseas rule
• Republican consent vs colonial governance
• Liberation rhetoric vs suppression (Philippines)

Unlike 1848:
• Debate is immediate
• Moral tension surfaces earlier
• Institutional restraint is stressed but intact

Procedure contains dissent without collapse.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RETURN TO ANGLIAN STRATEGIC GRAMMAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This war marks partial realignment with Anglian tradition.

Alignments:
• Maritime primacy
• Indirect control
• Coalition and legitimacy framing
• Preference for nodes over hinterlands

Divergences remain:
• Formal territorial possession persists
• Anti-colonial rhetoric constrains duration

The system experiments, then adapts.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Emergence as global naval power
• Permanent overseas footprint
• Institutionalization of informal empire
• Expansion reframed as security necessity

The war globalizes Anglia’s American extension.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Spanish–American War encodes **expansion by maritime procedure**.

Lessons:
• Sea power substitutes for land annexation.
• Informal empire reduces systemic strain.
• Legitimacy framing conditions durability.
• Procedure enables global reach without revolution.

This is the corrective to 1848.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Anglia’s grammar reasserts via the sea.
• Access outperforms absorption.
• Naval power globalizes procedural systems.
• Moral tension appears sooner but is managed.
• Informal empire stabilizes expansion.

Tension preserved without verdict.
