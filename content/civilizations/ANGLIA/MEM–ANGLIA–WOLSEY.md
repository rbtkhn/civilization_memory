I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Cardinal Thomas Wolsey as **Anglia’s aborted
centralizer**: the figure who attempted to concentrate authority,
administration, and diplomacy around a single ministerial will—anticipating
continental statecraft—yet failed because his power was personal rather
than procedural.

Wolsey reveals what Anglia *rejected* as much as what it tested.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FORMATIVE CONDITIONS & RISE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey rises through talent rather than lineage.

Context:
• Late medieval monarchy seeking administrative leverage
• Weak aristocratic discipline after the Wars of the Roses
• Henry VIII’s preference for delegation
• Church as primary bureaucratic reservoir

Wolsey accumulates offices at unprecedented speed.

Authority flows:
King → Minister → Offices (temporarily unified)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CENTRALIZATION THROUGH PERSON, NOT PROCEDURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey governs by accumulation, not institutionalization.

Methods:
• Personal control of chancery, finance, and diplomacy
• Reliance on royal favor
• Bypassing of noble councils
• Limited embedding in law or precedent

Power is effective but brittle.

Nothing constrains Wolsey except the king.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. FOREIGN POLICY & CONTINENTAL ASPIRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey seeks European arbiter status for Anglia.

Aims:
• Balance between Habsburg and Valois powers
• Diplomatic centrality through prestige summits
• Papal authority leveraged for secular influence

Anglia briefly appears as continental mediator.

This posture is unsustainable without structural backing.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CHURCH, LAW, AND ADMINISTRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey expands administrative reach using ecclesiastical tools.

Actions:
• Reform of legal courts (Star Chamber expansion)
• Church-based revenue extraction
• Educational patronage

Yet reforms lack procedural anchoring.

They depend on Wolsey’s presence to function.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. FAILURE MODE: PERSONALIZATION OF POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey’s collapse is sudden and total.

Triggers:
• Failure to secure Henry VIII’s annulment
• Loss of papal leverage
• Court faction hostility
• Absence of institutional allies

No procedure absorbs his fall.

The system ejects the individual cleanly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL REJECTION SIGNAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Wolsey’s failure clarifies Anglia’s path.

Lessons absorbed:
• Centralization must be procedural to endure
• Ministers cannot replace institutions
• Church power is unsuitable as permanent state scaffold
• Personal brilliance cannot substitute for legitimacy grammar

What Francia later embraces, Anglia discards.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. COMPARATIVE POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Relative figures:
• Wolsey ↔ Richelieu (attempt vs completion)
• Wolsey ↔ Cromwell (clerical vs bureaucratic rupture)
• Wolsey ↔ Cecil (personal rule vs procedural restraint)

Wolsey is a *negative template*.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Acceleration of break with Rome
• Shift toward lay bureaucracy
• Suspicion of overmighty ministers
• Preference for distributed governance

Wolsey’s fall makes later continuity possible.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Centralization without procedure collapses instantly.
• Personal authority cannot survive patron withdrawal.
• Church-based governance is structurally brittle.
• Anglia learns restraint by watching failure.
• Wolsey is Anglia’s Richelieu that never happened.

Tension preserved without verdict.
