I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **British North America** as Anglia's settler
imperial zone: the territory that expanded through colonization,
fractured through revolution, and survived in residual form as Canada.

North America is not one story.
It is **empire as fracture**—the zone where Anglia loses its most
populous colonies and retains the northern remnant.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SETTLER COLONIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
North America enters Anglia's system through migration and land grant.

Phases:
• 1607 — Jamestown (Virginia); 1620 — Plymouth (Massachusetts)
• Thirteen Colonies — chartered, proprietary, Crown
• Territorial expansion — Seven Years' War removes French barrier
• Population growth; local assemblies; transatlantic trade

Settlement differs from India or Hong Kong: **population transfer**
creates societies that replicate British institutions while adapting to
continental geography. The colonies are extensions of Anglia—until they
resist.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FRACTURE: AMERICAN REVOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Thirteen Colonies secede; empire contracts.

Context:
• 1775–1783 — War of American Independence
• 1776 — Declaration of Independence
• 1783 — Treaty of Paris; Britain recognizes United States
• Loyalist migration north (Canada, Caribbean)

Revolution is **procedural rupture**: the colonies reject metropolitan
authority, win by war and alliance (France), and secure recognition by
treaty. Britain exits the Thirteen Colonies by necessity—not by
design. The loss is structural; the empire continues elsewhere.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. RESIDUAL EMPIRE: CANADA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
North of the new United States, British possession endures.

Trajectory:
• Quebec Act (1774); Constitutional Act (1791) — Upper/Lower Canada
• War of 1812 — U.S. invasion resisted; status quo
• Confederation (1867) — Dominion of Canada
• Gradual autonomy; Commonwealth; enduring Crown link

Canada becomes **empire's North American remnant**—the zone where
Loyalist migration, French survivance, and British procedure combine.
Settlement continues; institutions persist; independence is incremental
rather than revolutionary.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTRAST: EXIT MODALITIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
North America encodes two imperial outcomes.

Thirteen Colonies:
• Exit by revolution and treaty
• No negotiated handover; war determines outcome
• Anglia loses; does not choose to relinquish

Canada:
• Exit by evolution and devolution
• Gradual autonomy; procedure over rupture
• Anglia retains link; Canada chooses continuity

The same continent produces **revolutionary exit** and **procedural
continuity**. Empire fractures in the south; endures in the north.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
North America encodes **empire as continental experiment**.

Lessons:
• Settler colonies can outgrow metropolitan control.
• Revolution produces exit without procedure; treaty follows war.
• Residual territory can retain institutional links when rupture is avoided.
• North America is Anglia's lost opportunity and enduring connection.
