I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Declaration of Independence as **Anglia’s most
radical rhetorical export**: the transformation of inherited procedural
grievance logic into universal moral declaration.

The Declaration is Anglian law spoken in Francia’s moral register.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INHERITED GRIEVANCE GRAMMAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Declaration follows Anglian legal form.

Structure:
• Statement of principles
• Bill of particulars
• Assertion of remedy

It reads as an indictment, not a manifesto.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. UNIVERSALIZATION OF PARTICULAR RIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Local grievances are reframed as universal truths.

Mechanism:
• Natural rights language
• Equality assertions
• Moral inevitability framing

Procedure is translated into philosophy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DECLARATION OVER INSTITUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The document asserts legitimacy without capacity.

Characteristics:
• No enforcement mechanism
• No institutional blueprint
• Reliance on future construction

Words precede systems.

Will and Ariel Durant, in *The Story of Civilization*, Vol. 7, *The Age of Reason Begins* (1961), note the Dutch Act of Abjuration (1581) as a declarative legitimacy formula: a ruler who destroys liberties may be deposed. This early declaration logic anticipates the later Anglian export of grievance-into-indictment that culminates in the Declaration of Independence (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

SOURCE — Durant, *The Age of Reason Begins* (Vol. 7) (ARC-T-MODERN):
> “In a document as famous in Dutch history as Parliament’s Declaration of Rights (1689) in the history of England, they proclaimed that a ruler who treats his subjects as slaves and destroys their liberties should no longer be accounted their legitimate sovereign, and may lawfully be deposed.”
> — Will Durant and Ariel Durant, *The Story of Civilization*, Vol. 7, *The Age of Reason Begins* (New York: Simon & Schuster, 1961) (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. STRATEGIC FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Declaration functions outwardly.

Uses:
• Alliance signaling (especially to Francia)
• Moral justification of rebellion
• Internal cohesion narrative

Legitimacy is projected before it is secured.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. TENSION WITH ANGLIAN TRADITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Declaration departs from Anglian restraint.

Contradictions:
• Universal claims vs incremental reform
• Moral certainty vs procedural ambiguity
• Declaration vs precedent

This tension persists in successor states.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Rights language globalized
• Revolution rhetorically normalized
• Ideals decoupled from institutions

The Declaration travels faster than Common Law.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Declarations accelerate legitimacy claims.
• Philosophy can precede capacity.
• Universal language magnifies contradiction.
• Procedure survives better than proclamation.
• The Declaration is Anglia’s most dangerous gift.

Tension preserved without verdict.
