I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Glorious Revolution as **Anglia’s procedural
revolution**: the regime change in which sovereignty is transferred
without civilizational rupture, violence is minimized, and legitimacy
is re-grounded through law rather than moral reset.

The Glorious Revolution is revolution by continuity.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. PRE-REVOLUTIONARY CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Crisis emerges from procedural breach, not ideological collapse.

Context:
• James II’s perceived absolutist drift
• Religious legitimacy anxiety
• Parliamentary marginalization
• Fear of dynastic succession breaking precedent

The threat is procedural discontinuity, not tyranny per se.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INVITATION INSTEAD OF INSURRECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Power transfer is negotiated.

Mechanism:
• Parliamentary invitation to William of Orange
• Conditional acceptance of the crown
• Legal framing of abdication rather than overthrow

Sovereignty moves through paperwork, not purges.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. LAW AS LEGITIMACY RESET
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Authority is reconstituted legally.

Outcomes:
• Bill of Rights (1689)
• Parliamentary supremacy affirmed
• Limits on royal prerogative
• Regularized succession logic

Legitimacy is restored by clarification, not destruction.

Will and Ariel Durant, in *The Story of Civilization*, Vol. 7, *The Age of Reason Begins* (1961), treat the Glorious Revolution as a deliberately managed deposition that preserved order and legitimacy by allowing James II to escape and by settling the constitutional breach without prolonged violence (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

SOURCE — Durant, *The Age of Reason Begins* (Vol. 7) (ARC-T-MODERN):
> “Subsequent history suggests that mercy would have been wisdom. When Charles’s son James II gave equally great offense, the Glorious Revolution of 1688, managed with aristocratic finesse, deliberately allowed him to escape to France; and the results of that deposition were permanent. However, it was the earlier Rebellion that made the later Revolution possible in all its swift effectiveness.”
> — Will Durant and Ariel Durant, *The Story of Civilization*, Vol. 7, *The Age of Reason Begins* (New York: Simon & Schuster, 1961), 221 (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RESTRAINT AS STRATEGIC ADVANTAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Violence is deliberately minimized.

Effects:
• Elite compliance preserved
• Institutional memory retained
• Administrative continuity uninterrupted

Restraint prevents escalation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONTRAST WITH CONTINENTAL REVOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia demonstrates an alternative model.

Contrast:
• Anglia: procedure-first adjustment
• Francia: morality-first reset

The Glorious Revolution shows that legitimacy can be repaired without terror.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Parliamentary sovereignty normalized
• Revolution de-fanged as concept
• Change routinized through law
• Stability increased through predictability

Revolution becomes a maintenance function.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. MYTH & MISREADING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Later narratives understate contingency.

Tensions:
• Violence minimized but not absent
• Consensus elite-driven
• Popular participation limited

Its success depends on institutional depth.
