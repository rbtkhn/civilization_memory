I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Mexican–American War as **Anglia’s imperial
divergence moment in its American extension**: the conflict in which an
Anglian-derived procedural republic successfully conducts aggressive
territorial conquest—revealing how institutional restraint can coexist
with, and even enable, expansionist ambition.

For Anglia, the war is fought **by its offspring**, yet exposes latent
tensions embedded in Anglian legal–procedural inheritance.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT & CIVILIZATIONAL POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war occurs after Anglia’s American separation stabilizes.

Context:
• Continental expansion pressure (Manifest Destiny)
• Secure eastern and northern borders
• Weak neighboring state (Mexico)
• Professionalized officer corps with Anglian traditions

The United States no longer fears imperial reabsorption.
It tests whether power can be expanded safely.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGAL–PROCEDURAL WAR INITIATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The conflict is framed through legality.

Mechanisms:
• Congressional authorization
• Territorial dispute formalization
• Treaty-oriented objectives
• Civilian supremacy maintained

War is justified procedurally, not dynastically.

This preserves Anglian grammar while pursuing non-Anglian ends.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE & SYSTEM EFFICIENCY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Military success is rapid and asymmetric.

Characteristics:
• Professional officer leadership
• Logistical competence
• Amphibious and overland coordination
• Decisive campaigns without total mobilization

Victory reflects system maturity, not desperation.

Institutions outperform ideology.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. TERRITORIAL ACQUISITION WITHOUT SYSTEMIC STRAIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war yields massive gains.

Outcomes:
• California
• Southwest territories
• Pacific access secured

Remarkably:
• No regime crisis
• No executive dictatorship
• No collapse of civil order

Expansion is absorbed procedurally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MORAL & POLITICAL CONTRADICTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite success, tension accumulates.

Contradictions:
• Republican ideals vs conquest
• Anti-imperial rhetoric vs territorial seizure
• Slavery expansion dispute ignited
• Internal legitimacy debates sharpened

The war plants seeds of future fracture.

Anglian procedure delays—but does not resolve—conflict.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RELATION TO ANGLIAN STRATEGIC TRADITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war diverges from classic Anglian restraint.

Differences:
• Continental annexation over maritime control
• Direct governance of conquered land
• Limited coalition reliance

Yet similarities persist:
• Legal framing
• Civilian control
• Institutional continuity

Anglian form enables non-Anglian substance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• U.S. emerges as continental power
• Sectional conflict intensified
• Expansion normalized as lawful
• Anglian restraint partially eroded

The war accelerates American divergence.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Mexican–American War encodes **expansion without collapse**.

Lessons:
• Strong institutions can absorb conquest
• Legalism can legitimize aggression
• Procedural systems defer moral reckoning
• Expansion tests restraint from within

The war is a quiet pivot point.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Procedure can authorize empire.
• Institutional strength masks ethical fracture.
• Expansion need not destabilize immediately.
• Legal legitimacy outpaces moral consensus.
• The Mexican–American War reveals Anglia’s limits when transplanted.

Tension preserved without verdict.
