────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Papacy as **Anglia’s rejected external sovereign**:
a sacral authority that initially legitimated kingship but was ultimately
expelled when it conflicted with Anglia’s emerging grammar of procedural,
territorial, and sovereign autonomy.

For Anglia, the Papacy is not a rival universalism to be managed.
It is a **foreign jurisdiction to be exited**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. EARLY PAPAL LEGITIMATION & INTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the early medieval period, Rome functions as legitimizer.

Functions:
• Sacral coronation
• Ecclesiastical continuity after Roman withdrawal
• Moral arbitration among rulers
• Integration into Latin Christendom

Papal authority is accepted as distant, symbolic, and stabilizing.

Legitimacy flows through shared Christian order.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. GROWING JURISDICTIONAL FRICTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conflict emerges as Anglia’s institutions mature.

Tensions:
• Papal taxation and appointments
• Competing legal jurisdictions (canon vs common law)
• Appeals beyond the realm
• Clerical immunity from royal courts

The issue is not belief, but **authority location**.

Rome indicates, but increasingly interferes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. COMMON LAW VS CANON LAW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legal incompatibility becomes decisive.

Contrasts:
• Common Law: precedent, locality, procedure
• Canon Law: codification, universality, hierarchy

Anglia resists external override of inherited process.

Law becomes territorial.

Procedure becomes sovereign.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. THE TUDOR BREAK: SOVEREIGN EXIT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Henrician Reformation finalizes separation.

Key actions:
• Act of Supremacy (1534)
• Royal supremacy over the Church of England
• Termination of papal jurisdiction
• Confiscation and redistribution of Church authority

The break is political before theological.

Rome is expelled as court of appeal.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. RELIGION NATIONALIZED, NOT ABOLISHED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia retains Christianity while rejecting Rome.

Outcomes:
• Church subordinated to crown-in-parliament
• Doctrinal moderation over radical reform
• Continuity of ritual with jurisdictional change

The Papacy is removed without destroying faith.

Religion is domesticated procedurally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Sovereignty defined territorially
• Law insulated from external moral authority
• Precedent over doctrine normalized
• Religious conflict absorbed institutionally

Anglia learns that exit is preferable to rivalry.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. COMPARATIVE POSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Contrasts:
• Francia manages the Papacy as rival universalism
• Anglia eliminates the Papacy as jurisdictional competitor

Francia bends Rome.
Anglia leaves it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Papacy encodes Anglia’s **first successful sovereignty exit**.

Lessons:
• External legitimacy can be withdrawn.
• Jurisdiction matters more than doctrine.
• Procedure outlasts sacral authority.
• Order can survive religious realignment.

Rome teaches Anglia how to leave.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Sovereignty requires final jurisdiction.
• External courts destabilize procedural order.
• Religion can persist after institutional severance.
• Exit can be stabilizing if done legally.
• The Papacy is Anglia’s first foreign system constraint.

Tension preserved without verdict.
