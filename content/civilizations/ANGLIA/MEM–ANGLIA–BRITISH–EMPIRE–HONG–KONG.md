I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves **Hong Kong** as Anglia's treaty-port imperial node:
the territory acquired through commercial-military pressure, governed as
free-trading entrepôt, and returned to China through negotiated procedure.

Hong Kong is not a dominion.
It is **empire as contract**—entry by treaty, exit by agreement.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ACQUISITION: TREATY PORTS & OPIUM WARS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hong Kong enters Anglia's system through the logic of forced commerce.

Phases:
• 1842 Treaty of Nanking — Hong Kong Island ceded (Opium War outcome)
• 1860 Convention of Peking — Kowloon ceded (Second Opium War)
• 1898 Convention for Extension — New Territories leased 99 years

The Opium Wars establish the precedent: trade access enforced by naval
superiority, territory secured by treaty. Hong Kong becomes the anchor
for China trade—free port, naval station, and commercial gateway.

Entry is commercial, not ideological. Legitimacy rests on treaty, not
conquest narrative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. GOVERNANCE: ENTREPÔT WITHOUT ASSIMILATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hong Kong is administered, not settled.

Patterns:
• Free port status — no tariff barrier
• Minimal interference in local society
• Crown Colony structure with appointed governor
• Commerce as primary governance logic

The territory serves as **transshipment node**—linking China, Britain,
and regional trade. Revenue derives from commerce, not land or
population extraction. Governance is light-touch compared to India or
South Africa.

Anglia does not seek to civilize Hong Kong.
It seeks to **use** it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. STRATEGIC VALUE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hong Kong anchors Britain's position in East Asia.

Functions:
• Naval station (Pacific presence)
• Financial and trading hub
• Gateway to China market
• Refuge for capital and enterprise (especially post-1949)

Strategic logic mirrors Gibraltar, Malta, Singapore: **chokepoint
control** supporting maritime dominance. The territory's value is
positional, not territorial in the settler sense.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. WWII & POSTWAR CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Japanese occupation (1941–1945) interrupts but does not end the pattern.

Postwar:
• Reoccupation (1945)
• Refugee influx from mainland (1949 onward)
• Industrialization and financial-center emergence
• New Territories lease expiry (1997) drives negotiation

The 99-year lease creates **temporal constraint**: exit is structurally
anticipated. Procedure must be invented for return.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. HANDOVER: PROCEDURAL WITHDRAWAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Sino-British Joint Declaration (1984) frames transfer as legal
agreement, not surrender.

Features:
• Negotiated timetable (1997)
• "One country, two systems" formula (50-year transition)
• Preserved legal and economic structures post-handover
• Minimal rupture in Anglia; cost externalized to Hong Kong

Anglia exits **by procedure**. Legitimacy of withdrawal rests on treaty
and negotiation. The empire does not collapse at Hong Kong—it
**contracts by agreement**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hong Kong encodes **empire as temporal contract**.

Lessons:
• Treaty can create and dissolve imperial possession.
• Commerce-based governance differs from extraction or settlement.
• Procedural exit preserves metropolitan legitimacy.
• Time-limited tenure constrains imperial overreach.

Hong Kong is Anglia's proof that empire can end without revolution—
when the terms are written in advance.
