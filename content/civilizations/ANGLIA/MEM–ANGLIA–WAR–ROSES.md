────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Wars of the Roses as Anglia’s internal
disintegration crisis: a prolonged dynastic civil war that hollowed
out the feudal aristocracy, delegitimized hereditary succession, and
prepared the ground for radical monarchical centralization.

For Anglia, the conflict represents elite self-destruction, not
popular revolution.
Power fractures at the top while society endures below.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRUCTURAL PRECONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The conflict emerges from accumulated institutional strain.

Key conditions:
• Weak Lancastrian kingship (Henry VI)
• Unresolved Plantagenet succession logic
• Over-mighty nobles with private armies
• Fiscal exhaustion after the Hundred Years’ War

Feudal loyalty outpaces royal authority.

The crown exists—but cannot command.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DYNASTIC FRACTURE (LANCASTER ↔ YORK)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war is framed as dynastic legality, not ideology.

Competing claims:
• Lancaster — incumbent but fragile legitimacy
• York — superior genealogical claim, inferior incumbency

Conflict logic:
→ When succession law is unclear, violence arbitrates.

Legitimacy becomes retroactive, awarded to survivors.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. WAR AS ARISTOCRATIC ATTRITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fighting is intermittent but cumulative.

Characteristics:
• Limited battles, high elite turnover
• Shifting allegiances
• Cycles of attainder and restoration
• Minimal mass mobilization

The nobility fights itself to extinction.

The state weakens,
but the aristocracy collapses faster.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ROYAL FIGURES & REGIME INSTABILITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Kingship oscillates repeatedly.

Dynamics:
• Henry VI — symbolic but incapacitated
• Edward IV — effective but faction-bound
• Richard III — decisive yet terminal

Rule becomes personal and provisional.

The crown survives,
but monarchy loses sanctity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SOCIAL & INSTITUTIONAL CONTAINMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite elite violence, society remains largely intact.

Stabilizers:
• Common law continuity
• Local governance persistence
• Limited ideological penetration
• Absence of peasant revolt

Civil war is top-heavy.

Anglia fractures vertically, not horizontally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. TERMINATION: TUDOR RESOLUTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The conflict ends through dynastic synthesis, not victory alone.

Resolution:
• Defeat of Richard III at Bosworth (1485)
• Henry VII marries Elizabeth of York
• Competing claims fused

Violence ends when lineage ambiguity is closed.

Legitimacy is rebuilt by marriage, not argument.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The aftermath reshapes Anglia permanently.

Enduring effects:
• Decimation of feudal nobility
• Rise of centralized Tudor monarchy
• Suppression of private armies
• Shift from feudal to bureaucratic rule

Civil war clears the board for absolutism.

Order follows exhaustion.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Dynastic ambiguity invites elite violence.
• Civil wars can strengthen states by destroying rivals.
• Aristocratic power is self-limiting when uncoordinated.
• Societal continuity can coexist with elite collapse.
• The Wars of the Roses are Anglia’s precondition for modern monarchy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
