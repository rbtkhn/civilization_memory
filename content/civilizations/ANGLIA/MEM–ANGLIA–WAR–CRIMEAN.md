I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Crimean War as **Anglia’s legitimacy–capacity
mismatch exposure event**: a conflict in which Anglia successfully
operates within an alliance to constrain rival expansion, yet suffers
severe reputational and administrative damage due to institutional
unpreparedness and public visibility.

For Anglia, Crimea is **victory without confidence**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

Winston Churchill, in *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 4 (*The Great Democracies* [1956–1958]), treats the Crimean War within "Recovery and Reform" section—"Crimean War"—framing it as Anglia's legitimacy–capacity mismatch exposure event, where strategic success coexists with administrative failure and public scrutiny (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT & ENTRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia enters to block Russian expansion.

Context:
• Ottoman decline
• Russian pressure on Black Sea access
• Balance-of-power logic
• Defense of sea-route integrity

The objective is containment, not conquest.

Crimea is peripheral geographically but central strategically.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ALLIANCE WARFARE WITH FRANCIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia fights alongside Francia.

Dynamics:
• Coalition legitimacy emphasized
• Naval and logistical coordination
• Shared operational burden
• No independent war aim dominance

Anglia accepts symmetry to preserve system stability.

Alliance cohesion substitutes for unilateral control.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. ADMINISTRATIVE FAILURE & LOGISTICAL BREAKDOWN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war exposes systemic weakness.

Failures:
• Medical neglect
• Supply mismanagement
• Bureaucratic inertia
• Officer-class rigidity

Institutional lag undermines battlefield success.

Procedure fails under expeditionary stress.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. MEDIA, PUBLIC SCRUTINY, AND SHOCK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Crimea is Anglia’s first modern media war.

Effects:
• War correspondence exposes incompetence
• Public outrage escalates rapidly
• Political accountability intensifies

Legitimacy is judged in real time.

Silence is no longer possible.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. FLORENCE NIGHTINGALE & REFORM IMPULSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Failure triggers correction.

Outcomes:
• Military medical reform
• Administrative modernization
• Professionalization acceleration

Exposure produces adaptation.

Anglia learns publicly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. STRATEGIC OUTCOME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The war ends with limited success.

Results:
• Russian naval presence constrained
• Ottoman integrity temporarily preserved
• Balance-of-power maintained

Yet:
• No strategic dominance achieved
• Moral authority dented
• Confidence shaken

Containment succeeds; prestige erodes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Crimea encodes **accountability through exposure**.

Lessons:
• Alliance war limits autonomy
• Administrative weakness undermines legitimacy
• Media accelerates reform pressure
• Procedure must adapt to visibility

Crimea modernizes Anglia by humiliation.
