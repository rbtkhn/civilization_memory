I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Anglia–France interface as **the continental
rival against whom Anglia defined procedure and coalition**: the
kingdom that held—or sought—Continental primacy, whose declaration
and centralization contrasted with Anglia's incremental, maritime,
alliance-based method.

For Anglia, France encodes **declaration versus procedure**—the power
that governed by principle and rupture where Anglia governed by
precedent and coalition. Medieval rival, Napoleonic enemy, twentieth-
century ally: the interface persists across regime changes.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. MEDIEVAL RIVALRY: THE HUNDRED YEARS' WAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
England held French lands; claimed the French crown; fought for
Continental foothold. Crécy, Poitiers, Agincourt—English tactics
defeated French chivalry. The Treaty of Troyes (1420) made Henry V
heir to France. Joan of Arc reversed momentum; by 1453 England was
expelled. Anglia's memory: Continental ambition failed; insular
consolidation followed. Procedure (Parliament, common law) survived
defeat; France's centralization produced different outcomes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. NAPOLEONIC CONFRONTATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon embodied everything Anglia opposed: personal rule, universal
claims, rupture-as-renewal. Trafalgar (1805) confirmed naval
supremacy; the Continental System failed because Britain's credit,
empire, and sea control could not be closed off. Waterloo (1815)
ended the cycle—coalition warfare, finance, and endurance prevailed.
Anglia did not defeat Napoleon alone; the coalition did. Procedure
and alliance overcame declaration and charisma.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. TWENTIETH-CENTURY ALLIANCE
──────────────────────────────────────────────
The Entente Cordiale (1904) realigned Anglia and France against
Germany. Two world wars made them allies. 1940: Britain fought on;
Mers-el-Kébir neutralized the French fleet to prevent German capture.
Liberation required Anglo-American power. The alliance was structural—balance
against Continental hegemony—not affective. Anglia's role: sea control,
finance, coalition persistence. France's role: Continental mass, legitimacy
claims, eventual EU core.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
France encodes **continental declarative rival and necessary ally**.

• Medieval: territorial and dynastic competition; expulsion (1453)
• Napoleonic: coalition against universalist ambition; Trafalgar,
  Waterloo
• 20th c.: alliance against Germany; structural, not sentimental
• Grammar contrast: declaration vs procedure, rupture vs continuity
