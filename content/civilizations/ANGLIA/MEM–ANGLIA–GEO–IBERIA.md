I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Iberia as **Anglia’s early oceanic gateway and
managed rival**: the civilizational space through which Anglia learned
imperial competition, maritime denial, and coalition containment.

Iberia matters to Anglia not as territory,
but as **precedent and pressure**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. GEOGRAPHIC SIGNIFICANAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberia anchors Atlantic access.

Attributes:
• Control of Atlantic exits
• Proximity to Mediterranean trade
• Early naval infrastructure
• Launch point for global routes

Iberia demonstrates the strategic value of
oceans over continents.

It teaches Anglia what to contest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. EARLY MODERN IMPERIAL COMPETITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia encounters Iberia as pioneer empire.

Dynamics:
• Spanish and Portuguese maritime dominance
• Treasure fleets
• Colonial monopolies

Anglia responds asymmetrically:
• Privateering
• Naval disruption
• Financial competition

Iberia is not confronted head-on.
It is **eroded over time**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. NAVAL DENIAL & SYSTEM LEARNING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberian weaknesses instruct Anglia.

Observed failures:
• Overreliance on bullion
• Underdeveloped financial institutions
• Centralized command rigidity

Anglia adapts by:
• Building credit systems
• Prioritizing sea-lane control
• Avoiding extractive imperial finance

Learning precedes dominance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. PENINSULAR WAR AS SYSTEMIC CONFIRMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Peninsular War validates Anglia’s model.

Outcomes:
• Naval supply dominance
• Coalition warfare success
• Support of local resistance
• Attritional victory without annexation

Anglia fights *through* Iberia, not *for* it.

Control of outcomes matters more than control of land.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN RELATIONSHIP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the modern era:
• Iberia integrated into Atlantic institutions
• Maritime cooperation normalized
• Rivalry replaced by alignment

Iberia becomes part of the system,
not a challenger to it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Iberia encodes **Anglia’s transition from learner to system manager**.

Lessons:
• Early movers can be surpassed by better systems.
• Naval denial defeats extractive empires.
• Finance outlasts bullion.
• Outcomes matter more than ownership.

Iberia teaches Anglia how empires fail.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Iberia shows Anglia the future of mismanaged empire.
• Sea power defeats treasure faster than armies.
• Denial is cheaper than conquest.
• Anglia outgrows Iberia by system design.
• Iberia is Anglia’s first imperial classroom.

Tension preserved without verdict.
