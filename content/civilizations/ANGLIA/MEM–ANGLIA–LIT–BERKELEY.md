I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves George Berkeley as **Anglia's bishop-philosopher
of immaterialism**: the thinker who carries Locke's empiricism to
idealism by denying matter and grounding existence in perception—
"to be is to be perceived" (esse est percipi)—while defending faith
and common sense against materialism.

Berkeley does not destroy empiricism.
He **radicalizes it into idealism**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT & FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Berkeley writes in the wake of Locke and Newton.

Context:
• Post–Glorious Revolution settlement
• Lockean empiricism dominant
• Newtonian science ascendant
• Anglican establishment and Irish church

Berkeley seeks to reconcile faith with the new philosophy.
He attacks matter to save spirit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ESSE EST PERCIPI
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Berkeley's first principle: existence is perception.

Core claim:
• For unthinking things, to exist is to be perceived
• No substance behind perceptions
• Matter is an unnecessary hypothesis

This is not skepticism.
It is **immaterialism**.

God perceives when we do not;
thus the world persists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EMPIRICISM WITHOUT MATTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Berkeley accepts Locke's premise—knowledge from experience—and
rejects his conclusion that material substance underlies ideas.

Mechanism:
• We have only ideas (perceptions)
• Ideas cannot resemble matter
• Therefore we have no warrant for matter

Hume will carry this further:
no self, no necessity—only habit.

Berkeley is the bridge between Locke and Hume.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RELATION TO ANGLIAN CIVILIZATIONAL LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Berkeley stands between Locke and Hume.

Parallels:
• Empiricism without materialism
• Restraint of metaphysical claims
• Defence of faith through philosophy

He does not fit Anglia's later preference for convention over
certainty—but he sharpens the empiricist tool kit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Berkeley encodes **empiricism pushed to idealism**.

Lessons:
• Perception is primary; substance is inference.
• Philosophy can defend faith by limiting matter.
• The empiricist line leads from Locke → Berkeley → Hume.
