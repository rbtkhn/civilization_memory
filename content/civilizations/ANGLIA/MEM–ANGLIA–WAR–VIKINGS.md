I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Viking wars as **Anglia’s civilizational
trial by annihilation**: a prolonged sequence of raids, invasions,
occupations, and settlements that threatened erasure but instead
forced the creation of durable political, legal, and defensive systems.

The Vikings do not merely attack Anglia.
They force Anglia to become governable.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INITIAL RAID PHASE (DISRUPTION)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early Viking activity is episodic but devastating.

Characteristics:
• Coastal and riverine raids
• Monastic destruction
• Loot-driven violence
• Minimal territorial control

Effects:
• Collapse of cultural memory centers
• Loss of literacy and administration
• Exposure of fragmented authority

Anglia proves vulnerable as a collection of kingdoms.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. INVASION & OCCUPATION PHASE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Raiding escalates into conquest.

Dynamics:
• Large-scale armies
• Territorial seizure
• Political replacement of local rulers
• Sustained military pressure

The Great Heathen Army transforms threat into permanence.

Survival becomes uncertain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. THE DANELAW AS STRUCTURAL DIVISION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conflict stabilizes into partition.

Conditions:
• Scandinavian settlement zones
• Parallel legal systems
• Cultural and linguistic integration
• Persistent frontier violence

War embeds itself into geography.

Division becomes semi-permanent.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. DEFENSE SYSTEMIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Response shifts from heroism to structure.

Developments:
• Fortified burh networks
• Standing defensive obligations
• Coordinated regional command
• Naval countermeasures

Defense becomes administration.

War produces institutions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LAW AS WAR TERMINATION MECHANISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legal integration ends what force cannot.

Outcomes:
• Recognition of mixed custom
• Gradual unification of law
• Absorption of Viking elites
• Transition from conquest to order

Law succeeds where battle exhausts.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. ABSORPTION & CIVILIZATIONAL DIGESTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Viking identity is not erased—it is integrated.

Effects:
• Linguistic inheritance
• Legal hybridity
• Christianization
• Elite assimilation

The enemy becomes ancestry.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Defense-first governance logic
• Early administrative coordination
• Acceptance of composite identity
• Political unification pressure

The Viking wars end.
Their imprint defines Anglia.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Existential war accelerates institutional maturity.
• Defense systems precede political unity.
• Law ends wars that force cannot.
• Cultural survival does not require exclusion.
• The Viking wars are Anglia’s crucible.

Tension preserved without verdict.
