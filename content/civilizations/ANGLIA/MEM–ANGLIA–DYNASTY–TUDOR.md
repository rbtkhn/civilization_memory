I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Tudor dynasty as the **post-civil-war stabilizers
and state-centralizers of Anglia**: the house that terminated feudal
fragmentation, fused monarchy with national sovereignty, and laid the
institutional foundations of the modern English state.

The Tudors represent **order after dynastic collapse**, achieved through
authority, religious rupture, and administrative consolidation.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN IN COLLAPSE: THE WARS OF THE ROSES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Tudor ascent followed decades of dynastic civil war.

Context:
• Collapse of Plantagenet legitimacy  
• Feudal magnate warfare  
• Weak crown authority  
• Fiscal and military exhaustion  

Henry VII’s victory at Bosworth (1485) restored:
• Dynastic continuity  
• Central royal authority  
• Financial discipline  

Foundational premise:
→ Peace requires a strong crown.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. DYNASTIC STABILIZATION & FEUDAL CONTAINMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early Tudor governance focused on neutralizing aristocratic power.

Core mechanisms:
• Elimination of rival claimants  
• Bonds and recognizances to discipline nobles  
• Restriction of private armies  
• Restoration of crown finances  

Legitimacy was enforced through **order and predictability**, not charisma.

The monarchy reasserted primacy over the feudal elite.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. RELIGIOUS RUPTURE & SOVEREIGNTY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Under Henry VIII, the Tudor state severed papal authority.

Key actions:
• Break with Rome  
• Establishment of the Church of England  
• Dissolution of the monasteries  
• Transfer of religious wealth to the crown  

Strategic consequence:
→ Sovereignty relocated from transnational church to national monarch.

Religion became an instrument of state power.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. ADMINISTRATIVE & LEGAL CENTRALIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Tudors expanded bureaucratic governance.

Key developments:
• Professionalized royal councils  
• Expansion of common law authority  
• Central courts strengthened over local jurisdictions  
• Early fiscal-military coordination  

The state penetrated society through **law and administration**, not mass force.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LEGITIMACY, PERFORMANCE, & PUBLIC ORDER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tudor legitimacy rested on:
• Dynastic stability  
• Religious settlement (however contested)  
• Internal peace  
• Economic recovery  

Failure modes:
• Religious rebellion  
• Succession crises  
• Court factionalism  

Yet legitimacy endured because disorder never returned to pre-1485 levels.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. ELIZABETHAN CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Elizabeth I completed the Tudor synthesis.

Key achievements:
• Religious compromise (Elizabethan Settlement)  
• Naval expansion and maritime orientation  
• Containment of continental entanglements  
• Cultivation of national identity  

Strategic posture:
→ Avoid continental overreach; consolidate island power.

Anglia shifted toward maritime destiny.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. SUCCESSION & DYNASTIC TERMINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Tudor line ended peacefully in 1603.

Consequences:
• Stable dynastic transfer to the Stuarts  
• Institutional continuity preserved  
• No return to civil war  

The Tudors succeeded by making themselves **replaceable**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Dynastic peace enables institutional growth.
• Religious sovereignty strengthens national authority.
• Centralization after civil war is durable if elite power is contained.
• Administrative governance outlasts charismatic rule.
• The Tudors transformed monarchy into a state framework.

Tension preserved without verdict.
