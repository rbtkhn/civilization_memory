I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the United States of America as **Anglia’s
externalized civilizational core**: the polity that separates
politically while inheriting, scaling, and transforming Anglian
procedural, legal, and strategic grammar to continental and then
global magnitude.

The United States is not post-Anglian.
It is **Anglia unbound by island constraints**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN AS PROCEDURAL SECESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
American independence is institutional continuity, not rupture.

Inherited structures:
• Common law reasoning
• Representative assemblies
• Protestant moral individualism
• Suspicion of standing authority

Break:
• Crown sovereignty
• Imperial hierarchy

Continuity exceeds rebellion.

The revolution preserves form while rejecting command.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. CONSTITUTIONALIZATION OF ANGLIAN GRAMMAR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The United States formalizes what Anglia leaves implicit.

Features:
• Written constitution
• Codified separation of powers
• Enumerated rights
• Explicit federalism

Procedure becomes declarative.

Flexibility is traded for clarity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. TERRITORIAL SCALE & INTERNAL CONTINENTALITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Geography transforms Anglian logic.

Effects:
• Continental expansion replaces maritime dependence
• Internal frontiers substitute for overseas colonies
• Rivers and railways complement sea power

Anglian procedure adapts to land mass.

Empire becomes internal before external.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. POWER WITHOUT IMPERIAL FORM (INITIAL PHASE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Early U.S. power is anti-imperial in posture.

Traits:
• Avoidance of European entanglements
• Expansion without formal empire
• Law and settlement over conquest narrative

Anglia’s restraint is magnified by distance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. GLOBAL ASCENT & SYSTEM INHERITANAD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the 20th century, the United States assumes Anglia’s system role.

Continuities:
• Maritime dominance
• Financial centrality
• Alliance management
• Norm and standard setting

Differences:
• Scale
• Speed
• Ideological self-consciousness

The system survives transfer.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. IDEOLOGICAL OVERLAY & TENSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
American universalism overlays Anglian procedure.

Consequences:
• Moralized foreign policy
• Rights-language expansion
• Periodic legitimacy overreach

Jeffersonian abstraction amplifies Anglian structure.

Ideology rides procedure.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The United States encode **procedural scalability**.

Lessons:
• Anglian systems travel without crowns.
• Procedure can govern continents.
• Separation can strengthen civilizational continuity.
• Ideology accelerates reach—and risk.

The United States are Anglia at scale.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Independence preserved Anglian form.
• Procedure survived geography.
• Scale magnified both strength and contradiction.
• Maritime logic adapts to continental power.
• The U.S. is Anglia’s greatest successful export.

Tension preserved without verdict.
