────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Belgium as **Anglia’s continental tripwire**: a
manufactured buffer whose neutrality and sovereignty are enforced not
for its own sake, but to prevent hostile consolidation of the Channel
coast and Low Countries.

Belgium matters to Anglia because **who controls it matters more**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ORIGIN AS STRATEGIC CONSTRUCT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium is not an organic Anglian interest.

Context:
• Post-Napoleonic settlement (1830)
• Separation from the Netherlands
• Fear of French or German consolidation
• British treaty arbitration

Belgium is created to stabilize the balance,
not to express identity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. NEUTRALITY AS ENFORCED CONDITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgian neutrality is instrumental.

Functions:
• Barrier to continental hegemony
• Delay mechanism against invasion routes
• Diplomatic constraint on rivals

Neutrality is guaranteed externally.

Belgium’s sovereignty is conditional.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. BELGIUM AS WAR TRIGGER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium functions as a legal and moral switch.

1914:
• German violation of neutrality
• Anglian treaty obligation activated
• Channel security threatened

Belgium converts continental war into Anglian necessity.

A small state compels a great war.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. INDUSTRIAL & LOGISTICAL VALUE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Beyond symbolism, Belgium matters materially.

Attributes:
• Dense rail networks
• Industrial concentration
• Port access (Antwerp)

Control of Belgium reshapes campaign geometry.

The Channel begins inland.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. MODERN ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the post-war era:
• NATO headquarters
• EU institutional hub
• Managed neutrality replaced by alliance integration

Belgium remains a coordination node.

Buffer becomes platform.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Belgium encodes **Anglia’s buffer logic**.

Lessons:
• Small states can anchor great-power commitments.
• Treaties substitute for annexation.
• Geography converts legal abstractions into strategy.
• Channel security extends to continental littorals.

Belgium is Anglia’s forward warning line.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Anglia defends Belgium to defend itself.
• Neutrality works only if enforced.
• Buffers externalize security costs.
• Legal triggers can outweigh material interest.
• Belgium transforms proximity into obligation.

Tension preserved without verdict.
