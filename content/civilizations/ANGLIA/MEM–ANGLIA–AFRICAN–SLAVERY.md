I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves African slavery as **Anglia’s deepest civilizational
contradiction**: a system that powered maritime capitalism, imperial
expansion, and Atlantic dominance while fundamentally violating the
procedural, legal, and moral grammar Anglia claimed to embody.

Slavery is not an anomaly.
It is a structural compromise.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. SLAVERY AS ATLANTIC INFRASTRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Slavery is embedded in Anglia’s system rise.

Functions:
• Plantation labor engine
• Trade route profitability
• Insurance and credit expansion
• Naval protection of commerce

Human beings become cargo within legal frameworks.

Procedure enables exploitation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LEGALIZATION OF DEHUMANIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Slavery operates through law.

Mechanisms:
• Property classification of persons
• Contract enforcement
• Admiralty and commercial courts
• Colonial legal bifurcation

Illegality is not required for injustice.
Order suffices.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. METROPOLITAN DISTANCE & MORAL DISPLACEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core Anglia is insulated from violence.

Dynamics:
• Slavery externalized to colonies
• Moral cost deferred geographically
• Profit internalized, cruelty distant

Distance preserves legitimacy illusion.

The system functions because suffering is offshore.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RESISTANCE, ABOLITION, AND SYSTEMIC TENSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Opposition emerges internally.

Sources:
• Religious conscience movements
• Legal contradiction recognition
• Slave resistance and revolt
• Economic transition pressures

Abolition challenges profit logic.

Moral argument collides with system interest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. ABOLITION WITHOUT REPARATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Slavery ends procedurally.

Characteristics:
• Legislative termination
• Compensation to owners
• No structural redress for the enslaved

Order corrects itself minimally.

Justice is partial and delayed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
African slavery encodes **the limits of procedural morality**.

Lessons:
• Law can stabilize injustice.
• Distance enables moral evasion.
• Economic systems shape ethics.
• Abolition corrects legitimacy but not damage.

Slavery exposes Anglia’s moral ceiling.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Procedure can coexist with atrocity.
• Capitalism absorbed slavery efficiently.
• Moral correction lagged profit.
• Abolition repaired legitimacy, not lives.
• Slavery is Anglia’s unresolved wound.

Tension preserved without verdict.
