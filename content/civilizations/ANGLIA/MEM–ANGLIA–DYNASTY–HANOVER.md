I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the House of Hanover as the **anti-dynastic dynasty**
of Anglia: rulers whose significance lies not in personal authority,
but in the final subordination of monarchy to institutions.

Hanover represents **dynastic irrelevance as civilizational strength**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ACCESSION & STRUCTURAL CONTEXT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hanover ascends via the Act of Settlement (1701).

Key features:
• Foreign origin
• Weak cultural legitimacy
• No claim to divine right primacy
• Dependence on Parliament

This weakness is structural, not accidental.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. MONARCHY UNDER CONSTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hanoverian kings rule within tight limits.

Key dynamics:
• Cabinet government emerges
• Prime Minister becomes functional executive
• Parliament controls finance and war
• Crown becomes symbolic stabilizer

Power migrates:
→ From dynasty to procedure.

Winston Churchill, in *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 3 (*The Age of Revolution* [1956–1958]), traces Britain’s rise to world leadership across the period from Marlborough (1704) to Waterloo (1815)—the Hanoverian period in which Parliament, finance, and coalition warfare define Anglia’s method (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

IV. EMPIRE WITHOUT DYNASTIC COMMAND
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The British Empire expands dramatically under Hanover.

Notable paradox:
• Territorial and economic expansion
• Minimal royal strategic direction
• Administration driven by Parliament, merchants, and bureaucracy

Empire becomes:
• Commercial
• Naval
• Legal-administrative

Not dynastic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LEGITIMACY TRANSFORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Legitimacy shifts from:
• Bloodline → Continuity
• Authority → Stability
• Command → Arbitration

The monarchy survives by **withdrawing from decision-making**.

Survival through abdication of power.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. LATE HANOVER & TRANSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
By Victoria’s reign:
• Monarchy is ceremonial
• Public symbolism replaces governance
• Dynastic politics cease to matter

The transition to modern constitutional monarchy is complete.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. CIVILIZATIONAL FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hanover’s function is negative capability:
• Prevents executive personalization
• Absorbs political change without rupture
• Stabilizes continuity through restraint

This is Anglia’s mature form.
