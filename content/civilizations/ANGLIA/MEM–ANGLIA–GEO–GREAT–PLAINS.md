────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Great Plains as **Anglia’s continental
absorption zone**: a vast land system that converts territory,
migration, and mechanized agriculture into scale, surplus, and
strategic depth.

Where the Great Lakes extend maritime logic inland,
the Great Plains **absorb the interior**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. THE GREAT PLAINS AS CONTINENTAL INTERIOR SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Great Plains function as a land-based system of scale.

Characteristics:
• Extreme horizontal depth
• Low natural barriers
• Climatic volatility
• High mechanization suitability

They reward expansion, not fortification.

Control is demographic and productive rather than defensive.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. AGRARIAN SCALE & MECHANIZED SURPLUS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Plains enable industrialized agriculture.

Functions:
• Large-scale grain production
• Mechanized farming adoption
• Surplus generation for export
• Food-price stabilization for urban centers

Land abundance substitutes for yield intensity.

The Plains feed Anglia’s industrial and maritime systems indirectly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SETTLEMENT, MIGRATION, AND ABSORPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Great Plains operate as a demographic sink.

Patterns:
• Mass settler migration
• Indigenous displacement
• Rail-driven town formation
• Rapid jurisdictional incorporation

The land absorbs population faster than institutions can form.

Order follows settlement, not the reverse.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. RAIL, LAW, AND PROCEDURAL INTEGRATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Plains require procedural imposition.

Mechanisms:
• Rail networks as governance skeleton
• Federal land law (homesteading, surveys)
• Standardized property regimes
• Market integration through futures and finance

Unlike the Lakes, water does not govern.
Procedure must replace geography.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. SECURITY FUNCTION & STRATEGIC DEPTH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Great Plains provide depth through emptiness.

Effects:
• Distance absorbs shock
• Mobilization space exceeds adversary reach
• Limited immediate strategic targets

Security is passive but extensive.

The Plains dilute threat rather than confront it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. ECOLOGICAL LIMITS & SYSTEM STRESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Plains expose environmental constraint.

Stress signals:
• Soil depletion
• Drought cycles
• Dust Bowl collapse
• Over-leverage of land productivity

Scale magnifies both output and failure.

The system punishes mismanagement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. MODERN TRANSFORMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In the modern era:
• Agriculture consolidates
• Population density declines
• Automation replaces labor
• Strategic value persists as food security

The Plains shift from settlement engine to reserve capacity.

Latent power replaces visible growth.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Great Plains encode **continental absorption power**.

Lessons:
• Land scale enables endurance through surplus.
• Rail and law must substitute for water.
• Empty space is strategic depth.
• Environmental limits enforce humility.

The Plains are Anglia’s quiet interior ballast.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The Plains convert territory into surplus.
• Scale absorbs demographic and strategic pressure.
• Procedure replaces geography where water is absent.
• Environmental failure is the dominant risk.
• Interior depth complements maritime reach.

Tension preserved without verdict.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
XIV. COUNTERFACTUAL BOUNDARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Plausible alternative:
• The Great Plains remain sparsely settled pastoral land
  without large-scale mechanized agriculture.

Why it did not occur:
• Rail integration, land law standardization, and global
  food demand made non-absorption procedurally unsustainable.
