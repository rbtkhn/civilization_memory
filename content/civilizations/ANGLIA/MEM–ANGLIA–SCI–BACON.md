I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Francis Bacon as **Anglia's prophet of
operative knowledge**: the figure who insists that knowledge
should be useful—"human knowledge and human power meet in one"—
and who advocates observation, experiment, and inductive reasoning
as the foundation of a renewed natural philosophy.

Bacon does not found modern science alone.
He **announces its program**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT & FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bacon writes at the threshold of the scientific revolution.

Context:
• Late Elizabethan and early Stuart England
• Scholastic and Aristotelian dominance in natural philosophy
• Growing interest in experiment and practical knowledge
• Bacon's career in law, politics, and natural philosophy

Bacon seeks a "Great Instauration" of knowledge:
renewal through method, not authority.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. KNOWLEDGE AND POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bacon's central claim: knowledge is for use.

Core ideas:
• Human knowledge and human power meet in one
• To know is to be able to produce effects
• Speculation without operation is vain

This is **operative knowledge**.

Science is not contemplation alone;
it is the basis of human improvement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. METHOD: OBSERVATION AND INDUCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bacon rejects deduction from first principles.

Key ideas:
• Observation and experiment as foundation
• Induction from particulars to general laws
• Rejection of idols (prejudice, tradition, language)

He does not complete the new method.
He **prescribes it**.

Newton and others will realize it.

PRIMARY SOURCE ANCHOR A — *NOVUM ORGANUM*

Source:
• Francis Bacon, *Novum Organum* (1620)

Verbatim Quote:
"Human knowledge and human power meet in one; for where the cause is not known the effect cannot be produced."

Context Function:
This anchors Bacon's view that knowledge is operative; to know is to be able to produce effects.

Provenance Confidence: HIGH

Bacon is Anglia's **prophet of scientific method**—
