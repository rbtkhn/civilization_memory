I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Gladstone as **Anglia’s Victorian moral-reform
instrument**: the statesman who translated liberal conviction,
parliamentary oratory, and Irish Home Rule advocacy into repeated
premierships and lasting legitimacy tension—reform without revolution,
moral rhetoric alongside imperial continuity.

Gladstone does not dismantle the empire.
He **challenges its conscience**—and in challenging, encodes Anglia’s
habit of moral argument within procedure.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

III. REFORM AS PARLIAMENTARY INSTRUMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gladstone operates **through Parliament and public moral argument**.

Core roles:
• Fiscal and electoral reform (budget, franchise)
• Irish Land Acts and Home Rule advocacy
• Moral rhetoric (Bulgarian Horrors, imperial conscience)
• Liberal Party consolidation

Authority flows through oratory and conviction—not through crown or
executive dominance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. IRELAND & HOME RULE — LEGITIMACY STRESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gladstone’s Irish policy encodes Anglia’s deepest legitimacy tension.

Characteristics:
• Home Rule bills (1886, 1893); defeat and division
• Liberal split; Unionist resistance
• Moral case for Irish self-government; imperial unity case against
• Procedure stretched by national question

He does not resolve the Irish question.
He **forces it onto the agenda**—and encodes the cost of deferral.
