I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Disraeli as **Anglia’s Victorian conservative-empire
instrument**: the statesman who translated Tory tradition, imperial
rhetoric, and Eastern Question diplomacy into a rival vision to
Gladstone—empire as romance, crown as symbol, and conservatism as
national identity.

Disraeli does not invent the empire.
He **narrates it**—and in narrating, encodes Anglia’s habit of
imperial self-understanding alongside parliamentary procedure.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

III. EMPIRE AS PARLIAMENTARY NARRATIVE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Disraeli operates **through Parliament and imperial symbolism**.

Core roles:
• Eastern Question; Congress of Berlin (1878)
• Imperial rhetoric (India, Crown as Empress)
• Conservative Party consolidation; One Nation
• Rivalry with Gladstone—empire vs. moral reform

Authority flows through narrative and symbol—crown, empire, nation—not
through Gladstone’s moral oratory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. CROWN & SYMBOL — LEGITIMACY CONSOLIDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Disraeli’s use of crown and empire encodes Anglia’s symbolic turn.

Characteristics:
• Victoria as Empress of India (1876)
• Imperial pageantry; Conservative identity
• Balance-of-power diplomacy (Berlin)
• Procedure and symbol combined

He does not restore royal power.
He **elevates its visibility**—and encodes empire as national story.
