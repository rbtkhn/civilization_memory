I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Alexander Hamilton as **Anglia’s administrative
reconstitution engine**: the architect who rebuilt Anglian financial,
legal, and executive capacity inside a republican shell after imperial
separation.

Hamilton restores system after rupture.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. POST-REVOLUTIONARY VOID
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton confronts systemic fragility.

Context:
• War debt
• Weak central authority
• Fragmented fiscal power
• Legitimacy without machinery

Independence exists; capacity does not.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FINANCE AS SOVEREIGNTY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton reintroduces Anglian financial logic.

Instruments:
• Assumption of state debts
• Public credit consolidation
• National bank creation
• Reliable revenue streams

Credit becomes national glue.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. EXECUTIVE PRIMACY & ENERGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton elevates administration.

Beliefs:
• Strong executive necessary for stability
• Law requires enforcement capacity
• Procedure must be backed by power

Energy in government replaces revolutionary spontaneity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. CONTINUITY WITH ANGLIAN SYSTEMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton consciously mirrors Anglia.

Parallels:
• Treasury-centered governance
• Credit as legitimacy
• Administrative professionalism

Republican form contains imperial machinery.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CONFLICT WITH JEFFERSONIAN IDEALISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton collides with declarative universalism.

Tensions:
• Administration vs abstraction
• Credit vs agrarian virtue
• State capacity vs rhetorical liberty

This conflict defines American oscillation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. FAILURE TO SECURE PERSONAL LEGITIMACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hamilton’s system outlives him.

Limits:
• Elitist reputation
• Weak popular appeal
• Vulnerability to moral attack

Procedure survives charisma.

VIII. LONG-TERM CIVILIZATIONAL CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects:
• Financial state normalized
• Executive authority institutionalized
• Anglian continuity preserved post-independence

Hamilton ensures survival of form over ideology.
