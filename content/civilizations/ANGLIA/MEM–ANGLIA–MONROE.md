III. THE MONROE DOCTRINE AS STRATEGIC SIGNAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Monroe Doctrine is declarative, not enforceable alone.

Core claims:
• No new European colonization in the Americas
• Non-interference norm asserted
• U.S. abstains from European power struggles

Power is signaled before it is possessed.

This mirrors Anglian practice of early declaration backed by future capacity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. DEPENDENCE ON ANGLIAN SEA POWER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The doctrine relies implicitly on Britain.

Reality:
• Royal Navy enforces the Atlantic order
• U.S. capacity is embryonic
• Declaration substitutes for immediate force

Anglia underwrites the rule it publicly disclaims.

Form precedes force.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Monroe emphasizes internal coherence.

Stability precedes assertion.
