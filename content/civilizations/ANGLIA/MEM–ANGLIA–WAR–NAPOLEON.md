I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Napoleonic Wars as Anglia’s defining test of
systemic endurance: the conflict in which Anglia confronted a
revolutionary–imperial continental challenger not by mirroring its form,
but by exhausting it through finance, sea power, coalition management,
and institutional patience.

For Anglia, Napoleon represents threat without succession.
He challenges power, but not the underlying order Anglia embodies.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. INITIAL SHOCK & STRATEGIC RECOGNITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The French Revolution is initially misread as a continental anomaly.

Correction follows quickly.

Anglian recognition:
• Revolutionary France is not a regime change, but a mobilization engine
• Ideology amplifies manpower and legitimacy
• Continental domination threatens maritime balance

Strategic premise:
→ Francia seeks decision through land; Anglia survives through time.

Napoleon is identified as systemic accelerant, not negotiable ruler.

Winston Churchill, in *A History of the English-Speaking Peoples*, Vol. 3 (*The Age of Revolution* [1956–1958]), traces the French Revolution and its shadow over Europe, the rise of Napoleon, and the long Anglo-French rivalry; the volume frames Britain’s rise to world leadership through coalition warfare and financial endurance until Napoleon’s defeat at Waterloo (ARC-T-MODERN) [ERC-SECONDARY].

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. ANGLO–NAPOLEONIC ASYMMETRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia refuses symmetry.

Key asymmetries:
• Navy over army
• Credit over conscription
• Coalitions over empire
• Endurance over decision

Napoleon seeks culmination points.
Anglia seeks non-culmination.

The war is structured as attrition of legitimacy, not territory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. WAR AS FINANCIAL–MARITIME SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia transforms war into infrastructure.

Core mechanisms:
• Naval blockade
• Global trade protection
• Long-term public credit
• Subsidization of continental allies

Foundational logic:
→ Anglia fights with balance sheets and sea lanes.

Victory does not require invasion of France.
It requires France to collapse under its own velocity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. COALITION MANAGEMENT AS GRAND STRATEGY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia functions as coalition stabilizer.

Key roles:
• Financier of resistance
• Diplomatic anchor
• Safe rear-area economy
• Continuity guarantor

Coalitions fail repeatedly—
but Anglia remains constant.

Napoleon defeats armies.
Anglia outlasts systems.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. NAPOLEON AS ANGLIAN ANTI-TYPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon embodies everything Anglia avoids.

Contrasts:
• Personal rule vs procedural authority
• Speed vs resilience
• Centralization vs dispersion
• Glory vs solvency

Anglia does not seek to replace Napoleon.
It seeks to survive him.

This difference determines outcome.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. PENINSULAR WAR & SYSTEMIC BLEEDING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Peninsular War becomes decisive.

Anglian advantages:
• Maritime supply
• Peripheral pressure
• Local resistance amplification

Napoleon is drawn into:
• Endless occupation
• Resource drain
• Prestige erosion

Anglia converts Spain and Portugal into imperial sand.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. DEFEAT WITHOUT CONQUEST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Napoleon is defeated, but not conquered by Anglia directly.

Terminal dynamics:
• Russian catastrophe
• Coalition massing
• Financial exhaustion
• Legitimacy collapse

Waterloo symbolizes closure,
but exhaustion precedes it.

Anglia wins by still being there.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. POST-WAR ORDER & ANGLIAN ASCENDANCY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After 1815, Anglia emerges structurally dominant.

Outcomes:
• Maritime supremacy unchallenged
• Financial system globalized
• Balance-of-power manager
• Empire expanded indirectly

Napoleon is gone.
Anglia’s system scales.

X. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Endurance defeats brilliance when systems are asymmetric.
• Financial legitimacy can outfight military genius.
• Coalitions matter more than victories.
• Sea power converts time into advantage.
• Anglia learned that survival is strategy.

Tension preserved without verdict.
