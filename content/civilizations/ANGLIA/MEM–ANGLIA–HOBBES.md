────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Thomas Hobbes as **Anglia’s theorist of existential
order**: the thinker who confronts civilizational collapse directly and
argues that authority must precede liberty if society is to survive.

Hobbes does not refine Anglia’s system.
He **rescues it from disintegration**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. CONTEXT & FORMATIVE CONDITIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hobbes is forged by catastrophe.

Context:
• English Civil War
• Regicide of Charles I
• Sectarian fragmentation
• Breakdown of authority

For Hobbes, disorder is not hypothetical.
It is lived experience.

Political theory becomes damage control.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. FEAR AS CIVILIZATIONAL FOUNDATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hobbes begins with pessimism.

Assumptions:
• Humans are equal in capacity to kill
• Competition is natural
• Trust collapses under stress

Without authority, life becomes:
“solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short.”

Order is not optional.
It is existential.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SOVEREIGNTY WITHOUT MORAL PRETENSE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hobbes strips authority of moral ornament.

Principles:
• Sovereign power must be absolute
• Division of authority invites collapse
• Law precedes justice

Obedience is rational self-preservation,
not moral endorsement.

This is **anti-ideal governance**.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. ABSOLUTISM AS TEMPORARY NECESSITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hobbes does not celebrate tyranny.

He argues necessity:
• Authority must be indivisible during crisis
• Liberty emerges only after order
• Debate follows survival

His absolutism is situational,
not metaphysical.

Fear is the stabilizer of last resort.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. RELATION TO ANGLIAN CIVILIZATIONAL LOGIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hobbes articulates Anglia’s darkest memory.

He explains:
• Why civil war cannot be romanticized
• Why procedure must dominate passion
• Why fragmentation invites catastrophe

Hobbes is Anglia’s **emergency doctrine**.

Locke refines what Hobbes saves.
