I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves the Crusades as **Anglia’s peripheral prestige wars**:
conflicts entered for dynastic honor, spiritual legitimacy, and elite
reputation rather than for institutional expansion or systemic gain.

For Anglia, the Crusades are participation without absorption.
They generate heroes, not structures.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. ENTRY CONDITIONS & MOTIVATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia enters the Crusades episodically.

Motivations:
• Dynastic honor
• Papal legitimacy signaling
• Noble prestige competition
• Continental peer alignment

The Crusades are external to Anglia’s core survival concerns.

No permanent institutional commitment follows.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. LIMITED CIVILIZATIONAL INVESTMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike Francia, Anglia does not internalize Crusader identity.

Characteristics:
• Small expeditionary contingents
• Reliance on continental allies
• Absence of sustained logistics base
• No Crusader-state governance imprint

Holy war remains optional, not formative.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SEA ACCESS WITHOUT SYSTEMIZATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Anglia’s maritime access offers tactical flexibility.

Effects:
• Easier embarkation and return
• Reduced overland dependency
• Faster disengagement

However:
• No naval doctrine emerges
• No permanent Levantine infrastructure

Sea access enables exit, not empire.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. HEROIC MEMORY OVER INSTITUTIONAL MEMORY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Crusading memory in Anglia is personalized.

Patterns:
• Individual kings and nobles celebrated
• Campaigns remembered as episodes
• No enduring administrative myth

Crusades do not become Anglia’s identity grammar.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. CIVILIZATIONAL DIFFERENCE FROM FRANCIA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Divergence emerges clearly.

Contrast:
• Francia: Crusades forge noble identity
• Anglia: Crusades produce isolated prestige

Anglia avoids myth absorption.
This limits glory—but preserves institutional focus.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LONG-TERM CONSEQUENCES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Enduring effects are muted.

Outcomes:
• Minimal institutional learning
• No permanent eastern commitments
• Reinforced preference for optional wars

Anglia learns that distance reduces obligation.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Participation does not require absorption.
• Prestige wars can remain externalized.
• Maritime access enables clean exits.
• Heroes do not equal institutions.
• Anglia resists identity capture by holy war.

Tension preserved without verdict.
