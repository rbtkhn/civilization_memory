I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves New Zealand as **Anglia’s treaty-based legitimacy
experiment**: a society where Anglian procedural authority is explicitly
negotiated with an indigenous population through formal agreement
rather than imposed solely by settlement or administration.

New Zealand is Anglia **with consent as a founding variable**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. FOUNDING THROUGH TREATY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Treaty of Waitangi (1840) defines origin.

Context:
• British imperial expansion
• Maori political organization
• Desire to preempt rival powers

Sovereignty is asserted—but framed contractually.

Legitimacy is declared through agreement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. PROCEDURE WITH MORAL CLAIM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Governance carries ethical expectation.

Features:
• Treaty interpretation disputes
• Legal pluralism
• Judicial remediation

Procedure is burdened with justice claims.

Law must justify itself morally.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IV. SMALL SCALE & SOCIAL COHERENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Geography supports institutional intimacy.

Effects:
• Limited population
• High state visibility
• Rapid policy feedback

Governance is personal as well as procedural.

Distance between ruler and ruled is narrow.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. EVOLUTIONARY AUTONOMY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Self-rule develops without rebellion.

Stages:
• Colonial administration
• Responsible government
• Dominion status
• Independent foreign policy

Continuity remains intact.

Authority transforms without rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. STRATEGIC ROLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
New Zealand contributes selectively.

Roles:
• Southern Pacific stability
• Alliance participation with restraint
• Normative influence disproportionate to size

Power is moralized rather than projected.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. IDENTITY THROUGH GOVERNANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
National identity emphasizes fairness.

Traits:
• Rule of law
• Social trust
• Political moderation

Legitimacy is lived, not asserted.

Procedure becomes culture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIII. CIVILIZATIONAL MEMORY FUNCTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
New Zealand encodes **ethical proceduralism**.

Lessons:
• Consent matters to durability.
• Law accumulates moral obligation.
• Small states can model legitimacy.
• Treaties outlive founders.

New Zealand is Anglia’s conscience case.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IX. CONTINUITY INSIGHTS (RAW)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Treaty created legitimacy tension and endurance.
• Procedure carried ethical weight.
• Autonomy grew peacefully.
• Scale amplified accountability.
• New Zealand is Anglia with a moral mirror.

Tension preserved without verdict.
