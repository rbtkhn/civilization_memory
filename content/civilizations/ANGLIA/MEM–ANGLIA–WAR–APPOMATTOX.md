I. MEMORY PURPOSE & SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This file preserves Appomattox as **the procedural termination point of
Anglia’s American civil war**: the moment when overwhelming coercive
force is deliberately restrained in order to preserve institutional
continuity, legitimacy, and post-war governability.

Appomattox is not a battlefield.
It is a **governance decision executed at the moment of victory**.

Contradictions are preserved without synthesis.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
II. STRATEGIC CONTEXT AT WAR’S END
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
By early 1865, Confederate military defeat is no longer theoretical.
After Gettysburg and Vicksburg, Confederate capacity erodes steadily
through attrition, logistical collapse, and manpower exhaustion.

Union advantages:
• Industrial dominance
• Superior logistics
• Control of river systems
• Sustained political legitimacy

Yet the final challenge is not victory—it is **ending the war without
destroying the system the war was fought to preserve**.

The Union must win without becoming revolutionary.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
III. APPOMATTOX AS PROCEDURAL CHOICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The surrender at Appomattox Court House represents a conscious choice
to frame victory as restoration rather than annihilation.

Grant’s terms:
• No imprisonment of Confederate soldiers
• Officers retain sidearms
• Soldiers paroled, not prosecuted
• Immediate cessation without humiliation ritual

This is not generosity alone.
It is **system preservation logic**.

The Union chooses legitimacy over vengeance.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
V. LEGITIMACY MANAGEMENT AT THE MOMENT OF FORCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Appomattox demonstrates a core Anglian principle:
**the manner of victory determines postwar stability**.

Key dynamics:
• No tribunals
• No mass executions
• No forced ideological confession
• No revolutionary re-foundation

The Union asserts that procedural authority was never illegitimate—only
contested.

This allows defeated elites to exit without martyrdom.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VI. DISTINCTION FROM REVOLUTIONARY ENDINGS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Unlike continental revolutions:
• No regime reset
• No constitutional replacement
• No mass purge
• No ideological rechristening

The Constitution persists.
Institutions absorb the trauma.
The system bends but does not break.

This distinguishes Anglian civil war resolution from French or Russian
patterns of terminal rupture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VII. LIMITS OF APPOMATTOX
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Despite its procedural success, Appomattox does not resolve all
contradictions.

Unresolved:
• Racial hierarchy persists
• Reconstruction legitimacy contested
• Southern political reintegration delayed
• Moral reconciliation incomplete

Procedure restores order.
Justice remains partial.

This gap becomes the terrain of postwar conflict.
